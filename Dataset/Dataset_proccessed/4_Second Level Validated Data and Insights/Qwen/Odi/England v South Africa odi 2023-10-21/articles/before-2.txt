South Africa's Rainy Affair In World Cups May Yet Again Cost Them A Spot In 2023 World Cup Semis As Arabian Storm Brews

South Africa have been clinical in their performance so far in the ODI World Cup 2023 and have looked more than impressive, both with the bat and ball. In fact, other than India and New Zealand, South Africa remain the only other side to have remained unbeaten so far in the tournament, having played two matches, with the third one underway against the Netherlands, which they are expected to win.
South Africa could not have asked for a better start to the tournament but what they are likely to witness in their next couple of matches scheduled to be held in Mumbai may evoke memories of past disappointments where a rainy affair eventually went on to cost them a spot - once in a World Cup final in 1992 and in another - a place in the next stage of the tournament in 2003.
The India Meteorological Department (IMD) has predicted a potential cyclonic storm emerging in the southeastern Arabian Sea and the nearby Lakshadweep region within the next 48 hours due to a low-pressure area expected to develop in the Arabian Sea.
The Proteas are scheduled to play England and Bangladesh at the Wankhede Stadium in Mumbai on the 21st and 24th of October. While the match between South Africa and England is expected to be clear, the match against Bangladesh might just fall in the eye of the storm.
'Decent chance of Cyclone around 22-23 October in Central Arabian Sea. Still too early to say about its track after that,' according to an X user who goes by the name @IndiaWeatherMan.
Mumbai Weather
Mumbai_Cyclone
"21st Eng-Sa should be fine. 24th Ban-Sa depends on Cyclone track. Right now anywhere between Yemen-Oman to Mumbai-Gujarat in play," he further said while responding to another user's query.
The ongoing match between South Africa and the Netherlands in Dharamsala was also delayed due to rain and was reduced to 43-overs per side. The weather is expected to be clear for the rest of the match.
1992 World Cup EXIT: The South Africans were well on their way to the final before rain played a decisive factor in their semi-final match against England. The Proteas needed 22 off 13 balls when rain forced the play to stop. After the rain rule was enforced, the target for South Africa was 21 off 1 ball. And that was that for South Africa.
2003 World Cup Group Stage Exit: South Africa needed a win against Sri Lanka to progress to the next stage of the tournament. The instructions were clear to Mark Boucher in the 45th over of the innings that the target for South Africa to win was 229 but it turned out that the Proteas needed one more run. Boucher, who was on-strike didn't intend to take a single off the last ball of the 45th over, mistakenly knowing that they had achieved the Duckworth-Lewis target. The rain didn't allow the players to return to the pitch. It was an error in calculation in a rain-interrupted contest that had once again had the final say in South Africa's untimely exit from the tournament.
