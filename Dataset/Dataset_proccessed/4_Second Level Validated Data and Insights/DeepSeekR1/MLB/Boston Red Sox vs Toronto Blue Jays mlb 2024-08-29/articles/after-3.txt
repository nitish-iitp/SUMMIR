BOTTOM LINE: The Boston Red Sox host the Toronto Blue Jays on Thursday.

Boston has a 69-64 record overall and a 31-37 record at home. The Red Sox have a 53-8 record in games when they have more hits than their opponents.

Toronto has gone 31-37 in road games and 65-70 overall. Blue Jays hitters have a collective .312 on-base percentage, the seventh-ranked percentage in the AL.

The teams meet Thursday for the 10th time this season. The Red Sox are ahead 6-3 in the season series.

TOP PERFORMERS: Rafael Devers leads the Red Sox with 28 home runs while slugging .571. Jarren Duran is 14-for-35 with five doubles, five home runs and seven RBI over the last 10 games.
