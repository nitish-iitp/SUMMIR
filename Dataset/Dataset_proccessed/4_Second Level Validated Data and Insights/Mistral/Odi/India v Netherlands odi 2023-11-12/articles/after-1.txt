IND v NED, World Cup 2023: Rohit Sharma breaks AB de Villiers record for most sixes in a calendar year in ODIs
IND vs NED, World Cup 2023: Rohit Sharma continued his aggressive approach at the top of the batting order as the India captain and Shubman Gill added 90 in the first 10 overs. During the powerplay, Rohit Sharma broke AB de Villiers's 8-year-old record in Bengaluru.

In Short
Rohit Sharma hit his 59th six of 2023 in ODI cricket on Sunday
Rohit broke an 8-year-old record held by AB de Villiers
Rohit has been ruthless for India at the top in Worl Cup 2023

India captain Rohit Sharma is on a record-breaking spree. The skipper continued his ruthless run at the top in the ongoing ODI World Cup as he gave India a rapid start in their final league game of the tournament against the Netherlands in Bengaluru on Sunday.

| IND vs NED Scorecard |

On Diwali day, there was fireworks in Bengaluru, this time from the two Indian openers as Rohit Sharma and Shubman Gill went after the Netherlands bowlers after Rohit Sharma won the toss and opted to bat. India raced to 73 for 0 in just 8 overs. Two batters had hit 8 boundaries and 4 sixes in the first 8 overs of the game.

Rohit Sharma was tied with AB de Villiers after the end of India's win over South Africa in terms of most sixes hit by a batter in ODI cricket in a calendar year. And Rohit broke the record on Sunday when he launched off-spinner Colin Ackermann into the long-on stands in the 7th over of the contest.

MOST SIXES IN ODIS IN A CALENDAR YEAR

Rohit Sharma - 59 in 2023 in 24 innings

AB de Villiers - 58 in 2015 in 18 innings

Chris Gayle - 56 sixes 2019 in 15 innings

Shahid Afridi - 48 sixes in 2002 in 36 innings

Rohit Sharma went from the word go as he hit off-spinner Aryan Dutt to a couple of boundaries in the first over of the match. Rohit swept Aryan and did not try to keep it down as he signaled his intentions from the go.

Shubman Gill was also in the mood to decimate the Dutch bowlers as he raced past 40, hitting 4 sixes by that time. The crowd was building up on the festival day at the M Chinnaswamy Stadium and they were treated to some ruthless, and high-quality hitting from the two Indian openers.

India won the toss and went in with an unchanged XI as they were in no mood to drop their intensity after having won 8 matches on the trot. india are assured of a semi-finals berth and they will face New Zealand in a repeat of the 2019 semi-final, in Mumbai on Wednesday.

Rohit Sharma's change in approach has shone through in the World Cup as the skipper has scored nearly 500 runs at a strike rate of over 120, playing fearless cricket at the top.
