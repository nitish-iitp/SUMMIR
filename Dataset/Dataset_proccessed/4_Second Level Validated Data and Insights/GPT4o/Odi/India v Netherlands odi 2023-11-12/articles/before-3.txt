India vs Netherlands Weather Report, World Cup 2023: Will rain appear again in Bengaluru?

By Tarkesh Jha  
November 11, 2023, 3:58:05 PM IST (Published)
2 Min Read
India will be playing their last match of the ICC Men's ODI World Cup 2023 league stages against Netherlands at the M. Chinnaswamy Stadium in Bengaluru on November 12, Sunday.



The clash between New Zealand and Pakistan at this venue was interrupted due to rains and hence there are doubts over whether the upcoming India game will have to deal with troublesome weather.

Bengaluru Weather Update - November 12, 2023 

The weather is likely to be mostly sunny and pleasant at the start of the game. The temperature will hover around 28°C with minimal (2%) probability of precipitation. Cloud cover will be significantly high at 70% and the wind will blow towards the eastern direction at 19 km/hr. Humidity will be around 48% and the dew point will be at 15°.

Also Read: Watch: Team India plays volleyball but with a twist ahead of Netherlands fixture in ODI World Cup

The climate will clear out by evening and the temperature will range around 19°C and the wind will blow at the same direction at 11 km/hr. Cloud cover will reduce marginally to 56% and the dew point will become 17°. Humidity will rise again to 79% and the wind gusts will be at 22 km/hr. Overall, it appears that there are no chances of rainfall or a shortened game on Sunday.

Predicted Playing XI

India: Rohit Sharma (c), Shubman Gill, Virat Kohli, Shreyas Iyer, KL Rahul (wk), Suryakumar Yadav, Ravindra Jadeja, Ravichandran Ashwin, Shardul Thakur, Mohammed Shami, Mohammed Siraj

Netherlands: Wesley Barresi, Max O'Dowd, Colin Ackermann, Sybrand Engelbrecht, Scott Edwards (c&wk), Teja Nidamanuru, Bas de Leede, Logan van Beek, Roelof van der Merwe, Aryan Dutt, Paul van Meekeren

Where to Watch

The match will begin at 2 PM IST on November 12 and it will be telecast live on the Star Sports Network. It will also be streamed digitally simultaneously on Disney+Hotstar, with it being free to view for mobile users.
