Cincinnati Reds vs Minnesota Twins Betting Pick & Prediction 9/15/24
Sep 15, 2024
By: Ryan Knuppel

From Target Field in Minneapolis, we have the Reds and Twins facing off in an interleague matchup. First pitch for this one is set for 1:05 PM ET. The money line odds have the Twins at -160, while the Reds are the slight underdog at +136. The over/under line is sitting at 8.5 runs, and the forecast calls for broken clouds and temperatures in the upper 60s.

David Festa will be starting for the Twins, while the Reds are sending Rhett Lowder to the mound. Cincinnati is 73-77 this season, and they have won two straight, putting them 4th in the NL Central. The Twins are 3rd in the AL Central, but they have lost two in a row.

MINNESOTA TWINS VS CINCINNATI REDS BETTING PICK

The Pick: Minnesota Twins Moneyline -160

This game will be played at Target Field at 1:05 ET on Sunday, September 15th.

HOW TO BET THE REDS VS TWINS:
We have the Twins winning by a score of 6 to 5
However, if you're looking for a run line pick, we like the Reds to cover
As for an over/under pick, we have this game finishing with 11 runs and like the over

Cincinnati cruised to an easy 11-1 win over the Twins in the most recent game of this series. The Reds had a huge 4th inning, scoring nine of their eleven runs. As for the Twins, they scored their only run in the 1st inning. Heading into the game, the Reds were at +128 on the money line.

Nick Martinez pitched well for the Reds in this one, going six innings and giving up just one run. He finished the game with six strikeouts but didn't issue a single walk. Simeon Woods Richardson had a rough outing for the Twins, giving up three earned runs in just three innings of work.

TJ Friedl and Jake Fraley each homered for the Reds, while Jonathan India scored twice and drove in two runs while going 2/4. Noelvi Marte also had a two-hit game at the plate.

Reds Records & Stats

Cincinnati is 73-77 overall and 14 games behind the Brewers in the NL Central. The Reds have won two straight games, and they are 21-25 against other teams in the NL Central. So far, they are 36-39 at home and have gone 37-38 on the road.

The Reds have won two straight games as the underdog, and they are 40-44 as the underdog overall. As for their record as the favorite, it is currently 33-33. Cincinnati's overall series record is 18-27-3, and they are looking to close out this series with a win, leading 2-0 so far.

When the Reds are on the road, they have been a solid bet against the run line, going 47-28. They have covered in their last two games and have been particularly good as the underdog, going 55-29 against the run line. Their average run differential in winning games is +3.8, while it drops to -3.2 in losses.

Today's over/under line for the Cincinnati Reds' road game against the Minnesota Twins is set at 8.5 runs. The Reds have played to an over/under record of 69-74 this season, with an average combined run average of 8.7 runs per game. When the over/under line has been set at 8.5 runs, their record is 18-15. Over the course of the season, 48.0% of their games have had over/under lines set at 8.5 runs. Their current over streak is at 2 games.

Rhett Lowder will be making his third start of the season for the Reds, and this time it will be on the road against the Twins. Lowder picked up a win in his last outing, going 5 innings and striking out 3 while allowing 5 hits. He has yet to give up a home run this season.

Elly De La Cruz has been a bright spot for the Reds this season, as his 24 home runs are the most on the team, and he is batting .257 for the season. He is also 2nd on the team with 69 RBIs. Spencer Steer is the team's top run producer, as his 88 RBIs are 15th in the league, and he is 3rd on the team with 19 homers. However, he is batting just .231 for the season.

Jeimer Candelario is also near the top of the Reds' home run leaderboard, with 20 long balls, but like Steer, he has struggled with his batting average this season, coming in at just .225. Over his last eight games, TJ Friedl is hitting .370 with two homers and has driven in seven runs. He is also on a three-game hitting streak.

Twins Records & Stats

With an overall record of 78-70, the Twins are 6.5 games behind the Guardians for the AL Central lead. Currently, they are 3rd in the division and have gone 28-20 against other teams in the AL Central. The Twins have dropped two straight games, and they are 3-7 across their last 10 contests.

Minnesota has been good at home this year, putting together a record of 41-33. On the road, the Twins are an even 37-37 this season. As the favorite, the Twins are 62-41 and 16-29 as the underdog. So far, they have been good as the home favorite, going 34-26 this year. The Twins are also 26-17-4 in series this year.

The Twins have been a solid run line bet this season, going 68-80 overall. They are 31-43 against the run line at home and 37-37 on the road. Minnesota has been favored in 103 games, going 45-58 against the run line in those contests. When they are the underdog, they have gone 23-22 against the run line.

Minnesota's games have gone over the total in four straight contests, and the Twins have a combined run average of 9.2 runs per game. Their over/under record for the season is 75-67, and their games have averaged an over/under line of 8 runs per game. When the over/under line has been set at 8.5 runs, their record is 23-22, and they have played 14 games with that line, which accounts for 9.5% of their games this season.

Right-hander David Festa gets the start for the Twins today as he faces off against the Reds at home. Festa has made 10 starts this year and has a record of 2-6 with an ERA of 5.08. His WHIP for the season is 1.25, and opponents are batting .229 off the right-hander this year. Festa's last outing came on September 9th, where he took the loss, giving up four earned runs in four innings of work. He has taken the loss in each of his last three outings. Festa has allowed at least one home run in four straight starts.

Carlos Santana has been swinging a hot bat for the Twins, going 5/14 in his last four games with a home run and three RBIs. For the season, he is batting just .239, but his 21 homers and 63 RBIs are both the best marks on the team. Ryan Jeffers and Willi Castro are also near the top of the Twins' home run leaderboard, with 20 and 11 homers, respectively.

As a team, the Twins are averaging 4.7 runs per game, which is 11th in the league. They have been a better offense at home, averaging 4.9 runs per game. Overall, they are the 9th ranked home run hitting team in the league and have the 8th best team batting average in the MLB.
