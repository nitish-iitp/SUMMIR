BOTTOM LINE: The Toronto Blue Jays will try to sweep a three-game series with a victory against the St. Louis Cardinals.

Toronto has a 37-37 record in home games and a 71-78 record overall. Blue Jays hitters have a collective .314 on-base percentage, the sixth-ranked percentage in the AL.

St. Louis is 35-39 on the road and 74-74 overall. The Cardinals have the seventh-ranked team batting average in the NL at .246.

The matchup Sunday is the third time these teams square off this season.

TOP PERFORMERS: Vladimir Guerrero Jr. has 41 doubles, a triple and 28 home runs while hitting .320 for the Blue Jays. Spencer Horwitz is 12-for-28 with four doubles and three home runs over the last 10 games.
