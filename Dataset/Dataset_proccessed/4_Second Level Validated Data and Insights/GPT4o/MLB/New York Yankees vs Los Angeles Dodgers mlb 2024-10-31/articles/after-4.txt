The Los Angeles Dodgers have captured their eighth World Series championship, their first since 2020 and their first following a 162-game season since 1988.

1955: Dodgers over Yankees in 7

After being defeated by the Yankees in the World Series four times in a span of eight years, the Dodgers finally broke through in a series best remembered for Jackie Robinson‚Äôs steal of home. Johnny Podres was the MVP ‚Äî the first time the award was ever given ‚Äî after pitching a shutout in Game 7. It was the Dodgers‚Äô only championship in Brooklyn. 1959: Dodgers over White Sox in 6

The first west coast World Series drew a record 92,706 fans to the Los Angeles Memorial Coliseum for Game 5. Dodgers reliever Larry Sherry got two wins and two saves and was named series MVP.

1963: Dodgers over Yankees in 4

It‚Äôs fitting that the only Dodgers championship clinched at home was the first World Series ever played at Dodger Stadium. Sandy Koufax struck out 15 to beat Whitey Ford in Game 1, and the two had a rematch in Game 4 with Koufax pitching a complete game for a 2-1 Dodgers victory.

1965: Dodgers over Twins in 7

Koufax didn‚Äôt start Game 1 because it fell on the Jewish holy day of Yom Kippur, but he would still start three games, including a shutout in the clincher on two days' rest. 1981: Dodgers over Yankees in 6

The Dodgers had lost to the Yankees in 1977 and 1978, and they lost the first two games of this series at Yankee Stadium. To get back in it, the Dodgers won three straight one-run games at home, and after finishing the series in New York, three Dodgers ‚Äî Ron Cey, Pedro Guerrero and Steve Yeager ‚Äî shared the MVP.

1988: Dodgers over A‚Äôs in 5

2020: Dodgers over Rays in 6

After a 60-game regular season, the Dodgers played four rounds of an expanded playoff format to win their first championship in more than 30 years. Freeman joins Dodgers World Series MVPs

Getty Images

Freddie Freeman has been named World Series MVP. He homered in the first four games of the series, setting a record by homering in six consecutive World Series games dating to 2021. He matched another record by driving in 12 runs, tying 1960 Bobby Richardson for most RBI in a World Series. (Richardson got seven games, and his team lost. Freeman got five.)

Here are the MVPs from the Dodgers‚Äô previous seven World Series:

1955: SP Johnny Podres

Podres pitched two complete games against the Yankees, allowing three runs (two earned) in Game 3 and then returning in Game 7 with an eight-hit shutout. Gil Hodges drove in the only two runs of the Dodgers‚Äô Game 7 win, but Podres went home with the hardware.

1959: RP Larry Sherry

The Dodgers rookie tossed 12 2/3 innings of one-run baseball against the White Sox. After multi-inning saves in Games 2 and 3, Sherry won Games 4 and 6. He relieved Podres in the fourth inning of Game 6 and finished the game, 5 2/3 scoreless to secure a World Series title.

1963: SP Sandy Koufax

Koufax punched out 15 and held the Yankees to two runs in a Game 1 complete game. Then, with the Dodgers up three games to none in the series (sound familiar?), Koufax again went the distance, striking out eight over nine innings of one-run ball to sweep the Yanks.

1965: Koufax

Another legendary Koufax performance. Three starts, 24 innings, 29 strikeouts and one earned run. After declining to pitch Game 1 on Yom Kippur, Koufax allowed two runs (one earned) over six innings in a Game 2 loss against the Twins. Guerrero rebounded from 0-fers in Games 1 and 2 to go 7-for-12 with four extra-base hits. He and Yeager went back-to-back in Game 5 to erase a Yankees lead. Seager won his first of two World Series MVPs by slashing .400/.556/.700 for the Dodgers against the Rays. After already capturing the NLCS MVP trophy, Seager scored seven runs, hit two homers and had five RBIs in the World Series. Seager won another World Series MVP last season with the Texas Rangers.
