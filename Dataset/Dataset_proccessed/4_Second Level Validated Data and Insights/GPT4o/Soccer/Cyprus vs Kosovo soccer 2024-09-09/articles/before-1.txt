Will Cyprus Continue Their Nations League Resurgence Against Kosovo?
Cyprus’ Defensive Discipline at Home
Cyprus have only lost one of their last five home games in the UEFA Nations League. This home form has been built on a solid defensive foundation, with the Cypriot defence conceding just one goal in those matches. Their clean sheet against Lithuania is a testament to their ability to shut down opposition attacks, particularly in a competition like the Nations League.
Kosovo’s Struggles in Front of Goal
Kosovo have failed to score in four of their last five matches, highlighting their struggles in front of goal. Despite maintaining high possession rates, they have been unable to convert this into meaningful chances, as shown by their one shot on target against Romania. Their attacking inefficiency continues to be a major concern as they head into this clash against Cyprus.
Low-Scoring Tendencies in Cyprus’ Matches
Cyprus’ recent matches have consistently seen few goals, with their last three competitive fixtures producing under 2.5 goals. In fact, Cyprus have only managed to score more than one goal in a match once in their last six outings, further supporting the case for another low-scoring affair against Kosovo.

Cyprus will welcome Kosovo to Larnaca for their UEFA Nations League clash, following a morale-boosting win against Lithuania. A victory here would mark a significant step forward for Cyprus, who endured a difficult Euro 2024 qualifying campaign but have shown signs of improvement under pressure. On the other hand, Kosovo will be looking for redemption after suffering a demoralising 3-0 defeat to Romania in their first match of the campaign. This match presents an intriguing contest between a rejuvenated Cypriot side and a Kosovo team seeking to regain momentum.

The stakes are high for both teams. Cyprus, after their away triumph, have the opportunity to surpass their points total from the last Nations League with a second consecutive win. However, Kosovo, bruised from their home defeat, will be eager to prove their resilience and avoid falling further behind in the group standings. With Kosovo coming in as favourites despite their recent struggles, Cyprus will need to maintain their solid home form to take something from this game. It is set to be a tightly fought contest where both teams will look to impose their strategies on the match.

Given the recent performances of both sides, the best bet for this match is under 1.5 goals. A low-scoring encounter appears likely when examining the data and trends of both teams. Cyprus have demonstrated a strong defensive display, especially in their previous match where they managed a 1-0 win against Lithuania. This defensive solidity, combined with their tendency to play conservatively at home, suggests that they will look to frustrate Kosovo and keep the game tight. The key for Cyprus will be their ability to contain Kosovo’s attack, which has been largely ineffective in recent matches.

On the other side, Kosovo have struggled to find the back of the net. Despite possessing individual talent in attack, Kosovo failed to score in four of their last five matches. In their most recent game against Romania, they registered only one shot on target, a clear indication that their attacking form is far from optimal. With Milot Rashica and Vedat Muriqi yet to hit top form for Kosovo, breaking down a resilient Cypriot defence could be a tall order.

Furthermore, Cyprus’ attacking output has not been prolific either, averaging only one goal per game in their last few matches. Ioannis Pittas may have found the net against Lithuania, but the hosts have struggled to create consistent scoring chances, managing just two shots on target in that game. They are more likely to rely on set pieces or the occasional counter-attack rather than controlling the game offensively. This lends further credence to the idea that this match will remain a low-scoring affair.

Our BettingTips4You.com expert Wolfgang Shotten summarised it best: “Both sides have shown their limitations in front of goal, and with Cyprus looking to build on their defensive strength, under 1.5 goals seems the safest choice. Kosovo have been wasteful with chances, and Cyprus are not likely to take risks at home.”

Considering all factors, Cyprus will look to keep the game close and deny Kosovo space to operate in the attacking third. A tight, cagey affair is likely, and a goal or less seems a strong possibility, making under 1.5 goals the best bet for this encounter.

The most likely correct score for this match is a 0-0 draw, which complements the best bet of under 1.5 goals. This prediction aligns with the current form and tactical approaches of both teams. Cyprus are unlikely to over-commit offensively and will instead focus on securing another clean sheet after their disciplined performance against Lithuania. With Joel Mall in goal and the Cypriot defence conceding very few chances, the hosts are well-equipped to stifle Kosovo’s attack.

Kosovo, meanwhile, are facing significant difficulties in converting their chances into goals. Despite maintaining possession in their defeat to Romania, they failed to create clear-cut opportunities, and their inefficiency in front of goal has been a recurring issue. With Mergim Vojvoda suspended, their defence could also be slightly weakened, but Cyprus may not have enough attacking potency to fully exploit that.

Given that both teams will be cautious in their approach, a goalless stalemate seems the most plausible outcome. Neither side is likely to dominate, and a lack of clinical finishing from Kosovo, combined with Cyprus’ defensively disciplined approach, suggests that we will see very few, if any, goals in this encounter.
