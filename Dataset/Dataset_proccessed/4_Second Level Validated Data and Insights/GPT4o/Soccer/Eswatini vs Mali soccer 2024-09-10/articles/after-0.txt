Mozambique¬†

On Independence Day 1975, Mozambique played its first match, a friendly against Zambia, and won 2-1. Two years later, Cuba became the first non-African opponent when the two countries met in a match that Cuba won 2-0. Mozambique qualified for the World Cup for the first time in the 1982 qualifying round. Mozambique was defeated 7-3 in the two matches by Zaire in the first round.¬†

Mozambique qualified for its first African Cup of Nations in 1986. In the qualifying round, they beat Mauritius, Malawi (on penalties) and finally Libya, winning again on penalties. In the final tournament, held in Egypt, Mozambique qualified for Group A, along with Senegal, C√¥te d'Ivoire and hosts Egypt. They were defeated 3-0, 2-0 and 2-0, without scoring a goal.¬†

After being absent for 12 years from Africa Cup of Nations soccer, Mozambique was placed in Group C with Egypt, Nigeria and Benin. In their first match, they played Benin and drew 2-2 after trailing 2-0, with goals from Miro and Fumo. They then lost 2-0 to eventual tournament winners Egypt and 3-0 to Nigeria, which eliminated them from the tournament. After the tournament, the all-time leading scorer and captain Tico-Tico retired from international soccer.¬†

Mozambique has yet to reach a fifth Africa Cup of Nations final despite several near misses. During the 2013 qualifiers, they reached the final round and beat Morocco 2-0 in the first leg in Maputo.
