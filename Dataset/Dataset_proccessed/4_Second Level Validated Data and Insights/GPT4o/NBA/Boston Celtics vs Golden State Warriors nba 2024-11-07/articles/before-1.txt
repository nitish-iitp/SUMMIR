Golden State Warriors to win with handicap (+6)

Both the Celtics and the Warriors enter this match with just one loss so far and while I don't expect these two teams to keep up the pace the same way, with the Celtics clearly being the better team, at the moment it's hard to look past the Warriors for this game. First of all, the Celtics will be without Jaylen Brown and Kristaps Porzingis, so even though they have a deep team, that is hard to compensate for.

On top of that, if we look at the recent history between these two teams, they mostly played very close games or the Warriors have been the one to win, with the exception of their last duel which ended 140-88 for the Celtics. All this considered, I can see Golden State pushing this game to the wire, which is why my first Celtics vs Warriors prediction is for the guests to win with handicap.

Over 230 total points

These two teams have played some of the best offense early in the new NBA season, with the Celtics averaging 123.6 points per game, more than any other team in the league. The Warriors are not far behind since they're third in the league with 121.6 points per game.

That being said, both the Celtics and the Warriors have been playing good defense too, but I expect to see a high-scoring game this evening, which is why over 230 total points is another of my Celtics vs Warriors picks.

Jayson Tatum to record 30+ points

The last of my Celtics vs Warriors tips is for Jayson Tatum to score 30+ points. Tatum has hit another gear to start this season and has averaged exactly 30 points per game so far, going over that line in half of the eight games he's played with the Celtics.

However, since Brown is also out for tonight, Tatum will have to take on even more responsibility on the offensive end and, unless he has a very bad shooting night, he should score at last 30 points.

The experienced, championship-winning core of Curry, Green, and Wiggins has been perfectly complemented by the young guys on the team and the Warriors will seemingly be a serious force in the Western Conference this season.

The only player out with an injury is De'Anthony Melton, who' dealing with a back injury, but shouldn't be out too long.
