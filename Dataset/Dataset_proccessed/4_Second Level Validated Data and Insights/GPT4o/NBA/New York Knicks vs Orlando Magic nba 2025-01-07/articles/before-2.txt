Knicks without NBA rebounding leader Towns because of sore knee against injury-depleted Magic

NEW YORK (AP) — Karl-Anthony Towns is sidelined Monday night with a sore right knee, leaving the New York Knicks without the NBA's leading rebounder for their game against the Orlando Magic.

Towns appeared to be hurting near the end of the Knicks' loss in Chicago on Saturday and was listed as questionable to play Monday. The Knicks decided before the game that he wouldn't.
