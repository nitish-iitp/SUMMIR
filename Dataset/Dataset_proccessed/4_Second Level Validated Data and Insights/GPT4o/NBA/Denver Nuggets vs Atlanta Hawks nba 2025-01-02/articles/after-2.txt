Nikola Jokic has dominated scoring 23 points to go with 17 rebounds and 15 assists for his 14th triple-double of the season as the Denver Nuggets beat the Atlanta Hawks 139-120 in NBA.
Russell Westbrook also hit 16 points and had 11 assists and Michael Porter and Jamal Murray contributed 21 points each for the Nuggets, who have topped 130 points in four straight games.
Trae Young led the Hawks with 30 points and nine assists, De'Andre Hunter scored 20 Vit Krejci added 14 points, Zaccharie Risacher finished with 13 and David Roddy 10.
The Nuggets scored 40 points in the first quarter on Wednesday but couldn't shake Atlanta, as the Hawks rallied from down nine early in the second to lead 62-57 before Denver responded to take a 74-71 lead at halftime.
Jokic nearly had a triple-double at intermission with 13 points, 10 rebounds and nine assists.
When he reached 20 points, 15 rebounds and 15 assists it was a first for the NBA in under 30 minutes.
The Nuggets took control with a dominant third quarter. Porter and Murray hit from 3-point range and Westbrook made a driving layup to quickly extend the lead to 84-75 in the first three minutes. The Hawks answered with two buckets, but then Denver put it away.
