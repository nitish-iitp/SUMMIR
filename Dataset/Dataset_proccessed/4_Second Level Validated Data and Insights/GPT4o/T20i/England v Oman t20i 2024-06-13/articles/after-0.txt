T20 WORLD CUP
England exploit painful gulf in class by blitzing Oman in 102 minutes
North Sound (England won toss): England (2pts) beat Oman by eight wickets
England chased Oman’s 47 in 3.1 overs – the largest win in men’s T20 World Cup history in terms of balls remaining
AFP
Mike Atherton, Chief Cricket Correspondent, Antigua
Thursday June 13 2024, 10.09pm BST, The Times

In need of a mighty boost to their net run rate, England found the perfect opponents in hapless Oman, who were ripe for the plucking and were totally outclassed. On a hot and breezy day at the Sir Vivian Richards Stadium in North Sound, England bowled Oman out in 13.2 overs for only 47, the fourth-lowest score at a T20 World Cup, and finished the match in time for a sundowner.
