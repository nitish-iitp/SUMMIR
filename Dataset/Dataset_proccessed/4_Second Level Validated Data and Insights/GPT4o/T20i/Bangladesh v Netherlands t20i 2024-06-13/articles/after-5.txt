BAN vs NED: Shakib Al Hasan scripts new T20I record for Bangladesh, ends lean patch
T20 World Cup 2024, BAN vs NED: Shakib Al Hasan became the sixth Asian batter to complete 2,500 runs in T20I cricket. Shakib got to the landmark after scoring an unbeaten 64, his first fifty in T20I since October 2022.

In Short
Shakib Al Hasan hit an unbeaten 64 against the Netherlands on Thursday
Shakib hit his first fifty in international cricket since October 2022
Bangladesh posted 159 in their quota of 20 overs in Kingstown

Senior all-rounder Shakib Al Hasan set a new T20I record for Bangladesh, becoming the country's first batter to score more than 2,5000 runs in men's T20Is. Shakib achieved the feat during Bangladesh's T20 World Cup 2024 match against the Netherlands in Kingstown, St. Vincent on Thursday, June 13. Shakib hit 64 not out after getting to a 38-ball fifty as he helped Bangladesh post 159 on the board in their quota of 20 overs.

Shakib Al Hasan held one end and played a terrific knock of 64 off 46 balls, hitting 9 boundaries on a spungy pitch at the Arnos Vale Ground. It was Shakib Al Hasan's first fifty in T20I cricket since October 2022 when he hit a ffity against Pakistan in a T20I tri-series match in Christchurch. Shakib had done 21 innings in T20I cricket without a fifty and pressure was mounting on the former Bangladesh captain. He was dismissed for 8 against Sri Lanka and 3 against South Africa in Group D of the T20 World Cup.

Shakib Al Hasan also became only the sixth Asian batter to complete the milestone of 2,500 runs. Shakib is featuring in his 9th T20 World Cup, having played every edition since the inaugural edition in 2007.

6. Mohammad Hafeez (Pakistan) - 2514 runs in 119 matches

Bangladesh got off to a poor start after Netherlands won the toss and opted to bowl. They lost their captain Najmul Shanto for 1 while Litton Das followed for 1 as they wer reeling at 23 for 2 in the 4th over.

However, Tanzid Hasan took oon the onus of scoring quickly in the powerplay, hitting 5 boundaries and a six for his 26-ball 35. Tanzid and Shakib Al Hasan, who walked in at No. 4, added 48 runs for the third wicket.

However, Tanzid fell in the 9th over following which Towhid Hridoy suffered his first failure of the ongoing tournament, departing for 9.

Nonetheless, Shakib used all his experience and hit an unbeaten 64 after having stitched a 41-run partnership with Mahmadullah, who hit 21-ball 25.

Bangladesh were hoping for a positive result in their crunch match as they suffered a heartbreaking 4-run defeat to South Africa, days after beating Sri Lanka in their opener.
