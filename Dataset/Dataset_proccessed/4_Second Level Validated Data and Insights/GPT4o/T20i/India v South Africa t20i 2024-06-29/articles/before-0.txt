Barbados weather 29 June 2024, India vs South Africa T20 World Cup final: What is the rain forecast?
Ahead of the T20 World Cup Final between India and South Africa, let's take a look at the weather forecast of the Kensington Oval in Barbados.

2 min read
Reported by: Republic Sports Desk
Follow:
Virat Kohli at the Kensington Oval in Barbados | Image: BCCI

The stage for the summit clash is all set as the opponents for the ICC Men's T20 World Cup have been officially named. Two undefeated sides will lock horns to get their hands on the silverware. While one team will look to bring their first-ever title, another is aiming to end their trophy-less run. The Rohit Sharma-led India will be up against the Aiden Markram-led South Africa, and the title will; be up for grabs. Ahead of the clash. All eyes will be locked in for the super-important clash. But will the weather in Barbados be a party pooper? Will it rain at the Kensington Oval? let's take a look.

India vs South Africa, T20 World Cup Final Weather Forecast: Weather in Barbados doesn't looks good

A lot of attention will be locked to in the ICC Men's T230 World Cup Final, which will take place at the Kensington Oval in Bridgetown, Barbados. It is an important clash as India and South Africa are hungry for a title. But given that the weather has been a lot of a party spoiler in the Caribbean, the weather forecast for Saturday will be super important as it will decide who will take the title home. But the weather in Bridgetown, Barbados is expected to be cloudy and windy with a thunderstorm in parts of the area. The hourly updates, as per weather.com, do not look promising as well as rainfall is predicted to happen in the daytime. Over 50% of rainfall is expected to happen in the Barbados area.

Weather Data: weather.com&nbsp;

If the weather affects the ICC Men's T20 World Cup 2024 final, the umpires will be compelled to cut down the overs. Even though there is a reserve day in place, the match officials will look to end the match on the same day. Rain will be a trouble for both teams in terms of their practice and the pitch could see significant changes due to the dampness.
