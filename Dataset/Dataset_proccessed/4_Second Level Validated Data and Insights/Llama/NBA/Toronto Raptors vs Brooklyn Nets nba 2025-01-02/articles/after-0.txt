RAPTORS
Immanuel Quickley returns and the Raptors’ losing streak is suddenly over. It’s no coincidence

The Raptors point guard wasn’t in mid-season form against the Nets after missing 22 games, but his impact was enormous at Scotiabank Arena.

Updated Jan. 2, 2025 at 12:47 p.m.
Jan. 2, 2025
3 min read
Save

Raptors point guard Immanuel Quickley was a step ahead of Keon Johnson of the Nets for a loose ball in Wednesday night’s game at Scotiabank Arena.

Mark Blinch/Getty Images
By Doug SmithSports Reporter

Eventually the Raptors will get Immanuel Quickley in top shape, at his best, controlling his team.

But what they need now from the young point guard is his presence, his effervescence, his will.
