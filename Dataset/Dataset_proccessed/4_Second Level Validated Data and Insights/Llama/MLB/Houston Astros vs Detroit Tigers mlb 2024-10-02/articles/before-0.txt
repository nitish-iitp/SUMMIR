How to watch Tigers vs. Astros AL Wild Card Game 1: Time, TV channel, FREE MLB live stream
Updated: Oct. 01, 2024, 10:03 a.m.|Published: Oct. 01, 2024, 10:02 a.m.

Detroit Tigers pitcher Tarik Skubal (29) pumps his fist to the crowd during a baseball game between the Detroit Tigers and the Chicago White Sox at Comerica Park in Detroit on Saturday, Sept. 28, 2024. The Tigers won the first game of the series 4-1. The Tigers clinched the playoffs for the first time since the 2014 season.Josh Boland | MLive.com

By Dustin Hockensmith | dhockensmith@pennlive.com

The Detroit Tigers and Houston Astros kick off Major League Baseball’s 2024 playoffs with a Tuesday afternoon matchup in the American League Wild Card round. Game 1 is scheduled to start at 2:30 p.m. ET (1:30 p.m. CT) with TV coverage on ABC and streaming on-demand.

How to watch: Live streams of the Astros vs. Tigers game are available with offers from FuboTV (free trial), DirecTV Stream (free trial) and Sling TV (low intro rate).
For a limited time, FuboTV is offering $30 off the first month after the free trial period. With the $30 offer, plans start at $49.99.

Detroit Tigers (86-76) at Houston Astros (88-73)

American League Wild Card Series Game 1

When: Tuesday, Oct. 1 at 2:30 p.m. ET (1:30 p.m. CT)

Where: Minute Maid Park, Houston, Texas

TV channel: ABC

Live streams: FuboTV (free trial) | DirecTV Stream (free trial) | Sling TV (low intro rate)

After spending much of the first half of the season at or below .500, the Tigers roared back from a low point of 37-46 in late June with a 47-30 record over their final 77 games. The Astros were in a similar situation with a slow start before finding their collective stride with a 17-6 record to cap the first half of the season.

Both teams will start their aces looking for a Game 1 edge with Detroit’s Tarik Skubal (18-4, 2.39 ERA) set to face Houston’s Framber Valdez (15-7, 2.91 ERA) in a battle of top left-handers.
