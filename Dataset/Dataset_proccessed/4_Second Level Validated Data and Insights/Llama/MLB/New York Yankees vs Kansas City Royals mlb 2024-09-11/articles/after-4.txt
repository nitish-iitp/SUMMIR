BOTTOM LINE: The New York Yankees and Kansas City Royals meet on Wednesday with the three-game series tied 1-1.

New York has an 83-62 record overall and a 38-32 record at home. Yankees hitters have a collective .434 slugging percentage to rank second in the AL.

Kansas City has an 80-66 record overall and a 35-36 record on the road. The Royals have the sixth-best team batting average in MLB play at .254.

The teams square off Wednesday for the seventh time this season. The Yankees lead the season series 4-2.

TOP PERFORMERS: Aaron Judge has 51 home runs, 117 walks and 126 RBI while hitting .320 for the Yankees. Gleyber Torres is 16-for-42 with two doubles and five RBI over the past 10 games.

Bobby Witt Jr. has a .334 batting average to lead the Royals, and has 41 doubles, 11 triples and 30 home runs. Salvador Perez is 10-for-36 with a home run and six RBI over the past 10 games.
