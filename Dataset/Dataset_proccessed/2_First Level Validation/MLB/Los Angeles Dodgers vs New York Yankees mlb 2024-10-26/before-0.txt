Dodgers' Freddie Freeman hits walk-off grand slam in Game 1 of World Series
By Mike Gavin

In a year that has been so improbable, the impossible has happened...again!

Vin Scully's call from 1988 echoed at Dodger Stadium on Friday as history repeated itself with Los Angeles trailing by one run and down to its final out. That's when Freddie Freeman crushed a 10th-inning, walk-off grand slam in Game 1 of the World Series to give the Dodgers a 6-3 win over the New York Yankees. It was the first walk-off grand slam in World Series history.

After the Yankees took the lead in the top of the inning, Gavin Lux drew a one-out walk and Tommy Edman singled to put the tying and winning runs on for Shohei Ohtani. The Yankees brought in Nestor Cortes, who had not pitched in a major league league game since Sept. 18 due to a left flexor strain. Cortes got Ohtani to hit a pop up into foul territory down the left field line, where Alex Verdugo made a running catch and flipped into the stands for the second out of the inning.

Mookie Betts was then intentionally walked to load the bases and bring Freeman to the plate. Freeman crushed the first pitch he saw from Cortes into the right-field stands, giving the Dodgers a thrilling comeback win. Freeman raised his bat in the air as he walked down the first base line, the ball soaring down the same path as Kirk Gibson's iconic walk-off home run in Game 1 of the 1988 World Series that gave the Dodgers a 5-4 win over the Oakland Athletics and led to Scully's famous call.
