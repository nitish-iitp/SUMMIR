Max Fried (9-8, 3.35 ERA) vs. Jake Irvin (9-12, 4.28 ERA)

 

Max Fried’s season with the Braves has been quite wonderful as he has a season ERA of 3.35 and a WHIP of 1.19 over 147.2 innings pitched in the season. Fried has 138 strikeouts this season, and only 51 of those are walks, which is an indication that he knows how to work the plate. Such an approach will be important in dealing with the Nationals’ lineup and helping Atlanta win the game.

 

On the other hand, for the Nationals, Jake Irvin is an entirely different proposition as he has a season-earned run average of 4.28 and a WHIP of 1.21. Though he has managed to strike out 140 batters this season he has also given up 26 home runs and that is a number that may work against the long-hitting Braves. His effort will be key in seeing whether the Nationals will be as up to the challenge as the odds suggest they might be.
