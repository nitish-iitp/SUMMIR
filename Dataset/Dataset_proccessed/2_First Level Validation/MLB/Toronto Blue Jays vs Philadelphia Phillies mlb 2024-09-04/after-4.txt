BOTTOM LINE: The Philadelphia Phillies meet the Toronto Blue Jays after Kyle Schwarber hit three home runs against the Blue Jays on Tuesday.

Toronto has a 34-34 record at home and a 67-73 record overall. The Blue Jays have a 54-10 record in games when they scored at least five runs.

Philadelphia is 35-31 on the road and 82-56 overall. Phillies pitchers have a collective 3.71 ERA, which ranks fourth in MLB play.

Wednesday's game is the fourth time these teams meet this season.

TOP PERFORMERS: Vladimir Guerrero Jr. leads the Blue Jays with a .328 batting average, and has 39 doubles, a triple, 28 home runs, 61 walks and 91 RBI. Will Wagner is 11-for-39 with two home runs and five RBI over the past 10 games.
