Portugal 2-1 Croatia: Cristiano Ronaldo scores 900th career goal
Cristiano Ronaldo scored his 900th goal of his career as Portugal got off to a winning start in the Nations League.¬†
By Sam Hargrave
5 September 2024 21:58 BST

Portugal got off to a winning start in the UEFA Nations League as Cristiano Ronaldo's 900th career goal helped his side secure a 2-1 victory over Croatia at Estadio da Luz.¬†

A fixture full of high-profile names did not disappoint.¬†

Diogo Dalot opened the scoring in the sixth minute to give the hosts the lead, before a historic Cristiano Ronaldo goal doubled the score for Portugal.¬†

Dalot netted again shortly after but this time in his own net which allowed Croatia a way back into the game.¬†

Portugal start their Nations League campaign perfectly as they look to win their second title after winning the inaugural edition in 2019.¬†

Story of the Match

Before the first half got underway the recently retired Pepe was honoured on his behalf of serving his nation for so long.¬†

Embed from Getty Images

The centre-back made 141 appearances for Portugal and a ceremony was held prior to kick-off to celebrate¬†his efforts and achievements for his nation.¬†

It did not take long for Portugal to take the lead, the sixth minute in fact.¬†

The Manchester United duo of Bruno Fernandes and Diogo Dalot collaborated for the opener. Fernandes threaded a ball through to Dalot who was a late arrival¬†into the box.¬†

His first touch allowed him to go past Borna Sosa and finish clinically into the bottom left corner with his left foot.¬†

Embed from Getty Images

Cristiano Ronaldo could have had his 900th career goal before he went on to do so as he saw¬†his driven effort with his left kept out by the hopeful foot of Dominik Livakovic.¬†

Portugal fans showed their appreciation towards the splendid build-up play by Ronaldo and Rafael Leao on the left side of the box before the attempt was denied.¬†

Croatia were not short of possession after the half-hour mark, having 45 per cent¬†of the ball. However they were struggling to break down the firm defensive structure of Roberto Martinez's side.¬†

Their best chance up until that point being Andrej Kramaric's half-volleyed effort which forced Diogo Costa into a smart save to his left.¬†

900 for Cristiano¬†

A relieved and emotional Ronaldo ran to the corner flag and collapsed to his knees after he netted his 900th career goal.¬†

PSG left-back, Nuno Mendes, delivered a precise and whipped ball into the box and the man of the moment was there to convert the ball past Livakovic with a side-footed volley.¬†

Embed from Getty Images

It will go down in the history books. A role model of the game we love.¬†

A young boy from Madeira who shared this love and still does as a veteran provided Lisbon and the rest of his compatriots a memory to cherish.¬†

Shortly after Portugal doubled their lead, the goal difference was swiftly back to one.¬†

Former Leicester City striker, Andrej Kramaric, zipped a dangerous ball into the box and a sliding Sosa was at the back post to keep the attack alive.¬†

The ball then glanced off of the unfortunate Dalot which resulted in the ball ending up in the roof of Costa's net.¬†

Dalot now had one at each end.¬†

Pedro Neto showed off his raw pace when he ran the length of the half with the ball. The flourishing run ended with a powerful shot from the Chelsea winger, however he saw his effort blaze against the crossbar.¬†

He must have done something of good quality as the run and effort got an applause from Cristiano Ronaldo.¬†

Portugal's hero Ronaldo was passionately cheered off of the pitch in the 87th minute, the fans showing their gratitude towards a figure who has produced some of Portugal's finest footballing moments.¬†

A national hero. A sporting hero. A role model for¬†all aspiring footballers out there.¬†

The referee blew the¬†whistle to signify the end of the 90 minutes.¬†

On a night of history and celebration, Portugal had made the perfect start to their Nations League campaign.¬†

Player of the match - Cristiano Ronaldo

Cristiano Ronaldo - does any reason have to be typed. The man has 900 career goals and on another night he could have had three tonight. A special moment topped off by a nostalgic performance.¬†
