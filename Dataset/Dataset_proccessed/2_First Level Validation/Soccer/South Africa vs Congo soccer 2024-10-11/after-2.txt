South Africa vs Congo: Bafana Bafana Deliver a Masterclass in AFCON Qualifier Triumph
October 13, 20243 Mins Read
Share

The Nelson Mandela Bay Stadium in Gqeberha witnessed a night of pure footballing brilliance as South Africa vs Congo in a must-win AFCON qualifier. With a coveted spot in the prestigious tournament at stake, the tension was palpable. But it was Bafana Bafana who rose to the occasion, delivering a performance that will be remembered for years to come.  

Advertisement
South Africa vs Congo: A Dominant Display from the Start

Right from kickoff, South Africa asserted their dominance. The atmosphere was electric, with the home crowd roaring their support as Bafana Bafana launched wave after wave of attack. Congo struggled to contain the South African onslaught, and it wasn’t long before the goals started flowing.

South Africa vs Congo: Mokoena the Maestro

Advertisement

Teboho Mokoena emerged as the hero of the night, bagging a brace that showcased his exceptional talent. His first goal, a perfectly placed header from an Oswin Appolis corner, set the tone for the match. His second, a thunderous strike from outside the box, left the Congo goalkeeper with no chance.  

Advertisement

South Africa vs Congo: Goals Galore

Mokoena’s brilliance wasn’t the only highlight of the match. Appolis, the architect of South Africa’s attacking play, provided another assist, this time setting up Bathusi Aubaas to score from close range. Lyle Foster also got in on the act, capitalizing on a thrilling assist by Elias Mokwana to further extend South Africa’s lead.

South Africa vs Congo: Second Half Substitutions

At half-time, Congo made a change, bringing on Silvère Ganvoula M’boussy in an attempt to change their fortunes. But South Africa remained in control. Hugo Broos introduced fresh legs, with Relebohile Mofokeng replacing the impressive Appolis, Iqraam Rayners coming on for the injured Foster, and Luke Le Roux replacing Aubaas.

Advertisement

South Africa vs Congo: Rayners Adds the Final Touch

Rayners made an instant impact, pouncing on a mistake by Congo goalkeeper Christoffer Mafoumbi to score South Africa’s fifth goal. Despite further substitutions, Congo couldn’t find a way back into the match.

South Africa vs Congo: A Resounding Victory

The final whistle blew, confirming a 5-0 victory for South Africa. The Nelson Mandela Bay Stadium erupted in celebration as Bafana Bafana fans savored a historic triumph. This emphatic win puts South Africa in a commanding position in their AFCON qualifying group and sends a clear message to their rivals: they are a force to be reckoned with.
