Referee Ahomalnto Dejinnanchi to Officiate Sierra Leone Vs Ivory Coast Friendly Match
by Awoko Publications  01/01/2024 in Sports Reading Time: 1 min read
 0  0  0

Freetown, SIERRA LEONE – Ahomalnto Dejinnanchi, a seasoned referee from Benin, has been appointed as the centre official for the highly anticipated friendly match between Sierra Leone and Ivory Coast scheduled for January 6, 2024, at the Laurent Poukou De San Pedro Stadium.

Assisting Dejinnanchi on the field will be two other referees from Benin, with Koutou Gbemassiandan Narcisse serving as Assistant 1 and Dodometin Detondji Joe Cortex as Assistant 2. The 4th Official duties will be handled by Kpan Clement Franklin, while Eugene Diomande, both from Cote d’Ivoire, will serve as the Commissioner.

The Ivorian Football Federation has unveiled a squad of 27 players in preparation for the friendly clash against Leone Stars of Sierra Leone. The match, set to take place at the Laurent Poukou De San Pedro Stadium, will play a crucial role in refining Ivory Coast’s strategy for the upcoming African Cup of Nations.

In response, the Sierra Leone Football Association has also announced a 21-man squad for the friendly encounter, highlighting the significance of this international match for both teams as they fine-tune their preparations for future competitions.

The friendly match is expected to be a strategic move for Ivory Coast as they gear up for their participation in the African Cup of Nations, providing a valuable opportunity for players to showcase their skills and build team chemistry ahead of the continental tournament. Football enthusiasts can anticipate an exciting and competitive encounter between Sierra Leone and Ivory Coast as the teams engage in a high-stakes friendly clash. B T/1/1/2024
