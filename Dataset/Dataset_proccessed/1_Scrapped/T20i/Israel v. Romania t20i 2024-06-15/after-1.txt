National News Headlines in English for 16 June 2024

Stay updated with 16 June 2024 news headlines in English for school assembly. Learn about various sectors´ happenings in our country. 

1. India Tops Immigrant Doctors in US, Ranks Third Overall:
India leads in sending doctors to the US, second for nurses. Of 987,000 US doctors, 26.5% are immigrants, and most are Indian. New Jersey, Florida, and New York attract a maximum number of immigrant doctors.

2. Karnataka CM Plans 41 Women’s Colleges in the Backward Region:
Chief Minister seeks a plan to spend ₹5,000 crore on developing the Kalyana Karnataka region by July 15, which may include establishing 41 all-women degree colleges in the backward area.

3. Air India’s Operational Woes Despite CEO’s Efforts:
Two years after the new CEO’s arrival, Air India progresses but remains stuck in personnel issues and operational troubles, with no respite in sight despite considerable strides made.

4. Supreme Court Orders CBI Probe into Fraudulent Nursing Colleges:
Supreme Court upholds Madhya Pradesh High Court’s order for CBI to reinvestigate 169 nursing colleges alleged to be running fraudulently, rejecting the agency’s earlier clean chit.

5. Hyundai’s Indian Arm Eyes $3 Billion IPO at $18-20 Billion Valuation:
Hyundai Motor India Limited gears up to file draft papers for India’s biggest-ever $3 billion IPO at a targeted valuation between $18-20 billion, surpassing LIC’s $2.7 billion listing.

6. UP Upgrades Technical University with Facial Biometrics, Live Monitoring:
Yogi government launches a major technological upgrade at Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Technical University, including facial biometrics for attendance tracking and live CCTV monitoring during exams.

7. Sarod Maestro Pandit Rajeev Taranath Passes Away at 92:
Internationally acclaimed sarod maestro Pandit Rajeev Taranath, 92, who performed worldwide, dies after a brief illness. He was the first Indian to play sarod at the Sydney Opera House.  

8. UGC Mandates Fee Refund Policy for Admission Withdrawals:
UGC introduces a new fee refund policy for 2024-25, mandating a 100 percent refund within 15 days before the admission deadline and a 90 percent refund below 15 days to address non-refund complaints.

9. PM Modi Calls for Prioritizing Global South Concerns at G7 Summit:
Addressing the G7 Outreach Session, PM Modi emphasized giving priority to the Global South’s concerns, especially Africa’s, recalling India’s presidency facilitating the African Union’s permanent G20 membership.

10. India Outlines Energy Transition Plan, Launches Tree Plantation Drive:
PM Modi elaborated on India’s energy transition based on availability, accessibility, affordability, and acceptability, targeting Net Zero by 2070, and launched the “Plant4Mother” tree plantation campaign at the G7 Summit.  

11. India’s Forex Reserves Hit New High of $655.8 Billion:
India’s forex reserves jumped $4.307 billion to a new all-time high of $655.817 billion for the week ended June 7, with foreign currency assets rising $3.773 billion.

International News in English for 16 June 2024

Explore global stories through international news in English. Discover updates from different countries:


1. Denmark Recalls Spicy Korean Noodles Citing ‘Acute Poisoning’ Risk:
Danish authorities recalled three Samyang instant ramen products, warning consumers of potential “acute poisoning” due to dangerously high levels of capsaicin, the compound giving chili peppers their heat.

2. Myanmar Blocks VPNs in Bid to Restrict Internet Access:
Myanmar’s military government launched efforts to block VPNs used to circumvent website bans, restricting free communication on the internet since the end of May after ousting Suu Kyi’s government.

3. Italy Denies Removing LGBTQ, Abortion References from G7 Declaration:
Italy’s far-right government denied removing references to abortion and LGBTQ rights’ protection in the G7 summit’s final declaration, contrary to previous G7 declarations.

4. Meloni Accuses Macron of ‘Campaigning’ at G7 Summit:
Italian PM Meloni accused French President Macron of election campaigning at the G7 summit over his call for a parliamentary election to counter the far-right’s growing influence.

5. Bangladesh Complains About Defective Chinese Military Hardware:
Bangladesh, a long-time buyer of Chinese military equipment, has lodged complaints with Beijing regarding supply of defective components and technical issues with imported Chinese military hardware.

6. UK PM Praises Italy’s Meloni, Announces Anti-Smuggling Coalition:
UK PM Sunak praised his “fantastic” Italian counterpart Meloni as he announced the G7’s launch of a coalition to tackle migrant smuggling, which they spearheaded since being elected.

7. Princess Kate Announces First Appearance After Cancer Diagnosis:   
Princess Kate announced her first public event since revealing her cancer diagnosis, garnering widespread support, including UK PM Sunak noting her message would resonate with cancer patients.

8. Ukraine Summit Aims to Pressure Russia Amid Allies’ Absence: A summit aimed at pressuring Russia to end the Ukraine war will be held in Switzerland, but China’s absence after Russia’s exclusion may limit its potential impact.

9. Peace Conference in Switzerland for the Ukraine War:
An international conference aiming to start a peace process for the Ukraine war takes place today at the Burgenstock Resort, with representatives from around 100 countries and international organizations attending.

10. Tensions at the G7 Summit between some countries went viral:
Tensions between Italian Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni and French President Emmanuel Macron were evident in a recent video showing their frosty handshake. The discord stemmed from disagreements over including abortion rights language at the G7 Summit

11.  Selective Attendance at the Global Peace Summit:
Several BRICS, SCO, and ASEAN countries skipped the Global Peace Summit on the Russia-Ukraine war, with only India sending a ministerial delegation.

1. Euro 2024: Veteran Stars Ready for Final Showdown:
Veteran European football stars like Cristiano Ronaldo and Luka Modric are gearing up for potentially their last UEFA Euro Championship in Germany, set to start on June 15. Top teams are ready for the intense competition.

2. England’s Vital Win in T20 World Cup:
England revived their T20 World Cup hopes with an 8-wicket win over Oman, keeping their Super 8 stage dreams alive after earlier disappointing performances.

3. Pakistan Exits T20 World Cup Amid Weather Woes:
Pakistan was eliminated from the T20 World Cup 2024 due to rain disruptions and poor performance. USA and India advanced from their group, with USA also securing a spot in the 2026 World Cup.

4. Germany Thrashes Scotland in Euro 2024 Opener:
Germany defeated Scotland 5-1 in the Euro 2024 opening match in Munich. Kai Havertz, Florian Wirtz, and Jamal Musiala scored in the first half, with Niclas Fuellkrug and Emre Can adding goals in the second half.

5. Loh Kean Yew Exits BWF Australian Open:
Singapore’s Loh Kean Yew was defeated by India’s Sameer Verma in the BWF Australian Open’s second round. Verma won a close match 21-14, 14-21, 21-19 to advance to the quarterfinals.

6. Messi Scores Twice, Closing Gap with Ronaldo:
Lionel Messi scored twice against Guatemala, bringing his international goals to 108, tying with Ali Daei. He is now just behind Cristiano Ronaldo, the top international goalscorer.

7. South African President Re-Elected Amid Coalition Talks:
South Africa’s parliament re-elected Cyril Ramaphosa as president. The African National Congress is negotiating a coalition government after losing its majority in the recent election.

8. Sumit Nagal Reaches Perugia Challenger Semifinals:
India’s Sumit Nagal advanced to the Perugia Challenger semifinals by defeating Maks Kasnikowski 6-4, 7-5. This win extends Nagal’s streak to eight matches.

9. Ronaldo Eyes More Milestones at Euro 2024:
At 39, Cristiano Ronaldo, the legendary Portuguese captain nicknamed CR7, aims for more glory marshalling the 2016 winners in the 2024 European Championship.

10. Germany Records Biggest Euro Opener Win Over Scotland:
In Euro 2024, Germany defeated Scotland 5-1 at the Allianz Arena, the biggest-ever opening match victory in European Championship history, with five different scorers.

What Happened Today in History

Understand the significance of what happened today in history and learn how to make a difference:

1. First Woman in Space (1963)

Valentina Tereshkova, a Soviet cosmonaut, became the first woman to travel into space. She orbited the Earth 48 times aboard Vostok 6, marking a significant milestone in space exploration.

2. End of FDR’s First Hundred Days (1933)

President Franklin D. Roosevelt’s first hundred days concluded with the signing of multiple landmark pieces of legislation, including the Banking Act, which established the FDIC, and the National Industrial Recovery Act.

3. Soweto Uprising (1976)

In South Africa, police opened fire on students protesting against the use of Afrikaans in schools, sparking the Soweto Uprising. This event became a major turning point in the struggle against apartheid.

4. China’s First Female Astronaut (2012)

Liu Yang became China’s first female astronaut when she was launched into space aboard the Shenzhou 9 spacecraft. She and her crew docked with the Tiangong-1 space station.

5. Helmut Kohl’s Death (2017)

Helmut Kohl, the former Chancellor of Germany who oversaw the reunification of East and West Germany, passed away. He was a central figure in post-Cold War European politics.
