National News Headlines in English for 11 June 2024

Stay updated with 11 June 2024 news headlines in English for school assembly. Learn about various sectors’ happenings in our country.

1. PM Modi Kickstarts Third Term: Releases ₹20,000 Cr to 9.3 Cr Farmers
Modi begins the third term with a focus on farmer welfare, authorizing the release of PM-Kisan funds. The scheme benefits 9.3 crore farmers with Rs. 20,000 crore. Industry bodies congratulate Modi on his consecutive term as Prime Minister.

2. Modi Cabinet 3.0: New Ministers Sworn in from Across India
PM Modi’s new cabinet includes over a dozen parliamentarians from southern states and ten from Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra. Alongside PM Modi, ministers such as Rajnath Singh, Amit Shah, and Nirmala Sitharaman took oath, marking the beginning of Modi cabinet 3.0.

3. SKM Chief Tamang to Take Second Term Oath as Sikkim CM
Sikkim’s CM Swearing-in Ceremony sees Prem Singh Tamang taking oath for his second consecutive term. His priorities, including water, electricity, and infrastructure development, underscore Sikkim Krantikari Morcha’s landslide victory, capturing 31 out of 32 seats.

4. Terrorist Attack in J&K: 10 Dead, 33 Injured, Nation Mourns
Tragedy strikes as terrorists attack a bus carrying pilgrims in Jammu and Kashmir, resulting in ten deaths and 33 injuries. Leaders including President Droupadi Murmu and PM Modi condemn the attack, expressing condolences to the victims’ families.

5. BRICS Diplomacy: India’s Foreign Policy Journey Under PM Modi Begins
India’s foreign policy under PM Modi’s third term begins with the BRICS Foreign Ministers’ meeting in Nizhny Novgorod, Russia. Chaired by Russian Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov, the meeting sets the stage for the upcoming BRICS Summit, emphasizing diplomatic ties among member nations.

6. Bangladesh Eyes Bhutan Hydro-Electricity via India
Bangladesh Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina expresses interest in importing hydroelectricity from Bhutan via India during a meeting with Bhutanese Prime Minister Dasho Tshering Tobgay in New Delhi. This discussion, held alongside PM Modi’s swearing-in ceremony, signals the potential for a tripartite agreement involving India. Bangladesh Foreign Minister Dr Hasan Mahmud highlights Bhutan’s eagerness to export hydroelectricity to Bangladesh.

International News in English for 11 June 2024

Explore global stories through international news in English. Discover updates from different countries:

1. Israeli Minister Benny Gantz Resigns Amid Gaza War
Israeli war cabinet minister Benny Gantz resigns, criticizing Netanyahu’s handling of the Gaza war. Calls for early elections and urges Defense Minister to act. Netanyahu responds, emphasizing current priorities.

2. Google to Shut Down GPay App in the US
Google announces closure of GPay app for US users from June 4, 2024. Peer-to-peer payments were also discontinued. Shift towards integrating services into the Google Wallet app. GPay remains active in India and Singapore.

3. Israeli Minister Benny Gantz Resigns, Calls for Early Elections
In a setback to Netanyahu’s government, Benny Gantz resigns, citing a lack of progress in the Gaza war. Urges Netanyahu to set election date. Netanyahu responds, emphasizing the need to focus on current challenges.

What Happened Today in History

Understand the significance of what happened today in history and learn how to make a difference:

1. Continental Congress Drafts Declaration of Independence 

June 11, 1776: “Continental Congress Initiates Declaration of Independence Drafting” – The Continental Congress takes the pivotal step of forming a committee to draft the Declaration of Independence, setting the stage for American independence from British rule.

2. Federal Intervention at the University of Alabama Desegregation

June 11, 1963: “Federal Intervention Ensures University of Alabama Desegregation” – Governor George Wallace’s opposition to desegregation at the University of Alabama is met with federal intervention, showcasing the federal government’s commitment to civil rights.

3. Battle of Stoney Creek: June 11, 1813, War of 1812

Battle of Stoney Creek: June 11, 1813, “British Repel American Attack in War of 1812″ – British forces successfully defend against an American assault during the War of 1812, securing a strategic victory near present-day Ontario, Canada.

4. Start of ‘Eighteen-Day Tornado Outbreak’ in U.S.

June 11, 1955: Onset of Devastating “Eighteen-Day Tornado Outbreak’ Across U.S.” – The United States experiences the start of a destructive tornado outbreak, spanning eighteen days and causing widespread devastation across the Midwest.

5. Reagan’s ‘Tear Down This Wall’ Speech: June 11, 1987

June 11, 1987, “Challenging Soviet Union” – President Ronald Reagan delivers his iconic speech at the Brandenburg Gate, urging the Soviet Union to dismantle the Berlin Wall and symbolizing the call for freedom and unity in Cold War-era Europe.
