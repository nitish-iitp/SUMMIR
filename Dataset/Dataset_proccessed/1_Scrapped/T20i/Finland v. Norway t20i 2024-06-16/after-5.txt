National News Headlines in English for 17 June 2024

Stay updated with 17 June 2024 news headlines in English for school assembly. Learn about various sectors´ happenings in our country. 

1. Opposition Slams Government Over NEET Controversy:
Opposition leaders are attacking the government over the NEET exam controversy, which has sparked protests nationwide. DMK criticises the National Testing Agency for damaging the exam’s integrity and accuses the government of supporting coaching centres. Congress demands a Supreme Court-monitored probe into the allegations of corruption.

2. Kargil Women Farmers Receive Mushroom Compost:
The Department of Agriculture in Kargil has started a trial distribution of button mushroom compost to progressive women farmers. This initiative aims to explore the commercial potential of mushroom production in the region, potentially transforming Kargil into an exporter of mushrooms during the off-season.

3. RBI Wins Risk Manager of the Year Award 2024:
The Reserve Bank of India has been honoured with the Risk Manager of the Year Award 2024 by Central Banking, London. The award recognizes RBI’s efforts in improving risk culture and awareness. Executive Director Shri Manoranjan Mishra accepted the award on behalf of RBI.

4. New Criminal Laws to be Included in Training Programs:
The Central government has directed all ministries to incorporate newly passed criminal laws in their training programs. Effective from July 1, the laws replace the old Indian Penal Code, Indian Evidence Act, and Code of Criminal Procedure. The new laws introduce stricter penalties and add new crimes.

5. Indian Air Force Participates in Exercise Red Flag 2024:
An Indian Air Force contingent participated in Exercise Red Flag 2024 in Alaska, USA. The exercise, held from June 4 to June 14, involved advanced aerial combat training with forces from several countries. The IAF contingent showcased the Rafale aircraft and other military assets in realistic combat scenarios.

6. PM Modi Sends Eid Greetings to Sheikh Hasina:
Prime Minister Narendra Modi sent warm Eid-Al-Adha greetings to Bangladeshi Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina. Modi emphasized the festival’s values of sacrifice, compassion, and brotherhood. He also wished Hasina good health and well-being ahead of her visit to New Delhi after the Eid vacation.

7. Smritivan Museum Nominated for Prix Versailles:
The Smriti Van Earthquake Memorial Museum in Bhuj has been shortlisted for the prestigious Prix Versailles Museums 2024 award. Prime Minister Narendra Modi and Gujarat Chief Minister Bhupendra Patel expressed pride in the museum’s global recognition for its architectural beauty and cultural significance.

8. Kargil Vijay Diwas Celebrated with Mini Marathon:
To mark the 25th anniversary of Kargil Vijay Diwas, the Indian Army organised a Mini Marathon in Kargil. Over 316 students participated in the event, which aimed to foster a spirit of remembrance and patriotism. The run was flagged off by Brigadier Pratheet Prashar and Deputy Commissioner Shrikant Suse.

9. Prerna Sthal Inaugurated in Parliament Complex:
Vice President Jagdeep Dhankhar inaugurated ‘Prerna Sthal’, housing statues of freedom fighters and leaders previously scattered across the Parliament complex. The new site aims to make these statues more accessible to visitors. The Congress criticized the move, claiming it reduced the prominence of these statues.

10. Hemis Monastery Festival Begins in Ladakh:
The annual Hemis Monastery Festival has started in Ladakh, attracting visitors from across India and abroad. Celebrated since the 17th century, the festival features monks performing sacred mask dances, honouring Guru Padma Sambhava. The event highlights Ladakh’s rich cultural and spiritual heritage.

11. Madhya Pradesh Opens New Jail Colony for Prisoners:
The Madhya Pradesh government has inaugurated an open jail colony in Ujjain, where prisoners can live with their families. Chief Minister Shivraj Singh Chouhan launched the initiative, aimed at rehabilitating prisoners and providing a more humane living environment.

12. TCS, Infosys, and Airtel Make Global Brand Impact:
TCS and Infosys, India’s top IT firms, and Airtel, a leading telecom giant, have clinched spots in the prestigious Top 100 Most Valuable Global Brands list by Kantar BrandZ 2024. TCS ranks 46th, with Airtel and Infosys at 73rd and 74th respectively, underscoring their global stature.

13. NCERT Alters Ayodhya Section in Class 12 Textbook:
The revised NCERT Class 12 Political Science textbook sparked controversy by referring to the Babri Masjid as a “three-domed structure,” reducing the Ayodhya section from four pages to two. Details on the BJP’s rath yatra, kar sevaks’ role, and post-demolition violence were omitted, raising debates on historical representation.

1. Thousands in Yemen Protest in Solidarity with Palestine:
Tens of thousands demonstrated in Yemen’s capital, Sana’a, showing solidarity with Palestine. Protesters demand an end to Israel’s destruction of Gaza and the lifting of its siege. The protests intensified after the arrest of a U.S.-Israeli spy network, aiming to end foreign interference in Yemen.

2. Former Arsenal Striker Kevin Campbell Dies at 54:
Kevin Campbell, former striker for Arsenal and Everton, passed away at 54 after a short illness. Campbell scored 148 goals in his career, playing for eight clubs, including Leyton Orient and Nottingham Forest. Arsenal and Everton mourn his loss, celebrating his legacy.

3. Eid Al Adha Celebrations Across the Globe:
Muslims celebrated Eid Al Adha on June 16th with prayers and rituals. The festival honours Prophet Ibrahim’s faith and the divine intervention of a sacrificial lamb. In Saudi Arabia, over 1.5 million pilgrims gathered at Muzdalifah, marking a significant moment in the Hajj pilgrimage.

4. G7 Leaders Back Major Infrastructure Projects:
The G7 summit in Italy concluded with leaders supporting the India-Middle East-Europe Economic Corridor (IMEC). They emphasised fair trade practices and committed to a free and open Indo-Pacific. The G7 also stressed the importance of the UN refugee agency’s work in Gaza.

5. Sri Lanka Reports 5.3% GDP Growth:
Sri Lanka’s economy grew by 5.3% in the first quarter of 2024. Efforts to overcome an economic crisis, aided by Indian support and an IMF package, have shown positive results. This growth brings optimism and potential investment opportunities for the island nation.

6. Tesla Shareholders Approve $45 Billion Pay Award for Elon Musk:
Tesla shareholders have reaffirmed a $45 billion pay award for CEO Elon Musk after a legal challenge. The vote, announced at Tesla’s annual meeting in Austin, Texas, demonstrates strong shareholder support for Musk and may influence the judge who voided the award to reinstate it. This decision reassures Musk’s admirers concerned about his potential departure from Tesla.

Sports News in English for 17 June 2024

Get the latest sports news in English and follow your favourite sports events:

1. Sumit Nagal Reaches Perugia Challenger Final:
Sumit Nagal, India’s top-ranked tennis player, advanced to the final of the Perugia Challenger after defeating Spain’s Bernabe Zapata Miralles. Nagal will face either Italy’s Luciano Darderi or Germany’s Daniel Altmaier in the final, seeking his first title in the tournament.

2. Spain Starts EURO 2024 Campaign with 3-0 Win:
Spain kicked off their UEFA EURO 2024 campaign with a 3-0 victory over Croatia. Goals from Álvaro Morata, Fabián Ruiz, and Dani Carvajal secured the win, marking Spain’s dominance in Group B. The match showcased Spain’s efficient and aggressive playing style.

3. Malaysian Pair Reaches Australian Open Badminton Final:
Malaysian women’s doubles pair Lai Pei Jing and Lim Chiew Sien reached the final of the Australian Open badminton championship. They defeated Hong Kong’s Yeung Nga Ting and Yeung Pui Lam in a thrilling match, continuing their impressive run in the tournament.

4. India Wins Bronze in Women’s Table Tennis at BRICS Games:
India’s women’s table tennis team won a bronze medal at the BRICS Games in Kazan. The team, comprising Poymantee Baisya, Moumita Dutta, and Yashini Sivasankar, lost to China in the semi-final but secured India’s first medal in the competition.

5. Gautam Gambhir to be Announced as India’s Next Head Coach:
Reports suggest Gautam Gambhir is set to be announced as India’s next head coach after the 2024 World Cup. The BCCI is expected to confirm his appointment this week, with Gambhir succeeding Rahul Dravid in the role.

6. Afghanistan Dominates in T20 World Cup 2024:
Afghanistan has dominated the T20 World Cup 2024, winning all three of their group matches. They secured victories against Uganda, New Zealand, and Papua New Guinea, qualifying for the Super 8 stage. Afghanistan will next face the West Indies on June 18.

Today’s Entertainment News in English 

Now, let’s dive into the entertainment world and see what news has taken place.

1. Anurag Kashyap Shares Experience of Working in Cinema:
Director Anurag Kashyap, known for films like “Black Friday” and “Gangs of Wasseypur,” shared insights into his career in Indian cinema. Despite challenges, Kashyap remains a prominent figure, currently working on the series “Bad Cop” and acting in the film “Maharaja.”

2. Rithala-Narela-Kundli Metro Corridor Approved:
The Centre has approved the construction of the Rithala-Narela-Kundli metro corridor. Delhi Lieutenant Governor V K Saxena advocated for the project, aiming to improve connectivity in the region. The metro corridor is expected to enhance urban infrastructure significantly.

3. Mumbai International Film Festival Launches Doc Film Bazaar:
The first ‘Doc Film Bazaar’ at the 18th Mumbai International Film Festival was inaugurated, providing financial support and a platform for budding documentary makers. The event aims to foster new talent and promote documentary filmmaking in India.

4. Paytm in Talks to Sell Ticketing Division to Zomato:
Paytm is reportedly in advanced talks with Zomato to sell its movie and events ticketing division. The move is part of a strategic overhaul amid declining sales. Other potential buyers are also interested, with negotiations still ongoing.

5. Jon Bon Jovi Returns to Performing After Vocal Surgery:
Rock legend Jon Bon Jovi is gradually returning to live performances following vocal cord surgery. The 62-year-old singer’s journey back to the stage is documented in the series “Thank You, Goodnight: The Bon Jovi Story,” highlighting his recovery and dedication to music.

6. Fans Catch Celebrities Off-Duty on Father’s Day:
Fans eagerly watch their favourite celebrities spending Father’s Day with their families. The internet buzzes with heartwarming images and stories of stars celebrating the day, especially those who grew up in the industry with famous parents.

What Happened Today in History

Understand the significance of what happened today in history and learn how to make a difference:

1. Battle of Bunker Hill (1775)
Marking a pivotal conflict in the American Revolutionary War, the Battle of Bunker Hill demonstrated the colonists’ resilience against British forces, despite ultimately losing the battle.

2. Iceland Declares Independence (1944)
On June 17, 1944, Iceland proclaimed its independence from Denmark, establishing itself as a republic after centuries of foreign rule.

3. Watergate Break-In (1972)
The infamous Watergate break-in occurred, leading to a major political scandal and the eventual resignation of U.S. President Richard Nixon.

4. Statue of Liberty Arrives in New York (1885)
The Statue of Liberty, a gift from France, arrived in New York Harbor on June 17, 1885, symbolising freedom and democracy.

5. End of Prohibition in Finland (1932)
Finland ended its Prohibition era on June 17, 1932, restoring the legal production and sale of alcoholic beverages after a 13-year ban.
