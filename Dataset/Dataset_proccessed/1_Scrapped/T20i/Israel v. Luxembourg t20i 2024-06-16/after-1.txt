National News Headlines in English for 18 June 2024

Stay updated with 18 June 2024 news headlines in English for school assembly. Learn about various sectors´ happenings in our country. 

1. India-US iCET Meeting to Review Key Defense and Technology Collaborations:

The second iCET meeting between India and the US kicks off in New Delhi today. National Security Advisers Ajit Doval and Jake Sullivan will assess progress on projects like GE-414 jet engines, semiconductor ventures, and the new Defense Industrial Cooperation Roadmap.

2. Deadly Train Collision in West Bengal: 15 Dead, 60 Injured:

A tragic collision near New Jalpaiguri station between the Sealdah-bound Kanchanjunga Express and a goods train resulted in 15 deaths and 60 injuries. PM Modi announced compensation for victims as heavy rains hampered rescue efforts, halting long-distance train services.

3. Renowned Wildlife Filmmaker Subbiah Nallamuthu Honoured with Lifetime Achievement Award:

Subbiah Nallamuthu received the prestigious Dr. V Shantaram Lifetime Achievement Award at the 18th MIFF. Known for his documentaries on the Royal Bengal Tiger, Nallamuthu’s work with National Geographic and BBC showcases a blend of factual depth and poetic visuals.

4. NCERT Defends Curriculum Changes Amid Criticism:

NCERT Director Dinesh Prasad Saklani justified textbook changes involving the Gujarat riots and Babri Masjid demolition, citing the need to avoid creating “violent and depressed citizens.” He emphasised these modifications as part of routine annual revisions, not a “saffronisation” of the curriculum.

5. CBIC Alerts Public to Fraudsters Posing as Customs Officers:

The CBIC warns of fraudsters impersonating Indian Customs officers to scam the public. A comprehensive awareness campaign, including ads and social media outreach, aims to educate citizens on recognizing and reporting these scams to protect their hard-earned money.

6. Indian Leaders Extend Heartfelt Eid-ul-Adha Greetings:

President Murmu, Vice-President Dhankar, and Prime Minister Modi conveyed warm Eid-ul-Adha wishes. Emphasising the festival’s themes of sacrifice and unity, they urged citizens to support the underprivileged and strengthen societal harmony on this auspicious occasion.

7. Indian Textile Exports Rise Despite Global Economic Slowdown:

Indian textile exports surged by 9.59% in May 2024, with apparel exports growing by 9.84%. The Commerce Ministry reported a 10.2% year-on-year increase in overall exports to USD 68.29 billion, showcasing India’s resilience amid challenging global market conditions.

8. India Advances with Ambitious Deep Sea Mission:

India is set to join the elite club of nations with a Deep Sea Mission, announced Dr. Jitendra Singh. This initiative aims to explore mineral resources, enhance ocean sciences, and conserve marine biodiversity, contributing to a sustainable blue economy.

9. Efforts Underway to Evacuate Stranded Tourists in Sikkim:

Over 1,200 tourists stranded in Sikkim’s Mangan district await evacuation as weather conditions improve. The district administration is providing lodging and food in Lachung, with evacuation planned by air or road when feasible. Tourists are urged to contact the Lachung police for assistance.

International News in English for 18 June 2024

Explore global stories through international news in English. Discover updates from different countries:

1. Reuters Institute Highlights AI’s Impact on Journalism and Revenue:

The Reuters Institute’s latest Digital News Report reveals how AI in news production and misinformation presents new challenges for newsrooms. The report, based on surveys of over 100,000 people from 47 countries, underscores the struggle for audience engagement and economic sustainability in the digital age.

2. China and Japan Trail Behind the US in 2024 World Economy Ranking:

The 2024 World Economy Ranking places China and Japan just behind the United States, followed by the UK, Germany, and India. Each nation’s economic influence is shaped by factors like natural resources, population size, politics, and trade agreements.

3. India Declines Joint Communiqué at Swiss Peace Summit for Ukraine:

India sent a senior diplomat to a Swiss peace summit for Ukraine but declined to join the joint communiqué issued by 80 nations. New Delhi emphasised that any peace initiative must include both Ukraine and Russia to be effective.

4. Indian Extradited to US Over Murder-For-Hire Plot:

Nikhil Gupta, an Indian national accused in a murder-for-hire plot against Sikh separatist Gurpatwant Singh Pannun, has been extradited from the Czech Republic to the US. Gupta is set to appear before a federal court in New York and is currently detained in Brooklyn.

5. Robert F. Kennedy Jr. and Elon Musk Advocate for Paper Ballots:

In response to voting irregularities in Puerto Rico’s primary elections, Robert F. Kennedy Jr. and Elon Musk have called for a return to paper ballots. They argue this ensures election integrity and prevents hacking of electronic voting systems.

6. Iceland Celebrates National Day with Special Google Doodle:

Iceland celebrates National Day on June 17, marking its transition to a republic in 1944. Google honours this day with a special doodle, commemorating both the new constitution and the birthday of independence leader Jón Sigurðsson.

7. US-India Strategic Partnership Forum Kicks Off Leadership Summit:

The US-India Strategic Partnership Forum begins its VII Annual Leadership Summit in Washington, focusing on critical and emerging technology initiatives. The summit aligns with US National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan’s visit to New Delhi for the iCET Dialogue.

8. Torrential Rains Prompt Evacuation of 36,000 in Fujian, China:

Heavy rains in Fujian, China, have forced the evacuation of 36,000 people and caused severe flooding. Mudslides have disrupted traffic, and rescue operations are underway. The government has issued high-temperature alerts amid ongoing extreme weather.

9. US-India Summit Gathers Key Leaders in Washington:

The US-India Strategic Partnership Forum’s VII Annual Leadership Summit in Washington features key Republican senators and senior White House officials. The summit addresses critical technology initiatives and supply chain strengthening between the two nations.

10. Bill Gates Advocates for Next-Gen Nuclear Power:

Bill Gates, discussing his TerraPower nuclear energy project, emphasised his commitment to investing billions in next-generation nuclear power. He highlighted the importance of global collaboration to reduce costs and address concerns over radioactive waste.

Sports News in English for 18 June 2024

Get the latest sports news in English and follow your favourite sports events:

1. Luka Modric Matches Cristiano Ronaldo’s Record in Croatia’s Euro 2024 Opener:

Luka Modric equalled Cristiano Ronaldo’s record by appearing in his fifth European Championship during Croatia’s Euro 2024 opener against Spain. Despite a 3-0 loss, Modric’s achievement places him alongside Ronaldo, who will soon set a new record by participating in his sixth Euro campaign with Portugal.

2. Babar Azam Reflects on Pakistan’s T20 World Cup Exit:

Pakistan captain Babar Azam acknowledged his team’s mistakes in their earlier T20 World Cup Group A matches against India and the USA. Despite a consolation win over Ireland, Babar emphasised that batting errors cost them crucial games, leading to their elimination from the tournament.

3. Sri Lanka Secures Dominant Win Over Netherlands in T20 World Cup:

Sri Lanka triumphed over the Netherlands by 83 runs in a Group D match of the T20 World Cup, securing their only win in the group stage. Charith Asalanka and Kusal Mendis led the charge, with Asalanka named Player of the Match for his quick-fire 46 runs off 21 balls.

4. Pakistan Claims Consolation Victory Over Ireland in T20 World Cup:

Pakistan defeated Ireland by three wickets in a T20 World Cup match in Florida. Despite both teams’ eliminations, Pakistan’s Babar Azam scored an unbeaten 32, helping his team chase down Ireland’s target of 107 runs and finish with 111 for seven in 18.5 overs.

5. Indian Archers Secure Olympic Quota in Women’s Individual Recurve:

Ankita Bhakat and Bhajan Kaur secured a quota place for India in the women’s individual recurve event at the Paris Olympics by reaching the quarter-finals of the Final Olympic Qualifier in Antalya, Turkey. Their success ensures India’s participation in both individual and mixed-team archery events.

Today’s Entertainment News in English 

Now, let’s dive into the entertainment world and see what news has taken place.

1. . MIFF Launches First-Ever Documentary Film Bazaar to Connect Filmmakers with Industry Stakeholders:

The 18th Mumbai International Film Festival (MIFF) introduced its inaugural Documentary Film Bazaar, held from June 16 to 18, 2024. This event, hosted at the NFDC complex, aims to elevate the documentary film industry by connecting filmmakers with buyers, sponsors, and collaborators. Attracting nearly 200 projects from 10 countries and showcasing 27 languages, the Bazaar highlights MIFF’s growing international appeal and diverse participation.

2. Decade-Old Mix-Up: Nishtha Jain’s ‘Gulabi Gang’ Misidentified as a Bollywood Film:

In 2014, non-fiction filmmaker Nishtha Jain’s documentary ‘Gulabi Gang’ won the National Film Awards, but many mistakenly attributed the win to a similarly named Bollywood film starring Madhuri Dixit and Juhi Chawla. Jain’s documentary, released in 2012, highlights the fight against a patriarchal society by the Sampat Pal-led women’s group in Bundelkhand, clad in pink sarees.

3. Kangana Ranaut Praises PM Modi’s Support for Women Amidst Meloni’s ‘Melodi’ Post:

Actor-turned-politician Kangana Ranaut praised Prime Minister Narendra Modi in response to a video shared by Italian Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni, captioned ‘Melodi.’ Ranaut highlighted Modi’s appeal to women, stating he makes them feel supported and empowered. Ranaut’s endorsement came via her Instagram story, emphasising Modi’s impact on female supporters.

4. Daniel Radcliffe Wins First Tony Award at 2024 Ceremony Hosted by Ariana DeBose:

The 2024 Tony Awards, held at the David H. Koch Theater in New York City, celebrated the best in theatre with host Ariana DeBose. Harry Potter star Daniel Radcliffe won his first Tony Award for his role in “Merrily We Roll Along,” securing the award for Featured Actor in a Musical for his performance as Charley Kringas. This marked Radcliffe’s first Tony nomination and win.

5. ‘Inside Out 2’ Tops North American Box Office with $155 Million Debut:

Pixar’s “Inside Out 2” dominated the North American box office, debuting with an impressive $155 million. The sequel continues the animated tale of Riley as she navigates new emotions entering high school. Analyst David A. Gross praised the film’s sensational opening, noting it as an exceptional standard for a Pixar sequel.

What Happened Today in History

Understand the significance of what happened today in history and learn how to make a difference:

1. June 18, 1815: Battle of Waterloo Ends Napoleon’s Reign

British and Prussian forces, under the command of the Duke of Wellington and Gebhard Leberecht von Blücher respectively, decisively defeated Napoleon Bonaparte’s French army. 

This historic battle near Waterloo in present-day Belgium marks the end of Napoleon’s rule as Emperor of the French and his final attempt to dominate Europe.

2. June 18, 1940: Winston Churchill’s “Finest Hour” Speech

British Prime Minister Winston Churchill delivers his iconic speech to the House of Commons during World War II. 

In the face of an imminent invasion by Nazi Germany, Churchill inspires courage and resolve among the British people, vowing to fight against tyranny and oppression.

3. June 18, 1812: United States Declares War on Britain

President James Madison of the United States signed a declaration of war against Great Britain, marking the start of the War of 1812. 

Tensions over trade restrictions, impressment of American sailors, and territorial expansion led to this conflict, which lasted until 1815 and shaped American national identity.

4. June 18, 1983: Sally Ride Becomes First American Woman in Space

Dr. Sally Ride, an astronaut aboard the Space Shuttle Challenger, becomes the first American woman to travel into space. 

Her mission aboard STS-7 marks a significant achievement in both gender equality and space exploration, inspiring future generations of women in STEM fields.

5. June 18, 1928: Amelia Earhart Becomes First Woman to Fly Across the Atlantic

Aviator Amelia Earhart, along with pilot Wilmer Stultz and co-pilot Louis Gordon, completes a transatlantic flight from Newfoundland, Canada, to Wales, becoming the first woman to fly across the Atlantic Ocean. 

This daring feat catapults Earhart to international fame as a pioneer in aviation and women’s rights.
