National News Headlines in English for 13 July 2024

Stay updated with 13 July 2024 news headlines in English for school assembly. Learn about various sectors´ happenings in our country. 

1. India Ranks Third in Schengen Visa Denials, Faces ₹109 Crore Loss:

Indian applicants faced high Schengen visa rejections in 2023, resulting in a ₹109 crore loss. This raises concerns about visa affordability and accessibility for European travel, impacting thousands of hopeful travellers.

2. IIT Madras to Host Global Hyperloop Competition in 2025:

IIT Madras will host a global hyperloop competition in 2025, showcasing a 410-meter vacuum tube. This initiative aims to establish India as a hub for hyperloop research and innovation on a global stage.

3. ETH Zürich Tops QS World University Rankings: Europe 2025:

ETH Zürich leads the QS World University Rankings for Europe 2025, ahead of Imperial College London and the University of Oxford. These rankings help students identify top universities for academic excellence in Europe.

4. UN Report Predicts India’s Population Peak in 2062:

India’s population is projected to peak at 1.701 billion in 2062, according to the UN. This demographic trend highlights the need for strategic investments in education, health, and infrastructure to harness the demographic dividend.

5. India Poised to Become Superpower by 2047, Challenges Ahead:

Financial Times predicts India could achieve superpower status by 2047. However, achieving this requires accelerating economic growth amidst global challenges, emphasizing political strength and strategic reforms.

6. Bombay HC Orders Compensation for Wrongly Accused Chinese Woman:

Bombay High Court directs compensation for Cong Ling, wrongfully detained in a gold smuggling case since 2019. The ruling emphasizes protecting foreign nationals’ rights and ensuring fair treatment under Indian law.

7. RBI Governor Advocates Credit Rating Upgrade for India:

RBI Governor urges credit rating agencies to upgrade India’s rating due to robust economic fundamentals and investor confidence. This could reduce borrowing costs and attract more foreign investments to the country.

8. MHA Cancels FCRA Registration Amid Environmental Concerns:

Ministry of Home Affairs revokes FCRA registration of a non-profit, citing environmental risks in an Adani Group project. This move reflects concerns over development impact and ecological sustainability in Gujarat’s Kutch region.

9. Insurance Regulator Issues Notice to Religare’s Care Health:

India’s insurance regulator issues a notice to Religare’s Care Health over stock options granted to its chairperson, amid ongoing ownership disputes. This scrutiny highlights regulatory oversight in the financial sector.

10. Schengen Visa Rejections Cost Indian Travelers ₹109 Crore in 2023:

Indian travellers faced significant financial losses due to Schengen visa rejections, totalling ₹109 crore. This highlights challenges in visa processing and its impact on travel plans to Europe for many Indians.

11. DRDO Awards Seven New Projects to Defence Industries:

DRDO allocates seven new projects to defence industries under its Technology Development Fund, supporting innovation and capability enhancement in India’s aerospace and defence sectors.

International News in English for 13 July 2024

Explore global stories through international news in English. Discover updates from different countries:

1. Japan’s Ministry of Defense Rebukes Over 100 for Security Lapses:

Japan’s Defense Ministry reprimanded over 100 officials for mishandling classified information, amid concerns about cyber threats. The lapses highlight challenges as Japan boosts military spending and enhances US cooperation.

2. France Evicts Homeless Migrants Ahead of 2024 Olympics:

France begins evicting thousands of homeless migrants from Paris, denying Olympic Games link. This move amid a housing crisis sparks controversy over social policies and preparations for the upcoming event.

3. SCO Leaders Meet, China Urges Support Amid NATO Summit:

SCO leaders meet in Kazakhstan; President Xi urges support among members. The summit, contrasting NATO’s stance on Ukraine, focuses on integrating new members like Iran and enhancing regional cooperation.

4. UN Warns Myanmar Junta’s Actions Threaten Country’s Stability:

UN warns Myanmar’s junta escalating conflict with ethnic armed groups, damaging country’s stability. Military losses lead to attacks on civilians, including schools and hospitals, intensifying humanitarian concerns.

5. Nepal’s PM Faces Confidence Vote Amid Political Turmoil:

Nepal’s PM faces fifth confidence vote amid coalition shifts. Opposition parties unite against him; rival KP Sharma Oli is likely to return to power. Political manoeuvring impacts stability and governance in Nepal.

6. South Korea, NATO Sign Agreement on Military Certification:

South Korea and NATO signed the first mutual recognition deal for military airworthiness certification. Agreement enhances cooperation; and speeds up the certification process for South Korean-made aircraft, strengthening defense ties.

7. NATO Accuses China of Enabling Russia-Ukraine Conflict:

NATO accuses China of aiding Russia’s aggression in Ukraine. Summit highlights concerns over China’s role in global security, including technological support to Russia and expanding military capabilities and cyber activities.

Sports News in English for 13 July 2024

Get the latest sports news in English and follow your favourite sports events:

1. Ben Stokes Joins Elite Club with 6,000 Runs and 200 Wickets in Test Cricket:

Ben Stokes became the third player, and first from England, to achieve over 6,000 runs and 200 wickets in Test cricket, joining the ranks of Garry Sobers and Jacques Kallis, showcasing his all-round prowess against West Indies.

2. Prakash Padukone to Mentor Indian Badminton Squad at the Paris Olympics:

Prakash Padukone, India’s badminton legend, will mentor the Indian squad at the Paris Olympics, emphasizing his role in nurturing talents like PV Sindhu and Lakshya Sen from his academy to international success.

3. Saurabh Netravalkar: From Cricket Captain to Music Maestro:

Saurabh Netravalkar, an accomplished cricketer and former software engineer, made headlines with his diverse talents, from leading India in T20 cricket to mastering music, showcasing his multi-faceted achievements beyond sports.

4. Paris Olympics Sets Record with 8.6 Million Tickets Sold:

The Paris Olympics has already achieved a milestone before the games begin, selling a record 8.6 million tickets, surpassing the previous record of 8.3 million set by the 1996 Atlanta Games.

5. Lamine Yamal’s Historic Goal and Upcoming Euro Cup Final:

Lamine Yamal made headlines globally after scoring a historic goal for Spain. Trailing 1-0 against France in the Euro Cup 2024 semifinal, Yamal scored a remarkable goal from outside the box into the top corner. At just 16 years and 362 days old, he became the youngest player to score in the tournament’s history.

What Happened Today in History

Understand the significance of what happened today in history and learn how to make a difference:

1. Live Aid Concert Raises Millions for Ethiopian Famine Relief:

On July 13, 1985, the Live Aid concert was held simultaneously in London and Philadelphia. Organized by Bob Geldof and Midge Ure, it raised millions for Ethiopian famine relief, drawing global attention to the crisis.

2. Iconic Hollywood Sign Completed as Real Estate Advertisement:

On July 13, 1923, the iconic Hollywood Sign was officially completed. Originally reading “Hollywoodland,” the sign was an advertisement for a new housing development in the hills above Hollywood, California.

3. First FIFA World Cup Final Held in Montevideo, Uruguay:

On July 13, 1930, the first FIFA World Cup final was held in Montevideo, Uruguay. The host nation, Uruguay, won the tournament by defeating Argentina 4-2, marking the beginning of the world’s most popular sports competition.

4. United Nations Establishes World Population Day to Raise Awareness:

On July 13, 1989, the United Nations Development Programme established World Population Day. This observance aims to raise awareness of global population issues, emphasizing the importance of family planning, gender equality, and maternal health.

5. The Rolling Stones Perform First Concert at Marquee Club in London:

On July 13, 1962, The Rolling Stones performed their first concert at the Marquee Club in London. This event marked the beginning of one of the most enduring and influential rock bands in history.
