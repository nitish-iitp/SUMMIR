In today’s School Assembly News Headlines, we will provide a concise summary of the top 10 news stories, keeping you informed on key developments in politics, education, science, and culture, both in India and around the world.

1. Omar Abdullah Takes Oath as Chief Minister of Jammu and Kashmir
Omar Abdullah has been sworn in as the Chief Minister of Jammu and Kashmir, with Surinder Kumar Choudhary as his deputy. The oath-taking ceremony, held at SKICC in Srinagar, was attended by key leaders from the INDIA bloc, marking a significant political shift in the region.

2. Severe Flooding Hits Southern India Due to Heavy Rains
Karnataka and Tamil Nadu are experiencing severe flooding caused by persistent heavy rainfall. The Indian Meteorological Department reported a depression over the Bay of Bengal, predicting further impacts along the coast between Puducherry and Nellore by October 17.

3. Over 200 Air India Passengers Rescued After Hoax Bomb Threat in Canada
More than 200 passengers from an Air India flight stranded in Canada due to a hoax bomb threat are being transported to Chicago by the Royal Canadian Air Force. This incident underscores the ongoing diplomatic challenges between India and Canada.

4. Visitor Visa Issuance to Indians Increases Despite Tensions with Canada
Amid rising diplomatic tensions, India has seen a slight increase in visitor visa issuances to Canada, with 365,750 visas granted from January to July this year, compared to 345,631 during the same period last year.

5. Manipur Assembly Members Call for Peaceful Resolution Amid Violence
Elected representatives from Manipur’s Kuki-Zo-Hmar, Meitei, and Naga communities convened in New Delhi, urging all communities to reject violence and work towards peace to prevent further loss of innocent lives amid ongoing unrest.

6. Uttar Pradesh Government Introduces Strict Ordinance Against Spitting and Food Contamination
The Uttar Pradesh government is set to implement a new ordinance targeting spitting and food contamination, led by Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath. The move emphasizes the need for stringent action against public health offenses.

7. Yogi Adityanath and Israeli Ambassador Discuss Security and Employment Opportunities
Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath met with Israeli Ambassador Reuven Azar to strengthen bilateral relations, focusing on security measures for the upcoming Mahakumbh 2025 and exploring employment opportunities for skilled workers from Uttar Pradesh in Israel.

8. India Emerges as Top Source Market for Tourism in Sri Lanka
With over 300,000 Indian tourists visiting Sri Lanka this year, India has become the largest source of tourism for the island. Improved connectivity through flights and ferry services has made travel easier, contributing to a total of 1.5 million tourists welcomed by mid-October.

9. 1.4 Million North Korean Youth Apply to Join Military Amid Rising Tensions
North Korean state media reported that around 1.4 million youths have applied to join the military, driven by heightened tensions following a drone incursion by South Korea. This reflects a strong commitment among the youth to defend their nation.

10. Myanmar Ties with China for Lowest Internet Freedom Record
Myanmar has recorded the lowest score for internet freedom, tied with China, according to a recent study. The findings highlight a continued decline in internet access since the military coup in 2021, marking the 14th consecutive year of deteriorating conditions globally.

National News Headlines in English for 17 October 2024

The national news headlines section in today’s school assembly will focus on important events and updates happening across the country. 

1. S Jaishankar Engages in Key Diplomatic Discussions at SCO Meeting in Pakistan
External Affairs Minister S. Jaishankar arrived in Islamabad on Tuesday to participate in the Shanghai Cooperation Organisation (SCO) meeting. He joined Pakistani Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif for an informal dinner, exchanging pleasantries and emphasizing diplomatic engagement during his visit.

2. Nirmala Sitharaman to Strengthen Economic Ties During Two-Nation Visit to Mexico and the USA
Union Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman departs today for a two-nation trip to Mexico and the USA. In Mexico, she will chair the Tech Leaders Roundtable in Guadalajara and meet with the finance minister, aiming to enhance bilateral economic cooperation and development initiatives.

3. Foundation Stone Laid for Indian Navy’s Strategic VLF Radar Station in Vikarabad
Defence Minister Rajnath Singh, alongside Telangana Chief Minister Revanth Reddy, laid the foundation stone for the Indian Navy’s Very Low Frequency (VLF) Radar Station in Vikarabad. The station is designed to enhance maritime security and facilitate secure communication between naval forces and command centres.

4. Industry Leaders Commend PM Modi’s Vision for Digital India at India Mobile CongressDuring the 8th India Mobile Congress in New Delhi, industry leaders applauded Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s vision for Digital India. 5. Joint Parliamentary Committee Reviews Waqf Amendment Bill to Enhance Property Management
The Joint Parliamentary Committee convened in New Delhi to discuss the Waqf Amendment Bill, 2024. Chaired by MP Jagdambika Pal, the committee evaluated measures to improve the administration and management of Waqf properties, with a report expected in the Winter Session of Parliament.

6. Canadian Trade Minister Reassures Businesses Amidst Diplomatic Tensions with India
In response to rising diplomatic tensions, Canadian Trade Minister Mary Ng reassured the business community of Canada’s commitment to strong commercial ties with India. This follows accusations from Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau regarding alleged criminal activities supported by the Indian government.

1. Supreme Court Orders Action Against Stubble Burning Violators in Haryana and Punjab
The Supreme Court criticized the Haryana and Punjab governments for failing to prosecute those guilty of stubble burning. The court summoned the chief secretaries of both states to explain the inaction during a hearing scheduled for October 23. It directed the Commission for Air Quality Management (CAQM) to penalize state officials for their negligence.

2. Manipur Assembly Members Urge Peaceful Resolution Amidst Ongoing Violence
Elected representatives from the Kuki-Zo-Hmar, Meitei, and Naga communities of Manipur convened in New Delhi to address the state’s turmoil. The Union Home Ministry announced that the meeting ended with a unanimous appeal for all communities to reject violence and prevent further loss of innocent lives.

3. Uttar Pradesh Government to Implement Strict Ordinance Against Spitting and Food Contamination
The Uttar Pradesh government is preparing to introduce an ordinance targeting spitting and the contamination of food with human waste. Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath led a high-level meeting to discuss the ordinance’s provisions, attended by senior officials from various departments, emphasizing the need for stringent action against such offenses.

4. Yogi Adityanath and Israeli Ambassador Discuss Strengthening Ties and Security Measures
Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath met with Israeli Ambassador Reuven Azar to enhance bilateral relations, focusing on security for the upcoming Mahakumbh 2025 in Prayagraj. The discussions included police modernization, anti-drone technology, and increasing employment opportunities for skilled workers from Uttar Pradesh in Israel.

5. Uttarakhand Disburses Financial Aid to Jamrani Dam Affected Families
Under the direction of Chief Minister Pushkar Singh Dhami, Uttarakhand has deposited ₹1.95 billion into the accounts of 494 families affected by the Jamrani Dam Multipurpose Project. The remaining beneficiaries will also receive their funds after verification, which is currently underway, ensuring timely compensation for those impacted.

Over three lakh Indian tourists have visited Sri Lanka this year, making India the largest tourism source. By mid-October, the island welcomed 1.5 million tourists, with improved connectivity through flights and ferry services enhancing travel ease and comfort.

Myanmar has tied with China for the lowest score on internet freedom, according to a new study. The report highlights deteriorating conditions following the military coup in 2021, with declines in internet access being reported globally for the 14th consecutive year.

China’s Taiwan Affairs Office stated it would not renounce the use of force concerning Taiwan. This announcement follows significant military drills around the island, described as warnings against “separatist acts” after Taiwan President Lai Ching-te’s recent speech.

US authorities imposed a record $4 million fine on Lufthansa for discriminating against over 100 Jewish passengers, denying them boarding due to COVID-19 mask violations. This incident, involving 128 travelers, highlights ongoing scrutiny of airline practices concerning passenger treatment.
Sports News in English for 17 October 2024

Catch up on the latest sports news in English and stay updated on your favorite sports events. Find out scores, highlights, and key moments from recent games and matches.

1. NFL Approval: Tom Brady Joins Las Vegas Raiders as Stakeholder
On Tuesday, NFL team owners granted approval for seven-time Super Bowl champion Tom Brady to join the ownership group of the Las Vegas Raiders. Brady, who retired after a legendary 23-season career in 2022, will acquire a 5% stake in the franchise, which is currently struggling with a 2-4 record this season.

2. Lionel Messi Dominates: Hat-Trick Propels Argentina to 6-0 Victory Over BoliviaLionel Messi showcased his brilliance by scoring a hat-trick and assisting in two additional goals as Argentina overwhelmed Bolivia 6-0 in the South American qualifiers for the 2026 World Cup. This match marked Messi’s second appearance since recovering from an injury sustained during the Copa America in July.

3. Women’s Hockey India League Auction: Udita Duhan Sets Record as Most Expensive Player
In a thrilling auction for the Women’s Hockey India League (HIL), Indian defender Udita Duhan emerged as the most expensive player, secured by the Shrachi Rarh Bengal Tigers for an astonishing ₹32 lakh.

4. West Indies Advance to ICC Women’s T20 World Cup Semifinals After Victory Over England
The West Indies have secured their place in the semifinals of the ICC Women’s T20 World Cup by defeating England by six wickets in Dubai. After electing to field first, the Caribbean side successfully restricted England to 141 runs for 7, with standout performances from Nat Sciver-Brunt, who scored 57 runs, and Afy Fletcher, who took three wickets.

5. Thomas Tuchel Appointed as England Men’s National Team Manager
Former Chelsea head coach Thomas Tuchel is set to become the new manager of the England men’s national football team after reportedly signing a new contract. Tuchel will be the third foreign manager to guide England, following in the footsteps of Sweden’s Sven-Goran Eriksson and Italy’s Fabio Capello.

ASML Holdings’ shares fell by 15.7% after the company’s third-quarter earnings were mistakenly published early. The report revealed a cautious outlook for 2025, causing concern among investors regarding the company’s projected sales figures.

3. UK Government Focuses on Strengthening Trade with India and Gulf Nations
The UK is enhancing its trade discussions with India and Gulf countries to boost economic ties. Business Minister Jonathan Reynolds announced that negotiations with the Gulf Cooperation Council would resume soon, aiming to create new investment opportunities.

4. Government Allocates Spectrum for Satellite Communications, Impacting Major Companies
India will allocate spectrum for satellite communications without an auction, favoring competitors of Reliance Jio. This decision, announced by Communications Minister Jyotiraditya Scindia, is expected to benefit companies like Elon Musk’s Starlink.

BEML Ltd has secured a contract to design and manufacture high-speed trainsets for Indian railways. The project, valued at approximately Rs 866.87 crore, marks a significant milestone in India’s efforts to develop its high-speed rail infrastructure.

YouTube will now label videos shot with real cameras to combat AI-generated content. The new “captured with a camera” label will appear in video descriptions, indicating that the footage has not been edited or altered, thus ensuring authenticity for viewers.

Lenovo has launched new hybrid AI PCs, including the ThinkPad X1 2-in-1 Gen 10 Aura Edition. These devices aim to combine laptop and tablet features while enhancing user experiences with advanced AI technology, making it accessible to everyday users.

Prime Minister Narendra Modi announced that India has transformed into a key smartphone manufacturing center during the India Mobile Congress 2024. He highlighted the growth from two manufacturing units in 2014 to over 200 today, marking a significant shift from being a mobile importer to a major exporter.

5. Intel and AMD Collaborate for Software Compatibility
Intel and AMD have formed a collaborative group to ensure software compatibility across their chipsets. This initiative addresses challenges from competitors like Arm Holdings, reinforcing the long-standing partnership that has defined the x86 computing architecture for over 40 years.
Education News Today for School Assembly

Today’s education news headlines for school assembly bring attention to key developments in the field of education.

1. Supreme Court Affirms Educational Rights for Candidates with Disabilities
The Supreme Court has ruled that a permanent disability of 40% does not automatically disqualify candidates from admission to educational institutions. In a landmark decision, the court confirmed the admission of a candidate with a 45% disability to the MBBS program, emphasizing that eligibility depends on a medical board’s assessment of the candidate’s ability to pursue the course.

2. GSEB Unveils Timetable for Class 10 and 12 Board Exams 2025
The Gujarat Secondary and Higher Secondary Education Board (GSEB) has announced the schedule for the upcoming Class 10 (SSC) and Class 12 (HSC) Board Exams, set to take place from February 27 to March 13, 2025. Students can access the detailed timetable, covering various academic streams, on the GSEB’s official websites.

3. IGNOU Extends Admission Deadline for July 2024 Session to October 31
The Indira Gandhi National Open University (IGNOU) has extended the fresh admission deadline for the July 2024 session to October 31, applying to all programs except for semester-based and certificate courses. This extension allows prospective students additional time to enroll in Open and Distance Learning (ODL) and online programs.

4. IIT Madras Opens Applications for Executive MBA Program with Upcoming Selection Process
The Department of Management Studies (DoMS) at IIT Madras is now accepting applications for its Executive MBA program, with a registration deadline set for October 20. Shortlisted candidates will undergo a selection process from November 8 to 10, which includes a written aptitude test and personal interview. Results will be available by December 2024.

5. Deadline for CLAT 2025 Registration Extended to October 22The Consortium of National Law Universities (NLUs) has announced an extension for the registration deadline for the Common Law Admission Test (CLAT) 2025 to October 22. Candidates interested in pursuing undergraduate and postgraduate law programs can apply on the official website before the new deadline, with a non-refundable application fee structure outlined for different candidate categories.

Top 5 Word of the Day for School Assembly News Headlines

The Word of the Day segment introduces students to a new vocabulary word daily as part of the school assembly news headlines.

Word 	Meaning 	Sentence 	Often Used In
Unify (Verb)	To bring together or combine into a whole.	The teacher’s goal was to unify the students under a common cause of environmental conservation.	Often used when talking about bringing different groups, ideas, or people together.
Unanimous (Adjective)	Fully in agreement; when everyone involved shares the same opinion.	The vote for the class president was unanimous, with everyone supporting the same candidate.	Used in decision-making contexts or voting.
Ubiquitous (Adjective)	Present, appearing, or found everywhere.	Smartphones have become ubiquitous, seen in the hands of people all around the world.	Often used to describe something common or widespread.
Undermine (Verb)	To weaken or damage something, especially gradually or secretly.	Spreading rumors can undermine trust between friends.	Common in discussions about authority, trust, or efforts.
Unprecedented (Adjective)	Never done or known before.	The team’s victory in the championship was unprecedented in the school’s history.	Used when referring to new, remarkable events or achievements.
Ultimatum (Noun)	A final demand or statement of terms, the rejection of which will result in retaliation or a breakdown in relations.	The company issued an ultimatum to the workers, stating that they must accept the new terms or face a strike.	Often used in negotiations or conflicts.
