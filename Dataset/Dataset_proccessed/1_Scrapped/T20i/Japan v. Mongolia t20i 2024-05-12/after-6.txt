National News Headlines in English for 16 May 2024

Stay updated with national news headlines in English. Learn about various sectors’ happenings in our country:

1. Supreme Court Urges Yoga Guru Ramdev to Use Influence Wisely: The Supreme Court advised Yoga Guru Ramdev to use his influence positively during a hearing on the Patanjali advertisement case. The court delayed a decision on contempt proceedings against Ramdev, his aide, and the company.

2. India-UAE Joint Committee on Consular Affairs meets in New Delhi: India and the UAE discussed consular issues like labour, visas, and migration at their 5th joint committee meeting. The UAE briefed India on measures for Indian workers’ welfare. Both sides reaffirmed their commitment to improving consular services and promoting people-to-people exchanges.

3. Supreme Court Orders Release of NewsClick Founder: The Supreme Court ordered the release of NewsClick founder Prabir Purkayastha, citing procedural faults in his arrest under anti-terror laws. The trial court will set bail conditions for him.

4. Indian Air Force Tests BHISHM Cube Mobile Hospital: The Indian Air Force successfully tested the BHISHM Cube, a mobile hospital, in Agra. It’s part of Project BHISHM to provide quick medical aid during emergencies. The cube can treat up to 200 casualties and is versatile for diverse situations.

5. Investigation Launched into Spice Contamination in Indian Brands: New Zealand’s food safety regulator is investigating possible contamination in spice products of Indian brands MDH and Everest. Earlier, the US, Australia, and Hong Kong had raised concerns over pesticide levels in these spices.

International News in English for 16 May 2024

Explore global stories through international news in English. Discover updates from different countries:

1. Proposed Ban on Sex Education in English Primary Schools: England is considering banning sex education for children under nine and restricting explicit discussions for those under 13. The new guidelines by Education Secretary Gillian Keegan aim to be published this week, suggesting no sex education before age nine.

2. Singapore’s Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong to Step Down: Singapore’s PM Lee Hsien Loong, 72, will retire after two decades in office, passing leadership to Lawrence Wong. Lee, following his father, Lee Kuan Yew, Singapore’s founding father, will stay as a senior minister. 

3. Canada Imposes First-Ever Sanctions on Israeli Settlers for Violence Against Palestinians: Canada has sanctioned four Israeli settlers for violence against Palestinians in the West Bank. This unprecedented move aims to deter further violence and follows similar actions by the US and Britain.

Sports News in English for 16 May 2024

Get the latest sports news in English and follow your favourite sports events:

1. Indian Men’s Cricket Team to Get New Head Coach: Rahul Dravid, the current head coach, will step down after India’s T20 World Cup campaign in June. The BCCI has started the search for a replacement, with applications invited until May 27.

2. Manchester City Inches Closer to Premier League Title: Erling Haaland’s double secures a 2-0 victory for Manchester City against Tottenham, bringing them within one win of a historic fourth consecutive Premier League title. City leads by two points ahead of Arsenal in the final round.

3. Swiatek Wins 11th Straight Clay Match, Nears Record at Rome Open: Iga Swiatek won her 11th straight clay-court match, beating Coco Gauff 6-4, 6-3 in the Rome Open semi-finals, moving closer to the Madrid-Rome double last achieved by Serena Williams 11 years ago.

2. DP World International League T20 Season 3 Set to Kickoff: The third season of the DP World International League T20 will commence on January 11, 2025, with 34 matches over a month. Indian cricket fans eagerly await the tournament, which will be broadcast on ZEE’s platforms worldwide.

World Important Day on 16 May 2024 

Understand the significance of World Important Day and learn how to make a difference:

1. The First Academy Awards Ceremony (1929)

Hollywood’s elite gather for the inaugural Oscars, honouring excellence in film. Wings takes flight as the first Best Picture winner, marking a historic moment in cinema.

2. Brown v. Board of Education (1954)

The Supreme Court rules segregation in public schools unconstitutional, sparking a monumental shift in civil rights and paving the way for desegregation across America.

3. IBM Personal Computer Released (1983)

IBM unveils its groundbreaking PC, revolutionising personal computing. With its accessible design and user-friendly interface, it has become a cornerstone of the digital age.

4.  Sudan Declares Independence (1956)

Sudan emerges as a sovereign nation, breaking free from British and Egyptian rule. The Declaration of Independence marks a pivotal moment in Sudanese history and national identity.

5. First Woman in Space (1963)

Valentina Tereshkova, a Soviet cosmonaut, makes history as the first woman to journey into space. Her pioneering mission inspires generations of women to reach for the stars.
