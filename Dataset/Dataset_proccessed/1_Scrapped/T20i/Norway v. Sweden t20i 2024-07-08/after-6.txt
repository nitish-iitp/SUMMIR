National News Headlines in English for 12 July 2024

Stay updated with 12 July 2024 news headlines in English for school assembly. Learn about various sectors´ happenings in our country. 

1. Commerce Minister Goyal Heads to Switzerland for $100 Billion Investment Talks:

Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal will visit Switzerland to meet investors and discuss the $100-billion investment commitment under the India-European Free Trade Association (EFTA) trade deal. The agreement involves EFTA nations Iceland, Switzerland, Norway, and Liechtenstein.

2. Paytm Agrees to Settle Termination Dispute After Labour Ministry Intervention:

Following the Labour Ministry’s intervention, Paytm has agreed not to recover the joining bonus and will pay the notice period amount to an employee after a termination dispute. The regional labour commissioner acted swiftly on the complaint.

3. CBI Reveals ISRO Espionage Case Fabricated by Kerala Cop:

The CBI has revealed that the 1994 ISRO espionage case against scientist Nambi Narayanan was fabricated by a Kerala cop after a Maldivian woman rejected his advances. The charges were filed against five former police officers.

4. PM Modi Visits Russia, Expected to Sign RELOS with President Putin:

During his visit to Russia, PM Modi was warmly welcomed by President Putin. They are expected to sign the Reciprocal Exchange of Logistics Agreement (RELOS), enhancing military cooperation and access to Russian military facilities in the Arctic.

5. USCIS Delays H-1B Modernization Phase 2 to December 2024:

USCIS has delayed implementing the second phase of the H-1B program modernization plan to December 2024. This phase will revise the definition of an H-1B specialty occupation, expand site visits, and enhance cap-gap protection for international students.

6. Arun Kumar Bansal Appointed MD and CEO of Paytm Payments Bank:

Paytm Payments Bank has appointed Arun Kumar Bansal as Managing Director and CEO. Bansal, a former IDBI Bank executive, received approval from the RBI for his new role and resigned from IDBI Bank.

7. Supreme Court Highlights Homemakers’ Role and Financial Support from Husbands:

The Supreme Court emphasized the vital role of homemakers and ruled that divorced Muslim women can seek alimony under Section 125 of the Code of Criminal Procedure. The court stressed the importance of husbands providing financial support to their wives.

8. Indian Government to Boost Space Sector Funding for Startups:

India plans to increase funding for the space sector to support startups and foster innovation. This move aims to drive advancements in space technology and encourage private sector participation, with IN-SPACe facilitating collaboration between ISRO and private companies.

9. Gautam Adani to Launch Shipbuilding Operations at Mundra Port:

Billionaire Gautam Adani plans to start shipbuilding operations at Mundra, India’s largest port, as global shipyards are fully booked until 2028. This initiative aligns with India’s goal to rise in global shipbuilding rankings under Maritime India Vision 2030 and Maritime Amrit Kaal Vision 2047.

10. Schoolnet Acquires Advitiya Sharma’s Startup Genius Teacher:

Edtech firm Schoolnet has acquired Genius Teacher, founded by Housing.com co-founder Advitiya Sharma. This acquisition is part of a broader consolidation trend in the edtech sector, with multiple notable deals reported in recent months.

11. PM Modi Visits Austria to Deepen Bilateral Ties:

Prime Minister Narendra Modi arrived in Austria for the first visit by an Indian PM in over 40 years. During his stay, he will meet Austrian President Alexander Van der Bellen and Chancellor Nehammer to discuss deepening bilateral relations and addressing geopolitical challenges.

12. RBI Approves Sale of Up to 51% Stake in Yes Bank:

The RBI has approved the sale of up to 51% stake in Yes Bank, potentially valuing the bank at around $10 billion. This decision marks a significant shift for Yes Bank, which faced a severe crisis just four years ago.

13. Defence Minister Launches GRSE Innovation Scheme in Kolkata:

Minister of State for Defence Sanjay Seth launched the “GRSE Accelerated Innovation Nurturing Scheme (GAINS 2024)” in Kolkata. The scheme aims to promote technological development and innovation in shipbuilding, encouraging participation from MSMEs and startups.

14. Population Day Highlights Need for Family Planning Awareness:

July 11 marks Population Day, focusing on challenges and opportunities related to rising population. In the Kodagu district, 1,533 women have undergone family planning surgery since April, while men have shown little interest, despite efforts to raise awareness and provide incentives.the

State News Headlines in English for 12 July 2024

Stay updated with the latest developments and impactful stories unfolding across your state.

Uttar Pradesh

1. Allahabad HC Rules Against Right to Convert in Religious Freedom Case:

The Allahabad High Court denied bail to Shriniwas Rav Nayak, accused of religious conversions under the UP Prohibition of Unlawful Conversion of Religion Act, 2021. The court emphasized that freedom of religion does not include the right to convert others.

2. UP Teachers Protest Against Online Attendance Order:

UP government’s order for Basic Shiksha Parishad teachers and non-teaching staff to mark attendance online faced backlash. Only 2% complied, while thousands protested, with Samajwadi Party’s Akhilesh Yadav supporting the teachers.

3. Ghaziabad Metro Corridors to Feature TOD Zones:

UP state officials directed the Ghaziabad Development Authority to designate 500 meters of transit-oriented development (TOD) zones along metro corridors. These zones will be included in the city’s draft Master Plan 2031, set to replace the current Master Plan 2021.

Karnataka

4. Bengaluru Start-Up Proposes Expandable Space Habitat:

Bengaluru-based start-up Akashalabdhi, incubated at IISc, is developing “expandable space habitats” for astronauts and researchers. Their prototype, ‘Antariksh HAB,’ aims to provide superior protection against orbital debris and radiation.

5. Bengaluru Metro to Install Platform Screen Doors at Key Stations:

Bengaluru Metro Rail Corporation Limited (BMRCL) will install platform screen doors at Nadaprabhu Kempegowda station and Sir M Visvesvaraya station. These doors enhance safety by preventing falls and synchronizing with metro coach doors.

6. YouTuber and Political Member ‘Sattai’ Durai Murugan Arrested:

‘Sattai’ Durai Murugan, a YouTuber and member of Naam Tamilar Katchi, was arrested near Tenkasi. He allegedly made derogatory remarks against former Chief Minister M. Karunanidhi during a campaign for his party candidate in the Vikravandi by-election.

International News in English for 12 July 2024

Explore global stories through international news in English. Discover updates from different countries:

1. Samsung Electronics Faces Unprecedented Labor Strike:

Samsung Electronics is dealing with a historic three-day strike by its largest union. This action, the biggest in Samsung’s 55-year history, raises concerns about labour unrest and the need for companies to collaborate with unions.

2. Skillsoft Launches GenAI Skilling Program with Microsoft:

Skillsoft, in collaboration with Microsoft, announced a new GenAI skilling program. This initiative aims to upskill employees in using Microsoft AI tools like Copilot and Azure Open AI, enhancing efficiency and creativity in various functions.

3. WhatsApp to Introduce Meta AI Features:

WhatsApp is set to integrate new Meta AI capabilities, allowing AI to respond to and edit images. Additionally, users will soon be able to create images using Meta AI, expanding the app’s functionality.

4. Russia Plans New Orbital Space Station by 2033:

Russia approved a schedule to develop a new orbital space station by 2033. The plan includes launching initial modules by 2027 and additional ones through 2033, marking a shift from its collaboration with the International Space Station.

5. US to Deploy Long-Range Missiles in Germany by 2026:

The US will deploy long-range missiles in Germany starting in 2026. This strategic move aims to counter growing threats from Russia and includes potent weapons like SM-6, Tomahawk, and developmental hypersonic missiles.

6. Italy Cracks Down on Migrant Visa Fraud:

Italian authorities arrested over 40 individuals for migrant visa fraud, targeting mafia exploitation of the system. The suspects face charges including abetting illegal immigration, money laundering, and false invoicing.

7. US Expresses Concerns Over India-Russia Relations:

The US reiterated its concerns about India’s relationship with Russia, especially following PM Modi’s visit to Russia. The US urged India to support efforts for a just peace in Ukraine, emphasizing the need for diplomatic resolutions.

Sports News in English for 12 July 2024

Get the latest sports news in English and follow your favourite sports events:

1. Igor Stimac Accuses AIFF of Mismanagement in Indian Football:

Former Indian head coach Igor Stimac continues to criticize the AIFF, claiming they lack the ability to manage Indian football effectively. He suggests a complete restructuring to promote development.

2.Sumit Nagal Advances to Second Round in Braunschweig ATP Challenger:

India’s top-ranked tennis player Sumit Nagal progresses in the Braunschweig ATP Challenger by defeating Felipe Meligeni Alves. He gears up for the Paris Olympics after a successful start in Germany.

3. Lakshya Sen Drops, Priyanshu Rajawat Climbs in Latest BWF Rankings:

Lakshya Sen falls to 19th place while Priyanshu Rajawat rises to 35th after a strong performance at the Canada Open. HS Prannoy remains the highest-ranked Indian men’s singles player at 13th.

4. Wimbledon Schedule Adjusted Due to Persistent Rain:

Wimbledon adjusts its schedule as persistent rain affects play, leading to changes in the men’s singles final date. Only courts with roofs have managed to avoid significant disruptions.

5. Gautam Gambhir Set to Become India’s New Head Coach:

Following India’s T20I World Cup win, Gautam Gambhir prepares to take over as head coach. He plans changes in the coaching staff, including considering Abhishek Nayar and Vinay Kumar for key roles.

Today’s Entertainment News in English 

Now, let’s dive into the entertainment world and see what news has taken place.

1. MM Keeravani to Compose Music for Ajay Devgn and Tabu’s Film:

Legendary music composer MM Keeravani will create the music for Ajay Devgn and Tabu’s upcoming film “Auron Mein Kahan Dum Tha.” This project follows his Oscar win for “Naatu Naatu” from SS Rajamouli’s 2022 film “RRR.”

2. Amitabh Bachchan Shines in “Kalki,” Box Office Success:

Amitabh Bachchan has garnered acclaim for his role in the blockbuster film “Kalki,” which has grossed over Rs 900 crore worldwide. The film’s visual effects, executed by a team of 1,200 artists led by Double Negative (DNEG), have drawn comparisons to Hollywood productions like “Dune” and “Star Wars.” Prime Focus, founded by Namit Malhotra in 1997, acquired DNEG in 2014, enhancing its global visual effects capabilities.

3. Superboys of Malegaon” to Premiere at Toronto International Film Festival (TIFF):

Directed by Reema Kagti, “Superboys of Malegaon” is set for its world premiere at the 49th TIFF. The film, inspired by real events in Maharashtra’s Malegaon, stars Adarsh Gourav, Vineet Kumar Singh, and Shashank Arora. Produced by Excel Entertainment and Tiger Baby, written by Varun Grover, the film explores the intersection of filmmaking and friendship. It will debut on September 13 at TIFF.

4. Aamir Khan’s Tennis Connection at Wimbledon 2024:

Aamir Khan has revealed his past as a tennis player during his visit to Wimbledon 2024. Khan, a former Maharashtra State Champion, shared insights into his tennis career and reminisced about attending Wimbledon for the second time, this year with his children. His conversation with tennis commentator Vijay Amritraj highlighted his early talent and the influence of his father on his decision to quit tennis at a young age.

5. Roger Federer Embraces His “Swiftie Era” with Taylor Swift:

Retired tennis legend Roger Federer attended Taylor Swift’s Eras Tour concert in Zurich, Switzerland, marking his official “Swiftie era.” Federer shared a backstage selfie with Swift on Instagram, showcasing their mutual admiration. The snapshot captured them smiling, with Federer expressing his enjoyment of the concert and the Swiftie fandom.

What Happened Today in History

Understand the significance of what happened today in history and learn how to make a difference:

1. The Birth of a Genius: Pablo Neruda’s Birthday (1904)

On July 12, 1904, renowned Chilean poet Pablo Neruda was born. His passionate, award-winning poetry continues to inspire readers worldwide, earning him the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1971.

2. Revolutionary Invention: The Patent of the Sewing Machine (1859)

Isaac Singer’s sewing machine patent on July 12, 1859, revolutionized the textile industry. It enabled faster, more efficient clothing production, transforming fashion and making quality garments accessible to more people.

3. Historic Mission: NASA’s Mars Rover Curiosity Lands (2012)

NASA’s Mars rover Curiosity landed on July 12, 2012. Its mission to explore Mars’ surface provided valuable data on the planet’s climate and geology, advancing our understanding of potential extraterrestrial life.

4. A Step Towards Independence: Declaration of Irish Independence (1921)

On July 12, 1921, Ireland declared independence from the United Kingdom, leading to the Anglo-Irish Treaty. This historic moment paved the way for Ireland’s full independence in 1949.

5. A Milestone in Aviation: The First Supersonic Flight of the Concorde (1971)

On July 12, 1971, the Concorde made its first supersonic flight, drastically reducing transatlantic travel time. The iconic aircraft remains a symbol of human innovation and aviation speed.
