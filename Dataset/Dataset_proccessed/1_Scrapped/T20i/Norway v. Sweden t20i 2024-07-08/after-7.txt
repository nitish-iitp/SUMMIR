National News Headlines in English for 8 July 2024

Stay updated with 8 July 2024 news headlines in English for school assembly. Learn about various sectors´ happenings in our country. 

1. Healthcare Sector Faces Rapid Consolidation:

India’s healthcare sector is rapidly consolidating with major collaborations, mergers, and acquisitions. This trend, driven by cost factors and advanced technology needs, has resulted in over $1,068 million in deals this year, enhancing cancer care standardization and clinical innovation across the country.

2. ISRO Unveils Enhanced Bharatiya Antariksha Station Design:

India’s space program progresses with the new Bharatiya Antariksha Station design. ISRO has doubled the station’s mass and crew capacity, now accommodating up to 6 astronauts. This 52-ton station marks a significant leap in India’s human spaceflight capabilities.

3. NTA Defends NEET-UG 2024 Results Integrity:

The National Testing Agency (NTA) has defended the integrity of NEET-UG 2024 results, stating that allegations of high scores from specific centres are unsubstantiated. An analysis of the top 100 candidates showed a wide distribution of top scores across various centers.

4. Delhi University Students Celebrate Forgiveness Day:

Delhi University students celebrate Forgiveness Day on July 7 by exchanging forgiveness bands and planning lunch dates to mend relationships. Students use beads and crystals believed to promote healing and forgiveness, with some crafting their bands by hand.

5. Government Plans Major Expansion of Ayushman Bharat Scheme:

The government plans to double the Ayushman Bharat health insurance scheme’s beneficiary base, including all citizens above 70 years, and increase coverage to ₹10 lakh per year. This expansion will cost an additional ₹12,076 crore annually and aims to enhance healthcare access.

6. India Develops Advanced Air-Independent Propulsion System:

India’s indigenously developed air-independent propulsion (AIP) system will significantly increase submarine underwater endurance. DRDO’s new system, to be fitted in a submarine next year, allows submarines to stay submerged for up to two weeks, enhancing operational stealth and effectiveness.

7. President Murmu Joins Millions for Jagannath Rath Yatra:

Odisha’s annual Lord Jagannath Rath Yatra began with President Droupadi Murmu attending. Due to celestial arrangements, the festival will last two days. The Odisha government has made special security and logistical arrangements for the event.

8. Uttar Pradesh Signs Major Defence Manufacturing Deals:

The Uttar Pradesh government has signed over 150 defence manufacturing deals worth ₹25,000 crore. These projects, part of the UP Defence Industrial Corridor, will create 40,000 jobs and contribute to India’s growing defence export sector, which hit a record ₹1.27 trillion in 2023-24.

9. National Medical Commission Approves 113 New Medical Colleges:

The National Medical Commission has approved 113 new undergraduate medical colleges for 2024-25. This expansion aims to increase MBBS seats, benefiting aspiring medical students and addressing the shortage of healthcare professionals across India.

10. Finance Minister Concludes Budget Consultations:

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman has concluded budget consultations with various stakeholders. The upcoming budget, to be presented on July 23, aims to set the path for a developed India by 2047, with historic steps and major economic decisions expected.

State News Today in English for 8 July 2024

Stay updated with the state news headlines today and impactful stories unfolding across your state.

Jammu Kashmir:

1. Political Start-ups Proliferate in Jammu and Kashmir Amid Election Struggles:

Increasingly, Jammu and Kashmir sees a rise in ‘political start-ups’ amidst long-standing unrest, featuring parties like Jammu and Kashmir Nationalist People’s Front and Jammu and Kashmir Workers Party, yet many struggle to gain electoral traction, say analysts and politicians.

Gujarat:

2. Amit Shah Urges Nationwide Expansion of Cooperative Societies:

Minister of Home Affairs and Cooperation, Amit Shah, calls for the establishment of primary agricultural credit societies (PACS) across every village and block, emphasizing cooperative sector growth through initiatives like banking partnerships and local dairy support.

Maharastra:

3. Maharashtra Government Mulls Election for Legislative Council Chairperson After Two-Year Vacancy:

The Maharashtra government is actively considering holding an election to fill the vacant chairperson position in the legislative council, pending for over two years, with discussions highlighting top BJP contenders and plans to finalize before upcoming assembly elections.

West Bengal:

4. MHA Takes Action Against Kolkata Police Officials Over Governor’s Allegations:

The Union Ministry of Home Affairs initiates disciplinary action against Kolkata Commissioner Vineet Goyal and DCP Central Indira Mukherjee for allegedly tarnishing the office of West Bengal Governor CV Ananda Bose. The action follows allegations of misconduct and hindrance in post-poll violence victim meetings reported by the Governor.

Assam:

5. Assam Flood Toll Rises to 78 as Grim Situation Persists:

Eight more fatalities in Assam’s ongoing flood crisis, including deaths in Dhubri and Nalbari districts, bring the toll to 78. The floods have devastated communities, displacing 2.42 million people across 30 districts, with urgent relief efforts underway.

International News in English for 8 July 2024

Explore global stories through international news in English. Discover updates from different countries:

1. ISRO Unveils Most Detailed Map of Adam’s Bridge:

ISRO scientists have created the most detailed map of Adam’s Bridge, confirming it as a submerged ridge connecting India to Sri Lanka. Using a US satellite, they found that 99.98% of the 29km limestone chain is underwater.

2. Ceasefire Ends as Fierce Fighting Resumes in Myanmar:

Fighting in northeastern Myanmar has resumed, ending a Chinese-brokered ceasefire. The Ta’ang National Liberation Army and other militias are attacking regime positions, aiming to eradicate military dictatorship and protect locals.

3. OpenAI Teases Next-Generation AI, GPT-5:

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman hinted at GPT-5 during the Aspen Ideas Festival. Despite advancements with GPT-4, the company aims to fix existing errors and improve the AI chatbot’s capabilities in its next version.

4. Indian Investors Face EB-5 Visa Slowdown Despite Surge:

The US EB-5 visa program saw a 42% increase in visas issued, but Indian investors received fewer visas recently. Monthly issuance at the US Consulate in Mumbai has dropped, with only four EB-5 visas issued in May.

5. SpaceX’s Polaris Dawn Mission to Break 50-Year Space Records:

SpaceX’s Polaris Dawn mission aims to send astronauts farther from Earth than in 50 years. Launching atop a Falcon 9 rocket, the Dragon spacecraft will reach 870 miles in orbit, surpassing previous crewed mission heights since 1972.

6. Left-Wing Coalition Stuns in France’s Snap Elections:

A left coalition unexpectedly won the most seats in France’s snap elections, placing President Macron’s centrist alliance second and the far right third. The lack of a majority has plunged France into political and economic uncertainty.

7. Modi’s Moscow Visit: A Pivotal Moment for India-Russia Ties:

Prime Minister Modi is set for a significant visit to Moscow on July 8-9, meeting President Putin for the 22nd India-Russia Annual Summit. They will discuss multifaceted relations and global issues of mutual interest.

8. French Prime Minister Attal to Resign Amid Election Defeat:

French Prime Minister Gabriel Attal announced his resignation following the centrist coalition’s defeat in legislative elections. Despite gaining more seats than expected, the coalition failed to secure a majority.

9. France Plunges into Uncertainty with Hung Parliament:

France’s election results indicate a hung parliament, with a leftist alliance taking the top spot. This unexpected outcome leaves the parliament divided, with no single group having a majority, complicating governance.

10. China Relocates 5,700 Residents After Dike Breach at Major Freshwater Lake:

Chinese authorities relocated 5,700 residents after a dike breach in Hunan province. Over 2,300 rescue workers built a second defence line as heavy rains caused severe flooding. The Miluo River reached its highest level in 70 years, prompting maximum emergency response.

Sports News in English for 8 July 2024

Get the latest sports news in English and follow your favourite sports events:

1. Jeswin Aldrin and Ankita Dhyani Qualify for Paris Olympics:

Long jumper Jeswin Aldrin and 5000m runner Ankita Dhyani qualified for the Paris Olympics through the world ranking quota. Their inclusion increases the Indian athletics team to 30 members, following recent updates by World Athletics.

2. BCCI Secretary Congratulates Team India on T20 World Cup Win:

BCCI secretary Jay Shah congratulated Team India for their T20 World Cup victory in the West Indies. He expressed confidence in winning the next World Test Championship and Champions Trophy under Rohit Sharma’s leadership.

3. AIFF Reviews High-Profile Applicants for Chief Coach Position:

The AIFF is reviewing applications for the chief coach position, with high-profile names like Philippe Troussier and Miguel Herrera among the candidates. The selection process includes coaches with World Cup experience.

4. Avinash Sable Sets New National Record in Steeplechase:

Avinash Sable broke his own national record in the 3000m steeplechase at the Paris Diamond League, finishing in 8:09.91. Meanwhile, Kishore Jena finished eighth in the men’s javelin throw event.

5. Chinese Table Tennis Fans Plan Protest at Paris Olympics:

Chinese table tennis fans plan to protest at the Paris Olympics over the exclusion of world No. 2 Wang Manyu from the team. Their campaign against the Chinese Table Tennis Association has turned into a social justice movement.

6. England Advances to European Championship Semi-Finals:

England advanced to the semi-finals of the European Championship, defeating Switzerland 5-3 on penalties. This marks their third semi-final appearance in four attempts under Gareth Southgate.

7. Priyanshu Rajawat Stuns Top Seed to Enter Canada Open Semi-Finals:

Indian shuttler Priyanshu Rajawat defeated world number four Anders Antonsen to reach the Canada Open semi-finals. The women’s doubles pair of Treesa Jolly and Gayatri Gopichand exited in the quarterfinals.

8. Russian Wrestlers Withdraw from Paris Olympics:

Ten Russian wrestlers, invited to compete as neutral athletes, withdrew from the Paris Olympics. Notable wrestlers, including Abdulrashid Sadulaev, were left off the invitation list, leading to the decision.

9. Ukraine’s Yaroslava Mahuchikh Sets New High Jump World Record:

Yaroslava Mahuchikh set a new world record in the women’s high jump, clearing 2.10 meters at the Paris Diamond League. She outperformed Australia’s Nicola Olyslagers, setting a Ukrainian record.

10. Abhishek Sharma’s Century Leads India to Victory Over Zimbabwe:

Abhishek Sharma scored a 46-ball century as India defeated Zimbabwe by 100 runs in the second T20I. India’s bowlers, including Mukesh Kumar and Avesh Khan, secured regular breakthroughs to clinch the victory.

Today’s Entertainment News in English 

Now, let’s dive into the entertainment world and see what news has taken place.

1. Karan Johar Advocates Cinema Revamp Amid Box Office Challenges:

Filmmaker Karan Johar suggests a major overhaul in defining theatrical success amidst Bollywood’s challenges. Highlighting evolving audience preferences and rising production costs, Johar emphasizes the need for films to resonate across diverse audience segments beyond multiplexes.

2. Aditya Sarpotdar Thrilled by Munjya’s Success, Talks Future Plans:

Director Aditya Sarpotdar celebrates the triumph of his Bollywood debut “Munjya” starring Sharvari Wagh and Abhay Verma. In an interview, he discusses the film’s impactful themes and challenges in creating realistic CGI for the narrative.

3. Taapsee Pannu Faces Production Hurdles with Dhak Dhak Release:

Actor-producer Taapsee Pannu reveals struggles faced in releasing “Dhak Dhak,” citing difficulties after co-producers exited post-payment. This follows her earlier success as a producer with “Blurr,” navigating the complexities of Bollywood’s production landscape.

4. Vashu Bhagnani’s Pooja Entertainment in Financial Turmoil:

Pooja Entertainment, led by veteran producer Vashu Bhagnani, faces financial scrutiny amid reports of a Rs 250 crore debt and unpaid dues. Despite revenue growth, issues persist with debts and delayed payments to cast and crew.

5. Armaan Malik’s Ex-Wife Payal Malik Breaks Down Post Bigg Boss OTT Controversy:

Payal Malik, Armaan Malik’s former spouse, emotionally addressed backlash following her Bigg Boss OTT 3 appearance, where she discussed Vishal Pandey’s comments on Kritika Malik. The video, later removed, sparked controversy online despite its deletion, with clips circulating widely on social media.

What Happened Today in History

Understand the significance of what happened today in history and learn how to make a difference:

1. 1497: Vasco da Gama Sets Sail for India:

Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama departs from Lisbon, marking the beginning of his first voyage to India. This journey would establish a direct sea route from Europe to Asia, significantly impacting global trade.

2. 1776: Liberty Bell Rings for the First Public Reading of the Declaration of Independence:

In Philadelphia, the Liberty Bell rings to mark the first public reading of the Declaration of Independence. This event symbolizes the birth of American freedom and the start of a new nation.

3. 1889: Wall Street Journal is Published for the First Time:

The Wall Street Journal, a key source of business news and financial information, publishes its first issue. It quickly becomes a leading newspaper, influencing economic journalism and financial markets worldwide.

4. 1947: Reports of the Roswell UFO Incident Emerge:

Reports of a UFO crash in Roswell, New Mexico, surface, sparking widespread speculation and conspiracy theories about extraterrestrial life. This event becomes a central topic in UFO folklore and American pop culture.

5. 1994: North Korea’s Kim Il-sung Dies:

North Korean leader Kim Il-sung dies, leading to a significant transition in the country’s leadership. His son, Kim Jong-il, succeeds him, continuing the regime’s strict policies and the nation’s isolationist stance.
