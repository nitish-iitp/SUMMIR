India vs West Indies 4th ODI 2007 Highlights
by
CricHighlightsVidz
Published on :
1/22/2022 01:26:00 PM
Watch the highlights of India vs West Indies 4th ODI 2007 - West Indies tour of India 4-match one-day international series of the 4th ODI match played between West Indies and India at Indian Petrochemicals Corporation Limited Sports Complex Ground, Vadodara in 31st January 2007.



Sachin Tendulkar brought up his 41st century off the last ball of the Indian innings © AFP


Sachin Tendulkar's record-equal blazing century, Half-centuries from Sourav Ganguly, Rahul Dravid, Rapid Knocks from MS Dhoni and Robin Uthappa sets up to India's record 160-run victory over West Indies and clinch the four-match series with 3-1 in a one-sided game of the fourth ODI.


Match Stats : 


India's 160-run win was their seventh largest victory by terms of runs in One-day international and It is their biggest win against West Indies in ODIs, previous biggest win by 102-runs at Kolkata in 1993.
West Indies' 160-run defeat was their second worst by margin of runs in ODIs.
4 : Sachin Tendulkar equalled a joint most centuries hit by any batsman against West Indies in One-day internationals, jointly 4-hundreds hit by Herschelle Gibbs.
West Indies' 181 was their fifth lowest score in the completed innings against India in Indian soil in One-day internationals.
This is the 7th time Ajit Agarkar has dismissed Chris Gayle in One-day internationals. The only other bowler who has had as much success against Chris Gayle is Brett Lee. Rana Naved-ul-Hasan and Shaun Pollock have dismissed him six times.
101 : Sourav Ganguly and Rahul Dravid set up the eighth highest second-wicket partnership for India against West Indies in One-day internationals.
118 : Rahul Dravid and Sachin Tendulkar set up the third highest third-wicket partnership for India against West Indies in One-day internationals.
India's 341 was their ninth highest score in One-day international and It is their highest total against West Indies in ODIs, previous highest was 338 at Nagpur in this series' first ODI.
India's 341 was the highest team total at Indian Petrochemicals Corporation Limited Sports Complex Ground (Reliance Stadium) Vadodara in One-day internationals, previous highest was 307 by New Zealand against Netherlands in the 1996 World Cup.


India scored massive amount of 341-3 in 50 overs with top scorer by Sachin Tendulkar hammered a unbeaten 100 off 76-balls - which was his 41st ODI hundred & his fourth century against West Indies in ODIs.


Rahul Dravid struck 78 off 109-balls including 7-fours, Sourav Ganguly scored 68 off 82-balls included 8-fours & a six.


MS Dhoni blasted a unbeaten 40 off 20-balls including 3-sixes & a four with strike rate of 200 and Robin Uthappa struck 28 off 17-balls included 3-fours & 2-sixes with strike-rate of 164.70.


West Indies best bowler by Chris Gayle, Marlon Samuels and Daren Powell each took one-wickets.


West Indies scored 181 for all-out in 41.4 overs with top scorer by Marlon Samuels struck 55 off 63-balls including 7-fours & a six.


Denesh Ramdin scored 40 off 52-balls including 5-fours and Devon Smith 24.


India best bowler by Anil Kumble, Ajit Agarkar both took 2-wickets, Yuvraj Singh, Zaheer Khan, Harbhajan Singh and Irfan Pathan each took one-wickets.


Sachin Tendulkar named Player of the match for his blistering unbeaten 100-runs knocked off facing 76-balls including 10-fours and a six with strike rate of 131.57.


Sachin Tendulkar earned player of the series for contributing 191-runs and took three wickets in the four-match ODI series.
