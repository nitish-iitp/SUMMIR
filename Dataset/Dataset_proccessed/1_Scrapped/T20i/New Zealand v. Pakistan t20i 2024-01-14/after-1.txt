Rizwan has 77 sixes well to qualify as Pakistani in the shortest form of the contest.

Mohammad Rizwan, Pakistani wicketkeeper-batsman, reached a T20. landmark on Sunday after the second innings of the game versus New Zealand at Seddon Park in Hamilton. The now-31-year-old batsman become Pakistani greatest six-hitter, with 77 sixes in T20s.
Pakistani wicket-keeper batsmen passed teammate Mohammad Hafeez, he was second on the list of records with 76 sixes.

Rizwan began Pakistani pursuit of 195 points wonderfully, striking a six in the first over against Tim Southee, however he got bowled by Adam Milne in his second round scoring 7 runs.

Superstar hitter Babar Azam, similar to the very first encounter, topped scoring on behalf of the Men in Greens with a score of 66 from 43 runs appearing on the scoreboard, featuring 9 boundaries and however his shots proved useless as the away team failed to cross the 195-run mark. New Zealand had moved towards clinching the match after falling missing of twenty-one runs in the match.
