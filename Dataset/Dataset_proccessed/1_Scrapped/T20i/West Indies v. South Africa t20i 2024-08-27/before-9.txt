Former Indian cricketer-turned-commentator, Aakash Chopra, has once again turned the spotlight on the unpredictable nature of Pakistan cricket, taking a subtle dig at the team following their humiliating 10-wicket defeat against Bangladesh in the first Test in Rawalpindi.

In a jaw-dropping twist during a recent Test match, the Pakistan cricket team made a shocking decision that stunned fans and experts as well. In a move that defied logic, Pakistan declared their first innings before reaching the 500-run mark, with four wickets still in hand.

Meanwhile, Pakistan's embarrassing loss to Bangladesh on their home soil added to their already challenging period.

They are already under heavy scrutiny due to disappointing performances, including their early exits in both the 2023 ODI World Cup and the 2024 T20 World Cup, and now a humiliating defeat at the hands of Bangladesh is just adding to their woes.

It was Bangladesh‚Äôs first-ever Test victory in 14 encounters against Pakistan. Bangladesh spinners shine bright in Rawalpindi Test

Well, Pakistan's downfall was partly due to questionable team selection, opting for an all-pace attack with only part-time spinners in their playing XI. This strategy backfired as Bangladesh's seasoned spinners, Mehidy Hasan Miraz and Shakib Al Hasan, took control in the second inning.

They combined to take seven crucial wickets, laying the foundation for Bangladesh's first Test victory over Pakistan. Mehidy recorded the stunning bowling figures of 4/21, while Shakib's experience shone through with 3/44, causing Pakistan's batting lineup to crumble on the fifth day.

The home team was bundled out for just 146 runs in 55.5 overs. Then, Bangladesh's openers, Zakir Hasan and Shadman Islam, confidently chased down the modest target of 30 runs in just 6.3 overs, sealing a historic win for their team at the Rawalpindi Cricket Stadium.
