In today’s school assembly news headlines, we will provide a concise summary of the top 10 news stories. You’ll stay informed about significant events in politics, education, science, and culture, both from India and across the globe.

1. Government and Meta Launch “Scam se Bacho” Campaign Against Online Scams
The Indian government, in collaboration with Meta, has launched the “Scam se Bacho” campaign in New Delhi. This initiative aims to raise awareness about online scams and enhance protection against cybercrimes, ensuring that citizens are better informed about potential threats in the digital landscape.

2. PM Modi Emphasizes NDA’s Commitment to National Development
During a meeting with NDA Chief Ministers and Deputy CMs in Chandigarh, Prime Minister Narendra Modi reaffirmed the alliance’s dedication to fostering national growth and empowering marginalized communities. The discussions focused on governance strategies aimed at uplifting the underprivileged.

3. Omar Abdullah’s Government Seeks Restoration of Jammu & Kashmir’s Statehood
In its inaugural cabinet meeting, the newly elected government of Chief Minister Omar Abdullah has passed a resolution urging the central government to restore statehood to Jammu & Kashmir. Abdullah plans to present this resolution to Prime Minister Modi in New Delhi.

4. ISRO Plans SPADEX Mission to Advance Space Docking Technology
The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is set to launch the Space Docking Experiment (SPADEX) mission, involving two 400kg satellites that will dock in orbit. This mission is crucial for future endeavors, including Chandrayaan-4 and the Bharatiya Antariksh Space Station.

5. Indian Railways Reduces Advance Ticket Booking Period
Beginning November 1, Indian Railways will shorten the advance ticket booking period from 120 to 60 days. This decision aims to improve operational efficiency while maintaining the existing 365-day booking window for foreign tourists.

6. Delhi-NCR Faces Severe Air Quality Crisis Amid Smog
The air quality in Delhi-NCR has dropped to ‘poor’ levels, with the AQI reaching 292. Environment officials have convened an emergency meeting to address this alarming situation, while the PM’s office is promoting roadside greening efforts to mitigate dust pollution.

7. Karnataka Withdraws Board Exam Notification for Rural Students
The Karnataka government has retracted its recent notification regarding board examinations for classes 5, 8, and 9 in three rural districts. This decision follows an appeal from the Organisation for Unaided Recognised Schools, emphasizing the need for accessible education.

8. Vice-President Dhankhar Highlights Global Inaction on Hindu Plight
Vice-President Jagdeep Dhankhar expressed concern over the global community’s silence regarding the challenges faced by Hindus in neighboring countries. His remarks were made during the National Human Rights Commission’s foundation day event, calling for increased awareness and action.

9. Supreme Court Clarifies Constitutionality of Benami Transactions Act
The Supreme Court of India has recalled its previous ruling that deemed Section 3(2) of the Benami Transactions (Prohibition) Act unconstitutional. This clarification addresses concerns raised in a review plea submitted by the government.

10. India Set to Become the World’s Third-Largest Economy by 2030
According to projections by S&P Global, India is on track to become the third-largest economy globally by 2030, aiming for a $30 trillion economy by 2047. This ambitious growth follows India’s rise to become the fifth-largest economy today.

National News Headlines in English for 19 October 2024

The national news headlines section in today’s school assembly will focus on important events and updates happening across the country.

1. Government and Meta Collaborate to Launch “Scam se Bacho” Campaign Against Online Scams
The government and Meta have joined forces for the “Scam se Bacho” campaign to tackle the growing issue of online scams. Launched in New Delhi, the campaign aims to create awareness and ensure citizens are better protected against online fraud and cybercrimes.

2. PM Modi Reaffirms NDA’s Focus on National Development and Uplifting the Underprivileged
After a meeting with NDA Chief Ministers and Deputy CMs in Chandigarh, Prime Minister Narendra Modi reiterated the alliance’s commitment to advancing national progress, discussing governance strategies and ways to empower the poor and marginalized communities.

3. Omar Abdullah’s Government Urges Central Government to Restore Statehood for Jammu & Kashmir
In its first cabinet meeting, Chief Minister Omar Abdullah’s government passed a resolution seeking the restoration of statehood for Jammu & Kashmir. Abdullah will present this resolution to Prime Minister Narendra Modi during his upcoming visit to New Delhi.

4. Retail Shareholders Increase in PSUs Despite Declining Share PricesData from the September quarter reveals a rise in retail shareholders for 56 out of 68 PSUs. However, only 16 of these entities saw positive returns, while the rest experienced share price declines of up to 30% from their 52-week highs, with companies like Canara Bank and IREDA leading the surge.

5. Indian Railways Reduces Advance Ticket Booking Period from 120 to 60 Days
Starting November 1, passengers can book train tickets 60 days in advance, down from the previous 120 days. This change aims to streamline operations, with no impact on certain daytime express trains or foreign tourist bookings, which retain a 365-day booking window.

6. Prime Minister Narendra Modi to Attend 16th BRICS Summit in Russia, Focusing on Global Development and Security
Prime Minister Narendra Modi will visit Russia to attend the BRICS Summit at Kazan from October 22-23 at the invitation of President Vladimir Putin. During his visit, PM Modi will hold bilateral meetings with BRICS leaders to assess the progress of initiatives and explore future collaborations, especially in the areas of multilateralism and global security.

7. ISRO to Launch SPADEX Mission: A Space Docking Experiment with Two Satellites to Enable Future Space Programs
The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) has acquired two 400kg satellites for its upcoming Space Docking Experiment (SPADEX) mission. These satellites will align in orbit and dock to form a single entity. The experiment is key to future missions like Chandrayaan-4 and the Bharatiya Antariksh Space Station.

8. Nawaz Sharif Advocates for a New Chapter in India-Pakistan Relations, Urges Focus on Future Cooperation
Former Prime Minister of Pakistan, Nawaz Sharif, called for a renewed relationship between India and Pakistan, urging both countries to “bury the past” and focus on the future. He also blamed former Pakistani PM Imran Khan for the recent deterioration in relations.

9. Vice-President Dhankhar Criticizes Global Silence on the Plight of Hindus in Neighboring Countries
Vice-President Jagdeep Dhankhar expressed concern over the global inaction regarding the mistreatment of Hindus in neighboring countries. Speaking at the National Human Rights Commission’s foundation day, he criticized international human rights advocates for failing to address the atrocities faced by Hindus, including violence and desecration of religious sites.

10. Supreme Court of India Reverses 2022 Ruling on Benami Transactions Act, Clarifying the Constitutionality of Section 3(2)
The Supreme Court of India has recalled its August 23, 2022, judgment declaring Section 3(2) of the Benami Transactions (Prohibition) Act unconstitutional. The court clarified that the constitutionality of the provision was not an issue in the original case, in response to a review plea filed by the government.

International News in English for 19 October 2024

Stay updated with the latest Global Stories and International News. This section highlights significant events and developments from around the world.

1. India Poised to Become World’s Third-Largest Economy by 2030
S&P Global projects India will surpass major economies, trailing only the U.S. and China by 2030. India, currently the fifth-largest economy, aims for a $30 trillion economy by 2047, up from $3.6 trillion.

IMF Managing Director Kristalina Georgieva urges world leaders to act on mounting global debt and low growth, citing serious risks to both advanced and emerging economies in her recent CNBC interview.

Sri Lanka’s National Election Commission sets spending limits for candidates and parties, with the highest cap at Colombo district: 5.75 million rupees for candidates and nearly 79.5 million for parties.

Israeli PM Benjamin Netanyahu to hold a security meeting on the progress of the hostage deal with Hamas, following confirmation of the death of Hamas chief Yahya Sinwar in a drone strike.

5. Google to Pause U.S. Election Ads Post-Polling to Prevent Misinformation
Google will halt election-related ads after U.S. polls close on November 5, to limit misinformation during the vote count. The policy follows a similar approach used during the 2020 elections.
Sports News in English for 19 October 2024

Catch up on the latest sports news in English and stay updated on your favorite sports events. Find out scores, highlights, and key moments from recent games and matches.

1. Indian Junior Men’s Hockey Team Set for Sultan of Johor Cup
India’s junior men’s hockey team, led by Captain Amir Ali and deputy Rohit, will compete in the 12th Sultan of Johor Cup in Malaysia starting tomorrow. Head Coach PR Sreejesh will guide the team, with India facing Japan in their first match. India is set to play Great Britain, Malaysia, Australia, and New Zealand in the group stages.

2. Harmanpreet Kaur Retained as Captain for India Women’s ODI Series
Harmanpreet Kaur will continue as India Women’s cricket captain for the upcoming ODI series against New Zealand, starting on October 24. The selectors expressed confidence in her leadership despite India’s T20 World Cup exit. Four uncapped players have been included in the squad, with the series marking the beginning of preparations for the ODI World Cup.

3. Red Bull Makes Car Adjustments Amid FIA Concerns at U.S. Grand Prix
Red Bull has made modifications to its car after discussions with the FIA ahead of the U.S. Grand Prix. This follows speculation about teams exploiting rules related to car setups under “parc ferme” conditions. Red Bull acknowledged the presence of a ride height adjustment device, addressing concerns raised by rival teams.

4. India Crumbles in First Test Against New Zealand
India’s cricket team collapsed for 46 in the first Test against New Zealand, marking their lowest home score. Virat Kohli was dismissed for 0, though he became India’s second-most capped player with 536 international matches, surpassing MS Dhoni. Sachin Tendulkar leads the record with 664 games.

5. Rafael Nadal Acknowledges Fitness Struggles After Loss to Alcaraz
Rafael Nadal admitted his fitness is below par after losing to Carlos Alcaraz in the Six Kings Slam event in Saudi Arabia. The 38-year-old, set to retire after the Davis Cup Finals, praised Alcaraz’s form, calling him an “animal” on the court, and acknowledged that he is no longer competing at the top level.
Education News Today for School Assembly

Today’s education news headlines for school assembly bring attention to key developments in the field of education.

1. IGNOU Tops NIRF 2024 Rankings Amid Ongoing Student Concerns
Indira Gandhi National Open University (IGNOU) has secured the top spot in the National Institutional Ranking Framework (NIRF) 2024 for open universities. However, students continue to voice concerns over delayed study materials, late assignment feedback, and inadequate communication from the university, impacting their learning experience.

2. Karnataka Withdraws Board Exam Notification for Rural Students
The Karnataka government has retracted its notification for conducting board examinations for classes 5, 8, and 9 in three rural districts for the current academic year. This decision follows an appeal by the Organisation for Unaided Recognised Schools, challenging a prior judgment from the Karnataka High Court.

3. NTA Eliminates Optional Questions in JEE Main 2025
The National Testing Agency (NTA) has announced the discontinuation of optional questions in Section B of the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Main 2025. Candidates will no longer have the option to choose five questions from a set of ten. This change applies to JEE Main papers for BE/BTech, BArch, and BPlanning, as the optional section was initially introduced as a temporary measure during the COVID-19 pandemic.

4. AICTE Launches Green Internships for Students
The All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE), in collaboration with 1M1B Green Skills Academy, has launched green internships for AICTE-affiliated colleges and universities. The initiative aims to provide 100,000 internship opportunities by 2030, with 10,000 internships available in the first year, promoting sustainability and environmental awareness among students.

5. IGNOU Releases June 2024 Term End Examination Results
IGNOU has announced the results for the Term End Examinations (TEE) held in June 2024. Students enrolled in undergraduate, postgraduate, diploma, or certificate courses can check their results on the official IGNOU website by entering their registration numbers and date of birth.

What Happened Today in History

The What Happened Today in History segment in the school assembly news headlines highlights significant events from history that occurred on the current date.

1. Battle of Yorktown Ends (1781)
On 19 October 1781, British General Cornwallis surrendered to American and French forces at Yorktown, Virginia, marking the decisive end of the American Revolutionary War. This victory paved the way for American independence from Britain.

2. First Blockbuster Film, The Jazz Singer, Premieres (1927)
On this day in 1927, The Jazz Singer became the first major movie with synchronized sound, revolutionizing the film industry. Its success ushered in the era of “talkies” and transformed entertainment worldwide.

3. United Nations Celebrates World Development Information Day (1972)
The UN General Assembly established 19 October as World Development Information Day in 1972 to raise awareness about development issues and encourage global cooperation. Schools can reflect on efforts toward reducing poverty and improving living conditions worldwide.

4. Napoleon Begins His Retreat from Moscow (1812)
On 19 October 1812, Napoleon Bonaparte began his disastrous retreat from Moscow after his invasion of Russia failed. The harsh winter and lack of supplies decimated his army, signaling a major defeat that affected European history.

5. World Pediatric Bone and Joint Day (Global Awareness Day)Celebrated on 19 October, this day raises global awareness about bone and joint health in children. Schools can use this as an opportunity to discuss the importance of exercise, posture, and nutrition in maintaining healthy bones and joints for growing students.
