National News Headlines in English for 15 June 2024
1. Italian PM Meloni’s “Namaste” Greeting at G7 Summit Goes Viral:
Italian Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni used the traditional Indian greeting “namaste” to welcome global leaders at the G7 summit. The gesture, captured in viral videos, received widespread praise and trended on social media, particularly on X.

2. PM Modi Attends G7 Summit in Italy’s Apulia Region:
Prime Minister Narendra Modi arrived in Apulia, Italy, to participate in the G7 Summit from June 13-15. This visit marks his first international trip since starting his third term. Modi’s attendance follows an invitation from Italian PM Giorgia Meloni.

3. Supreme Court Orders CBI Probe into NEET-UG 2024 Paper Leak:
The Supreme Court has issued a notice for a CBI investigation into alleged paper leaks and malpractices in the NEET-UG 2024 exam. The National Testing Agency and other respondents must respond by July 8, with the next hearing scheduled then.

4. CBSE to Pilot Open-Book Exams for Classes IX and XI:
CBSE will pilot open-book exams for Classes IX and XI in subjects including English, Science, and Mathematics. This initiative aims to shift from rote learning to competency-based assessments and will gather feedback from stakeholders.

5. India Introduces Strict Penalties for Mob Lynching in New Law:
The new Bharatiya Nyay Samhita law includes life imprisonment or the death penalty for mob lynching. This legal recognition aims to improve prosecution for such acts, addressing the growing issue of mob violence in India.

6.  P. K. Mishra and Ajit Doval Reappointed in Key Government Roles:
P. K. Mishra has been reappointed as Principal Secretary to the Prime Minister and Ajit Doval as National Security Advisor. Their terms will coincide with the Prime Minister’s tenure or until further orders, as approved by the Appointment Committee of the Cabinet.

7. India Repatriates Remains of 45 Nationals Killed in Kuwait Fire:
India will repatriate the remains of 45 nationals killed in a Kuwait City fire via a special flight. The bodies will arrive in Kochi before heading to Delhi. Minister Kirti Vardhan Singh is overseeing the repatriation and care for injured workers.

8. Election for Lok Sabha Speaker Set for June 26:
The Lok Sabha Speaker election will occur on June 26, during the first session of the 18th Lok Sabha. Newly elected members will take their oaths, and President Droupadi Murmu will address a joint sitting of both houses.

9. JoSAA Announces Top 20 Percentile Cut-Offs for BTech Admissions:
The Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA) announced the top 20 percentile cut-off marks for 2023 and 2024 board exams. These marks determine eligibility for BTech admissions, alongside JEE Advanced qualification, for seats in IITs, NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs.

10. Zomato Sets Guinness Record for Largest First Aid Lesson: 
Zomato achieved a Guinness World Record for the largest first aid lesson, training over 4,300 delivery agents in Mumbai. The event aimed to equip delivery partners with lifesaving skills and improve their working conditions.

International News in English for 15 June 2024

Explore global stories through international news in English. Discover updates from different countries:

1. Pope Francis to Address G7 Leaders on AI Risks and Rewards:
Pope Francis will address G7 leaders on artificial intelligence, marking the first time a Catholic Church head speaks at a G7 summit. His address will highlight the Vatican’s growing concern over AI’s implications for humanity.

2. Oracle to Train 200,000 Indian Students in Emerging Technologies:
Oracle will train 200,000 Indian students in cloud computing, data science, AI, and other technologies through a program with the Tamil Nadu Skill Development Corporation. This initiative is part of the state’s Naan Mudhalvan skill enhancement project.

3. Noam Chomsky Recovering in Brazil After Stroke, Wife Confirms: 
Noam Chomsky, the renowned linguist and activist, is recovering in Brazil after a stroke a year ago, his wife confirmed. Chomsky, a former MIT professor, revolutionized linguistics and is now a Laureate Professor at the University of Arizona.

4. NASA Clarifies Mistaken Broadcast of Astronaut Decompression Sickness Simulation:
NASA mistakenly broadcasted a simulation of astronauts with decompression sickness, causing concern. The audio was from a training exercise, not a real emergency, NASA clarified on its ISS X account, assuring no emergency occurred on the International Space Station.

5. New York Community Bancorp Acquires $37.8 Billion in Assets from Signature Bank:
New York Community Bancorp (NYCB) announced it has acquired $37.8 billion in assets from the insolvent Signature Bank. This significant transaction, involving Flagstar Bank, includes deposits and loan portfolios obtained from the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation.

Sports News in English for 15 June 2024

Get the latest sports news in English and follow your favourite sports events:

1. Pakistan’s T20 World Cup Hopes Hinge on USA-Ireland Clash Amid Florida Emergency:
Pakistan can advance to the T20 World Cup 2024 Super Eight with a narrow win over Ireland, provided the USA loses to Ireland. Meanwhile, Fort Lauderdale’s emergency due to flash floods affects tournament logistics.

2. Rohan Bopanna, N Sriram Balaji to Pair Up for Paris Olympics; Sumit Nagal Named Alternate:
India’s doubles No. 4, Rohan Bopanna, will compete with N Sriram Balaji at the Paris Olympics, confirmed by the AITA. Sumit Nagal remains an alternate in singles, awaiting potential entry due to withdrawals.

3. India’s Likith SP and Dhinidhi Desinghu Secure Bronze Medals at Singapore National Swimming Championship 2024:
Likith SP clinched bronze in men’s 100m breaststroke (1:02), while Dhinidhi Desinghu won bronze in women’s 200m freestyle (2:05). The championship serves as a final Olympic qualifier ahead of the June 23rd deadline.

4. Likith SP and Dhinidhi Desinghu Secure Bronze at Singapore National Swimming Championship 2024:
India’s Likith SP clinched bronze in the men’s 100m breaststroke with a time of 1:02, while Dhinidhi Desinghu achieved bronze in the women’s 200m freestyle with a time of 2:05. These performances come during the final qualification chance for Paris Olympics before the June 23rd deadline.

5. BRICS Games 2024 Begins in Kazan Amid Russia’s Sports Exclusion:
The sixth BRICS Games commenced in Kazan on Wednesday, coinciding with Russia’s Independence Day. Amid its ban from major sports events since the Ukraine invasion, Russia seeks to highlight sports as integral to its national identity, despite competing under neutral status at upcoming Olympics.

Today’s Entertainment News in English 

Now, let’s dive into the entertainment world and see what news has taken place.

1. Echo Recording Challenges ilaiyaraaja’s Copyright Claim in Court
Echo Recording argued in the Madras High Court that Ilaiyaraaja did not retain the copyright for 4,500 songs from the 1970s to the mid-1990s. They stated he failed to include specific clauses in agreements and did not prove his claim during the trial.

2. Maya Miriga to Be Screened at Il Cinema Ritrovato Festival
After the restored version of Shyam Benegal’s “Manthan” made headlines at Cannes, the same restoration organization will screen Nirad Mohapatra’s classic “Maya Miriga” at the Il Cinema Ritrovato festival in Bologna, Italy, from June 22 to 30.

3. Sheldon Cooper’s Love for Video Games and Rubik’s Cube Highlighted in ‘The Monster Isolation’:
In “The Monster Isolation” (Season 6, Episode 17) of The Big Bang Theory, Sheldon Cooper sneaks video games into a play he doesn’t want to attend, showcasing his gaming addiction. Notably, he also carries a Rubik’s Cube, highlighting his interest in cubing, a hobby seen in various episodes where he wears a T-shirt with a melting Rubik’s Cube image.

4. Supreme Court Stays Release of “Hamare Baarah” Amid Controversy:
The Supreme Court halted the release of Annu Kapoor’s film “Hamare Baarah” on June 14 following allegations that it portrayed married Muslim women in a derogatory light. The Bombay High Court was directed to expedite the decision on the matter.

5. ‘Panchayat Season 3’ Receives Dairy Brand’s Heartwarming Tribute:
Amidst widespread acclaim, the popular series “Panchayat Season 3” featuring Jitendra Kumar, Neena Gupta, Raghubir Yadav, and others received a special tribute from a renowned dairy brand on Instagram, celebrating the show’s success.

6. Kannada Actor Darshan Thoogudeepa Arrested in Murder Case:
Darshan Thoogudeepa’s arrest in connection with the murder of Renuka Swamy in Bengaluru has shocked Karnataka’s film industry. Allegedly provoked by social media comments about his girlfriend, Darshan’s arrest has drawn strong reactions, including from colleague Sanjjanaa Galrani.

7. Kerala CM Honours Cannes Winners of “All We Imagine As Light:
Kerala Chief Minister Pinarayi Vijayan felicitated the cast of “All We Imagine As Light” for winning the Grand Prix at Cannes. Directed by Payal Kapadia, the film marked India’s return to Cannes’ main competition after three decades, also honouring Santosh Sivan for his exceptional cinematography.

8. Gujarat High Court Halts Release of Aamir Khan’s Son Junaid’s Debut Film:
The Gujarat High Court blocked the Netflix release of “Maharaj,” Junaid Khan’s debut film, following objections from Pushtimarg followers claiming potential religious offense. Justice Sangeeta Vishen issued the order against the scheduled release.

9. Prime Video’s “The Boys” Season 4 Offers Dark Antidote to Superhero Fatigue:
Amidst a year marked by a scarcity of comic book cinema, “The Boys” Season 4 on Prime Video defiantly bucks the trend with its unapologetically gritty narrative. Building on its established universe, the season delves deeper into its characters’ psyches, offering a refreshing departure from conventional superhero storytelling.

10. Jr NTR’s “Devara” Release Date Advanced to September 27:
Director Koratala Siva reunites with Jr NTR for “Devara,” now slated for release in two parts. Originally set for October 10, the film’s first part, featuring Janhvi Kapoor’s Telugu debut and Saif Ali Khan as the antagonist, will now hit theatres on September 27, as announced by the makers.

What Happened Today in History

Understand the significance of what happened today in history and learn how to make a difference:

1. Signing of the Magna Carta (1215)

On June 15, 1215, King John of England signed the Magna Carta, limiting royal power and establishing legal rights for subjects, laying a foundation for modern democracy.

2. The Battle of Naseby (1645)

On June 15, 1645, during the English Civil War, Parliamentarian forces led by Sir Thomas Fairfax defeated King Charles I’s army at the Battle of Naseby, significantly weakening the Royalists.

3. Benjamin Franklin Flies a Kite (1752)

On June 15, 1752, Benjamin Franklin famously flew a kite during a thunderstorm, proving that lightning is a form of electricity and contributing to the understanding of electricity.

4. Foundation of IBM (1911)

On June 15, 1911, the Computing-Tabulating-Recording Company, later renamed IBM, was founded, eventually becoming a major player in the technology and computing industry.

5.  Operation Red Wings (2005)

On June 15, 2005, U.S. Navy SEALs launched Operation Red Wings in Afghanistan to capture Taliban leader Ahmad Shah, leading to a significant and deadly confrontation.
