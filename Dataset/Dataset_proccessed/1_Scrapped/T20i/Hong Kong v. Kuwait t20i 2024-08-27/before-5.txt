1. Seven Major Indian Educational Institutions Face GST Notices Over ₹220 Crore
The Directorate General of GST Intelligence (DGGI) has issued show-cause notices to seven prominent Indian educational institutions, including IIT Delhi and Punjab University, for unpaid Goods and Services Tax (GST) amounting to ₹220 crore. The DGGI argues that these institutions, including Sree Chitra Tirunal Institute and Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham, should pay GST on research grants they received, as their R&D services funded by these grants do not qualify for exemption.

2. India Donates Mobile Hospitals to Ukraine for Emergency Medical Care
India has donated four Bharat Health Initiative for Sahyog Hita & Maitri (BHISHM) cubes, mobile hospitals designed for rapid deployment in emergencies, to Ukraine. Prime Minister Narendra Modi highlighted that these cubes contain essential medical supplies and equipment. This donation, under Project Aarogya Maitri, aims to enhance Ukraine’s ability to provide emergency medical care and is part of India’s broader humanitarian aid efforts to developing nations.

3. High Corporate Inventory Levels Persist in India’s Economic Landscape
Corporate India’s inventory-to-sales ratio remains high at 65.4% in Q4FY24, surpassing the previous year’s 64.1%. This indicates ongoing weak consumer demand despite strong corporate balance sheets and high capacity utilization. The Reserve Bank of India’s recent bulletin pointed out that while investment conditions are favorable, high inventory levels reflect continued economic challenges and sluggish consumption.

4. India-Bangladesh Border Tensions Rise Over Cattle Fence Construction Dispute
Border Guards Bangladesh (BGB) halted the Border Security Force’s (BSF) construction of a cattle fence near the India-Bangladesh border in Coochbehar, leading to heightened tensions between the two countries. The fence aimed to prevent cattle from straying across borders and causing disputes. The issue will be addressed in a forthcoming meeting between the directors-general of both border forces in Delhi this October, with no violence reported.

5. SEBI Imposes Ban and Fines on Anil Ambani and Reliance Home Finance for Financial Misconduct
The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) has barred Anil Ambani and 24 others, including former key officials of Reliance Home Finance, from the securities market for five years. SEBI also imposed a ₹25 crore fine on Ambani and restricted him from holding directorial or key managerial positions. Reliance Home Finance was fined ₹6 lakh and banned from the market for six months due to fraudulent fund diversion schemes.

6. Union Commerce Minister Criticizes E-Commerce Compliance with Foreign Investment Laws
Union Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal has criticized e-commerce giants like Amazon for not fully adhering to India’s foreign direct investment (FDI) regulations. Goyal emphasized that foreign e-commerce companies are bypassing laws intended to protect small traders and retailers. He urged consumers to be mindful of the impact of their purchases and called for stricter compliance with legal standards to safeguard local businesses.

7. Indian Ministry of Statistics Enhances Data Accuracy for Economic Analysis
The Indian Ministry of Statistics will increase efforts to gather accurate gross state domestic product (GSDP) and district-level GDP data by working closely with state governments. Secretary Saurabh Garg announced plans to improve data quality through the E-sankhyiki portal and modern technology like tablets. This initiative aims to support better policy-making with precise and timely economic statistics.

8. Ukrainian President Zelenskyy Comments on India’s Role in Russian Economy and Conflict
Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy stated that India’s substantial oil purchases from Russia significantly impact the ongoing conflict. He suggested that if India altered its stance on Russian oil imports, it could pressure Russian President Vladimir Putin to end the war. External Affairs Minister S. Jaishankar confirmed that discussions between PM Modi and Zelenskyy addressed India’s energy purchases, emphasizing the need for stable and reasonable pricing.

9. BrahMos Aerospace Achieves Milestone in Indigenization Despite Challenges
BrahMos Aerospace, the joint Indo-Russian venture, has reached 70% indigenization in its BrahMos cruise missile production. This achievement supports India’s ‘Make in India’ initiative, enhancing local production capabilities and reducing dependence on foreign components. Despite challenges from the ongoing conflict affecting the Russian defense sector, this milestone reflects significant progress in the missile’s production.

10. India Seeks Return of Sikh Scriptures Seized in Qatar
India’s Ministry of External Affairs is actively working to secure the return of two copies of the Sikh scripture Guru Granth Sahib seized by Qatari authorities. Following concerns raised by the Shiromani Gurdwara Parbandhak Committee (SGPC), MEA spokesperson Randhir Jaiswal reported that one copy has been returned and efforts continue to release the second. This follows requests for intervention by Indian officials to ensure the respectful return of the holy texts.
State News Headlines in English for 25 August 2024

Now, let’s look at state news. We will review important events and updates from various regions to keep you informed about local happenings:

1. Jammu and Kashmir’s Free Health Insurance Scheme Faces Crisis: Private Hospitals Withdraw Services
The ambitious free health insurance program for Jammu and Kashmir, aimed at providing comprehensive medical coverage to all citizens, is facing a severe crisis. Private hospitals and dialysis centers, empaneled under the Ayushman Bharat Scheme, have announced their decision to cease treatment under the scheme from September 1. This drastic move is attributed to the non-payment of approximately ₹300 crore in dues that have accumulated since March. The decision to withdraw services has significant implications, potentially disrupting healthcare for thousands of patients who depend on this scheme for their medical needs.

2. Supreme Court Hears Challenge Against FIRs Filed Against YouTuber Savukku ShankarOn August 23, the Supreme Court addressed the challenge against 16 FIRs filed against YouTuber Savukku Shankar over an online interview. The bench, consisting of Justices JB Pardiwala and Manoj Misra, adjourned the hearing to allow Tamil Nadu State authorities additional time to submit relevant documents. Justice Pardiwala criticized the repeated detention of Shankar, questioning the motives behind the legal actions.

3. Deputy Chief Minister K. Pawan Kalyan Urges Villagers to Drive Rural DevelopmentK. Pawan Kalyan, Deputy Chief Minister of Panchayat Raj and Rural Development, has called for active community involvement in rural development. During a Grama Sabha at Mysooravaripalle in Railway Kodur mandal, Annamayya district, Kalyan emphasized the role of villagers in shaping their community’s future. He highlighted the significance of collective decision-making and the potential for transformative development if panchayat sarpanchs, who hold substantial local authority, efficiently discharge their responsibilities.

4. Revenue Minister Ponguleti Srinivas Reddy Challenges Opposition Leaders Over Alleged Illegal ConstructionRevenue Minister Ponguleti Srinivas Reddy has challenged former Municipal and Urban Development Minister KT Rama Rao and former Irrigation Minister T. Harish Rao to investigate whether his residence is located in a flood-prone zone or buffer area of a water body. Ponguleti’s challenge comes amid allegations that KTR’s relatives have built properties in such areas. He criticized KTR for inconsistencies regarding property ownership and legal challenges, asserting that his own residence, though registered in his son’s name, is not being misrepresented.

5. Malayalam Actor Siddique Reacts to Hema Commission Report on Film Industry
Malayalam actor Siddique, general secretary of the Association of Malayalam Movie Artists (AMMA), has responded to the findings of the Hema Commission report on the Kerala film industry. Siddique acknowledged the report’s revelations about sexual harassment and gender inequality but claimed AMMA was unaware of such issues within the industry. He attributed the delay in addressing the report to commitments related to an awards show for landslide victims in Waynad.
International News in English for 25 July 2024

Moving on to international news, let’s explore significant regional events and updates. These stories will keep you informed about global developments:

1. Eric Schmidt Proposes Controversial Growth Strategy for Startups: Emphasizes Rapid Imitation Over Intellectual Property
Former Google CEO Eric Schmidt has sparked controversy with his suggestion that startups should aggressively replicate successful platforms like TikTok, prioritizing rapid user acquisition over intellectual property concerns. Speaking at Stanford University, Schmidt outlined a strategy where entrepreneurs are encouraged to create copycat products quickly and deal with legal ramifications later if the product becomes popular. This approach highlights a provocative stance on the ethics of intellectual property in the tech industry.

2. Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s Diplomatic Visit to Kyiv: Engages with President Zelensky Amid Ongoing Ukraine-Russia Conflict
Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi has met with Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky in Kyiv to discuss the ongoing conflict with Russia. Modi’s visit, which follows his earlier embrace of Russian President Vladimir Putin, is seen as part of India’s balanced diplomatic approach. The meeting underscores India’s role in international diplomacy and peace-building, even as Western critics scrutinize Modi’s gestures with both leaders due to cultural and political implications.

3. Robert F. Kennedy Jr. Halts Presidential Campaign and Endorses Donald Trump: Family Reacts to Unexpected Decision
Robert F. Kennedy Jr., a member of the prominent Kennedy political family, has suspended his presidential campaign and endorsed former President Donald Trump. Kennedy’s decision, driven by his belief that he lacks a viable path to victory, adds a new twist to the 2024 presidential race. His endorsement has drawn criticism from his family, who view it as a betrayal of their core values, and has introduced unexpected dynamics into the upcoming election.

4. Gaza Reports First Polio Case in 25 Years: WHO Urges Immediate Vaccination Amid Conflict
A 10-month-old baby in Gaza has been diagnosed with polio, marking the first case in the region in 25 years. The World Health Organization has called for urgent vaccination campaigns to prevent the spread of the virus, which is exacerbated by the ongoing conflict between Israel and Hamas. The WHO is advocating for a humanitarian pause in the conflict to facilitate these vaccination efforts, highlighting the critical need for improved immunization in conflict zones.

5. Defence Minister Rajnath Singh Advocates for Stronger India-US Partnership During Washington Visit: Highlights Growing Strategic Alliance
During his official visit to Washington, Indian Defence Minister Rajnath Singh emphasized the growing strategic partnership between India and the United States. Singh described the two nations as “natural allies” with the potential to ensure global peace and stability. He highlighted India’s rising global stature and economic progress under Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s leadership, reinforcing the deepening ties between the two countries and their collaborative role on the world stage.

Business News Today Headlines

Stay updated with today’s top business news. Discover the latest market trends, corporate developments, and economic updates shaping the business world. Keep informed about key financial changes and industry shifts.

1. Cognizant’s TriZetto Sues Infosys Over Alleged Trade Secret Theft: Claims Unlawful Use of Healthcare Software Data
Cognizant’s TriZetto unit has filed a lawsuit against Infosys in a Texas federal court, accusing the Indian IT giant of stealing trade secrets related to healthcare insurance software. The lawsuit alleges that Infosys unlawfully accessed TriZetto’s databases to develop a competing product and is now seeking monetary damages and an injunction. Infosys has denied the allegations, and the legal action intensifies the rivalry between the two companies, already strained by a previous employee poaching dispute.

2. Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell Signals Rate Cuts: Prepares to Adjust Policy Amid Inflation Moderation
Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell has indicated that the time is right for the Fed to cut its key policy rate. In a speech at the Kansas City Fed’s conference in Jackson Hole, Wyoming, Powell emphasized the need to adjust policy to prevent further cooling in the labor market. He noted recent progress on inflation, which is returning to the Fed’s 2% target, and signaled that rate cuts could start next month. The announcement led to declines in Treasury yields and the dollar, with expectations of future rate cuts influencing market movements.

3. Nestle CEO Mark Schneider Ousted: Laurent Freixe Appointed Amid Company Performance Issues
Nestle has abruptly dismissed CEO Mark Schneider and appointed Laurent Freixe as the new chief executive. Schneider’s unexpected departure follows a board meeting and reflects concerns about the company’s performance under his leadership. The move marks the end of Schneider’s nearly eight-year tenure as the first outsider to lead Nestle in almost a century. Analysts view the sudden transition as a sign of dissatisfaction with Schneider’s management, despite his recent commitment to long-term leadership.

4. Orient Technologies IPO Sees Overwhelming Demand: Shares Allotted and Strong Grey Market Premium Expected
Orient Technologies is set to finalize its share allotment process following a highly successful IPO. The issue, which opened for bidding from August 21 to August 23, was subscribed 151.71 times overall, with significant interest from non-institutional investors and qualified institutional buyers. The IPO raised Rs 214.76 crore and has generated a strong grey market premium, indicating potential gains for investors. Notifications regarding the allotment and fund debits are expected by August 27.

5. RBI Monetary Policy Committee Minutes Reveal Calls for Rate Cuts: Concerns Over Inflation and Economic Growth
The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has released minutes from the August 2024 Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) meeting, highlighting debates over the repo rate. External members Ashima Goyal and Jayanth R. Varma urged a cautious approach, advocating for a rate cut to support economic growth amid persistent inflation concerns, particularly in food prices. The majority of the MPC, led by Governor Shaktikanta Das, chose to maintain the rate at 6.50%, focusing on balancing inflation control with economic growth.
Science and Technology News for 25 August 2024

Next, we’ll cover the latest in science and technology. Discover advancements in AI, trends in the smartphone market, and other key industry updates to stay current with essential developments:

1. Poco Unveils Poco Pad 5G in India: Features 12.1-Inch Display, 120Hz Refresh Rate, and Dolby Atmos
Poco is launching its first tablet in India, the Poco Pad 5G, available exclusively on Flipkart from August 23. This Android tablet features a 12.1-inch display with a 120Hz refresh rate and 600 nits peak brightness. It also includes a quad-speaker system, Dolby Vision HDR, and Dolby Atmos support. Poco promises that the tablet will offer top-tier features under Rs 24,000, mirroring its global specifications.

2. Russian Cosmonaut Sergei Korsakov Reassures ISS Crew’s Well-Being: NASA’s Sunita Williams Included
Russian cosmonaut Sergei Korsakov, recently back from a six-month ISS mission, has reassured that the ISS crew, including NASA astronaut Sunita Williams, is in good health. During his visit to India, Korsakov addressed concerns about the crew’s safety and operational stability. He expressed confidence in the space agencies’ planning and contingency measures, emphasizing that all possible options, including Dragon and Soyuz spacecraft, are being considered to ensure mission success.

3. IIT-BHU Researchers Develop Innovative Treatment for Gastro-Duodenal Ulcers: New Oral Bioactive Glass Shows Promise
Researchers at IIT-BHU, led by Prof. Sairam Krishnamurthy and Pankaj Paliwal, have developed a groundbreaking oral bioactive glass formulation to treat gastro-duodenal ulcers. This new treatment contains micronised barium oxide (BaBG) and demonstrates protective and therapeutic effects against ulcers. It neutralizes stomach acid, promotes healing through cell proliferation, and forms a protective barrier to shield ulcers from harmful agents.

4. Apple’s iOS 18 and iPadOS 18 Update for E.U.: Enhanced Control Over Default Apps and Browser Choices
Apple has announced significant updates to iOS 18 and iPadOS 18 for European users, in compliance with the Digital Markets Act. These changes will offer more control over default apps, including the ability to choose alternative browsers, messaging apps, and even remove pre-installed apps like Safari. The updated “browser choice screen” will include descriptions of browsers and will be available by the end of the year, reflecting Apple’s commitment to aligning with European regulatory requirements.

5. WhatsApp Introduces Direct Access to Privacy Checkup Screen: Enhanced Control for Users
WhatsApp is rolling out a new feature that allows users to access the privacy checkup screen directly from the privacy settings on Android. Previously, users could only access privacy settings through prompts from the app. The update provides a dedicated entry point for easier management of privacy settings. Users can now review and adjust who can see their profile photo, last seen status, about information, and group additions. This feature aims to give users greater control and flexibility in managing their privacy preferences.
Sports News in English for 25 August 2024

Catch up on the latest sports news today. From thrilling match results and player achievements to major events and updates across various sports, stay informed about all the exciting developments in the world of sports.

1. Shikhar Dhawan Announces Retirement from International and Domestic Cricket
Shikhar Dhawan has officially announced his retirement from both international and domestic cricket via a social media post on Saturday morning. Dhawan, who last played in April 2024 as the captain of Punjab Kings in the IPL, concludes his career with 24 centuries (17 in ODIs and seven in Tests) across 269 international matches. Reflecting on his career, Dhawan expressed his contentment with his achievements and emphasized moving forward with a sense of fulfillment and peace.

2. Josh Hazlewood to Miss T20I Series Against Scotland Due to Calf Injury
Josh Hazlewood will be sidelined for the upcoming T20I series against Scotland next month due to a calf strain sustained during training. Riley Meredith, who has not played T20Is since 2021, has been called up to replace him. Hazlewood’s injury is minor, and he is anticipated to return for the T20Is and ODIs against England later in September. Meredith, who has performed well in the English domestic circuit, is expected to provide valuable support in the T20I series.

3. Neha Sangwan Wins Under-17 World Wrestling Title Just a Week After Honoring Vinesh Phogat
Neha Sangwan, who recently made headlines for creating a garland of currency notes to welcome her idol Vinesh Phogat, has achieved a significant milestone by winning the Under-17 World Wrestling title in the 57 kg category. Sangwan, hailing from Balai, Haryana, defeated Japan’s So Tsutsui with a technical superiority score of 10-0 in the final held in Amman, Jordan. Her victory comes shortly after Vinesh Phogat’s call for more young girls to embrace wrestling.

4. Mohammad Rizwan’s Unbeaten 171 Spurs Pakistan’s Recovery in Test Against Bangladesh
Mohammad Rizwan played a crucial role in Pakistan’s first innings with an unbeaten 171 on Day 2 of the first Test against Bangladesh. His innings, which spanned 239 balls, helped Pakistan recover from a challenging position at 16-3 to post a formidable total of 448/6, with captain Shan Masood declaring the innings. Rizwan’s century, his third in Test cricket, was complemented by Saud Shakeel’s impressive 141. The day ended with a light-hearted moment as Rizwan playfully tossed his bat to fellow teammate Babar Azam.

5. West Indies Achieve Record T20 Chase Against South Africa at Brian Lara Academy
West Indies completed the highest successful T20 chase at the Brian Lara Academy in Tarouba to take a 1-0 lead in their three-match series against South Africa. After opting to bowl first, West Indies reduced South Africa to 42 for 5 in eight overs, despite a rain delay. South Africa’s recovery, led by a sixth-wicket partnership of 71 runs from 50 balls and a seventh-wicket stand of 60 runs from 25 balls, set a challenging total. Matthew Forde’s impressive bowling performance, including figures of 3 for 27, was instrumental in the West Indies’ successful chase.

What Happened Today in History

Let’s take a moment to remember important events that happened on this day in history. Discover significant milestones and historical moments from the past:

1. Discovery of the Tomb of Tutankhamun (1922)
On August 25, 1922, British archaeologist Howard Carter made a groundbreaking discovery in Egypt’s Valley of the Kings: the nearly intact tomb of Tutankhamun. The find, which included a wealth of artifacts and treasures, captivated the world and provided significant insights into ancient Egyptian culture and burial practices.

2. The Start of the Korean War (1950)
On August 25, 1950, the Korean War saw a major escalation as United Nations forces began counteroffensive operations against North Korean troops. The conflict, which began in June 1950, became a significant Cold War battleground, influencing global politics and military strategies for years to come.

3. Launch of the Space Shuttle Atlantis (1985)
On August 25, 1985, NASA launched the Space Shuttle Atlantis on its maiden voyage, STS-51-J. This mission marked a significant milestone for the shuttle program and demonstrated the shuttle’s ability to deploy military satellites, showcasing its versatility and importance in space exploration.

4. Signing of the Paris Peace Accords (1973)
On August 25, 1973, the Paris Peace Accords were formally signed, officially ending U.S. involvement in the Vietnam War. The agreement aimed to establish peace in Vietnam and set the stage for the eventual reunification of North and South Vietnam under communist control.

5. Death of John F. Kennedy Jr. (1999)
On August 25, 1999, John F. Kennedy Jr., the son of former U.S. President John F. Kennedy, tragically died in a plane crash off the coast of Massachusetts. The crash also claimed the lives of his wife, Carolyn Bessette-Kennedy, and her sister, Lauren Bessette. Kennedy Jr.’s death was a significant event in American history, drawing widespread media coverage and public mourning.
