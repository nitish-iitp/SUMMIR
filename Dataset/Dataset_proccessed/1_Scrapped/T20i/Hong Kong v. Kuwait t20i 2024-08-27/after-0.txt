National News Headlines in English for 27 August 2024

Let us move on to National News in English for 27 August 2024. These updates will provide a look at key events and important announcements from across the country.

1. RBI’s Unified Lending Interface (ULI) Set to Transform Rural Credit with Frictionless Access
The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) is piloting its Unified Lending Interface (ULI), aiming to simplify credit appraisals, especially for rural borrowers. RBI Governor Shaktikanta Das confirmed its nationwide launch during a conference on digital infrastructure.

2. NCERT Proposes New Evaluation Model for Class 12 Board Exams with Emphasis on Vocational Training
NCERT’s new proposal for Class 12 exams will factor in scores from Classes 9 to 11, encouraging vocational and skill-based education. The model aims for a holistic learning approach and standardised assessments across educational boards in India.

3. Anil Ambani Reviews Legal Options After SEBI Bans Him from Securities Market for Five Years
Anil Ambani is exploring legal options following SEBI’s five-year ban on accessing the securities market. The ban stems from fund diversion allegations linked to Reliance Home Finance, now owned by Authum Investment and Infrastructure.

4.  Indian Government Probes Telegram Over Extortion and Gambling, Ban Possible
The Indian government is investigating Telegram for criminal activities, including extortion and gambling. The app’s CEO, Pavel Durov, was arrested in Paris for inadequate moderation. A ban on Telegram could follow based on the probe’s findings.

5. Maharashtra First State to Implement Unified Pension Scheme After Central Government’s Approval
Maharashtra became the first state to adopt the Unified Pension Scheme (UPS), offering 50% of the last 12-month average salary as pension. This decision followed the Union cabinet’s approval, benefitting millions of government employees.

6. Maharashtra Becomes First State to Implement Unified Pension Scheme Amid Employee Demands
Maharashtra has implemented the Unified Pension Scheme (UPS), offering 50% of employees’ average salary from the last 12 months as pension. This move followed central government approval, addressing demands of post-2004 employees.

7. RBI Governor Highlights GEM, UPI, and ULI as Key Innovations in Digital Payments
RBI Governor Shaktikanta Das emphasized the transformative role of GEM, UPI, and ULI in India’s digital payment landscape. He introduced ULI as a new frictionless credit platform, enhancing the efficiency of credit disbursals.

8. Janmashtami Celebrations: President and Vice President Extend Greetings to the Nation
Janmashtami is being celebrated with enthusiasm nationwide. President Droupadi Murmu urged citizens to embrace Lord Krishna’s teachings, while Vice President Dhankhar highlighted the festival’s spiritual significance and its message of peace and unity.

9. India to Host Inaugural World Audio Visual and Entertainment Summit in Goa
India is set to host the first World Audio Visual and Entertainment Summit (WAVES) in Goa this November. The event will promote India’s media and entertainment industry, with global icons participating in challenges and discussions on entertainment economics.

10. Gold Demand in India Expected to Surge During Festive Season After Import Duty Cut
India’s gold demand is expected to increase during the festive season following a reduction in import duties from 15% to 6%. The move benefits retail consumers, although higher demand may affect India’s trade deficit and the rupee’s value.

Now, let us turn to the State News Headlines in English for 27 August 2024. This section covers key events and updates from various states across India.

1. Aerospace Scientists to Test First Nuclear Rocket Engine in Space by 2025
Aerospace scientists are set to test the first nuclear rocket engine in space by 2025. The DRACO project, developed by DARPA and Lockheed Martin, aims to revolutionize spaceflight, with NASA planning to use the technology for future Moon and Mars missions.

2. BJP Revises Candidate List for Jammu and Kashmir Assembly Elections
The BJP released, then deleted, a list of 44 candidates for the upcoming elections in Jammu and Kashmir, before issuing a revised list of 15 candidates for the first phase. The exclusion of key figures, including the state BJP president Ravinder Raina and former deputy CMs Nirmal Singh and Kavinder Gupta, caused significant backlash, prompting the change.

3.  Ladakh to Get Five New Districts to Strengthen Local Governance
Union Home Minister Amit Shah announced the creation of five new districts in Ladakh, including Zanskar, Drass, Sham, Nubra, and Changthang. The move, inspired by Prime Minister Modi’s vision for a developed Ladakh, aims to bring governance and services closer to the region’s people.

4. Telangana High Court Orders Status Quo on Demolition of Nagarjuna’s Convention Centre
The Telangana High Court has temporarily halted the demolition of actor Nagarjuna’s N-Convention Centre in Hyderabad. The court noted that proper notice was not served to Nagarjuna before the demolition began, as per his claims, while the state argued that the construction was unauthorized and violated local zoning laws.

5. Maharashtra to Install Panic Buttons in Schools for Student Safety
In response to recent incidents, Maharashtra’s School Education Minister, Deepak Kesarkar, announced plans to install panic buttons in schools across the state. The government will also introduce complaint boxes in every school, with the involvement of police authorities, as part of broader safety measures being implemented.

International News in English for 27 July 2024

Moving on, let us review the International News in English for 27 July 2024. This segment will cover significant global events and developments.

1. China Enhances Border Security Amid Myanmar Instability
China’s military has deployed army units and joint air-ground patrols in Yunnan province, near the Myanmar border. This move aims to test rapid mobility and maintain stability amid ongoing violence in Myanmar, posing a significant border security challenge for China.

2.  Explosions in Kyiv as Russia Launches Large-Scale Attack
Central Kyiv experienced explosions during rush hour as Ukraine faced a massive Russian missile and drone attack. Ukrainian and Polish forces are responding to the assault, which targeted several regions, following heightened warnings of such attacks around Ukraine’s Independence Day.

3.  Brazil’s Foreign Minister Visits India for Key Discussions
Brazil’s Foreign Minister Mauro Vieira has arrived in New Delhi to attend the 9th India-Brazil Joint Commission Meeting. His visit comes amid reports of Indian migrants held in São Paulo without visas. Discussions will focus on advancing G20 outcomes and bilateral cooperation.

4. Microsoft Rethinks Windows Security After Major IT Outage
Microsoft is enhancing Windows security following a global IT outage caused by a flawed CrowdStrike update. The incident, affecting millions of devices, has led to widespread disruptions and financial losses, prompting a review of security protocols and third-party software access.

5.  India-Singapore Ministerial Roundtable to Strengthen Ties
The 2nd India-Singapore Ministerial Roundtable on August 26, 2024, in Singapore, will see high-level talks between Indian and Singaporean officials. The roundtable aims to boost economic cooperation, improve trade relations, and attract more foreign direct investment, reflecting deepening bilateral ties.
Sports News in English for 27 August 2024

Catch up on the latest sports news today. From thrilling match results and player achievements to major events and updates across various sports, stay informed about all the exciting developments in the world of sports.

1. Pakistan’s Cricket Team Faces Criticism After Shock Test Loss to Bangladesh
Former cricketers Aakash Chopra and Michael Vaughan criticized Pakistan’s team following their unexpected defeat to Bangladesh in the first Test. The loss, due to effective spin bowling by Bangladesh, impacts Pakistan’s ICC Test standings.

2. India Wins Silver at Asian Surfing Championships 2024
India secured a silver medal at the Maruhaba Cup during the Asian Surfing Championships in the Maldives. Japan won gold, while India’s team performed strongly, finishing ahead of Taipei and China. This success follows India’s first-ever surfing quota for the 2026 Asian Games.

3. Paris Paralympics 2024 to Begin with Record Number of Indian Athletes
The Paris Paralympics 2024 will start on August 28, featuring a record 84 Indian athletes. The event runs until September 8, marking the largest Indian delegation in Paralympic history. Union Minister Dr. Mansukh Mandaviya has encouraged national support for the athletes.

4. Former Pakistan Cricketers Slam Team After 10-Wicket Defeat to Bangladesh
Ramiz Raja and Shahid Afridi criticized Pakistan’s cricket team and management following their 10-wicket loss to Bangladesh in Rawalpindi. The defeat highlights ongoing issues in team selection and strategy, extending Pakistan’s Test winless streak.

5. Australia Reveals Squad for Women’s T20 World Cup 2024
Australia has announced its squad for the Women’s T20 World Cup in the UAE. Captain Alyssa Healy leads the team, with Tahlia McGrath as vice-captain. The squad includes three new players and features a mix of experienced and emerging talent.

Business News Today Headlines

Stay updated with today’s top business news. Discover the latest market trends, corporate developments, and economic updates shaping the business world. Keep informed about key financial changes and industry shifts.


 1. Telegram CEO Pavel Durov’s Detention Sparks Controversy
Pavel Durov, the CEO of Telegram, was recently detained at Le Bourget airport in Paris over the weekend. The detention, reportedly on suspicion of failing to prevent the criminal use of Telegram, has raised concerns about its legitimacy. The initial detention period was extended by 24 hours late on Sunday.

2. iPhone 16 Series Launch Expected Soon with New Features and Pricing
The iPhone 16 series is approaching its official launch, with recent leaks indicating the event will occur in the coming weeks. Bloomberg reports that Apple is expected to unveil the new models, iPhone 16, iPhone 16 Plus, iPhone 16 Pro, and iPhone 16 Pro Max on September 10. 

3. Samsung Explores Innovative Foldable and Rollable Smartphone Designs
Samsung continues to lead the foldable smartphone market with its Galaxy Z Fold 6 and Galaxy Z Flip 6, and is now reportedly developing even more advanced designs. According to Tech Radar, Samsung is working on new form factors, including double-foldable and rollable smartphones. Although specific release dates are not yet available, Samsung’s ongoing innovation in the foldable phone space suggests the company will remain at the forefront of mobile technology. 

4. BYD Becomes Seventh-Largest Global Automaker by Vehicle Sales
China’s BYD has achieved a significant milestone by becoming the seventh-largest automaker globally by the number of vehicles sold during the April-June quarter. According to data from MarkLines, BYD sold 980,000 units, marking a 40 percent increase from the previous year. This growth is largely driven by the rising demand for BYD’s affordable electric vehicles (EVs). BYD’s success is notable as many leading global automakers, including Toyota and Volkswagen, saw declines during the same period.

5. Lamborghini Launches High-Performance Urus SE Hybrid SUV in India
Lamborghini has unveiled the Urus SE, its latest high-performance plug-in hybrid SUV, in India, with a starting price of Rs 4.57 crore (ex-showroom). The Urus SE features a 4.0-litre V8 twin-turbo petrol engine combined with an electric motor, delivering a total output of 800 horsepower and 950 Nm of torque. It boasts a top speed of 312 km/h.
Science and Technology News for 27 August 2024

Next, we’ll cover the latest in science and technology. Discover advancements in AI, trends in the smartphone market, and other key industry updates to stay current with essential developments:


1. Nvidia’s Upcoming Earnings Report Could Influence AI Sector
Nvidia’s stock is poised for a significant test with its upcoming earnings report, set to be released after the market closes on Wednesday. The results will be closely watched as they may impact other AI-related companies. Recent declines in shares of major tech firms like Amazon, Microsoft, and Alphabet highlight the volatility in the AI sector. 

2. China Criticizes U.S. Export Controls on Chinese Firms
China has condemned the U.S. decision to add 105 companies to its export control list, including 42 from China, due to alleged links with Russia. This move, announced on Friday, affects companies accused of supplying electronics to the Russian military or manufacturing drones used in the Ukraine conflict. The U.S. action complicates these firms’ access to American exports, requiring special licenses for transactions. 

3. Foxconn Promotes Gender Inclusivity in Indian Tech Industry
Foxconn is setting new standards for gender inclusivity in the Indian tech industry. Chairman Young Liu announced plans to advance women employees into leadership roles within design and technology, moving beyond traditional assembly line positions. With 48,000 employees in India, Foxconn has recently hired 25% married women, reflecting its commitment to providing broader career opportunities. 

4. Citigroup Defends Termination of Former Managing Director Amid Lawsuit
Citigroup has responded to a lawsuit filed by Kathleen Martin, a former managing director, claiming her termination was due to performance issues rather than retaliation for her whistleblowing. Martin alleges that her supervisor, Chief Operating Officer Anand Selva, instructed her to hide critical data governance information from regulators to avoid damaging the bank’s reputation. 

5. Zuckerberg and Ek Criticize European AI Regulations
Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg and Spotify CEO Daniel Ek have criticized European regulations on open-source artificial intelligence, arguing that Europe’s complex and fragmented rules could hinder innovation. In a joint statement, the CEOs highlighted that Europe’s regulatory environment is creating obstacles for open-source developers and impeding progress. They called for streamlined regulations to support the growth of open-source AI and benefit European developers and the broader creator ecosystem.
Today’s Entertainment News in English 

Turning to entertainment, we’ll cover the latest buzz from the world of movies, music, and celebrities. Catch up on the exciting news and stories making waves in the entertainment industry:

1. TIFF to Premiere Restored Classic “Awara” Celebrating Raj Kapoor’s Legacy
The Toronto International Film Festival (TIFF) will celebrate the centenary of Indian cinema legend Raj Kapoor with a world premiere of the 1951 classic “Awara.” The film will be shown in a 4K digital restoration, enhancing its original print, during TIFF’s 49th edition from September 5-15. The restoration was a collaborative effort between the National Film Development Corporation and the National Film Archive of India.

2. Karnataka CM Orders Suspension of Jail Officials After Actor Darshan Controversy
Following a viral video of actor Darshan with a notorious rowdy-sheeter in Bengaluru’s Central Prison, Karnataka Chief Minister Siddaramaiah has suspended seven jail officials for misconduct. The officials include jailers, assistant jailers, head warders, and a warder. The CM has also mandated a reassignment of Darshan and other inmates and requested a detailed report from the DG & IGP.

3. Shraddha Kapoor Shatters Records with “Stree 2” and Gains Massive Instagram Following
Shraddha Kapoor’s latest film “Stree 2” has set new box office records, with Kapoor becoming the second most-followed Indian celebrity on Instagram. She now boasts over 91.2 million followers, surpassing prominent figures like PM Modi and Priyanka Chopra. Kapoor’s success with “Stree 2” further establishes her as a leading action heroine.

4.  Veteran Actress Asha Sharma Passes Away at 88
Veteran actress Asha Sharma, known for her roles in “Mujhe Kucch Kehna Hai,” “Pyaar To Hona Hi Tha,” and “Hum Tumhare Hain Sanam,” has passed away at 88. She was also recognized for her television work in “Kumkum Bhagya,” “Mann Ki Awaz Pratigya,” and “Ek Aur Mahabharat.” Tributes highlight her kindness and significant impact on both film and television.

5. Malayalam Film Industry Faces Reckoning After Harassment Allegations
The Malayalam film industry is rocked by revelations of harassment following the Justice K Hema Committee’s report. High-profile resignations include Director Ranjith and actor Siddique. Chief Minister Pinarayi Vijayan has initiated a special team to investigate the allegations, as additional accounts of abuse surface, including a resurfaced case involving actor-politician Mukesh.
