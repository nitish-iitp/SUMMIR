National News Headlines in English for 28 August 2024

Here’s what’s happening across India with national news headlines for 28 August 2024. Catch up on the major stories making headlines today.

1. BJP Disavows Kangana Ranaut’s Comments on Farmers’ Movement
The Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) has stated that Kangana Ranaut’s recent remarks on the farmers’ movement do not represent the party’s views. The BJP clarified that she is not authorized to speak on its policy matters.

2. India and Brazil Discuss G20 Outcomes at Joint Commission Meeting
External Affairs Minister Dr. S. Jaishankar and Brazilian Foreign Minister Mauro Vieira are co-chairing the 9th India-Brazil Joint Commission Meeting in New Delhi. They aim to advance key G20 outcomes and strengthen their strategic partnership.

3. Heightened Security in Kolkata for ‘Nabanna Abhijan’ Rally
Security is intensified in Kolkata as students protest at the state secretariat demanding Chief Minister Mamata Banerjee’s resignation. Over 6,000 police personnel are deployed, with reports of clashes and the use of tear gas.

4. RBI Launches Unified Lending Interface to Revolutionize Credit
The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has announced the Unified Lending Interface (ULI) to facilitate seamless credit. RBI Governor Shaktikanta Das expects ULI to transform the lending sector similar to UPI’s impact on payments.

5. Modi and Biden Discuss Ukraine and Bilateral Relations in Recent Call
Prime Minister Narendra Modi and US President Joe Biden recently discussed regional and global issues, including the situation in Ukraine. Modi reiterated India’s support for dialogue and peace while appreciating Biden’s commitment to the India-US partnership.

6. India-Singapore Ministerial Roundtable Focuses on Future Cooperation
The second India-Singapore Ministerial Roundtable (ISMR) in Singapore highlighted areas for enhanced collaboration, including digitalization, skill development, sustainability, healthcare, and advanced manufacturing. Key Indian ministers and Singapore’s Deputy Prime Minister discussed the 60th anniversary of diplomatic relations and further cooperation in regional and global forums.

7. Biden Praises Modi for Peace Efforts During Call
US President Joe Biden praised Prime Minister Narendra Modi for his peace message and humanitarian support for Ukraine during their recent phone call. They discussed Modi’s recent visits to Ukraine and Poland and affirmed their commitment to peace and prosperity in the Indo-Pacific region.

8. Biden Commends Modi’s Peace Efforts in Ukraine
US President Joe Biden commended Prime Minister Narendra Modi for his recent peace initiatives in Ukraine during their phone call. This was the first discussion between the leaders following Modi’s trips to Russia, Poland, and Ukraine.

9. FIR Filed Against Contractor for Collapsed Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Statue
An FIR has been lodged against contractor Jaydeep Apte and structural consultant Chetan Patil for the collapse of the Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj statue in Maharashtra. The charges include fraud and endangering public safety due to alleged poor construction quality.

10. Supreme Court Reviews State Compliance on Judicial Pay Recommendations
Chief secretaries from 18 states and union territories appeared before the Supreme Court regarding the implementation of the Second National Judicial Pay Commission’s recommendations. The court noted compliance from several states and ordered the closure of proceedings against them.

1. State Government Extends Demolition Drive to All Districts
The state government has decided to expand its demolition of illegal structures on lake beds to all 33 districts, following the success of the Hyderabad initiative. Minister Ponnam Prabhakar emphasized the drive’s role in preserving water bodies for future generations.

2. Minister Calls for Public Participation in Lake and Pond Protection
Minister Ponnam Prabhakar urged local communities, organizations, and officials to help identify and report encroachments on lakes and ponds. He stressed the importance of conserving these water bodies for environmental balance and public health.

3. West Bengal Government Seeks More Time to Respond in OBC Reservation Case
The West Bengal government requested additional time to reply to responses in the case challenging the Calcutta High Court’s decision on OBC status for several castes. Senior advocate Kapil Sibal asked for time to address the extensive documents submitted by the opposing side.

4. Tensions Rise in Kolkata Over ‘Nabanna Abhijan’ Protest
Tensions increased in West Bengal as students’ group ‘Paschimbanga Chhatro Samaj’ planned a protest march to demand Chief Minister Mamata Banerjee’s resignation over a recent rape-murder case. The police rejected the group’s application for the march, citing concerns about public order.

5. Congress Announces First Nine Candidates for Jammu and Kashmir Assembly Elections
The Congress party has announced its first nine candidates for the Jammu and Kashmir assembly elections, including Ghulam Ahmad Mir and Vikar Rasool Wani. Other candidates include Surinder Singh Channi, Amanullah Mantoo, and Peerzada Mohd Syed.
International News in English for 28 July 2024

Time to look at the global scene with international news for 28 July 2024. See what’s making headlines around the world today

1. China Allocates $15 Billion to Western Pacific Military Exercises Amid Regional Tensions
A Taiwanese estimate reveals that China spent 7% of its 2023 defense budget, or $15 billion, on military exercises in the Western Pacific. This reflects China’s increased military presence and focus on the First Island Chain, impacting regional stability, according to Taiwan’s defense ministry.

2. New Mpox Strain Clade Ib Spreads Rapidly, Outpacing Tracking Efforts
The new strain of the mpox virus, clade Ib, is evolving quickly and spreading from the Democratic Republic of Congo. Limited resources hinder tracking efforts, as global concern rises over its impact following the World Health Organization’s health emergency declaration.

3. US National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan Visits China Amid Trade and Geopolitical Tensions
Jake Sullivan, US National Security Advisor, begins a three-day visit to China to discuss key issues including Ukraine, Taiwan, and fentanyl. This is the first visit by an American NSA to China in eight years, set against a backdrop of ongoing trade conflicts and geopolitical disagreements.

4. Mark Zuckerberg Accuses Biden Administration of Pressuring Facebook to Censor Covid-19 Content
Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg alleges in a letter to the US House Judiciary Committee that the Biden administration pressured Facebook to censor Covid-19 posts. Zuckerberg expressed regret for not being more vocal about these pressures and criticized the administration’s approach to content moderation.

5. Russia Intensifies Attacks on Ukraine, Killing Four and Targeting Power Grid
Russia has launched extensive missile and drone strikes on Ukrainian regions, resulting in at least four deaths. The attacks include a devastating strike on a hotel in Kryvyi Rih and continued assaults on Kyiv, where Ukrainian air defenses successfully intercepted multiple drones and missiles.
Sports News in English for 28 August 2024

Here’s your sports update with sports news in English for 26 August 2024. Find out what’s new in the world of sports today.


1. Harmanpreet Kaur to Lead India in ICC Women’s T20 World Cup 2024 
Harmanpreet Kaur will captain the Indian squad for the ICC Women’s T20 World Cup 2024 in the UAE, with Smriti Mandhana as vice-captain. The squad includes key players like Shafali Verma and Deepti Sharma, with the tournament scheduled from October 3 to 20 in Dubai and Sharjah. India is in Group A alongside Australia, New Zealand, Pakistan, and Sri Lanka.

2. India’s Schedule for ICC Women’s T20 World Cup 2024 Announced
India will begin their ICC Women’s T20 World Cup 2024 campaign on October 4 against New Zealand in Dubai. Their subsequent matches include games against Pakistan on October 6, Sri Lanka on October 9, and Australia on October 13. The tournament, now held in the UAE instead of Bangladesh, will feature semifinals on October 17 and 18 and the final on October 20.

3. Novak Djokovic Starts U.S. Open Campaign with Win Despite RustNovak Djokovic began his quest for a record 25th Grand Slam title with a 6-2 6-2 6-4 victory over Radu Albot in the U.S. Open first round. Despite a lackluster performance and numerous unforced errors, Djokovic secured his 78th win at Arthur Ashe Stadium, following his recent Olympic success.

4. England Revamps White-Ball Squad, Drops Bairstow and Ali
England’s new white-ball era sees the exclusion of Jonny Bairstow and Moeen Ali from upcoming matches against Australia. The squad overhaul, under interim coach Marcus Trescothick, includes five uncapped players as England seeks to revitalize its limited-overs teams after recent disappointing campaigns.

5. Dominic Thiem’s U.S. Open Farewell Ends in First-Round Defeat
Former champion Dominic Thiem was defeated 6-4 6-2 6-2 by Ben Shelton in the first round of the U.S. Open. Thiem, who has struggled with wrist injuries and announced his retirement, faced a challenging match against the young American, who aims to build on his previous success at the tournament.

Business News Today Headlines

Stay updated with today’s top business news. Discover the latest market trends, corporate developments, and economic updates shaping the business world. Keep informed about key financial changes and industry shifts.

1. LG Electronics Considers IPO for Indian Unit to Boost Revenue Goals
LG Electronics is contemplating an initial public offering (IPO) for its Indian business to support its target of reaching $75 billion in revenue by 2030. The move reflects the company’s strategy to enhance its revenue from enterprise clients and leverage India’s growing market.

2. Capitalmind Financial Services Receives SEBI Approval for New Mutual Fund Launch
Bengaluru-based Capitalmind Financial Services has obtained in-principle approval from SEBI to launch a mutual fund. This expansion follows its successful track record in portfolio management, with the firm managing over Rs 2,200 crore in assets.

3. Infosys to Defend Against Cognizant’s Trade Secrets Lawsuit Over Healthcare Software
Infosys plans to vigorously contest a lawsuit filed by Cognizant, alleging the misappropriation of trade secrets related to its healthcare software. The lawsuit claims Infosys used Cognizant’s data to develop new software, breaching confidentiality agreements.

4. Canada to Cut Temporary Foreign Worker Numbers Amid Concerns Over Program’s Impact
Canada will reduce the number of temporary foreign workers as it addresses concerns over the program’s impact on wages and worker rights. The move comes amid criticisms of the program and growing political pressure to manage immigration more effectively.

5. Canada’s Trudeau Considers Reductions in Permanent Resident Streams Amid Immigration Concerns
Prime Minister Justin Trudeau is evaluating potential cuts to permanent resident streams, as public opinion grows critical of Canada’s immigration policies. The review aims to balance support for immigration with effective integration and success pathways for newcomers.
Science and Technology News for 28 August 2024

Next, we’ll cover the latest in science and technology. Discover advancements in AI, trends in the smartphone market, and other key industry updates to stay current with essential developments:

1. Apple Sets September 9 for Major Fall Event, Unveiling Expected for iPhone 16 Series
Apple has scheduled its highly anticipated fall event for September 9, 2024, where the company is expected to unveil the iPhone 16 series, Watch 10, and more. The event will be held at Steve Jobs Theater in Apple Park at 10 a.m. PT (10:30 p.m. IST) and will be streamed live on Apple’s website, Apple TV app, and YouTube.

2. Apple Partners with Bharti Airtel for Exclusive Streaming Offers in India
Apple has partnered with Bharti Airtel to offer exclusive access to Apple TV+ and Apple Music for Airtel Xstream customers. The partnership will enhance Airtel’s fiber plans by including Apple TV+ and provide Apple Music and Apple TV+ offers exclusively to Airtel customers later this year.

3. Rebel Foods Seeks $120 Million Funding Round Led by Temasek
Rebel Foods, known for brands like Faasos and Behrouz Biryani, is in talks to raise $120 million in a new funding round led by Singapore’s Temasek. The funding would value the company at around $1 billion, with half of the amount going as primary capital and the rest in secondary transactions. 

4. Zomato and Paytm to End Up with Similar Cash Reserves Post-Transaction
Zomato and Paytm are set to each have over Rs 10,000 crore in cash following Zomato’s acquisition of Paytm’s entertainment ticketing business. While Zomato has successfully turned its fortunes around with strategic acquisitions, Paytm has struggled with market perception and business adjustments.

5. YouTube Increases Subscription Prices in IndiaYouTube has announced a price hike for its Premium membership in India, with the Family plan seeing a 58% increase to ₹299 per month. The company stated that the price adjustment is necessary to continue improving services and supporting creators, as per a notification shared by a user on X (formerly Twitter).
Today’s Entertainment News in English 

Turning to entertainment, we’ll cover the latest buzz from the world of movies, music, and celebrities. Catch up on the exciting news and stories making waves in the entertainment industry:

1. Mariah Carey in Mourning After Losing Mother and Sister on Same Day
Mariah Carey is reportedly in shock after the sudden loss of both her mother and sister on the same day over the weekend. Her representative has confirmed the tragic news and requested privacy for Carey during this difficult time.

2. Rajkummar Rao and Shraddha Kapoor’s ‘Stree 2’ Breaks Box Office Records
The horror-comedy sequel “Stree 2,” starring Rajkummar Rao and Shraddha Kapoor, has achieved a milestone by crossing the Rs 400-crore mark in just 12 days. In an exclusive interview, Rajkummar Rao discusses the film’s success, his character Vicky, and the potential for a third instalment.

3. Ben Affleck’s New Relationship with Kick Kennedy Raises Eyebrows
Following his recent divorce from Jennifer Lopez, Ben Affleck is reportedly dating Kick Kennedy, daughter of Robert F. Kennedy Jr. This development comes amid speculation about Affleck’s personal life and his past relationship with Jennifer Garner, who is noted for her ongoing support for him.

4. Captain Devi Sharan’s Harrowing Hijack Experience Revisited
Captain Devi Sharan recounts the traumatic hijacking of IC 814 on Christmas Eve of the millennium. The plane, carrying 188 passengers, was hijacked by five masked men, leading to a critical situation when the Lahore ATC refused an emergency landing request, leaving Sharan with only one option—crash-landing.

5. Kerala Opposition Leader Criticizes Government’s Handling of Film Industry Abuse Report
V.D. Satheesan, Leader of Opposition in the Kerala Assembly, accuses the Left government of shielding perpetrators of sexual abuse in the Malayalam film industry by not adequately addressing the Justice Hema Committee Report. He also questions the composition of the investigation team, which he argues should be led by senior women officers.
