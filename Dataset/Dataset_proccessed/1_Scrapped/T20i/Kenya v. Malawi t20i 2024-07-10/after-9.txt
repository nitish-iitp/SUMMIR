National News Headlines in English for 14 July 2024

Stay updated with 14 July 2024 news headlines in English for school assembly. Learn about various sectors´ happenings in our country. 

1. Surge in Demand for India-Made Bicycles Across Europe and Africa:

India’s bicycle exports to the UK, Germany, and the Netherlands increased significantly due to Europe’s growing health and environmental focus. Mozambique and Chad also saw a rise in imports, with Mozambique becoming India’s top export destination for bicycles. In FY2024, India’s bicycle exports grew by 16.95%, reaching $54.38 million, up from $46.5 million in FY2023.

2. India Abstains from UN Resolution on Russia-Ukraine Conflict:

India abstained from voting on a UN General Assembly resolution demanding Russia cease its aggression against Ukraine. The resolution also called for the withdrawal of Russian forces from the Zaporizhzhia nuclear power plant. The resolution saw 99 votes in favour, nine against, and 60 abstentions, including India.

3. India to Become the World’s Second Largest Economy by 2031:

RBI Deputy Governor Michael Debabrata Patra predicted that India could become the world’s second-largest economy by 2031 and the largest by 2060. He made this statement at the Lal Bahadur Shastri National Academy of Administration, noting India’s significant economic growth to a USD 3.6 trillion economy in the last financial year.

4. India Plans to Overhaul 200 State-Run Firms for Profitability:

India is set to revamp over 200 state-run firms to enhance profitability, moving away from Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s privatisation efforts. The new plan, to be unveiled in the annual budget, includes selling underutilized land and monetizing other assets. This strategy aims to rejuvenate the $600 billion state sector.

5. Union Budget 2024-2025 to Focus on Shipbuilding Industry:

Union Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman will present the Union Budget on July 23, with significant expectations for the shipbuilding sector. The Maritime India Vision 2030 aims for India to rank among the top 10 global shipbuilders, with aspirations to break into the top five by 2047. The budget is likely to include proposals for shipbuilding clusters on India’s east and west coasts.

6. Rahul Gandhi Appeals for Civility Towards Smriti Irani on Social Media:

Congress leader Rahul Gandhi urged the public to avoid derogatory language towards former Union Minister Smriti Irani. Despite past political rivalry, Gandhi emphasized the importance of respectful discourse in a social media post, highlighting the need for civility in political engagements.

7. India’s Foreign Exchange Reserves Hit Record High:

India’s foreign exchange reserves reached an all-time high of $657.16 billion following its inclusion in a key JPMorgan Chase & Co. emerging market index. The reserves grew by $5.2 billion in the week of July 5, driven by inflows in equity and bonds.

8. Jio Financial Services Approved as Core Investment Company by RBI:

The Reserve Bank of India approved Jio Financial Services Ltd.’s conversion from a non-banking financial company to a core investment company. This move follows the demerger from Reliance Industries and aims to enhance the company’s investment capabilities in equity and bonds.

State News Headlines in English for 14 July 2024

Stay updated with the latest developments and impactful stories unfolding across your state.

1. Delhi Police File FIR Against Resident for Derogatory Comments Online:

Delhi police filed an FIR against a resident for making lewd comments about the widow of Kirti Chakra awardee Captain Anshuman Singh. The action followed a letter from the National Commission for Women, urging immediate arrest and a detailed report.

2. Punjab and Haryana High Court Clarifies Shambhu Border Access:

The Punjab and Haryana High Court clarified that the Shambhu border opening directive is for all citizens, not just specific groups. This response came after a PIL seeking to allow public access, including businessmen and vendors, to cross the border.

3. Mahayuti Secures Majority in Maharashtra Legislative Council Elections:

In the Maharashtra legislative council elections, Mahayuti secured all nine of their candidates’ seats. Mahavikas Aghadi won two out of three seats, with senior leader Jayant Patil losing due to insufficient votes.

4. UPMRC Extends Lucknow Metro Operating Hours:

Starting July 15, Lucknow Metro’s operating hours will extend to 10:30 PM from both CCS Airport and Munshi Puliya stations. This change aims to provide greater convenience and accessibility for passengers, according to UPMRC MD Shri Sushil Kumar.

5. Karnataka IT Minister Unveils 27th Bengaluru Tech Summit:

Karnataka IT Minister Priyank Kharge launched the 27th Bengaluru Tech Summit, set for November 19-21. The event will showcase Bengaluru’s tech innovation, featuring over 50,000 attendees, 460 speakers, and 500 startups.

1. Immigrants in Canada Face Worst Job Crisis in a Decade:

Canada’s unemployment rate for recent immigrants hit 12.6% in June, the highest in a decade. This economic downturn significantly impacts Indians, who form the largest group of recent immigrants.

2. Microsoft Settles Antitrust Complaint with CISPE:

Microsoft reached an agreement with CISPE, resolving an antitrust complaint over licensing practices and avoiding a potential EU investigation. This settlement aims to address concerns about Microsoft’s impact on the European cloud computing market.

3. Elon Musk Makes “Sizeable” Donation to Trump Campaign PAC, Stirring Political Speculation:

Tesla and SpaceX CEO Elon Musk has reportedly made a significant contribution to America PAC, a super political action committee supporting Donald Trump’s 2024 presidential bid. Despite his previous claims of political independence, Musk’s donation signals a notable shift in his public stance. The exact amount remains undisclosed pending PAC disclosures on July 15.

4. Pakistan and IMF Reach $7 Billion Agreement to Bolster Economic Stability:

Pakistan and the International Monetary Fund (IMF) have finalized a staff-level agreement for a 37-month Extended Fund Facility (EFF) worth approximately $7 billion. This arrangement follows the successful economic stability achieved under the previous Stand-by Arrangement in 2023. The IMF aims to support Pakistan in solidifying macroeconomic stability and fostering conditions for robust, inclusive, and resilient growth in the financially challenged nation.

5. Nepal Approves Rail Connectivity Agreement with China Amid Political Turbulence:

Nepal’s Prime Minister Pushpa Kamal Dahal ‘Prachanda’ gave the green light to an agreement facilitating rail connectivity between Nepal and China under Beijing’s Belt and Road Initiative. The decision, deemed more operational than political, underscores Nepal’s commitment to participate in China’s ambitious infrastructure endeavors, as stated by government sources and Communication Minister Rekha Sharma endeavours.

Sports News in English for 14 July 2024

Get the latest sports news in English and follow your favourite sports events:

1. P V Sindhu Trains Under Prakash Padukone for Paris Olympics:

P V Sindhu, aiming for a third Olympic medal, has been training under legendary player Prakash Padukone in Bengaluru. Her preparation includes a support staff of 12 members, enhancing her prospects for the Paris Olympics.

2. Gautam Gambhir Faces Challenges in Appointing Support Staff:

Newly appointed head coach Gautam Gambhir’s choices for India’s support staff have faced resistance from BCCI. His preferred candidates for bowling and fielding coaches were not approved, as the board maintains an all-Indian support staff policy.

3. Carlos Alcaraz Advances to Wimbledon Final:

Carlos Alcaraz reached his second consecutive Wimbledon final after defeating Daniil Medvedev. Alcaraz will face either Novak Djokovic or Lorenzo Musetti in the final, with Spain also competing in the Euro 2024 final on the same day.

4. Yuvraj Singh Rolls Back the Years, Leads India Champions to Victory Over Australia Champions in WCL Semi-finals:

Yuvraj Singh showcased his vintage form, scoring a blistering 59 off 28 balls with four fours and five sixes, powering India to a commanding total of 254/6 against Australia in the World Championship of Legends (WCL) semi-finals in Northampton. Known for his prowess in big matches, Yuvraj once again delivered against Australia, leaving fans reminiscing about his dominance over the Aussies in his playing days.

5. Rohit Sharma Makes Wimbledon Debut Amidst London Vacation:

Indian T20 World Cup-winning captain Rohit Sharma made a surprise appearance at Wimbledon, enjoying Friday’s semi-final clashes featuring Carlos Alcaraz vs Daniil Medvedev and Novak Djokovic vs Lorenzo Musetti. Rohit, celebrating India’s recent T20 victory, graced the iconic Centre Court during his London vacation.

Today’s Entertainment News in English 

Now, let’s dive into the entertainment world and see what new has taken place.

1. Alec Baldwin’s Involuntary Manslaughter Case Dismissed:

A judge dismissed Alec Baldwin’s trial for involuntary manslaughter due to withheld evidence. Baldwin had accidentally shot cinematographer Halyna Hutchins on the set of “Rust” in 2021.

2. Anant Ambani and Radhika Merchant’s Grand Wedding in Mumbai:

Anant Ambani and Radhika Merchant’s wedding at Jio World Drive in Mumbai was a star-studded event. Celebrities like Shah Rukh Khan, Salman Khan, and international stars attended the grand celebration.

3. Rebel Wilson Embroiled in Legal Battle with Film Producers:

Rebel Wilson faces a defamation suit following her social media allegations against her film’s producers. Wilson accused them of misconduct and blocking the film’s premiere at the Toronto International Film Festival.

4. Kamal Haasan’s Indian 2 Receives Mixed Reviews:

Kamal Haasan’s sequel to the 1996 hit, Indian 2: Zero Tolerance, opened to mixed reviews. The film, directed by S Shankar, sees Haasan reprising his role as Veerasekaran Senapathy.

5. BTS Members Jung Kook and Jimin Star in New Travel Show:

BTS members Jung Kook and Jimin star in a new travel show on Disney Plus, titled “Are You Sure?”. The show features the duo engaging in various adventures, providing fans a glimpse into their off-stage personalities.

What Happened Today in History

Understand the significance of what happened today in history and learn how to make a difference:

1. French Revolution Begins with the Storming of the Bastille in 1789:

On July 14, 1789, revolutionaries stormed the Bastille prison in Paris, marking the start of the French Revolution. This pivotal event led to the fall of the monarchy and the rise of the French Republic, forever changing the course of French history. Bastille Day is now celebrated annually in France as a symbol of liberty and the fight against oppression.

2. United States Establishes the National Park Service in 1916:

The United States took a significant step toward environmental conservation on July 14, 1916, by establishing the National Park Service. This agency was created to protect and manage the nation’s national parks and monuments, ensuring their preservation for future generations. The National Park Service continues to safeguard America’s natural and cultural heritage.

3. John Logie Baird Demonstrates Color Television Publicly for the First Time in 1928:

On July 14, 1928, Scottish inventor John Logie Baird gave the first public demonstration of colour television in London. This groundbreaking event showcased the potential of television technology, setting the stage for the future of broadcasting and entertainment.

4. NASA’s Mars Pathfinder Mission Successfully Lands Sojourner Rover on Mars in 1997:

July 14, 1997, marked a milestone in space exploration as NASA’s Mars Pathfinder mission deployed the Sojourner rover on the Martian surface. The mission provided invaluable data about Mars’ terrain and climate and demonstrated new technologies for future planetary exploration.

5. NASA’s New Horizons Spacecraft Completes Historic Flyby of Pluto in 2015:
On July 14, 2015, NASA’s New Horizons spacecraft made a historic close approach to Pluto, capturing unprecedented images and data of the distant dwarf planet. This mission revealed Pluto’s complex geology and expanded our understanding of the outer reaches of the solar system, sparking renewed interest in space exploration.
