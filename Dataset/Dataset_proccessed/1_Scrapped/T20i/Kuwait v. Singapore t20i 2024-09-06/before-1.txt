National News Headlines in English for 5 September 2024

National news headlines in Englisht will cover the most important national news in India, focusing on government announcements, economic updates, and major events shaping the country today.

1. Prime Minister Narendra Modi Engages in Key Talks with Brunei’s Sultan to Strengthen Bilateral Relations
Prime Minister Narendra Modi met with Sultan Haji Hassanal Bolkiah of Brunei in Bandar Seri Begawan today. The discussions aimed to bolster India-Brunei ties by expanding trade, commercial links, and cultural exchanges. Both leaders agreed to enhance their partnership, focusing on areas like defence, trade, energy, and space technology, while also addressing regional and global issues of shared interest.

2. India to Host the Second International Conference on Green Hydrogen from September 11-13 in New Delhi
India will hold its second International Conference on Green Hydrogen in New Delhi from September 11 to 13. New and Renewable Energy Minister Pralhad Joshi highlighted the event’s goal to advance green hydrogen technologies, foster global scientific and industrial connections, and support India’s shift toward clean energy.

3. World Bank Predicts Positive Growth for India Amidst Global Challenges
The World Bank forecasts a 7% growth for India in the financial year 2024-25, despite global uncertainties. The growth is expected to continue strongly through 2026-27. The Bank also projects a decrease in the debt-to-GDP ratio and a stable current account deficit, noting that India remains the fastest-growing major economy, driven by infrastructure investments and increased real estate activity.

4. Lab Mitra’ Initiative in Varanasi Receives National e-Governance Gold Award for Innovative Reforms
The ‘Lab Mitra’ initiative, launched in Varanasi, has won the National e-Governance Gold Award for its innovative approach to administrative reforms and e-governance. The program, which provides lab services at 20 hospitals and has served over 2.5 lakh patients, is now being expanded throughout Uttar Pradesh.

5. TRAI Takes Action Against Spam Calls with Disconnections and Service Blocks
The Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) has disconnected 2.75 lakh telephone numbers and blocked services to 50 entities as part of a crackdown on spam calls. TRAI’s measures follow a surge in complaints about unregistered telemarketers, aiming to reduce spam by enforcing strict regulations on promotional calls from unregistered sources.

6. Himachal Congress Government Criticized for Financial Mismanagement Amidst Alliance Talks
The BJP has criticized the Congress government in Himachal Pradesh, accusing it of leading the state into financial ruin with broken promises. The BJP likened the government to a “leaky boat,” suggesting that its mismanagement has plunged the state and its people into financial despair. Meanwhile, Congress is negotiating alliances with AAP and other parties for the Haryana Assembly elections, following Rahul Gandhi’s push to advance the I.N.D.I.A initiative by accommodating potential allies.

7. Deadline Approaches for Padma Awards 2025 Nominations
The Union Home Ministry has announced that the deadline for nominations and recommendations for the Padma Awards 2025 is September 15. Nominations can be made by anyone, including self-nominations, via the Rashtriya Puraskar portal. The awards, which include Padma Vibhushan, Padma Bhushan, and Padma Shri, are among the highest civilian honors in India and recognize exceptional contributions across various fields.

8. President Droupadi Murmu to Present National Teachers Award 2024
On September 5, 2024, President Droupadi Murmu will award the National Teachers Award 2024 to 50 educators at Vigyan Bhavan, New Delhi. The awards recognize outstanding educators from colleges, universities, and polytechnics who have significantly impacted their students’ lives. Each recipient will receive a silver medal and a certificate of appreciation. The selection process involves a preliminary search committee and a final jury review.

9. EBI Faces Criticism for Approving ICICI Bank and ICICI Securities Merger
SEBI has come under scrutiny for approving the merger between ICICI Bank and ICICI Securities. The decision, which bypassed the usual price discovery process meant to protect minority shareholders, has led to legal challenges and raised concerns about the regulator’s impartiality.

10. Premji Invest Plans $10-$15 Million Investment in AI Startup Galileo
Premji Invest, led by Wipro founder Azim Premji, is preparing to invest $10-$15 million in San Francisco-based AI startup Galileo. This funding will be part of Galileo’s Series B round, aimed at enhancing machine learning models.

1. Kerala MLA P V Anvar Files Complaint Against Top Officials
In Kerala, LDF MLA P V Anvar has stirred political controversy by submitting a complaint against Chief Minister’s political secretary and senior IPS officers to CPI(M) state secretary M V Govindan. Anvar’s complaint follows earlier submissions to Chief Minister Pinarayi Vijayan and highlights issues involving close aides of the CM. Andhra Pradesh Chief Minister N Chandrababu Naidu has assessed the ongoing flood relief operations with state ministers and district collectors. He instructed officials to undertake sanitation work to remove mud and debris from flood-affected homes. The state government will assist in rebuilding damaged houses and expedite insurance claims. Medical camps are set up to prevent disease outbreaks, and a comprehensive damage report will be submitted to the central government for further financial aid.

Aparna Yadav, BJP leader and daughter-in-law of Samajwadi Party founder Mulayam Singh Yadav, has been appointed Vice-Chairperson of the Uttar Pradesh Women Commission. The UP government has also appointed Babita Chauhan as Chairperson and Charu Chaudhary as another Vice-Chairperson. The new leadership will serve a one-year term. Aparna Yadav joined the BJP in January 2022.

4. DRDO Unveils New 30mm High Explosive Preformed Fragmentation Shell
The Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) has delivered the production documentation for the new 30mm High Explosive Preformed Fragmentation (HEPF) shell to the Director General of Naval Armament Inspection in Pune. Developed by DRDO’s Armament Research and Development Establishment (ARDE), these shells are designed for AK-630 naval guns and offer enhanced fragmentation capability to counter drone threats at sea.

5. UP Government Extends Deadline for Asset Declaration by Employees to September 30
The Uttar Pradesh government has extended the deadline for government employees to declare their movable and immovable assets by a month. Employees now have until September 30 to submit the details on the human resource portal. Earlier, the government had warned that salaries for August would be withheld for employees who failed to update their asset details by the original August 31 deadline.
International News in English for 5 September 2024

International news in English section will bring students up to speed with major international events, global political changes, and important updates from around the world.

1. Nepal Rashtra Bank to Issue New Banknotes Featuring Revised Map
Nepal Rashtra Bank is preparing to release new banknotes featuring a revised map that includes territories disputed with India. The updated banknotes will incorporate Indian regions of Kalapani, Lipulekh, and Limpiyadhura as part of Nepal, with production expected to take between six months and a year to complete. The decision to update the currency was made by Nepal’s cabinet under former Prime Minister Pushpa Kamal Dahal ‘Prachanda’ on May 3. 

2. Egyptian President to Meet Turkish Counterpart in Major Diplomatic Visit
Egyptian President Abdel Fattah al-Sisi is set to meet Turkish President Tayyip Erdogan in Turkey on Wednesday, marking the first presidential-level visit between the two countries in 12 years. This visit follows Erdogan’s trip to Cairo in February, which was his first to Egypt since 2012, signaling a thaw in previously strained relations. The two leaders will discuss the state of Turkey-Egypt relations and explore potential joint initiatives. 

3. 8,000 Rohingya Refugees Flee Myanmar to Bangladesh Amid Escalating Violence
Approximately 8,000 Rohingya Muslims have recently fled to Bangladesh due to intensifying violence in Myanmar’s Rakhine state. The conflict between Myanmar’s ruling junta and the Arakan Army, an ethnic militia, has exacerbated the situation. Mohammad Shamsud Douza, a senior official overseeing refugees in Bangladesh, confirmed the influx of Rohingya refugees over the past two months. 

4. World Bank Upgrades India’s Economic Growth Forecast to 7% for 2024-25
The World Bank has revised its growth forecast for India’s financial year 2024-25 to 7%, up from the earlier estimate of 6.6%. This adjustment reflects India’s impressive economic performance, positioning it as the fastest-growing major economy worldwide. The Indian economy achieved an 8.2% growth rate in the financial year 2023-24, driven by substantial public infrastructure investments and increased household real estate investments. 

5. Nvidia’s Stock Falls Dramatically Amid DOJ Antitrust Investigation
Nvidia’s stock has dropped significantly, erasing nearly $279 billion from its market capitalization, following a report of an antitrust investigation by the Department of Justice (DOJ). The probe focuses on allegations that Nvidia might be obstructing customers from switching to alternative AI chip suppliers. Although the investigation has not yet resulted in formal charges, it has caused considerable disruption in the tech industry.
Sports News in English for 5 September 2024

Sports news in English keeps you informed on the latest sports updates, including key matches, records, and performances in cricket, football, and other major sports events happening today.

1. Leicester City Wins Appeal Against Premier League’s Profit and Sustainability Decision
Leicester City has successfully appealed a decision regarding an alleged breach of the Premier League’s profit and sustainability rules, avoiding a potential points deduction. The Premier League expressed surprise and disappointment at the appeal board’s ruling, which found that Leicester’s accounting period ended on June 30, 2023, after their relegation. The decision means that the league cannot take action against Leicester for exceeding the allowed loss threshold during their time in the Premier League from the 2019/20 to 2022/23 seasons.

2. FIA Confirms Compliance of McLaren and Mercedes with Regulations Amid Flexing Wing Concerns
The FIA has addressed concerns over flexing front wings on McLaren and Mercedes cars, affirming that all teams comply with the regulations. Following the Italian Grand Prix, where Ferrari won with a strategic tire choice and McLaren dominated the front row in qualifying, questions were raised by Ferrari and Red Bull. Red Bull’s Helmut Marko suggested a review of the front wings.

3. Indian Paralympians Celebrate Medal Wins at Paris 2024
India’s Paralympic athletes have been celebrated by the President, Vice President, and Prime Minister for their impressive performances at the Paris 2024 Paralympics. President Droupadi Murmu congratulated Sharad Kumar and Mariyappan Thangavelu for winning silver and bronze in men’s high jump, noting their continued excellence. She also praised Ajeet Singh and Sundar Singh Gurjar for their silver and bronze in men’s javelin throw, highlighting their contributions to India’s strong performance in the event.

4. Deepthi Jeevanji Wins Bronze in Women’s 400m T20 at Paris Paralympics
India’s Deepthi Jeevanji earned a bronze medal in the women’s 400m T20 final at the Paris Paralympics, finishing with a time of 55.82 seconds. She was edged out by Ukraine’s Yuliia Shuliar and Turkey’s Aysel Onder, who clocked 55.16 and 55.23 seconds, respectively. Despite not achieving her best time, Jeevanji’s performance is notable as she entered the Games with high expectations following her world-record-setting run.

5. India Sets Record at Paris Paralympics with Five Medals in One Day
India’s Paralympic team marked a historic achievement by securing five medals on a single day at the Paris 2024 Paralympics, surpassing their previous best of 19 medals from Tokyo 2020. This impressive performance, including four medals in para athletics within a short span, has already made Paris 2024 the most successful Paralympics for India.

1. SEBI Faces Scrutiny Over ICICI Bank and ICICI Securities Merger
The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) is under intense scrutiny following its approval of the merger between ICICI Bank and ICICI Securities. This decision, which bypassed the usual price discovery process, has sparked criticism and legal challenges. SEBI’s move allowed ICICI Bank, which already owned 75% of ICICI Securities, to delist the brokerage without the standard safeguards for minority shareholders.

2. CapitaLand Investment Plans to Double Funds in India

Singapore’s CapitaLand Investment has announced plans to more than double its funds under management (FUM) in India by 2028, aiming to grow from S$7.4 billion ($5.66 billion) as of June 30. This expansion supports the company’s global goal of reaching S$200 billion in FUM by 2028. Group CEO Lee Chee Koon highlighted India’s appeal to global corporations and institutional investors and noted potential opportunities in renewable energy and real estate private credit.

3. Infosys Delays Joining Dates for Campus Hires
Infosys has issued final offer letters to over 2,000 freshers from the 2022 batch, but around 700 campus hires are still waiting for their joining dates. These candidates, who were selected through the “HackWithInfy” competition, have faced delays due to an industry slump and fears of a recession. 

4. India’s Services Sector Sees Strong Growth in the 5 Months
India’s services sector experienced its fastest growth in five months in August, with the HSBC final India Services Purchasing Managers’ Index rising to 60.9 from July’s 60.3. This increase reflects resilient demand and easing inflationary pressures. The index has consistently stayed above the 50-mark, indicating growth, and reached its highest level since March. 

5. Sebi May Ease Registration for Foreign Investors in Government Bonds
The Securities and Exchange Board of India (Sebi) is considering simplifying the registration process for foreign investors in government bonds, following significant inflows of Rs 23,507 crore in August.
Science and Technology News for 5 September 2024

Discover the latest advancements and breakthrough in Science and Technology, including new inventions, research developments and innovations shaping the future.

1. Intel Introduces New AI Processors to Challenge Qualcomm and AMD
Intel is launching its Lunar Lake or Core Ultra processors, which are designed to offer superior battery life and AI performance compared to Qualcomm’s Snapdragon X Elite series and AMD’s new HX lineup. The processors, available in new laptops by the end of this month, are claimed to provide up to 14 hours of battery life during office work, which is five hours longer than Qualcomm’s current offerings.

2. Apple to Upgrade All iPhones to OLED Screens by 2025
Starting in 2025, Apple will equip all iPhone models with OLED screens, replacing the current LCD screens used in some budget models like the iPhone SE. OLED screens, known for their bright colors and sharp images, do not require a backlight and are thinner and more energy-efficient than LCD screens. This change will end Apple’s reliance on Japanese screen manufacturers Japan Display (JDI) and Sharp for iPhone displays.

3. WHO Study Finds No Cancer Link to Mobile Phone Use
A large study by the World Health Organization (WHO) and New Zealand’s Ministry of Health has found no evidence linking mobile phone use to brain or neck cancers. The study reviewed 64 observational studies from 1994 to 2022 and concluded that mobile phones, which emit non-ionizing radiation, do not increase cancer risk. This finding addresses long-standing concerns about the potential health effects of mobile phone radiation.

4. Brazil Suspends Access to X (formerly Twitter) Due to Legal Dispute
Brazil’s Supreme Court has ordered the suspension of X, previously known as Twitter, following Elon Musk’s failure to appoint a new legal representative in an ongoing case. As a result, millions of Brazilian users were unable to access the platform. The court’s decision led internet providers and mobile companies in Brazil to enforce the suspension.

5. Garena Free Fire MAX Thrills Players with Daily Redeem Codes and Stunning Gameplay
Garena Free Fire MAX captivates players with its dynamic gameplay, stunning visuals, and regular updates. Daily redeem codes offer exclusive in-game rewards like skins, weapons, and diamonds, enhancing excitement and player engagement.

What Happened Today in History

Explore important what happened today in history on this date in history. The section will help students understand the importance of past events and their impact on the present.


1. 1960: First Meeting of OPEC
On September 5, 1960, the Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC) held its inaugural meeting in Baghdad, Iraq. Founded by Iran, Iraq, Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, and Venezuela, OPEC aimed to coordinate and unify petroleum policies among member countries. This meeting marked the beginning of OPEC’s role in stabilizing oil prices and influencing global oil markets.

2. 1972: Munich Olympics Tragedy
On September 5, 1972, the Munich Olympics suffered a tragic event when a group of Palestinian terrorists known as Black September took eleven members of the Israeli Olympic team hostage. The standoff ended in violence, with all hostages being killed. This tragic event highlighted the dangers of political extremism and had a significant impact on international security at sports events.

3. 1997: Mother Teresa’s Death
Mother Teresa, the renowned Catholic nun and missionary known for her humanitarian work, passed away on September 5, 1997. She founded the Missionaries of Charity and dedicated her life to serving the poor and sick. Her death was widely mourned, and she was later canonized as a saint by the Catholic Church in 2016.

4. 2001: The Launch of iTunes
On September 5, 2001, Apple launched iTunes, a digital media player and store. This innovation transformed the music industry by allowing users to buy and download music online. iTunes played a crucial role in popularizing digital music and contributed to Apple’s growth as a major technology company.

5. 2014: Nobel Prize Winner Malala Yousafzai’s Return to School
On September 5, 2014, Malala Yousafzai, the Pakistani activist for female education and the youngest-ever Nobel Prize laureate, returned to school in Birmingham, England, after recovering from an assassination attempt by the Taliban. Her return symbolized her commitment to advocating for girls’ education worldwide.
