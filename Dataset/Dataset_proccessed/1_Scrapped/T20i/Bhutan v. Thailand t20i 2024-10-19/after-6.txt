1. Khalistani Terrorist Issues Warning Against Air India Flights This November
Gurpatwant Singh Pannun, a Khalistani terrorist, has issued a warning against flying Air India between November 1 and 19, claiming potential attacks are linked to the “40th anniversary of the Sikh genocide.” In response, airlines are reviewing their security measures following previous threats and hoaxes.

2. Kerala Government Opposes Centre’s New Fireworks Regulations for Thrissur Pooram Festival
The Kerala government has criticized a new gazette notification from the Centre that restricts fireworks during the Thrissur Pooram festival. Revenue Minister K Rajan expressed concerns that these conditions could negatively impact the festival’s traditional celebrations.

3. Germany to Offer 90,000 Labor Visas to Skilled Indian Workers Amid Workforce Shortage
Germany is set to issue 90,000 labor visas to skilled Indian workers to combat its workforce shortage. This initiative, approved by Chancellor Olaf Scholz’s Cabinet, targets key sectors such as IT, healthcare, and engineering, in response to increasing demand for skilled labor.

4. Assam Launches Mission Basundhara 3.0 to Grant Land Rights to Indigenous People
Assam’s Chief Minister Himanta Biswa Sarma inaugurated Mission Basundhara 3.0, aiming to secure land rights for indigenous communities. The initiative also plans to digitize land-related services and publish maps, enhancing accessibility and transparency for residents.

5. Deonar Dumping Ground in Mumbai Faces Health Risks Due to Pollution Concerns
The Deonar dumping ground, one of Mumbai’s oldest landfills, poses severe pollution threats, particularly to lower-income residents. Toxic gases and particulate matter emitted from the site significantly increase health risks among the local population.

6. Israel Explores New Proposals for Hostage Release Negotiations in Gaza
Israel is reportedly formulating new strategies for negotiating the release of hostages held in Gaza. Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu discussed these ideas during a high-level security Cabinet meeting, indicating that the feasibility of the proposals is under evaluation.

7. Heart-Stopping Finish in ICC Women’s T20 World Cup Final: Australia Defeats New Zealand
In a thrilling ICC Women’s T20 World Cup final, Australia clinched victory over New Zealand after Ellyse Perry’s last-minute save. New Zealand needed five runs off the final ball, but Perry’s crucial stop secured Australia’s championship win.

8. Liverpool Tops Premier League Standings After Thrilling Victory Against Chelsea
Liverpool emerged victorious against Chelsea with a 2-1 win, thanks to Curtis Jones’ decisive goal. This victory marks Liverpool’s 10th win in 11 matches under new manager Arne Slot, solidifying their position at the top of the Premier League.

9. Mumbai University Reschedules Exams Due to Assembly Elections
The University of Mumbai has announced rescheduled exams for online and distance learning courses due to the upcoming Assembly elections. Exams originally set for November 19 and 20 will now take place on November 30 and December 7, while exam timings and venues will remain unchanged.

10. The two-year programme has an annual tuition fee of Rs 45,275, and the entrance exam is scheduled for November 3.

1. Allahabad High Court Grants 15-Day Extension for Responses to Demolition Notices in Bahraich
The Allahabad High Court has granted a 15-day extension for residents facing demolition notices for illegal structures along the Kundasar-Mahasi-Nanpara-Maharajganj road in Bahraich. Affected individuals can now file responses by November 4, with state authorities required to issue reasoned decisions thereafter.

2. Dramatic Rise in High-Income Tax Filers in India Revealed in Latest Report
The number of individuals with taxable incomes over ₹1 crore has soared to nearly 230,000 in FY23, a fivefold increase from 44,078 in 2013-14. The overall taxpayer count has also risen from 3.3 crore to 7.5 crore, indicating a growing wealth gap in the country.

3. EPFO Reports Significant Increase in Membership, Highlighting Job Growth
In August 2023, the Employees’ Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO) added 18.53 lakh net members, with approximately 9.3 lakh new enrollments. This rise, including 2.53 lakh women, reflects increased employment opportunities and awareness of employee benefits across the country.

4. Launch of eShram Platform Aims to Support Unorganized Workers Nationwide
Labour Minister Dr. Mansukh Mandaviya launched the eShram platform in New Delhi, aimed at providing unorganized workers access to government schemes. This initiative consolidates information on social security and welfare schemes, facilitating better awareness and support for this workforce.

5. Khalistani Terrorist Issues Warning Against Air India Flights This November
Gurpatwant Singh Pannun, a Khalistani terrorist, has warned against flying Air India from November 1 to 19, citing potential attacks linked to the “40th anniversary of the Sikh genocide.” This follows previous threats, with airlines reviewing security measures in light of hoaxes.

6. Kerala Government Opposes Centre’s New Fireworks Regulations for Thrissur Pooram Festival
The Kerala government criticized a recent gazette notification from the Centre restricting fireworks for the Thrissur Pooram festival. State Revenue Minister K Rajan expressed disappointment, stating that the conditions could harm the festival’s traditional celebrations.

7. Germany to Offer 90,000 Labor Visas to Skilled Indian Workers Amid Workforce Shortage
Germany plans to issue 90,000 labor visas to skilled Indian workers to address its workforce shortage. This initiative was approved by Chancellor Olaf Scholz’s Cabinet, targeting sectors like IT, healthcare, and engineering due to the increasing demand for skilled labor.

8. UDAN Scheme Successfully Operates 601 Routes to Enhance Regional Air Connectivity
As part of the UDAN scheme, 601 routes and 71 airports have been operationalized, enhancing regional air connectivity. The initiative aims to make air travel more affordable and accessible, particularly serving remote areas across the country.

9. New Leadership Appointed for NAFED Following Government Reorganization
The Appointments Committee of the Cabinet has assigned the additional charge of Managing Director of NAFED to an official from the Ministry of Cooperation. This decision follows a previous assignment by Union Agriculture Minister Shivraj Singh Chouhan, marking a significant administrative shift.

10. Key Appointment Changes at NAFED as New Managing Director Takes Charge
The Appointments Committee of the Cabinet has appointed a new official as Managing Director of NAFED, following recent changes in leadership. Chaired by Prime Minister Narendra Modi, this committee oversees key government appointments and organizational leadership.
State News Headlines in English for 22 October 2024

Today’s state news highlights significant announcements from local governments across various Indian states.

1. Odisha Prepares for Severe Cyclonic Storm ‘Dana’ Expected to Make Landfall Thursday
Odisha is on high alert as severe cyclonic storm ‘Dana’ approaches its coast, likely to make landfall on Thursday morning. The state government has canceled leave for all officers and staff, urging vigilance as the Indian Meteorological Department predicts wind speeds of 120 km/h.

2. Telangana CM Aims to Transform State into Trillion-Dollar Economy with Global Investments
Telangana Chief Minister A Revanth Reddy announced his vision to turn Telangana into a trillion-dollar economy, positioning Hyderabad as a global investment hub. Speaking at the Indian School of Business summit, he emphasized the need for the city to compete with major global metropolises.

3. Assam Launches Mission Basundhara 3.0 to Grant Land Rights to Indigenous People
Assam Chief Minister Himanta Biswa Sarma inaugurated Mission Basundhara 3.0 in Guwahati, aiming to secure land rights for indigenous communities. The initiative will also digitize land-related services and publish maps for residents, enhancing accessibility and transparency.

4. Deonar Dumping Ground in Mumbai Faces Health Risks Due to Pollution Concerns
The Deonar dumping ground, one of Mumbai’s oldest landfills, continues to pose severe pollution threats, particularly to lower-income residents. The site emits toxic gases and particulate matter, significantly increasing health risks among the local population.

5. RML Hospital Launches Pollution-Related Illness Clinic Amid Delhi’s Air Quality Crisis
Ram Manohar Lohia (RML) Hospital will open a special weekly clinic for pollution-related illnesses starting tomorrow, responding to deteriorating air quality in Delhi-NCR. The clinic will operate every Monday, offering assessments from specialists in various medical fields, including respiratory and psychiatric care.
International News in English for 22 October 2024

Stay updated with the latest international news. This section focuses on major global stories and developments that are shaping our world today, covering political events, economic changes, and social issues affecting various nations.

1. Japan’s Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba Faces Declining Approval Ratings Ahead of General Election
Approval ratings for Japan’s newly appointed Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba have dropped ahead of the general election on October 27. Recent polls indicate that the ruling coalition may struggle to secure a majority, raising concerns for the Liberal Democratic Party (LDP), which has dominated Japanese politics for decades.

2. Israel Explores New Proposals for Hostage Release Negotiations in Gaza
Israel is reportedly formulating new strategies for negotiating the release of hostages in Gaza, with Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu discussing these ideas during a high-level security Cabinet meeting. According to a statement from Netanyahu’s spokesperson, the feasibility of these proposals is currently under evaluation.

3. Germany Aims to Address Workforce Shortages by Increasing Labor Visas for Skilled Indian Workers
Facing a significant workforce shortage, Germany plans to boost labor visas for Indian workers to 90,000, as revealed by a report from Mint. On October 16, Chancellor Olaf Scholz’s Cabinet approved a draft to streamline the migration process, addressing high demand in sectors such as IT, healthcare, and engineering.

4. Russian President Vladimir Putin Set to Meet Global Leaders at BRICS Summit Amid Controversy
In the coming days, Russian President Vladimir Putin will meet with several world leaders, including China’s Xi Jinping, India’s Narendra Modi, and Turkey’s Recep Tayyip Erdogan, in Kazan for a BRICS summit. This gathering challenges predictions that Putin would become isolated due to the ongoing war in Ukraine and an international arrest warrant against him.

5. China Cuts Benchmark Lending Rates to Stimulate Economic Growth Amid Economic Challenges
China has reduced its benchmark lending rates as part of a series of stimulus measures to revive its economy. The one-year loan prime rate (LPR) was cut by 25 basis points to 3.10%, while the five-year LPR also saw a reduction to 3.6%. These changes follow previous rate cuts and are expected to further support economic activity.

Britain’s six-time Olympic track cycling champion Chris Hoy has disclosed that he has “two to four years” to live after being diagnosed with terminal prostate cancer that has metastasized to his bones. Education News Today for School Assembly

Today’s education news brings attention to important advancements and initiatives within the educational sector.

1. UP DElEd Registration Deadline Extended: New Date is October 22
The Uttar Pradesh Examination Regulatory Authority has extended the registration deadline for the 2024 Diploma in Elementary Education (UP DElEd) programme to October 22, pushing it back from the original deadline of October 9. Candidates can pay the application fee until October 23 and print their forms until October 25. Registration can be completed via the official website, updeled.gov.in, with fees set at Rs 700 for General/OBC, Rs 500 for SC/ST, and Rs 200 for PwD candidates.

2. Union Health Minister Commits to Maintaining AIIMS Standards
Union Health Minister J P Nadda has pledged to uphold the teaching and faculty standards at all newly established AIIMS across the country, emphasizing the importance of protecting the institute’s brand. Speaking at the BJMFCON 2024, Nadda highlighted that while AIIMS-Delhi was established in the ’60s, it became a prominent name only in the ’80s, indicating the necessity of maintaining high educational standards.

3. Mumbai University Reschedules Exams Due to Assembly Elections
In light of the upcoming Assembly elections in Maharashtra, the University of Mumbai has announced a rescheduling of certain exams for online and distance learning courses. All exams originally slated for November 19 and 20 will now be held on November 30 and December 7, respectively. The exam timings and venues will remain unchanged.

4. Jamia Millia Islamia Launches New MTech Programme in Environmental Health
Jamia Millia Islamia has introduced an MTech programme in Environmental Health, Risk, and Safety Management, with applications open until October 27. The two-year programme, consisting of four semesters, has an annual tuition fee of Rs 45,275. The entrance exam will take place on November 3, and applicants must fill out a separate form available on the JMI website, jmicoe.in.

5. NEET PG 2024 Counselling Schedule to Be Released Soon
The Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) is anticipated to release the NEET PG 2024 Counselling schedule shortly. Once available, it will be posted on the MCC’s official website, MCC.nic.in. The registration process for all-India quota PG admissions has already begun, but candidates are awaiting the detailed schedule.
Business News Today Headlines

Let’s dive into today’s business news headlines. This section will cover significant updates in the business world, including market trends, company announcements, and economic developments.

1. New Unified Securities Markets Code to Decriminalise Financial Regulations
The government is drafting the Unified Securities Markets Code (SMC), which aims to decriminalise several provisions within key financial market laws. This code will consolidate and replace the SEBI Act of 1992, the Depositories Act of 1996, the Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act of 1956, and the Government Securities Act of 2007.

2. India Targets High-Value Tourism to Establish Global Status
Commerce and Industry Minister Piyush Goyal highlighted the importance of attracting high-value tourists to position India as an international tourist destination. Speaking at the Indian Chamber of Commerce Annual Plenary Session on October 18, he noted that the government is focusing on infrastructure and connectivity improvements.

3. India’s Renewable Energy Target of 500 GW May Boost Solar Imports to $30 Billion
India’s ambitious goal of installing 500 GW of renewable energy capacity by 2030 is projected to increase annual solar equipment imports to around $30 billion, primarily due to reliance on Chinese products, as stated by the Global Trade Research Initiative (GTRI). 

4. GST Exemption Proposed for Health Insurance Premiums for Seniors and Life Insurance
A ministerial panel has proposed exempting the Goods and Services Tax (GST) on health insurance premiums for senior citizens and term life insurance premiums, garnering widespread support. Additionally, the Group of Ministers (GoM) on rate rationalisation is considering a GST adjustment for various products, including proposed cuts in tax rates for packaged water over 20 litres, bicycles under Rs 10,000, and exercise notebooks.

5. India’s Data Speeds Plummet Despite Rapid 5G Rollout
India has experienced a sharp decline in average median data speeds, falling from 12th to 26th place among 112 countries, according to Ookla Speedtest. This decline comes despite India having one of the fastest 5G rollouts and being the highest consumer of data globally. Overall median download speeds dropped by 15% from 107.03 megabits per second (Mbps) in April-June to 91.7 Mbps in July-September, the lowest since October 2023. Upload speeds also decreased by 11% in the same period.

What Happened Today in History

The What Happened Today in History segment in the school assembly news headlines highlights significant events from history that occurred on this date, giving students insight into important moments that have shaped the world.

1. 1962: Cuban Missile Crisis Begins
The world teetered on the brink of nuclear war when the Cuban Missile Crisis erupted on October 22, 1962. U.S. President John F. Kennedy addressed the nation, announcing the discovery of Soviet missiles in Cuba, leading to a tense standoff between the U.S. and the Soviet Union.

2. 1797: The First Parachute Jump
André-Jacques Garnerin made the first successful parachute descent from a hot-air balloon on October 22, 1797, in Paris. His daring jump from 3,200 feet was a historic step in the field of aeronautics.

3. 1879: Thomas Edison Tests the First Electric Lightbulb
On October 22, 1879, Thomas Edison tested his first successful long-lasting electric lightbulb. This invention revolutionized the way people lived and worked, paving the way for the widespread use of electricity.
4. 1964: Jean-Paul Sartre Declines the Nobel Prize
French philosopher and writer Jean-Paul Sartre famously declined the Nobel Prize for Literature on October 22, 1964. Sartre believed that accepting the prize would compromise his integrity and independence as a writer.

5. 1999: Maurice Papon Sent to Prison
On October 22, 1999, Maurice Papon, a former Vichy official, was sent to prison for his role in deporting French Jews during World War II. His conviction symbolized France’s reckoning with its wartime past.
