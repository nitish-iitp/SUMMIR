Match Preview

In the fourth match of the Nigeria tour of Kenya series, Kenya put up a total of 144 runs for the loss of 8 wickets in their allotted 20 overs. Despite their efforts, Nigeria responded strongly, achieving 158 runs for the loss of 5 wickets within the same number of overs. This performance ensured Nigeria’s victory by a margin of 14 runs, highlighting their effective chase.

Nigeria’s innings was anchored by wicketkeeper Sulaimon Runsewe, who scored a steady 60 runs off 51 balls. He was well-supported by Selim Salau, who contributed 42 runs from 37 deliveries, and Isaac Danladi, who added a quick 18 off 15 balls. On the bowling front for Kenya, captain Rakep Patel made a notable impact with 2 wickets for 13 runs in his 2 overs. Additionally, Francis Muia Mutua took 2 wickets, conceding 28 runs in his 3 overs, and Gerard Muthui claimed 1 wicket for 28 runs in his full 4 overs, striving to contain Nigeria’s batting lineup.

Kenya’s batting efforts were led by Sachin Bhudia, who top-scored with 38 runs off 29 balls, and wicketkeeper Sukhdeep Singh, who made a significant contribution with 37 runs from 29 deliveries. Shem Ngoche added a steady 17 runs off 17 balls. Nigeria’s bowling attack was spearheaded by Mohammed Abdulmumuni Taiwo, who took 2 wickets for 15 runs in his 4 overs. Captain Sylvester Okpe also performed well, claiming 2 wickets for 19 runs in 2 overs, while Chiemelie-Emmanuel Udekwe added 2 wickets for 28 runs in his 4 overs, effectively restricting Kenya’s total and securing the win for Nigeria.
