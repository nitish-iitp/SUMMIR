National News Headlines in English for 22 July 2024

Stay updated with 22 July 2024 news headlines in English for school assembly. Learn about various sectors´ happenings in our country. 

1. Ola CEO Urges Stronger Data Rules in India After Microsoft Outage:

Ola CEO Bhavish Aggarwal urges India to adopt stricter data localization after a global Microsoft outage, stressing that 80% of India’s data stored abroad makes the country vulnerable to international IT disruptions.

2. 373 Indian Students Return from Bangladesh Amid Violent Protests:

The BSF reported that 373 Indian and three Nepali students returned to India from Bangladesh due to violent anti-quota protests. Most students crossed via the Srimantpur and Akhaura Integrated Check Posts in Tripura. Another 300 students are expected to return today.

3. Government Convenes All-Party Meeting for Budget Session Cooperation:

The government held an all-party meeting in Delhi to ensure smooth functioning of the Budget Session. Union Ministers and leaders from various political parties, including Congress, NCP, DMK, and AIMIM, attended the meeting, seeking cooperation for both Houses of Parliament.

4. PM Modi to Open 46th World Heritage Committee Session in Delhi:

Prime Minister Narendra Modi will inaugurate the 46th Session of the World Heritage Committee in New Delhi. This is the first time India is hosting the event, which will discuss new site nominations, conservation reports, and utilization of World Heritage Funds until July 31st.

5. India Secures Fourth Place in International Maths Olympiad with Four Golds:

Prime Minister Narendra Modi celebrated India’s fourth-place finish in the International Maths Olympiad, the country’s best performance yet. The Indian contingent won four Gold and one Silver Medal, inspiring young mathematicians and boosting the popularity of mathematics in India.

6. Kerala Boy Dies of Nipah Virus, Health Officials Urge Vigilance:

A 14-year-old boy from Malappuram, Kerala, succumbed to Nipah virus infection at Kozhikode Medical College Hospital. Despite being on ventilator support, he passed away due to cardiac arrest and internal bleeding. Health officials have implemented strict measures and urged the public to stay vigilant.

6. EPFO Records Highest Monthly Member Addition in May:

EPFO added 19.50 lakh net members in May, the highest since April 2018. Around 9.85 lakh new members enrolled, and 14 lakh re-joined. The Ministry of Labour attributes this surge to increased job opportunities, better awareness, and effective outreach programs.

7. Guru Purnima Celebrated with Reverence Across India:

Guru Purnima, dedicated to spiritual and academic teachers, is celebrated today. Followers remember Lord Buddha’s first sermon on this day. Prime Minister Narendra Modi extended greetings to the nation, acknowledging the wisdom and guidance of Gurus in shaping lives.

8. Health Officials Review AES and Chandipura Virus in Gujarat, Rajasthan, and MP:

DGHS Atul Goel and experts reviewed AES and Chandipura virus cases in Gujarat, Rajasthan, and Madhya Pradesh. They concluded that infectious agents contribute minimally to AES. A multidisciplinary central team will investigate the AES cases in Gujarat comprehensively.

9. Defence Minister Urges MPs to Avoid Interruptions During Speeches:

Defence Minister Rajnath Singh urged MPs to refrain from interruptions during parliamentary speeches, emphasizing the importance of respect for democracy. Parliamentary Affairs Minister Kiren Rijiju highlighted the need for cooperation from both government and opposition for smooth parliamentary sessions.

10. Baba Ramdev Supports UP Government’s Kanwar Yatra Order:

Yog Guru Baba Ramdev supported the UP government’s Kanwar Yatra order for shop owners to display their names, asserting there is no need to hide one’s identity. He emphasized that purity in work matters more than religious or community identity.

State News Headlines in English for 22 July 2024

Stay updated with today’s news headlines in English for school assembly, including state news headlines and impactful stories unfolding across your state.

Karnataka:

1. Bengaluru IT Firms Request 14-Hour Workdays, State Considers Law Update:

Bengaluru IT companies are asking the state government to extend daily working hours to 14, including 12 hours of regular work and 2 hours of overtime. The request aligns with a previous controversial suggestion by Infosys co-founder Narayana Murthy for longer work weeks.

Maharashtra:

2. BJP’s Bawankule Dares MVA to Promise Maratha Reservation from OBC Quota:

BJP Maharashtra chief Chandrashekhar Bawankule challenged the Maha Vikas Aghadi (MVA) to pledge Maratha reservation from the existing OBC quota. Amid ongoing tensions, he criticized former CM Uddhav Thackeray for failing to maintain Maratha reservations and accused MVA of planning to scrap current welfare schemes.

Telangana:

3. Telangana Government Honors Writer Jukanti Jagannatham:

The Telangana government nominated writer and poet Jukanti Jagannatham for the Sri Dasarathi Krishnamacharya Award, including a cash prize of one lakh rupees. Chief Minister A Revanth Reddy congratulated Jagannatham on this honor, acknowledging his literary contributions.

Jammu and Kashmir:

4. J&K LG Manoj Sinha Urges Unity Against Divisive Forces at Hausla 2.0 Launch:

Lieutenant Governor Manoj Sinha called for unity against divisive forces in Jammu and Kashmir, vowing to eliminate terrorism. Speaking at the Hausla 2.0 and JK Startup Portal launch, he emphasized supporting women and young entrepreneurs to foster economic empowerment in the region.

Telangana:

5. Heavy Rains Cause Major Inflows in Telangana’s Medigadda Barrage, Sparking Political Tensions:

Recent heavy rains have caused significant inflows into Telangana’s Medigadda barrage, part of the Kaleshwaram Lift Irrigation Project, reigniting political tensions between the BRS and Congress. The barrage, with all gates open following previous repairs, recorded water levels over 93 meters and substantial inflows.

International News in English for 22 July 2024

Explore global stories through international news in English. Discover updates from different countries:

1. Bangladesh Supreme Court Reduces Quota System, Sparks Deadly Clashes:

Bangladesh Supreme Court reduced the government job quota for war veterans’ relatives from 30% to 5%, sparking protests that left over 100 dead. The lower court had reinstated the quota system, leading to violent clashes, curfews, and internet suspensions across the country.

2. Nepal PM KP Sharma Oli Seeks Confidence Vote in Parliament:

Newly appointed Nepal Prime Minister KP Sharma Oli will seek a vote of confidence from the House of Representatives on July 21. Oli, supported by the Nepali Congress, aims to secure his position following the ousting of former PM Pushpa Kamal Dahal ‘Prachanda’.

3. Japan Sees 22% Surge in Corporate Bankruptcies Amid Economic Strain:

Japan experienced a 22% increase in corporate bankruptcies in the first half of the year, with 4,887 cases reported, the highest since 2014. Rising costs, labour shortages, and a weakening yen have significantly impacted small and micro enterprises.

4. Bangladesh Supreme Court Invalidates High Court Quota Ruling, Urges Students to Return:

Bangladesh’s Supreme Court declared the High Court’s decision to restore a controversial civil service quota system illegal, reducing the quota for war veterans’ descendants. Protests against the initial reinstatement led to nationwide unrest, prompting curfews and internet shutdowns.

5. Netanyahu Vows Severe Retaliation After Israeli Strikes on Yemen’s Houthis:

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu pledged heavy retaliation against aggressors following airstrikes on Houthi-held targets in Yemen. The strikes targeted key facilities in Hudaydah, leading to Houthi threats of further attacks on Israeli cities and ships.

6. Israeli Airstrikes Hit Houthi Targets in Yemen’s Hudaydah:

Israeli warplanes struck Houthi targets in Yemen’s Hudaydah, retaliating for a drone attack on Tel Aviv. The operation, called ‘Outstretched Arm,’ marked Israel’s farthest strike from its territory, demonstrating its extended military reach and resulting in multiple casualties and fires.

7. Netanyahu to Meet Biden in Washington Amid Gaza War Tensions:

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu will meet US President Joe Biden in Washington on Tuesday, announced Netanyahu’s office. The meeting comes as Netanyahu faces pressure to negotiate a Gaza war ceasefire with Hamas and address tensions between Israel and the United States.

8. J.D. Vance: From Hillbilly Elegy Author to Vice Presidential Candidate:

J.D. Vance, author of “Hillbilly Elegy,” is now the Republican candidate for US Vice President. His memoir, which highlighted the struggles of the rural white population, has been recommended by Barack Obama to understand the social upheaval in the US.

9. ICJ Declares Israeli Occupation of Palestinian Territories Illegal:

The International Court of Justice issued an advisory opinion declaring Israel’s occupation of Palestinian territories illegal, prompting varied international reactions. The Palestinian presidency hailed the decision, while Israel rejected it, asserting the legality of its settlements.

10. Violence in Bangladesh Over Quota System Reforms Leaves Over 130 Dead:

Protests in Bangladesh over the government job quota system have resulted in over 130 deaths. Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina’s controversial remarks and the existing quota system, which favours war veterans’ families, have fueled the unrest, leading to violent clashes and calls for reform.

1. India Records First 200-Plus Total in Women’s T20Is:

India’s women’s cricket team achieved a milestone by scoring 201 for 5, their highest T20I total, surpassing the previous record of 198 for 4 against England in 2018. Richa Ghosh’s strike rate of 220.68 during her unbeaten 64 off 29 balls is the highest for India in a 50-plus score in women’s T20Is, breaking Smriti Mandhana’s record.

2. Mohammed Shami Denies Linking Comments on Kohli to Amit Mishra:

Veteran Indian fast bowler Mohammed Shami refuted media reports linking his recent comments about Virat Kohli to former cricketer Amit Mishra. Shami emphasized his strong friendship with Kohli and Ishant Sharma, highlighting their support during his injury layoff. Shami missed IPL 2024 and the ICC T20 World Cup 2024 due to an Achilles tendon operation.

3. Sairaj Bahutule Named Interim Bowling Coach for India’s Sri Lanka Tour:

Sairaj Bahutule has been appointed as the interim bowling coach for India’s white-ball series in Sri Lanka, replacing Morne Morkel. Bahutule, a former leg spinning all-rounder, will join the coaching staff led by Gautam Gambhir. The decision was made considering the spin-friendly conditions in Sri Lanka.

4. Paris Olympics 2024 Set to Begin Next Week:

The Paris Olympics 2024 will commence on July 26, with over 10,000 athletes participating. India is sending a contingent of 117 athletes across 16 disciplines, aiming to surpass their previous medal haul of seven from Tokyo 2020. Approximately 9 million tickets for the event have already been sold.

5. Kush Maini Wins Formula 2 Sprint Race at Hungarian Grand Prix:

Kush Maini made history as the first Indian driver to win a Formula 2 race, securing victory in the Sprint Race at the Hungarian Grand Prix. Maini inherited the win after Richard Verschoor was disqualified due to a technical irregularity in his car, violating FIA regulations.

6. Speculations on IPL 2025 Line-Ups: KL Rahul to Captain RCB:

Early speculations suggest that RCB might replace captain Faf Du Plessis with KL Rahul for IPL 2025. Tensions between Rahul and his current franchise, Lucknow Super Giants, have fueled rumours of a potential switch. The final decision awaits the BCCI’s retention rules for the upcoming season.

7. Rishabh Pant to Remain with Delhi Capitals Amid Speculation:

Contrary to recent rumours, sources confirmed that Rishabh Pant will stay with Delhi Capitals for IPL 2025. Despite media claims of a potential move to Chennai Super Kings, Pant, along with Axar Patel and Kuldeep Yadav, is expected to be retained by DC.

8. India Unlikely to Participate in 2025 Champions Trophy in Pakistan:

Due to ongoing political tensions, India may not travel to Pakistan for the 2025 Champions Trophy. The BCCI is reportedly requesting the ICC to relocate India’s matches to Sri Lanka or Dubai. Pakistan’s Hasan Ali suggested the tournament will proceed without India if necessary.

9. LeBron James Saves Team USA from Historic Upset Against South Sudan:

Team USA narrowly avoided an upset by South Sudan, winning 101-100 thanks to LeBron James’ layup with eight seconds remaining. South Sudan’s basketball team, the Bright Stars, will represent the country in the Paris Olympics, giving Team USA a serious challenge.

10. AIFF Appoints Manolo Marquez as Head Coach of Men’s National Team:

The All India Football Federation’s Executive Committee appointed Manolo Marquez as the new head coach of the Senior Men’s National Team. The decision was made during a meeting chaired by AIFF President Kalyan Chaubey, attended by key members and special invitees.

Today’s Entertainment News in English

Now, let’s dive into the entertainment world and see what news has taken place.

1. Aespa’s Karina Tops Girl Group Member Brand Reputation Rankings:

Aespa’s Karina has reached the top spot in the girl group member brand reputation rankings for July 2024, dethroning IVE’s Wonyoung. Karina’s brand reputation saw a 157.78% increase from June, achieving a total score index of 7,210,532.

2. Legal Notice Sent to Makers of Kalki 2898 AD for Hurting Religious Sentiments:

Former Congress leader Acharya Pramod Krishnam has sent a legal notice to the makers of the film Kalki 2898 AD, accusing them of inaccurately portraying Lord Kalki and hurting religious sentiments. The notice states that the film’s depiction deviates from Hindu Puranic Scriptures and could cause confusion and distress among Hindus, potentially eroding faith and religious sentiments.

3. Brazilian Rock Singer Ayres Sasaki Dies of Electrocution During Concert:

Brazilian rock singer Ayres Sasaki tragically died at 35 during a live performance in Salinópolis, Pará. Sasaki was electrocuted after hugging a wet fan, which caused a nearby cable to send out a fatal electric shock. Authorities are investigating the incident, and the Solar Hotel, where the concert took place, is cooperating fully.

4. Saiee Manjrekar Embraces Girl-Next-Door Roles, Open to Diverse Characters:

Actor Saiee Manjrekar, known for her roles in “Dabangg 3” and “Major,” is comfortable playing girl-next-door characters and plans to take on diverse roles in the future. Manjrekar, 22, believes her current image reflects her youth and naivety but expects to shed it as she gains more experience.

5. Saiee Manjrekar Talks Future Roles and Career Aspirations:

Saiee Manjrekar, comfortable in her girl-next-door roles, acknowledges her image stems from her youth and naivety. The 22-year-old actor, who debuted in Salman Khan’s “Dabangg 3” and has appeared in films like “Ghani,” “Major,” and “Skanda,” is set to play the younger version of Tabu’s character in “Auron Mein Kahan Dum Tha.”
