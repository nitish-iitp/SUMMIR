10 Lines Today’s News Headlines in English for School Assembly

In today’s school assembly news headlines, we will provide a concise summary of the top 10 news stories, keeping you informed on key developments in politics, education, science, and culture, both in India and around the world.

1. Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau Accuses India of Threats Amid Diplomatic Crisis
Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau has raised serious allegations against the Indian government, claiming it threatened Canadian officials following the expulsion of six Canadian diplomats from India.

2. Garena Free Fire Max Thrives as Popular Battle Royale Game in India
Following the ban of Garena Free Fire by the Indian government in 2022, Garena Free Fire Max has emerged as a leading battle royale game in India. Its vibrant graphics and engaging gameplay have captivated players across the country.

3.Blinkit Introduces New Employment Contracts for Top Executives
Quick commerce leader Blinkit has implemented changes to the employment contracts of its top executives, increasing their notice period from zero to two months. This decision aims to strengthen stability within the company as it navigates challenges in the fast-paced delivery industry and seeks to retain its key leadership talent.

4. Vivo Unveils New X200 Smartphone Series with Advanced Features
Chinese smartphone manufacturer Vivo has launched its latest X200 series in its home country, which includes three models: Vivo X200, X200 Pro, and X200 Pro Mini. All models are equipped with the powerful MediaTek.

5. Nvidia CEO Praises Elon Musk’s AI Company xAI for Extraordinary Achievements
In a recent interview, Jensen Huang, CEO of Nvidia, expressed admiration for Elon Musk’s AI venture, xAI, praising its groundbreaking accomplishments. Huang emphasized Musk’s exceptional skills in engineering and large system management.

6. HCL Technologies Reports Strong Performance Amid Cautious OutlookHCL Technologies has announced that it exceeded its internal expectations for the first half of FY25. However, CEO C Vijayakumar has urged caution regarding macroeconomic uncertainties, reflecting on previous experiences in late 2022 that indicated weakness in the market.

7. New Zealand Advances to Semifinals After Defeating Pakistan in Women’s T20 World Cup
New Zealand triumphed over Pakistan by 54 runs in the Women’s T20 Cricket World Cup held at Dubai International Cricket Stadium. Batting first, New Zealand set a target of 111 runs, scoring 110 for 6 in 20 overs.

8. Italy’s Giovanni Di Lorenzo Stars in 4-1 Nations League Victory Over Israel
Italy secured a convincing 4-1 victory against Israel in the Nations League, with defender Giovanni Di Lorenzo scoring twice. This win solidifies Italy’s position at the top of League A Group Two with 10 points, putting them in a strong position to qualify for the quarterfinals.

9. Germany Secures Spot in UEFA Nations League Quarter-finals with Victory Over Netherlands
Germany clinched a 1-0 win against traditional rivals the Netherlands, ensuring their place in the UEFA Nations League quarter-finals. Jamie Leweling, a debutant from Stuttgart, scored the crucial goal in Munich, contributing to Germany’s success in League A Group 3 and solidifying their position among the tournament’s top teams.

10. India to Host ISSF Junior World Cup in 2025, Highlighting Growth in Shooting SportsThe International Shooting Sport Federation (ISSF) confirmed that India will host the ISSF Junior World Cup in 2025. This announcement was made by ISSF President Luciano Rossi during a press conference in New Delhi.

1. Trudeau Alleges Indian Government Threats Amid Diplomatic Crisis Following Expulsion of Canadian Diplomats
Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau claimed that India threatened and coerced Canadian officials, escalating diplomatic tensions after six Canadian diplomats were expelled by India. The allegations stem from a report by the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP), citing threats to public safety.

2. Reliance Jio Chairman Akash Ambani Urges Government to Expedite Data Center Policy Update
At the India Mobile Congress 2024, Reliance Jio Chairman Akash Ambani urged the government to quickly finalize the 2020 draft data center policy to keep Indian data within national borders, emphasizing India’s transition from 2G to 5G technology.

3. Prime Minister Modi Opens ITU-WTSA 2024, Marking First Hosting in India and Asia-Pacific
Prime Minister Narendra Modi inaugurated the International Telecommunication Union-World Telecommunication Standardization Assembly (WTSA) 2024 in New Delhi, marking the first time this major conference is held in India and the Asia-Pacific region, addressing digital technology standardization.

4. Paramesh Sivamani Assumes Role as 26th Director General of Indian Coast Guard
Paramesh Sivamani has assumed the role of Director General of the Indian Coast Guard. A specialist in navigation, he is recognized for his service with several awards, including the President’s Tatrakshak Medal.

5. Modi Launches ITU-WTSA 2024, Highlighting India’s Role in Global Digital Standards
Prime Minister Modi inaugurated the ITU-WTSA 2024 in New Delhi, marking a significant moment as it is the first time the assembly is hosted in India. This event focuses on standardizing global digital technologies.

6. Australia Introduces First Work and Holiday Visa Program for Indian Youth Aged 18-30
Australia has launched a new visa for Indians aged 18 to 30, allowing them to work and travel in the country. This initiative, explained by Assistant Minister Matt Thistlethwaite, aims to help young Indians gain skills and experience Australian culture.

7. Tamil Nadu Government Prepares for Heavy Rainfall, Deploys NDRF Teams and Closes Schools
Anticipating heavy rains, the Tamil Nadu Government has put 13 National Disaster Response Force (NDRF) teams on standby. Schools and colleges in affected areas will close, and IT firms are advised to implement work-from-home measures.

8. Indian Navy’s First Multi-Purpose Vessel ‘Samarthak’ Launched by L&T
Larsen and Toubro launched the first of two multi-purpose vessels for the Indian Navy, named ‘Samarthak.’ The vessel, introduced by Chief of Naval Staff Admiral Dinesh K Tripathi, will serve a multi-dimensional role in naval operations.

9. Delhi Government Implements Complete Ban on Firecrackers Until January 2025 for Pollution Control
The Delhi government has imposed a complete ban on the manufacturing, storage, sale, and use of firecrackers until January 1, 2025. This decision aims to reduce pollution during Diwali celebrations.

State News Headlines in English for 16 October 2024

This section of State News Headlines in English will focus on news pertinent to different Indian states. It will encompass local government announcements, educational initiatives, and regional development project. 

1. Maharashtra Government Approves 31.64-Km Pune Metro Phase 2 Stretch
The Maharashtra government has officially approved a 31.64-km stretch of the Pune Metro Phase 2 project during a cabinet meeting on Monday. This development will enhance urban transportation in the city.

2. Tamil Nadu Braces for North East Monsoon with Expected Heavy Rains as Low Pressure Develops in Bay of Bengal
Tamil Nadu is set to receive its first spell of North East Monsoon rains as a low-pressure area in the Southeast Bay of Bengal is expected to strengthen and move toward North Tamil Nadu and Puducherry in the next two days. Heavy rains are anticipated across coastal regions and some inland areas.

3. Telangana Government Launches Job Portal and Announces Increase in Reservation for Persons with Disabilities
Telangana’s Women and Child Welfare Minister D. Anasuya Seethakka has unveiled an online job portal for persons with disabilities and plans to increase their reservation from 1% to 4%. During the launch, she issued joining orders to 10 new employees, promising job offers based on qualifications.

4. Jammu and Kashmir’s Lieutenant Governor Invites Omar Abdullah to Form Government Following Revocation of President’s Rule
Jammu and Kashmir’s Lieutenant Governor Manoj Sinha has invited National Conference leader Omar Abdullah to form a new government after revoking President’s rule. Abdullah, elected unanimously as the Leader of the Legislature Party, is expected to take oath soon, marking a new political chapter for the region.

5. Delhi-NCR Implements Stage-I of Graded Response Action Plan Due to Poor Air Quality Levels Reaching 234
The Commission of Air Quality Management has activated Stage-I of the Graded Response Action Plan across Delhi-NCR due to an Air Quality Index (AQI) of 234, classified as ‘Poor.’ Starting today, a 27-point action plan will be enforced to improve air quality in the region.
International News in English for 16 October 2024

Stay updated with the latest Global Stories and International News. This section highlights significant events and developments from around the world.

1. 75th International Astronautical Congress Opens in Milan with Focus on Sustainable Space Technologies
The 75th International Astronautical Congress (IAC) opened at the MiCo Convention Center in Milan, Italy, attracting over 10,000 space experts and professionals. Themed “Responsible Space for Sustainability,” the congress aims to promote sustainable development in space technologies while balancing scientific progress and environmental protection.

2. Malaysia Prepares for Budget Cuts and New Taxes to Strengthen Fiscal Position Amid Falling Revenue
Analysts predict that Malaysia will introduce subsidy cuts and new taxes in its upcoming budget as Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim aims to bolster the country’s fiscal position. Expected announcements include a tax on high-value goods and sugar-sweetened beverages to balance economic growth with rising living costs.

3. Hong Kong to Announce Economic Measures, Including Liquor Tariff Cuts, to Revive Post-Pandemic Recovery
In its annual policy address, Hong Kong is set to unveil measures aimed at revitalizing its economy, including reducing liquor tariffs. Despite a projected growth of 2.5-3.5% for the year, consumption and retail spending remain sluggish as the city struggles to recover from the pandemic’s economic impacts.

4. China Enforces Tax on Overseas Investment Gains for Ultra-Rich Amid Revenue Reevaluation
China has initiated enforcement of a long-overlooked tax on overseas investment gains targeting its wealthiest citizens. Recent reports indicate that high-net-worth individuals have been asked to assess their tax liabilities, including potential arrears, as authorities step up scrutiny on overseas wealth.

5. Japan Expresses Concerns to China Over Military Drills Near Taiwan, Scrambles Fighter Jets
Japan has raised concerns with China regarding military drills conducted around Taiwan, prompting the country to scramble fighter jets near its southern island of Yonaguni. Deputy Chief Cabinet Secretary Kazuhiko Aoki stated that the government is closely monitoring the situation and has conveyed its apprehensions to Chinese officials.
Sports News in English for 16 October 2024

Catch up on the latest sports news in English and stay updated on your favorite sports events. Find out scores, highlights, and key moments from recent games and matches.

1. New Zealand Defeats Pakistan by 54 Runs, Advances to Women’s T20 Semifinals
In the Women’s T20 Cricket World Cup, New Zealand triumphed over Pakistan by 54 runs at Dubai International Cricket Stadium. Batting first, New Zealand scored 110 runs, setting Pakistan a target of 111. Pakistan struggled, finishing at 56 runs in 11.4 overs, leading to India’s exit from the tournament.

2. Giovanni Di Lorenzo Stars in Italy’s 4-1 Victory Over Israel in Nations League
Italy’s Giovanni Di Lorenzo scored twice in a commanding 4-1 victory against Israel in the Nations League, propelling Italy to the top of League A Group Two with 10 points. The win positions Italy well for a spot in the quarterfinals, needing just one point from their remaining two matches.

3. Germany Secures UEFA Nations League Quarter-Final Spot with Victory Over Netherlands
Germany clinched a spot in the UEFA Nations League quarter-finals with a narrow 1-0 win against the Netherlands, thanks to Jamie Leweling’s debut goal in Munich. This victory solidifies Germany’s position among the top two in Group 3 of League A, alongside France’s win over Belgium.

4. Romanian Grandmaster Kirill Shevchenko Disqualified for Cheating in Spanish Team Championship
Grandmaster Kirill Shevchenko has been expelled from the 2024 Spanish Team Championship for allegedly using a mobile phone during matches. His draws in the first two rounds have been converted to losses. Shevchenko, who previously won the European Team Chess Championship with Ukraine, was representing C.A. Silla.

5. India to Host ISSF Junior World Cup in 2025, Confirms ISSF President
Luciano Rossi, President of the ISSF, announced that India will host the ISSF Junior World Cup in 2025 during a press conference in New Delhi. Rossi praised India’s significant contributions to shooting sports and its commitment to developing athletes and infrastructure in the discipline.

The Karnataka Examination Authority (KEA) has announced the results for the KEA VAO Compulsory Kannada Language Test 2024. Candidates who took the exam can check their results on the official KEA website by entering their Register Number and Date of Birth.

On October 15, India and the world celebrated World Students’ Day to honor Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, the country’s 11th President and a renowned scientist. Dr. Kalam’s famous quote, “To succeed in life and achieve results, you must understand and master three mighty forces — desire, belief, and expectation,” resonated with students and educators alike.

4. IGNOU Extends Registration Deadline for December 2024 Term End Examination
The Indira Gandhi National Open University (IGNOU) has announced an extension for the registration deadline for the December 2024 Term End Examination (TEE) on October 14.Students enrolled in both Open and Distance Learning (ODL) and online programs can submit their application forms without incurring any late fees until October 20.

5. IIIT Bangalore Secures 74th Position in National Institutional Ranking Framework
The International Institute of Information Technology, Bangalore (IIIT Bangalore) has achieved 74th place in the National Institutional Ranking Framework 2024 for the engineering category. Established in 1999, the institute focuses on IT and research in computer science, electronics, and data sciences.
Business News Today Headlines

Let’s Dive into the business news today headlines for school assembly and see what new has taken place.

1. Oyo Parent Company Oravel Stays in Talks for $200 Million Funding to Acquire G6 Hospitality
Oravel Stays, the parent company of Oyo, is negotiating to secure $200 million in a primary funding round to facilitate the acquisition of G6 Hospitality, owner of Motel 6 in the US. The funding aims to close at a valuation between $4.5 billion and $5 billion, surpassing its previous valuation of $2.4 billion.

2. Supreme Court Ruling Allows Adani Power’s ₹27,000 Crore Offer for KSK Mahanadi Project to Proceed
In a landmark decision, the Supreme Court of India has overturned a Telangana High Court order that postponed insolvency proceedings against KSK Mahanadi, clearing the way for Adani Power’s ₹27,000 crore offer. This offer guarantees a 92 percent recovery for lenders amid the generator’s financial troubles.

3. Purplle Raises ₹1,500 Crore in Funding Round Led by Abu Dhabi Investment Authority
Online beauty retailer Purplle has secured ₹1,500 crore in funding, with existing investors like Blume Ventures and Premji Invest increasing their stakes, alongside new investor Sharrp Ventures. The funding round was spearheaded by the Abu Dhabi Investment Authority, bolstering Purplle’s growth ambitions.

4. Supreme Court Rules on Adani Power’s Offer for KSK Mahanadi Project, Ensuring Recovery for Lenders
The Supreme Court has ruled in favor of Adani Power’s ₹27,000 crore bid for the KSK Mahanadi project, allowing the company to proceed with its offer. The decision overturns a previous order that deferred insolvency proceedings, ensuring a 92 percent recovery for the project’s lenders.

5. RSS Calls for Review of India’s Development Model Amid Environmental Concerns
On its 99th Founder’s Day, the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS) emphasized the need for policymakers to reassess India’s current development model, citing its contribution to environmental crises. Sarsanghchalak Mohan Bhagwat outlined immediate, micro-level solutions that society can implement to address these issues.

What Happened Today in History

The This Day in History segment in the school assembly news headlines highlights key events from history that took place on the current date.

1. 1964 – China Conducts First Nuclear Test
On 16th October 1964,China successfully conducted its first nuclear test, becoming the fifth country to possess nuclear weapons. This event marked a significant development in global nuclear power dynamics and the Cold War arms race.

2. 1793 – Marie Antoinette Executed During French Revolution
On this day in 1793, Marie Antoinette, the former queen of France, was executed by guillotine in Paris. Her death symbolized the fall of the monarchy during the French Revolution and the rise of republicanism in France.

3. 1945 – United Nations Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) Established
On 16th October 1945, the United Nations founded the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) to combat world hunger and improve nutrition. This organization continues to play a vital role in global food security.

4. 1987 – “Black Monday” Stock Market CrashOn 16th
October 1987, global stock markets experienced a massive crash, which became known as “Black Monday.” The crash wiped out billions of dollars in market value, affecting economies worldwide and prompting financial reforms.

5. 1978 – Pope John Paul II Elected
On 16th October 1978, Pope John Paul II was elected as the 264th pope of the Roman Catholic Church. He was the first non-Italian pope in over 400 years and became known for his influential role in global religious and political matters.
