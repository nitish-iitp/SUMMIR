Afghanistan play to their strengths to defeat Australia in mammoth T20 World Cup upset
Openers Rahmanullah Gurbaz and Ibrahim Zadran build the foundation with a century partnership and Gulbadin Naib (4/20) gives Pat Cummins a taste of his own medicine, as Afghanistan score a famous win over Australia
Written by Lalith Kalidas
Updated: June 24, 2024 07:55 IST
5 min read
Afghanistan players celebrate an Australia wicket during the men's T20 World Cup cricket match at the Arnos Vale Ground, Kingstown, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines on Sunday. (AP Photo)

And so when Australia surprisingly elected to bowl first at the Arnos Vale Stadium, where the previous three wins were etched out by teams defending totals, even as low as 106 and 115, Afghanistan were not quite unruffled as they fell back onto their group-stage XI. It was a unit that best exuded the strengths of Afghanistan even as their middle-order frailties laid bare open.

Openers Rahmanullah Gurbaz and Ibrahim Zadran did not stutter even as Australia nailed down 25 dot balls within the Powerplay.
