The Today’s News Headlines section for school assembly highlights the most significant and impactful news stories across various categories, including national, international, state, business, science and technology, entertainment, and more. 

1. India Mourns the Loss of Ratan Tata, Visionary Leader and Industrial Pioneer, at 86
Ratan Tata, Chairman Emeritus of Tata Sons, has passed away at 86. Known for his visionary leadership and philanthropy, Tata’s legacy of excellence and innovation has left an indelible mark on Indian industry.

2. Apple Unveils Exciting AI Features in iOS 18.1 Update for iPhones
Apple is generating buzz among users with its latest AI-driven features set to launch with the iOS 18.1 update. These tools, powered by both Apple’s own language models and OpenAI’s ChatGPT, will introduce six new capabilities to the iPhone 16 series and iPhone 15 Pro models. However, analysts suggest that it may take another two to three years to fully realize these features’ potential.

3. Pro-Ukrainian Hackers Disrupt Russian State Media on Putin’s Birthday
In a surprising move, pro-Ukrainian hacktivists launched a cyberattack on Russia’s state broadcaster, VGTRK, coinciding with President Vladimir Putin’s birthday. This unprecedented action sent a strong message, although VGTRK claimed no major damage occurred. The broadcaster’s servers faced significant downtime, raising concerns about potential data loss.

4. Nobel Prize in Physics Awarded to AI Pioneers John Hopfield and Geoffrey Hinton
In a historic achievement, AI scientists John Hopfield and Geoffrey Hinton received the Nobel Prize in Physics for their groundbreaking contributions to artificial intelligence. Their work has revolutionized machine learning, spurring scientific progress while also igniting discussions on the risks associated with AI technology.

5. September 2023 Records Second Warmest Global Temperature Amid Climate Change
According to the EU climate monitor Copernicus, September 2023 was the second-warmest September on record globally, contributing to what is likely to be the hottest year ever. Extreme rainfall and storms around the world highlighted the increasing severity of climate events, prompting urgent discussions about climate change.

6. New Zealand’s Reserve Bank Cuts Interest Rates to Boost Economic Growth
The Reserve Bank of New Zealand has announced a significant interest rate cut of 50 basis points, lowering the rate from 5.25% to 4.75%. This marks the second consecutive reduction aimed at stimulating economic growth amid ongoing concerns about inflation and the overall economy.

7. FTSE Russell to Add India’s Sovereign Bonds to Key Emerging Markets Index
India’s sovereign bonds will be included in FTSE Russell’s Emerging Markets Government Bond Index starting September 2025. This decision follows similar announcements from JP Morgan and Bloomberg Index Services, potentially opening the door for billions in investments into Indian bonds.

8. China’s Economic Growth Projection Downgraded by World Bank
The World Bank has revised China’s economic growth forecast for 2025 down to 4.3%, down from an earlier estimate of 4.8%. This adjustment reflects ongoing uncertainties surrounding the effectiveness of China’s recent stimulus measures, underscoring challenges in the global economy.

9. Chinese Drinkers Face Higher Prices as Government Imposes Tariffs on European Brandies
The Chinese government has announced provisional tariffs of 30.6% to 39% on European brandies, including Remy Martin, after the EU imposed duties on Chinese electric vehicles. This tit-for-tat move may affect prices for consumers and influence ongoing trade negotiations between China and the EU.

10. India Meteorological Department Forecasts Heavy Rainfall in Multiple States
The India Meteorological Department (IMD) has predicted heavy rainfall in Lakshadweep, Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, and Tripura. Conditions are favorable for the further withdrawal of the Southwest Monsoon from parts of Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh, and Maharashtra over the next few days.

11. Taylor Swift Takes a Stand Against Jet Tracker with Legal Action
Taylor Swift is taking legal action against a college student tracking her private jet movements, labeling his actions as stalking. Swift’s lawyer sent a cease-and-desist letter, aiming to protect her privacy amid growing concerns about personal safety in the digital age.

1. Ratan Tata, Iconic Industrialist and Philanthropist, Passes Away at 86
Ratan Tata, Chairman Emeritus of Tata Sons, one of India’s largest conglomerates, has passed away at the age of 86. Just days before, the revered industrialist had shared a social media post dismissing rumors about his health, stating that he was only undergoing routine medical check-ups due to his advancing age.

2. CAG Holds First State Finance Secretaries Conference to Enhance Financial Management
The Comptroller and Auditor General (CAG) of India, Girish Chandra Murmu, held the inaugural State Finance Secretaries Conference in New Delhi, stressing the need for synchronized expenditure management.
3. WHO Declares India Trachoma-Free, Achieves Milestone in Southeast AsiaThe World Health Organization has declared India free from trachoma as a public health issue. India became the third Southeast Asian nation to achieve this, recognized during a Regional Committee meeting in New Delhi.

4. Azim Premji University to Launch Book on Youth Mental Health for World Mental Health Day 2024
Azim Premji University will celebrate World Mental Health Day 2024 with a book launch and discussion on mental health among youth. Titled Creative Expressions on Mental Health Among Youth, the book will feature young people’s views on mental health and ways to break the stigma through open conversations.

5. Germany’s ‘Festival of Germany’ Highlights Strengthening Indo-German Ties in October 2024
October 2024 marks a significant period for Indo-German relations, dubbed the “Festival of Germany” by German Ambassador Philipp Ackermann. The month includes German Chancellor Olaf Scholz’s visit to India for the India-Germany Inter-Governmental Consultations, co-chaired by Indian PM Narendra Modi, focusing on deepening bilateral ties.

6. Taiwan to Open New Office in Mumbai to Boost Indo-Taiwan Trade Relations
Taiwan’s de facto ambassador to India, Baushuan Ger, announced the opening of a new Taiwanese office in Mumbai on October 16. This office, alongside its existing presence in Delhi and Chennai, aims to enhance trade, economic ties, and support for businesses, signaling deeper engagement between Taiwan and India.

7. Parliamentary Standing Committee to Examine Regulations on AI and Social Media Impact
The Parliamentary Standing Committee on Communications and Information Technology will deliberate on the implications of artificial intelligence and social media regulations for the 2024-25 period, focusing on their societal impacts and the need for effective oversight.

8. Political Tensions Escalate as Samsung Workers’ Protest Gains Momentum in Tamil Nadu
The ongoing month-long protest by Samsung workers in Tamil Nadu has sparked political tensions within the ruling DMK alliance, highlighted by the preventive detention of 11 union officials ahead of a visit by alliance leaders to show solidarity with the workers.

9. Reserve Bank of India Holds Repo Rate Steady at 6.5% Amid Economic Concerns
The Reserve Bank of India has decided to maintain the repo rate at 6.5%, balancing inflation concerns with the necessity for economic growth, following a three-day Monetary Policy Committee meeting led by Governor Shaktikanta Das.

10. Education Sector Anticipates 12-14% Revenue Growth Due to Increased Enrollment
Schools and colleges across India are projected to experience a 12-14% growth in revenue, driven by rising enrollments and fee adjustments, according to CRISIL Ratings, indicating a continued positive trend in the education sector post-pandemic.

11. UNFPA Commends India for Significant Progress in Maternal Health Initiatives
The United Nations Population Fund has recognized India’s extraordinary achievements in improving maternal health, noting a remarkable 70% reduction in the Maternal Mortality Ratio from 2000 to 2020, highlighting the nation’s commitment to family planning and healthcare.
State News Headlines in English for 9 October 2024

Today’s state news headlines in English for school assembly focus on key events happening in different Indian states. Some of the important updates include:

1. Tamil Nadu Government Identifies Faulty Thermostat as Cause of Fire at Tata Electronics’ iPhone Factory
The Tamil Nadu government has reported that a failing thermostat control likely caused the fire at Tata Electronics’ iPhone factory in Hosur on September 28. The fire destroyed the anodising plant, with initial investigations indicating the thermostat failure occurred in one of the baths.

2. Tamil Nadu Chief Minister Celebrates JKNC-Congress Victory in Jammu and Kashmir Polls
Chief Minister M K Stalin hailed the JKNC-Congress alliance’s win in Jammu and Kashmir as a “stupendous victory,” heralding a just and inclusive future for the region. He congratulated the alliance and the people of Jammu & Kashmir on social media, emphasizing the importance of this victory for democracy.

3. Telangana Cabinet Sub-Committee Proposes Judicial Commission for SC Sub-Classification Study
In Telangana, a Cabinet Sub-Committee has recommended forming a judicial commission to study sub-classification within Scheduled Castes. Chaired by Minister Capt. N. Uttam Kumar Reddy, the meeting emphasized the need for a legally sound process to assess intra-backwardness among different SC castes.

4. Andhra Pradesh CM Discusses Key Infrastructure Projects with Union Ministers in Delhi
Andhra Pradesh Chief Minister N. Chandrababu Naidu held discussions in Delhi with Union Ministers, focusing on advancing infrastructure and economic development projects. He highlighted projects like the Vijayawada Eastern Bypass and Hyderabad-Vijayawada expressway expansion, with assurances for prioritization from Union Road Minister Nitin Gadkari.

5. CBI Takes Over Investigation of Online Stock Trading Scam in Assam
Assam Chief Minister Himanta Biswa Sarma announced that the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) has taken over 41 cases related to an alleged online stock trading scam. The scam emerged in late August, involving significant investments in one of Deepankar Barman’s companies, and the state government pledges support for the investigation.
International News in English for 9 October 2024

Today’s international news headlines in English school assembly include major global developments that impact the world.

1. New York City Deputy Mayor Sheena Wright Resigns Amid Federal Investigation
New York City Deputy Mayor Sheena Wright has become the tenth high-ranking official to resign from Mayor Eric Adams’ administration as federal investigations intensify. Wright’s resignation follows a federal raid on her home last month, during which her electronics were seized. Mayor Adams expressed gratitude for Wright’s service, highlighting her contributions to children and working-class families in New York.

2. 7-Eleven’s Japanese Owner Sees Share Surge After Couche-Tard Increases Takeover Offer
Shares in Japan’s Seven & i, the owner of 7-Eleven, surged following reports that Canada’s Alimentation Couche-Tard (ACT) raised its takeover offer by nearly 20%. Seven & i previously rejected ACT’s initial $40 billion proposal, citing undervaluation and regulatory challenges. The revised offer stands at $18.19 per share, totaling around $47.2 billion, marking a potential record foreign buyout of a Japanese company.

3. Hurricane Milton Approaches Florida, Evacuations Ordered Amid Catastrophic Threat
Hurricane Milton is approaching Florida’s west coast, prompting mass evacuations as officials warn of dire survival odds for those who remain. The Tampa Bay area, home to over 3.3 million residents, faces the threat of widespread destruction. Milton, a Category 5 hurricane at its peak, is expected to weaken but still poses significant risks as it nears landfall.

4. Brazil Lifts Ban on Elon Musk’s X Following Supreme Court Order
Elon Musk’s microblogging site, X, has resumed operations in Brazil after a Supreme Court ruling lifted its ban, which lasted over a month. The court mandated the country’s communication regulator to restore access within 24 hours. X was blocked due to non-compliance with court orders, including failing to deactivate accounts of supporters of former president Jair Bolsonaro.

5. North Korea Appoints New Defence Minister Amid Rising Tensions
North Korea has appointed No Kwang Chol as its new defence minister, succeeding Kang Sun Nam, as reported by state media. The announcement comes amid expectations that North Korea may strengthen its stance against South Korea. No has previously participated in discussions with former US President Donald Trump, and there are speculations about the country scrapping a historic inter-Korean agreement.

Today’s education news headlines for school assembly bring attention to key developments in the field of education.

1. IIT Roorkee Responds to Candidate Requests: GATE 2025 Registration Deadline Extended Until October 11

The Assam State School Education Board (SSEM) has officially released the answer key for the Assam Direct Recruitment Examinations (ADRE) conducted on September 29, 2024, for Class-III posts, including Bachelor Degree Level and HSLC Level Driver positions.

UNESCO is now accepting nominations for the second edition of the UNESCO-Al Fozan International Prize for the Promotion of Young Scientists in Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM). This esteemed prize is awarded every two years to five talented individuals under the age of 40 for their notable contributions to research, education, or scientific collaboration.

The Bihar government has announced a revised policy aimed at improving the transfer and posting process for government school teachers. Heavy Rainfall Expected in Several Indian States as IMD Confirms Monsoon Withdrawal

What Happened Today in History

What Happened Today in History will showcase significant historical events that occurred on this day in the past. This section may include important milestones in world history, groundbreaking discoveries, and notable personalities born on this date.

1. World Mental Health Day (1992)
World Mental Health Day is observed on 10th October each year to raise awareness about mental health issues around the world and to mobilize efforts in support of mental well-being. The day provides a platform for discussions on how to improve mental health care.

2. Battle of Tours (732 AD)
On 10th October 732, the Battle of Tours took place in France, where Frankish leader Charles Martel successfully halted the Muslim expansion into Western Europe. This battle is considered a turning point in European history.

3. Establishment of Taiwan (1945)
On 10th October 1945, Taiwan was officially restored to China after 50 years of Japanese occupation following World War II. This day is still celebrated in Taiwan as the ‘Double Tenth’ or National Day.

4. Opening of the Panama Canal (1913)
The Panama Canal opened for trial runs on 10th October 1913, linking the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans. This engineering marvel transformed global trade routes, drastically reducing shipping times and costs.

5. Signing of the Partial Nuclear Test Ban Treaty (1963)
On 10th October 1963, the Partial Nuclear Test Ban Treaty, which prohibited nuclear tests in the atmosphere, outer space, and underwater, came into effect. This marked a significant step towards arms control during the Cold War.
