National News Headlines in English for 14 June 2024

Stay updated with 14 June 2024 news headlines in English for school assembly. Learn about various sectors’ happenings in our country:

1. NEET UG 2024 Re-test Announced for 1,563 Candidates Who Received Grace Marks:

The Supreme Court has allowed the medical entrance exam NEET UG 2024 to proceed without the grace marks awarded to 1,563 candidates. These candidates can now choose to retake the exam on June 23rd. Their previous marks, excluding the grace marks, will be used if they don’t participate in the re-test. The re-test results will be declared by June 30th, and counselling for admissions will start on July 6th.

2. India Slips in World Economic Forum’s Gender Gap Index:

India’s ranking on gender equality has fallen two spots to 129th according to the World Economic Forum’s latest report. While India performs well in terms of female education enrollment and political representation, the country struggles with economic parity for women. Bangladesh, Nepal, Sri Lanka, and Bhutan all rank higher than India in South Asia.

3. India Provides Humanitarian Aid to Gaza at Jordan Conference:

India has pledged continued humanitarian support for Gaza at a high-level conference in Jordan. The conference focused on finding urgent solutions to the ongoing humanitarian crisis in the region.

4. PM Modi Heads to Italy for G7 Summit:

Prime Minister Narendra Modi will be departing for Italy today to participate in the 50th G7 Summit. This will be his first overseas trip after winning a third term in office. He is also expected to hold a meeting with the Italian Prime Minister.

5.  India’s Retail Inflation Falls to Lowest Level in a Year:

India’s inflation rate has dropped to its lowest level in a year, reaching 4.75% in May 2024. This marks the third consecutive month with inflation below 5%. Core inflation, which excludes food and fuel prices, is also at a record low, indicating a potential slowdown in economic growth.

6. Air India Upgrades Virtual Assistant with Latest AI Technology:

Air India has announced that their virtual assistant, “Maharaja,” is now powered by OpenAI’s most advanced large language model, GPT-4. This upgrade will allow Maharaja to better understand and respond to customer queries related to flights, baggage allowances, and other travel needs.

7. Madhya Pradesh Launches New Intra-State Air Service:

Madhya Pradesh is launching a new air service connecting eight cities within the state. The “PM Shri Tourism Air Service” will be inaugurated by the Chief Minister and will operate under a public-private partnership. Online booking for the service is now available.

1. India Falls to 129th in Global Gender Gap Index, Iceland Leads: 

The World Economic Forum’s Global Gender Gap Index 2024 shows India fell to 129th place, while Iceland remains first. India ranks fifth in South Asia, behind Bangladesh, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Bhutan, and Pakistan.

2. Human Rights Concerns Rise in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Amidst Lack of Representation: 

The Human Rights Commission of Pakistan raised concerns about Khyber Pakhtunkhwa’s human rights and law and order. Without provincial elections, citizens were unrepresented, and no legislation was passed in 2023.

3. Kuwait Fire Tragedy: 40 Indian Workers Killed, Many Injured: 

A fire in a six-story building in Kuwait killed at least 40 Indian nationals and injured many more. Most deaths were from smoke inhalation as the fire broke out early Wednesday. The building housed low-paid workers.

4. US Federal Reserve Holds Interest Rates, Hints at Possible Cut:

The US Federal Reserve kept interest rates between 5.25% and 5.50%. They hinted at a possible rate cut this year but need more positive inflation data before lowering rates.

5. IMF Backs Sri Lanka with $337 Million for Economic Stability:

The IMF supports Sri Lanka’s economic stability. After the first review, Sri Lanka received $337 million in December 2023. A second review could grant another $337 million, pending approval.

6. Athens Acropolis Closes Partially as Heat Wave Hits 43°C:

The Acropolis in Athens will partially close from midday to 5 p.m. due to a heat wave, with temperatures expected to reach 43°C on Wednesday and Thursday. There’s also a high risk of wildfires.

7. Record 175,000 Indian Pilgrims in Mecca for Hajj, New Services Launched:

A record 175,000 Indian pilgrims are in Mecca and Medina for the annual Hajj pilgrimage. The Indian government provided new services, including hotel accommodations and high-speed rail transport for 33,000 pilgrims.

Sports News in English for 14 June 2024

Get the latest sports news in English and follow your favourite sports events:

1. India Secures Super Eight Spot with Seven-Wicket Victory Over USA:

India defeated the USA by seven wickets at Nassau County Stadium in New York, securing a Super Eight spot with six points. Pakistan’s qualification now depends on the USA vs. Ireland game on June 14.

2. Sumit Nagal Advances to ATP Perugia Challenger Pre-Quarterfinals:

Sumit Nagal advanced to the ATP 125 Perugia Challenger pre-quarterfinals with a 7-6(1), 6-2 win over Nerman Fatic. Nagal, ranked 73rd, will face Alessandro Giannessi in the next round.

3. Yuki Bhambri Reaches Stuttgart Open Men’s Doubles Semifinals:

Yuki Bhambri and Albano Olivetti reached the Stuttgart Open Men’s doubles semifinals, defeating Theo Arribage and Sadio Doumbia 6-3, 6-2. They will face the winners of the Hassan-Masur vs. Cash-Galloway match.

4. Renowned Football Coach T K Chathunni Passes Away at 80:

Former footballer and coach T K Chathunni, aged 80, passed away in Kerala. He coached top clubs like FC Kochin, Churchill Brothers, Dempo SC, Mohun Bagan, Salgaocar, and Kerala Police.

5. AIFF Demands Probe into Controversial Goal in India vs Qatar Match:

AIFF has requested an investigation into a controversial goal awarded to Qatar in their World Cup qualifier. The goal, allowed by referee Kim Woo-Sung, helped Qatar win 2-1 against India.

6. Rutherford and Joseph Propel West Indies to T20 World Cup Super Eight:

Sherfane Rutherford’s unbeaten 68 and Alzarri Joseph’s bowling led West Indies to a 13-run win over New Zealand, securing a spot in the T20 World Cup Super Eight.

7. Harbhajan Singh Condemns Kamran Akmal’s Disrespectful Remarks About Arshdeep Singh:

Despite Kamran Akmal’s apology for his comments about Arshdeep Singh, Harbhajan Singh criticised him, urging respect for religious sentiments.

1. Netflix’s ‘Hierarchy’ Becomes a Global K-Drama Hit:

Netflix’s K-Drama “Hierarchy” hit number two on international charts, depicting elite high school life and a mysterious newcomer.

2. AI Project Reimagines ‘Lagaan’ Cast with Current Indian Cricketers:

AI enthusiasts use AI to create realistic images of historical figures in modern settings. Sahid SK’s “Lagaan Reimagined” project features Indian cricketers as characters from the film.

3. Anurag Kashyap Declares Golden Period of OTT Platforms Over:

Anurag Kashyap says OTT platforms have become like TV, driven by algorithms over quality, and believes their golden period is over.

4. Sunny Deol Announces ‘Border 2’ After Success of ‘Gadar 2’:

Sunny Deol announced “Border 2” after “Gadar 2” success, returning to his role from the original 1997 film about the India-Pakistan war.

5. BTS’s V Partners with ‘Compose Coffee’ for 2024 FESTA Event:

BTS’s V teams up with ‘Compose Coffee’ to offer free coffee at the 2024 FESTA event. Jin will also hold a fan event following his military discharge.

6. Karan Johar Takes Legal Action Against ‘Shaadi Ke Director Karan aur Johar’ Film:
Karan Johar seeks a court injunction to stop the release of “Shaadi Ke Director Karan aur Johar, citing unauthorised use of his name.

7. South Korean Artist Accuses Kalki 2898 AD Makers of Plagiarism:

Sung Choi, a South Korean artist known for his work with Marvel and Disney, accused the creators of Kalki 2898 AD of copying his designs without permission. He shared a screenshot on social media to support his claim.

What Happened Today in History

Understand the significance of what happened today in history and learn how to make a difference:

1. Magna Carta Signed (1215)

King John of England sealed the Magna Carta, a document that limited the monarch’s power and established constitutional law principles.

2. Benjamin Franklin’s Kite Experiment (1752)

Benjamin Franklin conducted his famous kite experiment to demonstrate the electrical nature of lightning, laying the groundwork for understanding electricity.

3. First Battle of Kalka River (1219)

Mongols led by Genghis Khan’s general Jebe defeated Russian forces near the Kalka River, marking an early Mongol victory in their westward expansion.

4. Charles Goodyear Patents Vulcanized Rubber (1844)

Charles Goodyear received a patent for vulcanised rubber, revolutionising industries reliant on rubber materials like tyres and footwear.

5. Mount Pinatubo Eruption (1991)

Mount Pinatubo in the Philippines erupted, one of the most powerful volcanic eruptions of the 20th century, affecting global climate and causing significant environmental and societal impacts.
