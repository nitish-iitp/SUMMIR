National News Headlines in English for 30 August 2024

Here’s what’s happening across India with national news headlines for 30 August 2024. Catch up on the major stories making headlines today.


1. Tamil Nadu Urged to Adopt NEP for Release of Education Funds
Tamil Nadu Education Minister Anbil Mahesh Poyyamozhi accused the Union government of withholding ₹573 crore in funds under the Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan. He claims the Centre is pressuring the state to adopt the National Education Policy (NEP)-2020 for fund release.

2. GIFT City Halts Approvals for Domestic Family Investment Funds Following RBI Concerns
GIFT City has ceased allowing domestic family offices to establish investment funds in its finance hub due to concerns from the Reserve Bank of India (RBI). The RBI fears that relaxed capital controls could lead to tax evasion and money laundering, disrupt exchange rate management, and affect forex reserves. Sandip Shah, head of the IFSC Department at GIFT City

3. Passport Seva Portal Temporarily Closed for MaintenanceThe Ministry of External Affairs announced the shutdown of the Passport Seva portal for five days, from August 29 to September 2, 2024, for technical maintenance. Passport-related services will be unavailable, and appointments will be rescheduled.

4. INS Arighat to Boost India’s Maritime Security
Defence Minister Rajnath Singh is set to commission INS Arighat, India’s second nuclear-powered ballistic missile submarine. This modern vessel, named after the Sanskrit word for ‘destroyer of enemies,’ will greatly enhance India’s maritime security.

5. Delhi High Court Extends Interim Protection for Sacked IAS Trainee
 The Delhi High Court extended protection from arrest for sacked IAS trainee Puja Khedkar until September 5. Khedkar faces charges of falsifying documents to pass the UPSC exam but has denied all accusations, claiming the UPSC lacks the authority to disqualify her.

6. National Sports Day Honors Hockey Legend Major Dhyan Chand
On August 29, National Sports Day was celebrated across India to honor hockey icon Major Dhyan Chand. Prime Minister Narendra Modi emphasized the government’s commitment to sports, encouraging youth participation and paying tribute to India’s sports achievements.

7. Jammu and Kashmir Assembly Elections Phase Two Begins
The Election Commission of India announced the second phase of the Jammu and Kashmir Assembly Elections. Nominations are open until September 5, with polling scheduled for September 25 in 26 constituencies across six districts, including Srinagar and Rajouri.

8. Finance Ministry Amends Rules for Global Listing of Indian Firms
The Finance Ministry amended the Securities Contracts Regulation Rules to allow Indian companies to list directly on international exchanges. This change aims to help start-ups and tech companies access global capital through GIFT IFSC, offering them new growth opportunities.

9. UPSC Introduces Aadhaar-Based Authentication for Candidate Verification
The Union Public Service Commission will now use Aadhaar-based authentication to verify candidates during registration and recruitment stages. This move follows the recent case involving Puja Khedkar, who fraudulently attempted to exceed her Civil Services Examination attempts.

10. SEBI Develops New API Standards for Financial Exchanges
SEBI Chairperson Madhabi Puri Buch announced the development of a standardized Application Programming Interface (API) for exchanges and depositories at the Global Fintech Fest. She declined to comment on allegations made by the Hindenburg report regarding the Adani group investigation.

1. Former Jharkhand CM Champai Soren Resigns from JMM and Ministerial Posts, Set to Join BJP
Champai Soren, former Chief Minister of Jharkhand, has resigned from his ministerial positions in the Hemant Soren government as well as his posts within the Jharkhand Mukti Morcha (JMM). In a letter to JMM President Shibu Soren, he expressed discontent with the party’s policies and working style, which led to his decision to quit. Champai Soren, who handled the portfolios of Water Resources, Higher Education, and Technical Education, has also stepped down as the MLA of Seraikela constituency. He is scheduled to join the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) tomorrow, stating that his move aligns with the best interests of Jharkhand.

2. Gujarat Floods Claim 28 Lives; Over 17,000 Evacuated Amid Rescue Operations
The death toll from rain-related incidents in Gujarat has increased to 28, as continuous downpours have caused widespread flooding in several regions. According to Gujarat Health Minister Rushikesh Patel, over 17,800 people have been evacuated, with 12,000 rescued in Vadodara alone by August 28. Prime Minister Narendra Modi held a telephone conversation with Gujarat Chief Minister Bhupendra Patel, offering full support for rescue and relief operations and providing guidance on sanitation and public health in the affected areas.
 
3. Telangana Among States to Receive Private FM Radio Expansion in 10 Cities 
The Union Cabinet has approved the rollout of private FM radio in 234 cities and towns, including ten locations in Telangana such as Adilabad, Karimnagar, and Khammam. The decision is part of the third phase of the Private FM Radio Policy, with 730 channels set to be auctioned at a reserve price of ₹784.87 crore.

4. Indian Navy to Lead Investigation into Damage of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Statue at Rajkot Fort
A joint technical committee, headed by the Indian Navy, will investigate the damage caused to the statue of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj at Rajkot Fort in Sindhudurg. The statue, which was unveiled in December 2023 as part of Navy Day celebrations, honors the Maratha Navy’s contribution to maritime defense and its historical significance. The committee will include officials from the Maharashtra government and technical experts. 

5. Union Cabinet Approves ₹4,136 Crore Assistance for Hydro-Electric Projects in North Eastern States
The Union Cabinet has greenlighted a scheme to provide financial assistance to state governments in the North Eastern region for the development of hydro-electric projects. With a budget of ₹4,136 crore, the scheme will run from 2024-25 to 2031-32 and aims to support the creation of 15,000 MW of cumulative hydro capacity. The initiative will involve the formation of a Joint Venture Company between Central Public Sector Undertakings and state governments to ensure smooth execution of these projects, enhancing energy security and development in the region.
International News in English for 30 August 2024

Time to look at the global scene with international news for 28 July 2024. See what’s making headlines around the world today.

1. India and Russia Strengthen Disaster Management Cooperation with 2025-2026 Working Plan
The second meeting of the Joint Russian-Indian Commission on Cooperation in the Field of Emergency Management was held in Moscow, Russia, led by Indian Minister of State for Home, Nityanand Rai, and his Russian counterpart, Kurenkov Aleksandr Vyacheslavovich. The two nations signed a working plan for disaster management collaboration during 2025-2026. This plan, aimed at enhancing joint emergency management strategies, follows previous agreements between the countries, such as the 2010 Inter-Governmental Agreement and the establishment of the Indo-Russian Joint Commission in 2013. The meeting also emphasized the continued exchange of best practices in disaster prevention and mitigation.

2. India Makes First Payment for Hybrid Power Projects in Northern Sri Lanka
India has made the first payment for hybrid power projects in northern Sri Lanka, aimed at meeting the energy needs of Delft, Nainativu, and Analaitivu islands. The payment was handed over by Indian High Commissioner Santosh Jha to Sri Lankan officials. Funded by an $11 million Indian grant, these projects are being developed under a 2022 MoU between the two countries. Combining solar and wind power, the initiative is expected to be completed by April 2025, marking a significant step in strengthening India-Sri Lanka energy cooperation.

3. Typhoon Shanshan Strikes Japan’s Kyushu Island, Triggering Emergency Response  
Typhoon Shanshan, Japan’s strongest typhoon of the year, made landfall on Kyushu Island, bringing with it torrential rain and winds of up to 252 kilometers per hour. The Japan Meteorological Agency has issued a rare special typhoon warning for most of Kagoshima Prefecture. The storm has already claimed three lives, injured nine, and caused significant disruption, including the suspension of central Japan bullet train services. Prime Minister Fumio Kishida has urged authorities to work closely with local governments to ensure the safety of residents through timely evacuations and response measures.

4. Telegram CEO Pavel Durov Charged in France Over Criminal Activity on the Platform  
Pavel Durov, the founder and CEO of Telegram, has been charged in France for allowing criminal activities such as the dissemination of child pornography, drug trafficking, and fraud on his messaging platform. Durov has been accused of refusing to cooperate with authorities, including declining to run wiretaps and share documents requested by law enforcement. The Russian-born billionaire, arrested in Paris, has been granted bail under the conditions that he remains in France and reports to police twice a week. In response, Telegram stated that Durov has “nothing to hide” and emphasized that the platform complies with European laws.

5. UAE Cancels US$20 Billion Fighter Jet Deal with France Amid Telegram CEO’s Arrest Fallout 
The United Arab Emirates (UAE) has cancelled its $20 billion deal with France for 80 Rafale fighter jets, following the arrest of Telegram CEO Pavel Durov in France. Durov, a UAE citizen, was detained on charges of financial crimes and cyber offences related to activities on Telegram. Although he has been released on bail, the diplomatic tensions between France and the UAE have escalated. Durov has consistently denied the allegations, attributing them to his refusal to allow Western governments backdoor access to Telegram. The cancellation of the fighter jet deal highlights the growing diplomatic strain between the two nations.
Sports News in English for 30 August 2024

Here’s your sports update with sports news in English for 26 August 2024. Find out what’s new in the world of sports today.


1. Bangladesh Wins First SAFF U-20 Championship After Defeating Nepal
Bangladesh emerged victorious in the SAFF U-20 Championship football tournament by defeating host Nepal 4-1 in the final, marking their first win in the tournament’s history. The competition, which began on August 18, 2024, saw participation from Sri Lanka, India, the Maldives, Bhutan, Nepal, and Bangladesh. India had previously won the championship in 2019, 2022, and 2023. The biennial event is organized by the South Asian Football Federation for male footballers under the age of 20.

2. India’s Para-Athletes Set to Compete at Paris 2024 Paralympics with Record Medal Aspirations
India’s para-athletes are gearing up for the 2024 Paris Paralympics, targeting a record-breaking 25 medals after their impressive 19-medal haul at the Tokyo Paralympics. India’s largest-ever contingent of 84 para-athletes will compete across various events. Today, Aruna Tanwar will compete in para taekwondo [K44-47kg], while Jyoti Gaderiya aims to advance in para cycling’s C1-3 3000m individual pursuit.

3. Speculation Grows Over Rohit Sharma’s Future in IPL Amid Franchise Changes
With the upcoming IPL mega auction, there is growing speculation about the future of Rohit Sharma, India’s T20 World Cup-winning captain. Rohit, who has led Mumbai Indians to five IPL titles, may face changes in his association with the franchise after playing under Hardik Pandya in the 2024 season. The decision to hand the captaincy to Pandya has sparked discussions on what’s next for the cricketing legend.

4. Max Verstappen Faces Setback in F1 Title Race Ahead of Italian Grand Prix
Max Verstappen heads to the Italian Grand Prix facing challenges as his previously dominant form has waned since his last win at the Spanish GP in June. A five-race drought and his recent defeat at his home GP, where he finished behind Lando Norris, have raised questions about his chances for a fourth straight world title. Red Bull’s dominance in Formula One also appears less certain, with McLaren showcasing superior performance on the grid.

5. Jacob Oram Appointed New Zealand’s Bowling Coach Ahead of India Series
Former New Zealand all-rounder Jacob Oram has been appointed as New Zealand’s new bowling coach. He will begin his role on October 7, with New Zealand’s three-match ICC World Test Championship series in India as his first assignment. Oram, who has over 200 international caps and has played in multiple ICC events, brings extensive experience to the team, having previously supported both New Zealand’s men’s and women’s teams as bowling coach.

Business News Today Headlines

Stay updated with today’s top business news. Discover the latest market trends, corporate developments, and economic updates shaping the business world. Keep informed about key financial changes and industry shifts.


1. SEBI Issues Show Cause Notice to Religare Enterprises Chairperson Rashmi Saluja
The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) has issued a show cause notice to Rashmi Saluja, Chairperson of Religare Enterprises, in connection with an investigation into alleged insider trading violations. The investigation focuses on trades made by Saluja on September 21 and 22, 2023, just days before the Burman family’s open offer for Religare Enterprises was announced on September 25, 2023. 

2. Gautam Adani Tops 2024 Hurun India Rich List
Gautam Adani, with a wealth of ₹11.6 lakh crore, has replaced Mukesh Ambani to secure the top spot in the 2024 Hurun India Rich List. This report highlights that India minted a new billionaire every five days last year, reflecting the nation’s emergence as Asia’s wealth creation engine. While China saw a 25% decline in its number of billionaires, India experienced a 29% increase, reaching a record 334 billionaires. 

3. Resourceful Automobile Makes Muted Stock Market Debut
Resourceful Automobile, a Delhi-based company, made a subdued debut on the stock market on August 29, with shares listing at ₹117, the same as the issue price, on the BSE SME platform. The company’s ₹12-crore public offer was significantly oversubscribed by 419 times, receiving bids for 40.76 crore shares against the 9.76 lakh shares available. Despite the oversubscription, the listing gains fell short of grey market expectations, where shares were trading at a premium of 89%.

4. Argentina Cuts Import Tariff to Cool Inflation
In an effort to cool inflation, Argentina is reducing a key import tariff, known as the PAIS tax, from 17.5% to 7.5%. This move comes as President Javier Milei balances conflicting economic goals, including controlling inflation and closing a fiscal deficit. The Milei administration initially raised the tax after taking office in December 2023 to boost federal revenues, but the higher tariff led to increased import prices.

5. Berkshire Hathaway Reaches $1 Trillion Market Capitalization
Berkshire Hathaway, led by Warren Buffett, has become the first non-technology company in the U.S. to reach a $1 trillion market capitalization. The Omaha-based conglomerate’s stock rose by 0.8% on Wednesday, closing at $696,502.02 per share, which pushed its market value past the $1 trillion mark.

Despite a 122% revenue increase, NVIDIA’s stock fell 7% after hours. The decline came as the company’s forecast fell short of investor expectations, despite strong demand for its AI technology and a $50 billion share buyback.

Comet C/2023 A3, initially thought to disintegrate, remains intact and bright. Discovered in China, it continues to shine and has been spotted by NASA’s spacecraft, soon becoming visible to the naked eye.

4. Stone Age People Used Advanced Techniques for Monumental ConstructionThe Dolmen de Menga burial mound, built around 3800-3600 BC in Spain, showcases advanced Stone Age engineering. With stones weighing up to 150 tonnes, it demonstrates remarkable construction techniques for its time.

5. China’s Export Restrictions Disrupt Global Semiconductor SupplyChina’s export restrictions on germanium and gallium have led to a surge in global prices and disrupted supply chains. These materials are crucial for semiconductors, highlighting China’s major influence in the global tech industry.

1. 1935: U.S. Congress Passes the Neutrality Act
The U.S. Congress passed the Neutrality Act to prevent American involvement in overseas conflicts by imposing restrictions on trade and travel with warring nations, aiming to keep the U.S. neutral in international disputes.

2. 1963: Hot Line Established Between U.S. and Soviet Union 
The United States and the Soviet Union established a direct communications link known as the “Hot Line” to prevent misunderstandings and reduce the risk of nuclear war during the Cold War.

3. 1975: The Collapse of the Watergate Investigation
The Watergate scandal saw a significant event on this day when key figures in the investigation were dismissed, leading to a major political crisis in the U.S. and the eventual resignation of President Richard Nixon.

4. 1984: First Successful Operation of a Commercial Satellite
The launch of the first commercial communications satellite marked a milestone in global telecommunications, expanding the reach of broadcast and communication services across continents.

5. 1999: India and Pakistan Agree to Ceasefire in Kargil Conflict
India and Pakistan agreed to a ceasefire to end the Kargil Conflict, a major military confrontation that took place in the Kargil district of Jammu and Kashmir, paving the way for peace talks between the two nations.
