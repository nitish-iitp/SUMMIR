National News Headlines for School Assembly 7 June 2024

Here are the top national news headlines for the school assembly 7 June 2024 –

India's representation of women in the Lok Sabha is lower than that of 150 other nations.
IMD: Monsoon is predicted to reach Mumbai in three to four days.
The leaders of the Cong want Rahul Gandhi, the hero of the LS polls, to lead the opposition.
Delhi court: Prolonged campaigns suggest Kejriwal is not afflicted with a fatal illness.
Adhir Chowdhury predicts "difficulties" for himself following Baharampur's defeat.
Following Nitish's endorsement of Modi, JD(U) proposes the Agniveer and caste census demands.
The jab at Devendra Fadnavis from Sanjay Raut is titled "Villain in Maharashtra politics."
Gandhi, Shivaji, and Ambedkar statues are moved within Parliament; the Cong responds.
The NHRC gives "Nata Pratha" significant consideration and notifies four states.
Kishori Lal Sharma states, "The people of Amethi gave me much more than I asked for."
Attendance of global dignitaries is expected in the Narendra Modi 3.0 oath event.
The Supreme Court orders Himachal Pradesh to provide excess water to Delhi right away.
Odisha: Four female candidates secure seats in the Lok Sabha.
Higher results in NEET: NTA were attributed to revisions in NCERT books and grace marks for tardiness.

Activists claim that the paramilitary RSF of Sudan raids a village and kills at least 100 people.

The US launches investigation into OpenAI, Microsoft, and Nvidia.
To be clear, Donalds never claimed that "the Black family was together" during the Jim Crow era.
The PM of Slovakia aims at opponents in his first speech following the shooting.
While negotiators negotiate a truce-hostage agreement, Israel attacks Gaza.
The WHO says that the first human death from bird flu was reported in Mexico.

NASA's "lightning fast" Boeing launch was captured in space.

Luka Modric, the legend of Real Madrid and Croatia, has a message ahead of Sunil Chhetri's last game for India.
