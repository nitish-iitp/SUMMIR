EOU, C-DAC join hands to prevent cybercrime in state

Patna: In a bid to enhance its capabilities in areas such as cybercrime prevention, cyber awareness, cyber security, technical cooperation and cyber forensics, the Economic Offences Unit (EOU) of Bihar Police on Wednesday signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with the Centre for Development of Advanced Computing (C-DAC), Patna.
The main objectives of the MoU include research in cybersecurity and forensics, development of software for cybersecurity and cybercrime, capacity building of researchers and officers, cybercrime investigation and capacity building in cyber forensics.
The MoU was signed by C-DAC director Aditya Kumar Sinha and EOU DIG Manavjit Singh Dhillon.
EOU ADG, Nayyar Hasnain Khan, said the signing of MoU was aimed at enhancing capabilities in cybercrime prevention, cyber awareness, cyber security, technical cooperation and cyber forensics.
“The MoU also covers developing and implementing advanced technology solutions, including artificial intelligence, data analysis, high-performance computing and advanced software solutions. It also includes the development of cyber-related infrastructure, networking, data storage and cyber security systems, as well as technical expertise and assistance,” Khan said.

It will also facilitate high-quality cyber training for police officers and help C-DAC experts understand new dimensions of cybercrime and cyber security, conduct research and develop solutions,” Khan added.
