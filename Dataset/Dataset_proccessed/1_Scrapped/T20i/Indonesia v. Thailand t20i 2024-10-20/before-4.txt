National News Headlines in English for 15 October 2024

The national news headlines section focuses on important events and updates happening across the country. It highlights key political developments, social issues, and significant achievements or incidents that affect the nation.

1. Indian Competition Commission Set to Penalize WhatsApp for Abusing Market Dominance
The Competition Commission of India (CCI) is likely to impose a penalty on WhatsApp for violating competition laws through its 2021 privacy policy update, which enabled data sharing with parent company Meta. The CCI’s investigation found that both WhatsApp and Meta abused their dominant position, prompting possible legal action.

2. President’s Rule Officially Revoked in Jammu and Kashmir, Clearing Path for New Government
The Union Home Ministry has withdrawn President’s rule in Jammu and Kashmir, as per a gazette notification signed by President Droupadi Murmu. The move, effective immediately, opens the way for the appointment of a new chief minister and the formation of a government in the union territory.

3. Competition Commission of India to Issue Order Against WhatsApp for Competition Law Violations
The Competition Commission of India (CCI) is preparing to take action against WhatsApp for violating competition laws related to a controversial 2021 privacy policy update, which allowed user data sharing with its parent company, Meta. This may lead to penalties for the messaging giant.

4. President’s Rule in Jammu and Kashmir Revoked, Allowing for New Government Formation
The Centre has lifted President’s rule in Jammu and Kashmir, enabling the appointment of a new Chief Minister. This decision, communicated through a notification by President Droupadi Murmu, is a significant step towards restoring local governance in the union territory.

5. Air India Flight Diverted to New Delhi After Bomb Threat; IndiGo Flights Also TargetedAn Air India flight from Mumbai to New York was diverted to New Delhi Airport due to a bomb threat. Following this, two IndiGo flights received similar threats. Authorities promptly implemented security protocols to ensure the safety of all passengers involved.

6. Draft Sports Governance Bill Proposes New Dispute Resolution Mechanism for Athletes
A proposed sports governance bill in India suggests that athletes and officials must resolve disputes through a new Appellate Sports Tribunal before approaching the courts. This change aims to streamline the dispute resolution process in sports governance.

7. RBI Governor Highlights Potential Volatility in Capital Flows Amid Global Monetary Policy Changes
RBI Governor Shaktikanta Das warned that varying global monetary policies, including tightening and easing measures, could lead to increased volatility in capital flows and exchange rates, posing challenges for the Indian economy.

8. G N Saibaba Remembered as a Dedicated Teacher and Human Rights Activist After His Acquittal
G N Saibaba, a former Delhi University professor, is being honored by friends and colleagues as a committed educator and tireless human rights advocate. After spending a decade in jail on Maoist-related allegations, he was acquitted earlier this year by the Bombay High Court.

9. NCPCR Urges Mandatory Disclaimers on OTT Platforms for Adult Content Accessibility
The National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR) has written to the Ministry of Information and Broadcasting, calling for mandatory disclaimers on OTT platforms. These disclaimers should inform viewers about legal implications if children access adult content, emphasizing child protection laws.

10. Prime Minister Internship Scheme Portal Sees Over 155,000 Registrations in One Day
The newly launched Prime Minister Internship Scheme portal has gained significant traction, registering 155,109 candidates within just one day, according to government sources. Announced by Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman in this year’s budget, the scheme aims to tackle youth unemployment by linking young talent with companies seeking skilled individuals.

International News in English for 14 October 2024

Explore the latest global stories and international news. Stay connected with significant events and developments happening around the world and understand their impact on global affairs.

1. Iranian and French Presidents Discuss Ceasefire in Hezbollah-Israel Conflict
Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian and French President Emmanuel Macron have engaged in talks aimed at resolving the ongoing conflict between Hezbollah and Israel, exploring potential avenues for a ceasefire.

2. Oil Prices Drop Amid China’s Declining Inflation and Economic Concerns
Oil prices fell on Monday, with Brent crude dropping 1.1% to $78.18 per barrel and U.S. West Texas Intermediate crude down 1.2% to $74.73 per barrel. The decline follows concerns about fuel demand in China, the world’s largest crude importer, after reports of falling inflation and uncertainty over economic stimulus plans.

Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co. (TSMC) is ramping up its global expansion by planning new chip manufacturing plants across Europe, focusing on the growing market for artificial intelligence (AI) chips, as stated by Wu Cheng-wen, Taiwan’s National Science and Technology Council Minister.

4. NASA’s Europa Clipper Mission Set to Launch in Search of Extraterrestrial Life
NASA is launching its Europa Clipper mission today, October 14, to explore Europa, a moon of Jupiter, for signs of extraterrestrial life. Originally scheduled for October 10, the launch was postponed due to Hurricane Milton, marking a significant step in understanding the habitability of other celestial bodies.

5. SpaceX Successfully Tests Starship Booster Recovery System
SpaceX achieved a remarkable feat as its Starship booster safely returned to the launch pad in Texas using innovative mechanical arms to catch the rocket mid-descent. This successful test is a major advancement towards creating a fully reusable spacecraft for future missions to the Moon and Mars.

The Indian stock market entered a phase of consolidation, experiencing moderate declines in the week ending October 11. The downturn was attributed to valuation concerns, a subdued outlook for Q2 FY25 earnings, and the recent victory of the BJP in Haryana polls, coupled with ongoing selling by foreign institutional investors (FIIs).

5. Domestic Institutional Investors Counter Foreign Selling in Stock Market
In October, domestic institutional investors (DIIs) have emerged as a stabilizing force in the Indian stock market, purchasing stocks worth ₹57,792 crore between October 1 and 11. This influx of capital comes as foreign portfolio investors (FPIs) retreated, selling shares amounting to ₹58,394 crore during the same period.

What Happened Today in History

This part highlights key events from history that took place on the current date. It connects students with important moments from the past, creating historical understanding and curiosity.

1. The Birth of the First Modern Computer (1945)
On October 15, 1945, the first electronic general-purpose computer, ENIAC (Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer), was completed. Developed by John W. Mauchly and J. Presper Eckert, this revolutionary machine marked the beginning of the digital age. ENIAC was capable of performing complex calculations much faster than any existing technology and paved the way for the development of modern computing.

2. International Day of Rural Women (2008)
In 2008, the United Nations declared October 15 as the International Day of Rural Women. This day recognizes the crucial role that rural women play in enhancing agricultural and rural development, improving food security, and eradicating rural poverty. It aims to promote gender equality and empower rural women, ensuring their voices and contributions are acknowledged globally.

3. The Launch of the First Successful Satellite, Sputnik 1 (1957)
On October 15, 1957, the Soviet Union launched Sputnik 1, the world’s first artificial satellite. This event marked the beginning of the space age and sparked the Space Race between the United States and the Soviet Union. Sputnik 1 transmitted radio pulses back to Earth, and its successful launch demonstrated the capabilities of space technology, leading to significant advancements in both space exploration and satellite communications.

4. The Formation of the United Nations International Drug Control Programme (1988)
On October 15, 1988, the United Nations established the International Drug Control Programme (IDCP) to combat the global drug problem. The IDCP aims to promote international cooperation in drug control and prevention, enhance the capacity of countries to address drug-related issues, and ensure that drugs are used for medical and scientific purposes. This initiative reflects the global commitment to reducing the impact of drug abuse on societies.

5. The Release of the First Major Version of Python (2000)
On October 15, 2000, Python 2.0 was released, introducing many features that shaped the future of programming. This version included list comprehensions, garbage collection, and support for Unicode, making Python more powerful and user-friendly. Python has since become one of the most popular programming languages in the world, widely used in web development, data analysis, artificial intelligence, and more.
