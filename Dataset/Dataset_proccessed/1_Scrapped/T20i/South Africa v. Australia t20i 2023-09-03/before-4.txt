  Narendra Modi Stadium  

The Narendra Modi Stadium is also formerly known as Motera Stadium. It is a cricket stadium located in Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel Sports Complex, Ahmedabad, Gujarat, India.

It is the most gigantic stadium in the globe, with a seating capability of 132,000 viewers. The stadium is operated by the Gujarat Cricket Association and functions as a medium for Test, ODI, T20I, FC, List-A, T20, and Indian Premier League cricket matches.

The stadium was first built in 1983 and was foremost reconditioned in 2006. It evolved as the frequent medium for international matches in the area. In 2015, the stadium was shut and destroyed before being fully reconstructed by February 2020, with an estimated cost of ₹800 crore (US$100 million).

Apart from cricket, the stadium has organized many events managed by the Government of Gujarat. It has organized matches during the 1987, 1996, and 2011 Cricket World Cups.

The stadium has organized 14 Tests, 27 ODIs, 6 T20s matches, and 2 IPL matches as of 2022.

On 24 February 2021, the stadium was again named as the Narendra Modi Stadium by the Gujarat Cricket Association after the present Prime Minister of India, Narendra Modi, a Gujarat resident, who was also the president of the Gujarat Cricket Association (2009–2014) and the chief minister of the state from 2001 – 2014.

It organized its first-ever pink ball test match on 24 February 2021 between India and England. The stadium will organize the final of the 2023 ICC World Cup on 19 November.

The Narendra Modi Stadium in Ahmedabad is a batting-friendly wicket. The average first innings score in T20Is at the stadium is 193. The teams batting first have succeeded in five of the eight matches played at the arena in the IPL 2023 season.

The pitch is predicted to be suitable for batting in the first innings. The ball arrives onto the bat well and there is not largely for the bowlers to accomplish. However, the pitch can slow down as the match moves, and the spinners can play with the pitch.
