National News Headlines in English for 13 June 2024

Stay updated with 13 June 2024 news headlines in English for school assembly. Learn about various sectors’ happenings in our country.

1. PM Modi Thanks World Leaders for Congratulatory Messages: 

Prime Minister Narendra Modi expressed gratitude to world leaders for their congratulatory messages upon assuming office for the third time. He replied to messages and calls on social media, affirming India’s commitment to enhancing bilateral ties with nations like Cuba and Paraguay.

2. Shashi Shekhar Vempati Joins BARC Board: 

Former Prasar Bharati CEO Shashi Shekhar Vempati has been appointed to the Broadcast Audience Research Council (BARC) board as an independent member. He brings extensive experience in media and technology to the role.

3. Lieutenant General Upendra Dwivedi to be Next Army Chief:

Lieutenant General Upendra Dwivedi has been appointed as the next Chief of the Army Staff, succeeding General Manoj Pande. With vast operational experience, he is poised to lead India’s armed forces effectively.

4. Constitution of India Pocket Edition Gains Popularity:

A pocket edition of the Constitution of India published by Eastern Book Company garnered attention during Lok Sabha polls, selling 5,000 copies within three months. Its accessibility and relevance resonate with voters.

5. N. Chandrababu Naidu Sworn in as Andhra Pradesh Chief Minister for Fourth Term:

Telugu Desam Party (TDP) leader N. Chandrababu Naidu took oath as the Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh for the fourth time on Wednesday (June 12). His victory comes after a resounding win for TDP in the state’s assembly elections, held concurrently with the general elections.

6. Renowned Sarod Maestro Rajeev Taranath Passes Away:

Sarod maestro Rajeev Taranath, renowned for his international performances, passes away at 91. His legacy in classical music leaves a lasting impact on the cultural heritage of India.

7. India Reports Human Case of Bird Flu: 

WHO confirms a human case of bird flu in India, raising concerns about public health and surveillance measures. Vigilance and prompt action are necessary to prevent further spread of the virus.

Russia and Belarus commence tactical nuclear weapons drills amid geopolitical tensions, signalling Moscow’s strategic stance in the region. The exercises underscore security concerns and regional dynamics.

Meta announces plans to utilise user data from Europe to train its artificial intelligence models, emphasising localization and cultural relevance. The move sparks discussions on data privacy and corporate responsibility.

Economist Harry Dent predicts a stock market downturn surpassing the Great Recession, citing prolonged monetary stimulus and market volatility. Investors brace for potential economic repercussions.

A UN investigation accuses Israel of committing crimes against humanity during the war in Gaza, highlighting violations of international law and humanitarian norms. The findings prompt calls for accountability and justice.

A boat capsizes off the coast of Yemen, resulting in the deaths of 49 migrants and leaving 140 others missing. The incident underscores the risks and challenges faced by migrants seeking safer shores.

Pre-events for International Yoga Day are organised in Bangladesh, promoting wellness and empowerment through yoga. The initiatives aim to foster community engagement and holistic well-being.

The Ministry of Information and Broadcasting announces the third edition of Antarashtriya Yoga Diwas Media Samman (AYDMS), honouring excellence in media across various categories. The awards celebrate creativity and innovation in journalism.

Sports News in English for 13 June 2024

Get the latest sports news in English and follow your favourite sports events:

1. Vishnu Saravanan Prepares for Second Olympics:

Vishnu Saravanan gears up for his second Olympics after securing an Olympic quota place in men’s one-person dinghy. His optimism and determination reflect India’s growing presence in international sailing.

2. Indian Deaf Cricket Team to Face England in T20 Series: 

The Indian Deaf Cricket Team embarks on its first bilateral T20 series against England, marking a significant milestone in disability sports. The series promotes inclusivity and diversity in cricket.

3. India to Host FIH Hockey Men’s Junior World Cup:

India secures hosting rights for the FIH Hockey Men’s Junior World Cup, underscoring its commitment to promoting hockey at the grassroots level. The tournament promises to showcase emerging talent on the global stage.

4. Roger Federer Receives Honorary Doctorate:

Tennis legend Roger Federer receives an honorary doctorate for his philanthropic work and contributions to the sport. His inspirational commencement speech resonates with students and fans worldwide.

5. Indian Tennis Players Secure Paris 2024 Olympic Quotas:

Rohan Bopanna and Sumit Nagal secure Paris 2024 Olympics quotas for India in tennis, highlighting the nation’s growing prowess in the sport. Their achievements inspire aspiring athletes across the country.

6. T20 World Cup 2024: India vs. USA Match:
The clash between Team India and Team USA in the T20 World Cup 2024 sparks excitement and anticipation. The match highlights cricket’s global appeal and showcases emerging talent on the international stage.

7. Erik Ten Hag Set to Remain as Manchester United Manager

Manchester United is reportedly set to retain manager Erik ten Hag, as indicated by sources close to the club. Despite speculation surrounding his future following a disappointing season, where United finished eighth in the Premier League, ten Hag is expected to continue leading the team into the 2024-25 season.

What Happened Today in History

Understand the significance of what happened today in history and learn how to make a difference:

1. 1944: D-Day Allies Capture Cherbourg

Allied forces captured the strategic port of Cherbourg during World War II, facilitating the supply of troops and equipment for the ongoing liberation of Europe from Nazi occupation.

2. 1966: Miranda Rights Established

The U.S. Supreme Court, in Miranda v. Arizona, ruled that suspects must be informed of their constitutional rights, including the right to remain silent and the right to legal counsel.

3. 1971: The New York Times v. United States

The U.S. Supreme Court upheld the First Amendment rights of the press in the landmark case, allowing The New York Times to publish the Pentagon Papers, a classified study of the Vietnam War.

4. 1983: Pioneer 10 Reaches Outer Solar System

NASA’s Pioneer 10 became the first spacecraft to reach the outer solar system, passing beyond the orbit of Neptune, providing valuable data about the region beyond the known planets.

5. 2000: Pope John Paul II Visits Ukraine

Pope John Paul II made a historic visit to Ukraine, strengthening ties between the Roman Catholic Church and the Eastern Orthodox Church, and promoting reconciliation and peace in the region.
