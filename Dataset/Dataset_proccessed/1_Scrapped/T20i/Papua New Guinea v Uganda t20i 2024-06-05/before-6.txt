ESPN PRESENTS EXCLUSIVE COVERAGE OF ICC MEN’S T20 WORLD CUP 2024
Cricket
Cricket Fans Can Catch All the Action Across ESPN's Platforms Starting Saturday, June 1 Through June 29
 Walter NapoliMay 31, 2024

ESPN is the exclusive home of the ICC Men’s T20 World Cup 2024 across the Caribbean. The anticipated tournament will be available live across ESPN’s linear and digital platforms starting with the Group Stage that begins Saturday, June 1, through the Final on Saturday, June 29.

In addition to live match coverage, ESPN will provide extensive news and information content including preview shows, highlights and replays. Coverage highlights include:

Live coverage of all 55 matches across ESPN, ESPN2 and ESPN Extra; all West Indies games will be air on ESPN beginning with their debut match against Papua New Guinea on Sunday, June 2 at 9:30 a.m. ET.
ESPN’s coverage will kick off with two preview shows – ICC Men’s T20 World Cup 2024: The Preview (Parts 1 & 2) – setting the stage for the marquee event. The shows are available on demand via the ESPN App.
ICC Men’s T20 World Cup 2024: The Experts’ View: A special show featuring analysis from experts will also be available on demand via the ESPN App.
Throughout the tournament, the Around the Wicket show, powered by ESPNCricinfo, will preview and review the biggest matches every Tuesday night.
Complete highlights and recap shows on demand after every game on the ESPN App and ESPN Player.
Fans can also relive some of the greatest T20 World Cup matches from past editions on the ESPN App.
ESPN CricInfo: coverage before, during, and after the ICC Men’s T20 World Cup:
Extensive video and written preview content, including interviews and profiles on leading players, team-by-team previews.
Two writers from the editorial team on the ground in the US and the West Indies through the tournament.
Top analysts and talent for live wrap-around shows around key matches.
Social media and video content from the venues and from edit teams in Mumbai and London.

The ICC Men’s T20 World Cup 2024 is set to be a historic event, with 20 teams competing across venues in the West Indies and the United States from June 1-29. This will be the first time an ICC global event is held in the Americas outside the West Indies. The defending champions England will aim to retain their title, while powerhouses like West Indies, India, Australia, and Pakistan will be among the favorites. The West Indies, co-hosts and two-time champions, will be a formidable force on home soil. Their star-studded squad includes the likes of Rovman Powell, Nicholas Pooran, and Andre Russell, making them a team to watch out for.
