Pitch Report

Â 

Out of the three venues, the Abu Dhabi wicket is perhaps the best batting surface. It is a well-balanced pitch with help for both pacers and spinners. However, batters need to be watchful of the early assistance for pacers. Spinners will also enjoy bowling in these conditions. The average first-innings score in the last six T20Is played here has been 131 runs.

Â 

Teams chasing have won 6 of the 8 matches played here during this T20 World Cup as the pitch tends to get a little better to bat on as the evening progresses. That being said, a first-innings score of around 170 runs can be a competitive one here.

Batters

KL Rahul has not been able to replicate his Indian T20 League form in this tournament. He has scored 1578 runs in 51 T20I matches at an average of 38.48 and a strike rate of 141.01, including 2 centuries.
Najibullah Zadran is a dangerous hard-hitting middle-order batter for Afghanistan. He bats with aggression and clears the long boundaries with a lot of ease. He has scored 88 runs in three matches at a strike rate of 133.33 and has a high score of 59 runs against Scotland.

All-rounder

Bowlers

Rashid Khan has been in excellent form with the ball for Afghanistan in this World Cup. He is hard to pick at times and is a genuine wicket-taking threat. He has taken 7 wickets in three matches at an average of 7 and an economy rate of just 4.74.
