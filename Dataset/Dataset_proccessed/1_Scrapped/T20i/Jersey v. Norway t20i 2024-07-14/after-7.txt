Stay updated with 18 July 2024 news headlines in English for school assembly. Learn about various sectors´ happenings in our country.


1. India Unveils e-Mobility R&D Roadmap to Lead Global Automotive Sector:

India’s Principal Scientific Adviser, Professor Ajay Kumar Sood, launched the “e-mobility R&D Roadmap for India” on July 16, 2024, in New Delhi. The roadmap outlines future technological needs in energy storage, EV aggregates, materials recycling, and charging infrastructure to achieve self-reliance in the next five years.

2. Defence Sector Sees Surge in Orders Post-Elections:

ICICI Securities reported a surge in defence orders after the Lok Sabha elections. HAL received an RFP for 156 LCH Prachand helicopters and will deliver the first Tejas Mk-1A this month. Companies like GRSE, BEL, Cochin Shipyard, and HAL secured significant orders totaling over Rs 7,350 crore.

3. Indiyaa Distribution Network Fined ₹10 Lakh for Misleading Ads:

The Central Consumer Protection Authority fined Indiyaa Distribution Network LLP ₹10 lakh for misleading advertisements of its joint-pain product, ‘Dhuandhar Oil’. This is part of broader actions against unethical practices by direct-selling firms.

4. Retail Investors Increase Stake in NSE-Listed Companies:

Retail investors and domestic mutual funds now own 38% of the floating stock on the NSE, up from 33.5% in June 2021. This data from the NSE’s ownership tracker highlights the growing participation of individual investors in the stock market.

5. Paytm Shares Drop After SEBI Warning on Transactions:

Paytm shares fell by 1.47% after SEBI issued a warning to One 97 Communications for exceeding transaction limits with Paytm Payments Bank in FY22. SEBI highlighted several non-compliances and viewed the violations seriously.

6. Ashoora-e-Muharram Observed with Reverence Across India:

Ashoora-e-Muharram was observed solemnly across India, commemorating the martyrdom of Hazrat Imam Hussain. Tazia processions and religious meetings marked the day, with ulemas discussing the Karbala incident. Extensive security measures were in place for the occasion.

7. India and Switzerland Strengthen Trade Ties with TEPA Talks:

Union Minister Piyush Goyal discussed the Trade and Economic Partnership Agreement with Swiss counterpart Guy Parmelin. Both sides highlighted TEPA’s role in enhancing trade and investment. Goyal encouraged Swiss companies to invest in India’s growing market during his two-day visit to Switzerland.

8. Indian Navy Launches THINQ2024 Quiz for Students:

The Indian Navy launched THINQ2024, a national quiz competition themed ‘Viksit Bharat.’ Open to students from Class 9 to 12, the quiz aims to foster intellectual growth and patriotism. Schools can register on the THINQ2024 website for the four-stage hybrid competition.

9. NITI Aayog Reorganized, New Members Inducted:

The government revised NITI Aayog’s composition, adding Agriculture Minister Shivraj Singh Chouhan as an ex-officio member. New members include HD Kumaraswamy and Jagat Prakash Nadda. Union Ministers Piyush Goyal and Anurag Thakur did not retain their seats.

10. Four Leaders Resign from NCP in Maharashtra Amid Internal Turmoil:

Four top leaders resigned from NCP in Maharashtra’s Pimpri Chinchwad, dealing a blow to Ajit Pawar’s faction. Ajit Gavhane, Yash Sane, Rahul Bhosale, and Pankaj Bhalekar are likely to join Sharad Pawar’s party, indicating a potential shift in political dynamics.

Stay updated with the state news headlines today and impactful stories unfolding across your state.


Uttar Pradesh:

1. UP Government Halts Digital Attendance for Teachers Amid Protests:

The Uttar Pradesh government suspended its digital attendance order for teachers following protests. An expert committee will review the issue and provide suggestions. The decision will be based on the committee’s report, addressing teachers’ concerns and potential improvements.

Tamil Nadu:

2. Tamil Nadu Gains as Gujarat’s Export Share Declines:

Tamil Nadu and Andhra Pradesh increased their share of India’s merchandise exports in FY24, while Gujarat and Maharashtra saw declines. Gujarat’s share dropped to 30.7% from 32.7% in FY23, and Maharashtra’s share decreased for the third consecutive year to 15.4%.

3. Heavy Rains Close Schools in Several Tamil Nadu Districts:

Due to heavy rains, Kozhikode, Palakkad, Idukki, Alappuzha, Kannur, Thrissur, Kottayam, and Wayanad districts declared a holiday for educational institutions on July 17. Exams will proceed as scheduled, but all other activities are suspended.

New Delhi:

4. Supreme Court Issues Notice on Manish Sisodia’s Bail Pleas:

The Supreme Court asked the CBI and ED to respond to AAP leader Manish Sisodia’s bail pleas by July 29 in the alleged liquor policy scam. Sisodia has been incarcerated for 16 months, and his trial has seen no progress since October last year.

West Bengal:

5. Calcutta HC Restrains Mamata Banerjee from Defaming Governor:

The Calcutta High Court restrained West Bengal CM Mamata Banerjee from making defamatory statements against Governor CV Ananda Bose until August 14, 2024. The court emphasized that freedom of speech does not permit defamation, and the case will be heard next on August 14.

International News in English for 18 July 2024

Explore global stories through international news in English. Discover updates from different countries:


1. Iran Open to Resuming Nuclear Deal Talks with US:

Iran’s acting foreign minister, Ali Bagheri-Kani, stated that Iran is open to resuming negotiations with the US to restore the nuclear agreement abandoned by the Trump administration in 2018. This comes ahead of Bagheri-Kani’s address to the UN Security Council.

2. Romania Approves Culling of 500 Bears Amid Overpopulation Concerns:

Romania’s parliament approved the culling of nearly 500 brown bears to address overpopulation and safety concerns following a deadly bear attack. The country has the largest brown bear population in Europe outside Russia, with recent years seeing multiple fatal attacks.

3. French Prime Minister Resigns Amid Political Gridlock:

French President Emmanuel Macron accepted the resignation of Prime Minister Gabriel Attal’s government, which will now serve in a caretaker capacity. The resignation follows a snap election resulting in a hung parliament, leaving parties scrambling to form a coalition.

4. Greece Orders Businesses to Halt Outdoor Work Amid Heatwave:

Greece has mandated businesses to stop heavy outdoor work during afternoon hours due to extreme heat. The measure, in effect from midday to 5:00 pm until July 19, affects builders, delivery riders, and others. Temperatures are expected to reach 43°C by Thursday.

5. Iran Open to Resuming Nuclear Deal Talks with US:

Iran’s acting foreign minister, Ali Bagheri-Kani, stated that Iran is open to resuming negotiations with the US to restore the nuclear agreement abandoned by the Trump administration in 2018. This comes ahead of Bagheri-Kani’s address to the UN Security Council.

6. Bangladesh Concludes Rath Yatra Festival with Ceremonial Pulling of Ulto Rath:

Bangladesh’s Rath Yatra festival ended with the ceremonial pulling of Ulto Rath ropes in Dhaka and Dhamrai. The festival, which began on July 7, featured processions, religious activities, and cultural events, drawing hundreds of devotees to celebrate Lord Jagannath’s return journey.

7. Violent Clashes in Bangladesh Quota Reform Movement Leave 3 Dead:

Bangladesh’s quota reform movement turned violent, resulting in three deaths and over 100 injuries. Protests disrupted traffic and train services in Dhaka and other cities. Clashes occurred between students, police, and political activists, escalating tensions across the country.

8. Japan to Boost Presence in Central Asia to Counter China:

Japan is strengthening its presence in Central Asia to counter China’s influence. The first Central Asia-Japan Summit will be held in August 2024, involving Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Uzbekistan, and Turkmenistan. This initiative aims to enhance cooperation and balance regional power.

9. New Website to Help Indian Students Find Internships in the US:

The Indian Consulate in New York launched a website to assist Indian students in finding internships in the US. Several companies have agreed to consider Indian students. The Consulate advises direct application to companies, as it has no role in selection.

10. IMF Raises India’s FY25 Growth Forecast to 7 percent: 

The IMF increased India’s growth forecast for FY25 to 7% from 6.8%, citing improved rural private consumption. The FY26 growth estimate remains at 6.5%. The global economy is expected to grow 3.2% this year, unchanged from April’s forecast.

Sports News in English for 18 July 2024

Get the latest sports news in English and follow your favourite sports events:


1. Colombian Football Federation President Arrested at Copa América Final:

Colombian Football Federation President Ramón Jesurún and his son were arrested after a chaotic Copa América final, charged with felony battery for altercations with stadium security officers in Miami Gardens.

2. Sumit Nagal Achieves Career-High ATP Ranking:

Sumit Nagal climbs to a career-best ATP ranking of world No. 68, marking a meteoric rise in 2024 with titles at the Chennai Open and Heilbronn Neckarcup, solidifying his status among India’s top male tennis players.

3. Indian Army Athletes Set for Paris Olympics Participation:

Thirteen Indian Army athletes gear up for the Paris Olympics across seven disciplines, showcasing exceptional skills and past international success, reinforcing the army’s sporting prowess on a global stage.

4. World Chess Announces ‘Clash of Claims 2’ Amid Controversy:

World Chess sparks controversy with ‘Clash of Claims 2’ sequel, renaming after criticism from GMs Vladimir Kramnik and Jose Martinez’s hybrid match drama, set to unfold in London amid heightened anticipation.

5. Indian Table Tennis Stars Seeded for Paris 2024 Olympics:

Sreeja Akula and Manika Batra lead India’s Paris 2024 Olympic table tennis campaign, seeded high with recent career highlights including WTT titles, poised to elevate India’s medal hopes on the global stage.

6. Colombian Football Federation President and Son Arrested After Copa América Chaos:

Ramón Jesurún and his son Ramon Jamil Jesurun were arrested post-Copa América chaos, facing charges for aggressive altercations with stadium security, highlighting tumultuous scenes following Argentina’s victory in Miami.

7. India Prepares for Champions Trophy After T20 World Cup Victory:

India focuses on the 2025 Champions Trophy after the T20 World Cup triumph, planning a strategic build-up with limited ODIs against Sri Lanka and England, navigating player rest post-IPL under new coach Gautam Gambhir.

8. Neeraj Chopra Aims for Gold at Paris Olympics:

Neeraj Chopra eyes Olympic gold in Paris after Tokyo triumph, backed by Leander Paes for another historic medal, setting high expectations for India’s javelin thrower and badminton star PV Sindhu.

9. PCB Requires Evidence from BCCI for Champions Trophy Participation:

PCB demands BCCI evidence on India’s Champions Trophy stance amid security concerns, hinting at possible venue shifts, including a proposed ‘Hybrid Model’ involving UAE, ahead of crucial ICC discussions.

10. Jarmanpreet Singh’s Rollercoaster Journey in Indian Hockey:

Jarmanpreet Singh reflects on a tumultuous hockey career, from promising beginnings and a Hockey India League stint to a doping ban setback, resilience in overcoming adversity to reclaim sporting aspirations.

Michel&Amazonka’s traditional Mongolian-themed uniforms for Team Mongolia go viral, hailed as Olympic winners by netizens. The fashion duo’s designs for the Paris Olympics ceremonies dominate online admiration and praise.

Byeon Woo Seok’s hit time slip drama, previously a sensation on Korean TV, arrives on Netflix on August 1. Fans rejoice as Netflix picks up the series, anticipating its global availability with fervent excitement.

4. Indian Cinema Shifts Focus from Star Power to Sleeper Hits:

Amidst challenges post-pandemic, Indian cinema witnessed a surge in sleeper hits like The Kerala Story and Manjummel Boys. These low-budget successes redefine box office success, challenging the dominance of star-driven blockbusters.

Kardashian sisters visit Mumbai, serving children at an ISKCON temple during Anant Ambani’s wedding. They depart after a weekend of cultural exploration, sharing moments with Jay Shetty and Aishwarya Rai Bachchan.
