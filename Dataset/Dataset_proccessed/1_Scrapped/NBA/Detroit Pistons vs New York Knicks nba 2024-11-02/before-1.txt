Dr. D : 11/2/2024 4:51 pm : link
I always felt that when Ewing was in his prime, the FO failed to surround him with enough talent to win a title. I think for most of those years, Jordan had a better supporting cast than Ewing had. Pippen was voted a top 75 player of all time (Horace Grant & the Worm were pretty good too). With all due respect, Starks and Oak were nice role players, but might not be in the top 750 of all time.

On the '93/94 team Starks was counted on to be the #2 scorer and we saw how that worked out vs Houston. On a real championship caliber team, Starks wouldn't have been counted on for that . 6th man or #3 or 4 with a better SF or PF, but not #2.

'93/94 finished 57-25 reg season.
Ewing 24.5 ppg
Starks 19 (inefficient pts many nights)
Oak 11.9 (pretty 2 dimensional)
C. Smith 10 (don't get me started)
Rivers 7.5
The team averaged 98.5 ppg

Starks might not be starting on the '24 team and he wouldn't have been asked to keep shooting on as horrible a night as he had vs. Houston in the finals. The current team has other guys to go to.

By '98/99, Ewing was past his prime, as was LJ.

Ewing 17.3 ppg
Sprewell 16.4
Houston 16.3
LJ 12.0
Ward 7.6
Camby 7.2

It was a shortened season and they finished 27-23 in reg season, averaged 86.4 pts/game.

I think with time, the '24 team will be a good defensive team, relatively comparable to the '90s teams, but the O is far superior.

This team has 4-5 good shooters (counting Deuce), 3 or 4 of them can score 20+ efficiently on any given night. The 90s teams didn't have that. We'd have at least 1 championship if they did. I know the current team doesn't have a title yet either, but I'm more optimistic about that than I ever was during the 90s.
