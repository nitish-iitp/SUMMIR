History
The Cleveland Cavaliers extended their win streak to a franchise-record 10 games, becoming a dominant force in the NBA. Evan Mobley's performances have been pivotal, establishing him as a reliable three-point shooter and a key component of the Cavaliers' offensive strategy. The team's depth and coach Kenny Atkinson's effective workload management have kept key players rested for the playoffs. This strategic approach has helped Cleveland maintain the top seed in the Eastern Conference and showcase their potential as championship contenders. Despite missing Luka Doncic and Kyrie Irving, the Dallas Mavericks faced a challenging 119-104 loss to the Cavaliers. The Cavaliers' ability to win in various ways further establishes their dominance in the league.

Kenny Atkinson has implemented a strategic workload management system that has allowed the Cavaliers' stars to play significantly fewer minutes, potentially benefiting them in the playoffs. Donovan Mitchell, Evan Mobley, and Darius Garland are averaging career-low minutes, with only one player exceeding 31 minutes per game.
Evan Mobley credited his recent improvement in three-point shooting to increased confidence rather than changes in plays or strategy, indicating a positive development in his overall game.
During a four-game road trip, the Cavaliers not only secured victories but also demonstrated their ability to win in different styles against strong Western Conference teams, highlighting their versatility as championship contenders.
