Indiana Pacers (18-18, eighth in the Eastern Conference) vs. Brooklyn Nets (13-22, 12th in the Eastern Conference)

New York; Monday, 7:30 p.m. EST

BOTTOM LINE: Brooklyn takes on Indiana looking to end its five-game home slide.

The Nets are 7-18 in conference matchups. Brooklyn gives up 113.5 points to opponents and has been outscored by 5.8 points per game.

The Pacers are 10-14 against Eastern Conference opponents. Indiana ranks fifth in the Eastern Conference scoring 50.7 points per game in the paint led by Pascal Siakam averaging 9.0.

The Nets are shooting 45.1% from the field this season, 2.6 percentage points lower than the 47.7% the Pacers allow to opponents. The Pacers average 12.9 made 3-pointers per game this season, 0.7 fewer makes per game than the Nets give up.

TOP PERFORMERS: D'Angelo Russell is averaging 12.4 points and five assists for the Nets.

Tyrese Haliburton is averaging 18.3 points and 8.8 assists for the Pacers.

LAST 10 GAMES: Nets: 3-7, averaging 101.0 points, 42.3 rebounds, 23.5 assists, 7.7 steals and 3.0 blocks per game while shooting 42.5% from the field. Their opponents have averaged 110.5 points per game.
