Boston Celtics respond to recent skid with emphatic, 142-104 blowout of Pacers
Justin Quinn, Celtics Wire
Fri, Dec 27, 2024¬∑2 min read

The Boston Celtics dropped their first pair of games back to back since April 2024 against the Orlando Magic and Philadelphia 76ers early this week, so it was no surprise to see the Celtics come out locked on defense in their home tilt against the Indiana Pacers on Friday (Dec. 27) night. The Pacers kept it close until a 15-2 run in the first quarter broke the game open for Boston.

The destruction continued apace in the game's second frame, with Boston going up by as much as 25 points as the team regained its stroke from deep and the ball movement to make it happen. The Celtics carried a 22-point lead into the penultimate frame, and would push it to 32 points before the fourth quarter began.

The final frame saw the starters get pulled at about the halfway mark as Boston pulled too far ahead to lose, the Celtics ultimately winning 142-104 to get back on track with their highest-scoring game of the season to date.

Celtics standouts
Dec 27, 2024; Boston, Massachusetts, USA; Boston Celtics guard Jaylen Brown (7) passes the ball against the Indiana Pacers in the first quarter at TD Garden. Mandatory Credit: David Butler II-Imagn Images

Star forward Jaylen Brown led all scorers with 44 points, 5 rebounds, 3 assists, and 4 steals. Costar on the wing Jayson Tatum added 22 points, 13 boards, 4 assists, and 3 steals, and veteran forward Al Horford chipped in 13 points, 5 rebounds, 3 assists, and 1 block.

Reserve guard Payton Pritchard put up 18 points, 8 boards, 10 assists, and 1 steal to lead the bench, and Jordan Walsh added 9 points and 5 rebounds.

Pacers standouts
Dec 27, 2024; Boston, Massachusetts, USA; Boston Celtics guard Derrick White (9) blocks the shot of Indiana Pacers guard Tyrese Haliburton (0) in the second quarter at TD Garden. Mandatory Credit: David Butler II-Imagn Images
More

Star Pacers floor general Tyrese Haliburton put up a team-high 19 points, along with 9 assists. Bennedict Mathurin added 18 points and 6 boards, and Pascal Siakam put up 14 points and 9 rebounds among the starters.

Off Indy's bench, Jarace Walker (15 points, 5 assists) and Thomas Bryant (12 points, 7 boards) played well also.

Team stats of note
Dec 27, 2024; Boston, Massachusetts, USA; The Boston Celtics bench reacts after guard Jaylen Brown (7) shoot a three point basket against the Indiana Pacers in the second half at TD Garden. Mandatory Credit: David Butler II-Imagn Images
More

The Celtics shot 51.1% from the floor to the Pacers being held to 41.8% overall, just above what Boston shot from deep (40.4%) compared to Indiana (just 28.2%).

Boston also won the battle of the boards, grabbing 52 to Indy's 39, and racked up 31 assists to the Pacers' 26.

The Celtics next play the Pacers in a home-and-home miniseries on Sunday, Dec. 29, 2024.
