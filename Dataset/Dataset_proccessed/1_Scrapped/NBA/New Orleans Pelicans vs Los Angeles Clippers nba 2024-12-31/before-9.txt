History
The Los Angeles Clippers secured victories against both the New Orleans Pelicans and the Golden State Warriors, showcasing strong performances despite missing star player Kawhi Leonard. Norman Powell's 35 points were crucial in the Clippers' narrow 116-113 win over the Pelicans, where James Harden's decisive free throws in the final minute sealed the game. Against a shorthanded Warriors team without Steph Curry and Draymond Green, the Clippers won 102-92, with Powell scoring 26 points and Ivica Zubac contributing 17 points and 11 rebounds. The Clippers have maintained a strong defensive stance and have been relying on key players like Harden and Powell during Leonard's absence. With these wins, the Clippers have improved their standing, continuing their successful streak even as Leonard prepares to return. The Warriors, on the other hand, are struggling, having lost six of their last seven games, and face challenges with key players sidelined.
