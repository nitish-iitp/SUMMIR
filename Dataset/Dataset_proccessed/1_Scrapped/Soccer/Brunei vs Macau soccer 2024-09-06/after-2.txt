1. Congress Criticizes SEBI’s Handling of Adani Group Investigation
The Congress party has sharply criticized the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) for its approach to the ongoing investigation into the Adani Group. Congress alleges that SEBI has mishandled accusations against two Mauritius-based Foreign Portfolio Investors (FPIs), who are implicated in a report by Hindenburg Research from January 2023. These FPIs have been accused of violating foreign investor norms and playing a role in Adani Group’s attempts to evade SEBI regulations. The controversy escalated when the FPIs petitioned the Securities Appellate Tribunal to seek relief from complying with SEBI’s new investor guidelines, which are set to take effect on September 9th.

2. Health Ministry Releases Annual Report on India’s Health Infrastructure and Human Resources
Union Health Secretary Apurva Chandra released the 2022-23 edition of Health Dynamics of India in New Delhi. The publication provides extensive data on health infrastructure and human resources as of March 31st, assisting stakeholders in effective health planning and management. Chandra emphasized the integration of the Health Management Information System (HMIS) with other ministry portals, which will reduce the workload of health functionaries.

3. 54th GST Council Meeting Tackles Key Economic Issues
The 54th GST Council meeting, chaired by Union Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman, took place in New Delhi. Key discussions included uniform GST rates for steel, iron, and aluminum milk cans, and the exemption of GST on services provided by the Indian Railways, such as platform tickets and retiring rooms.

4. Integrated Defence Staff Conducts First Tri-Service Review Programme
The Integrated Defence Staff has begun its first Combined Operational Review and Evaluation Programme for Major General and equivalent officers of the Tri-Services in Delhi. The programme focuses on fostering jointness and preparing for future threats through panel discussions and lectures from eminent speakers in various fields.

5. Rashtriya Poshan Maah Campaign Reports Success
The seventh Rashtriya Poshan Maah campaign has reported over 1.79 crore activities so far. This month-long campaign emphasizes nutrition and environmental sustainability, with key themes including anemia, growth monitoring, and complementary feeding. The campaign, which will continue until September 30th, actively engages various districts across India.

International News in English for 10 September 2024

Moving on, let us review the International News in English for 10 September 2024. This segment will cover significant global events and developments.

1. WHO Chief Urges Global Action to Address Sudan’s Severe Crisis Amid Famine and Displacement
Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus, head of the World Health Organization (WHO), expressed profound shock over the inadequate global response to the crisis in Sudan. After visiting a children’s hospital in Port Sudan, Ghebreyesus highlighted the dire humanitarian situation, including famine, mass displacement, and disease. Despite recent guarantees from peace talks in Switzerland to improve aid access, the absence of the Sudanese army from negotiations has impeded progress. Ghebreyesus called for urgent international intervention to address the “perfect storm of crises” affecting millions in Sudan.

2. Senator Bernie Sanders Calls for Overhaul of Wealth Tax System to Address Growing Inequality
In a recent interview on NBC’s “Meet the Press,” Senator Bernie Sanders reiterated his criticism of the U.S. wealth tax system, targeting the “billionaire class.” Sanders criticized the capital gains tax structure, which he argues unfairly benefits the wealthy, and urged Vice President Kamala Harris to consider increasing her proposed 28% tax rate on long-term capital gains. He stressed the need for a tax system that ensures the wealthiest individuals pay their fair share to combat the widening wealth gap.

3. South Korea Hosts International Summit to Develop Guidelines for Military Use of Artificial Intelligence
South Korea has convened a two-day international summit in Seoul to establish responsible practices for deploying artificial intelligence (AI) in military applications. Represented by over 90 countries, including the U.S. and China, the summit addresses the dual-edged nature of AI in defense. South Korean Defence Minister Kim Yong-hyun highlighted the potential of AI to enhance military capabilities while also posing risks if misused, citing recent Ukrainian advancements with AI-enabled drones in the Russia-Ukraine conflict.

4. Sri Lankan Election Commission Implements Campaign Restrictions Ahead of Presidential Polls
The National Election Commission of Sri Lanka has summoned presidential candidates and their representatives to discuss the upcoming election. Chairman R.M.A.L. Ratnayake outlined new regulations, including restrictions on door-to-door campaigning, limiting participants to a maximum of five individuals. The commission emphasized the need for legal action against any groups exceeding this limit. The 2024 Presidential Election is set for September 21, with the commission also planning a separate discussion with election monitoring organizations.

5. Algerian President Tebboune Declared Re-Elected Amid Allegations of Electoral Irregularities 
Algerian authorities have declared President Abdelmadjid Tebboune the winner of the recent election with 95% of the vote, avoiding a runoff. However, rival candidate Abdelaali Hassani Cherif has accused officials of manipulating results and failing to deliver vote-sorting records. With a voter turnout of 48%, concerns about the election’s integrity have been raised, despite electoral commission head Mohammed Charfi’s assurances of transparency and fair competition
Sports News in English for 10 September 2024

Catch up on the latest sports news today. From thrilling match results and player achievements to major events and updates across various sports, stay informed about all the exciting developments in the world of sports.

1. Jannik Sinner Secures Historic US Open Victory, Becoming First Italian Man to Win the Title
Jannik Sinner achieved a major milestone by winning the US Open Tennis Championship, defeating Taylor Fritz with scores of 6-3, 6-4, 7-5. This victory, Sinner’s second Grand Slam title of the year following his Australian Open win, makes him the first Italian man to capture the US Open crown. The triumph further cements his position as the world number one, marking his 55th match win and sixth title of the season. Fritz, the home favorite, expressed his disappointment after a strong tournament run, having previously not progressed past the quarterfinals at a major final.

2. India Announces Squad for Test Series Against Bangladesh, with KL Rahul and Rishabh Pant Returning
India has revealed its squad for the forthcoming two-match Test series against Bangladesh, starting on September 19 at Chennai’s MA Chidambaram Stadium. KL Rahul and Rishabh Pant make notable comebacks, with Pant returning to Test cricket for the first time since December 2022 after recovering from a severe car accident. Left-arm pacer Yash Dayal also earns his maiden Test call-up. Bangladesh enters the series with momentum from a historic 2-0 clean sweep over Pakistan, while India looks to build on its recent 4-1 series win over England.

3. US Soccer Star Alex Morgan Retires After Final Game, Reflects on a Remarkable Career
Alex Morgan, the iconic US soccer player, played her final match on Sunday, marking the end of an illustrious career. The 35-year-old striker, who announced her retirement alongside news of her second pregnancy, played the first 13 minutes of the San Diego Wave’s 4-1 loss to North Carolina Courage in the National Women’s Soccer League. Morgan expressed her gratitude to teammates and fans, reflecting on a career filled with significant achievements and memorable moments.

4. Paris Paralympics 2024 Closes with Spectacular Ceremony, Sets New Standards for Future Games
The Paris Paralympics 2024 concluded with a dazzling closing ceremony at Stade de France, featuring a vibrant performance by 24 French electronic music artists despite heavy rain. The event, attended by 4,400 athletes from 168 delegations, showcased a dynamic set from 20 DJs and was introduced by electronic music pioneer Jean-Michel Jarre. The ceremony marked the passing of the Paralympic flag from Paris Mayor Anne Hidalgo to Los Angeles Mayor Karen Bass, as the city prepares to host the next Paralympic Summer Games in 2028. IPC President Andrew Parsons praised the Paris Games for setting new benchmarks in competition, organization, and gender parity.

5. Indian Boxer Deepali Thapa Becomes First Asian Schoolgirl Champion, Leading India to Seven Female Titles
Indian boxer Deepali Thapa made history as the first-ever Asian Schoolgirl Champion, securing the 33 kg title at the Asian Championships in Al Ain, UAE. Thapa’s victory was part of a remarkable performance by Indian female boxers, who captured a total of seven titles. Notable wins included Bhoomi in the 35 kg category, Nischal Sharma in the 37 kg, Rakhi in the 43 kg, Naitik in the 52 kg, and Trushana Vinayak Mohite in the 67 kg. Gurseerat Kaur claimed the final title, culminating a successful day for Indian boxing.
Business News Today Headlines

Stay updated with today’s top business news. Discover the latest market trends, corporate developments, and economic updates shaping the business world. Keep informed about key financial changes and industry shifts.


1. Foreign Investment in Indian Capital Markets Surges to Nearly ₹11,000 Crore in September
Foreign investors have injected nearly ₹11,000 crore into Indian capital markets in the first week of September. According to depositories data, Foreign Portfolio Investors (FPIs) contributed ₹10,978 crore to equity markets and ₹367 crore to debt markets, bringing the total net investment for the month to ₹11,345 crore. This substantial influx follows three months of consistent net inflows. So far this year, FPIs have invested ₹1.6 lakh crore in Indian markets, including ₹53,859 crore in equities and ₹1.09 lakh crore in debt.

2. Electric Two-Wheeler Manufacturer Ather Energy Plans ₹3,100 Crore IPO Amid Rivals’ Success
Ather Energy has unveiled plans for an initial public offering (IPO) valued at ₹3,100 crore, as detailed in its draft red herring prospectus. This move follows the successful listing of rival Ola Electric. The IPO will feature a fresh issue of shares and an offer for sale of up to 22 million shares by existing investors and promoters. Notably, Tarun Sanjay Mehta and Swapnil Babanlal Jain will each sell 1 million equity shares, while Caladium Investment Pte Ltd, National Investment and Infrastructure Fund II, and 3State Ventures Pte. Ltd. are among the corporate shareholders participating in the offer.

3. Transport Minister Predicts Equal Cost for Petrol, Diesel, and Electric Vehicles Within Two Years
Transport Minister Nitin Gadkari has forecast that the cost of petrol and diesel vehicles will equal that of electric vehicles (EVs) within the next two years. Speaking at the 64th ACMA annual session, Gadkari expressed no objections to subsidies for EVs, despite previously suggesting they were no longer necessary due to decreased production costs and increased consumer adoption. Last year, EVs held a 6.3% market share in India, reflecting a 50% increase from the previous year.

4. China’s Consumer Inflation Hits 6-Month High Due to Rising Food Costs Amid Economic Pressures
China’s consumer inflation accelerated to a six-month high of 0.6% in August, driven primarily by increased food prices resulting from extreme weather events rather than a rebound in domestic demand. The rise in consumer price index (CPI) reflects the impact of severe weather on agricultural produce, while deflation in producer prices and ongoing economic challenges, including a housing downturn and rising trade tensions, exert additional pressure on the world’s second-largest economy.

5. GST Council to Discuss Taxation of Insurance Premiums and Online Gaming in Upcoming Meeting
The Goods and Services Tax (GST) Council is preparing for a critical meeting to address the taxation of insurance premiums and online gaming. This meeting could have significant implications for both sectors and broader economic conditions. Currently, health insurance premiums are taxed at 18%, with discussions underway about potentially implementing a uniform lower GST rate across all insurance types, including life, health, and general insurance. Details on these discussions will emerge following the council’s deliberations.

1. Apple’s ‘It’s Glowtime’ Event Set for September 9, 2024: What to Expect
Apple is set to unveil its latest innovations at the ‘It’s Glowtime’ event on September 9, 2024. Traditionally, Apple’s September events have focused on introducing new flagship devices, such as the latest iPhones and Apple Watches. Although major updates for Macs and high-end iPads typically follow later in the year, usually in October or November, this event is expected to follow the same pattern. Attendees can anticipate announcements about new iPhone models and potentially updated Apple Watches. Stay tuned for more details about the upcoming innovations.

2. Elon Musk Outlines Ambitious Mars Colonization Timeline on X
Elon Musk has outlined a bold vision for human settlement on Mars in a series of posts on X. This follows Bill Ackman’s call for a health-focused ad campaign. Musk emphasized that reducing the cost of transporting payloads to Mars is crucial for building a self-sustaining city. Currently, it costs about a billion dollars per ton to land payloads on Mars, but Musk aims to lower this to $100,000 per ton. He acknowledged the challenge, describing it as “extremely difficult but not impossible,” highlighting the need for significant technological advancements.

3. Amazon Great Indian Festival Set to Offer Major Discounts on Smartphones and More
The Amazon Great Indian Festival is approaching, with significant discounts expected on high-end smartphones and other products. The sale is anticipated to start a few days earlier for Prime subscribers and include specific deals for bank card users, along with daily lightning deals. Last year’s event featured discounts on popular models such as the iPhone 13, iPhone 14, and Samsung Galaxy S23 series. The upcoming festival promises similar offers and more, providing an excellent opportunity for shoppers to snag bargains on top tech products.

4. Foreign Investment in Indian Capital Markets Reaches Nearly ₹11,000 Crore in September
Foreign investors have injected nearly ₹11,000 crore into Indian capital markets in the first week of September. According to depositories data, Foreign Portfolio Investors (FPIs) contributed ₹10,978 crore to equity markets and ₹367 crore to debt markets, bringing the total net investment for the month to ₹11,345 crore. This follows three months of consistent inflows. Year-to-date, FPIs have invested ₹1.6 lakh crore in Indian markets, with ₹53,859 crore in equities and ₹1.09 lakh crore in debt.

5. Adani Group Warns of Unsustainable Dues from Bangladesh’s Godda Power Project
The Adani Group has alerted Bangladesh’s new interim government about a growing backlog of dues, totaling $500 million, from the Godda power project. Adani Power described the situation as “unsustainable,” despite ongoing dialogues with the Bangladesh government. The company has continued to meet its supply commitments and obligations to lenders and suppliers. The new interim government, led by Nobel laureate Muhammad Yunus, faces the challenge of revitalizing the economy following the ouster of former Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina.
Today’s Entertainment News in English

Turning to entertainment, we’ll cover the latest buzz from the world of movies, music, and celebrities. Catch up on the exciting news and stories making waves in the entertainment industry:

1. Toronto Premier of “Harbin” Draws Enthusiastic Fans and Korean Stars 
Movie enthusiasts braved long waits outside the Toronto International Film Festival (TIFF) for a chance to see Korean actors Lee Dong-Wook and Hyun Bin at the premiere of “Harbin” on Sunday. The historical drama, which explores the resistance efforts of Korean fighters against Japanese rule in the early 20th century, features both actors making their TIFF debut. Directed by Woo Min-ho, known for “The Man Standing Next,” “Harbin” follows a courageous resistance army planning an assassination in Harbin, China. Lee Dong-Wook expressed his gratitude for the warm reception, marking his first appearance at TIFF.

2. Anupam Kher Makes Directorial Comeback with “Tanvi The Great
Anupam Kher is returning to directing after 22 years with the film “Tanvi The Great,” and he’s enlisted Game of Thrones star Iain Glen for the project. The collaboration has generated significant buzz, particularly after a photo of the two actors was leaked. Kher, who previously worked with Glen on the British series “Mrs Wilson,” expressed his excitement about Glen’s involvement and praised his theatrical prowess. The film marks a significant moment in Kher’s career as he steps back into the director’s chair.

3. Thalapathy Vijay’s “The GOAT” Faces Mixed Reception and Extended Cut Possibility
Thalapathy Vijay’s new film, “The Greatest of All Time (The GOAT),” directed by Venkat Prabhu, premiered last Thursday but struggled to attract audiences outside Tamil Nadu. Prabhu revealed that the original director’s cut of the film is an impressive 3 hours and 40 minutes long. Although it’s not confirmed, there are hints that this extended version might be released on OTT alongside the theatrical cut. The movie, produced by AGS Entertainments, features a stellar cast including Sneha, Meenakshi Chaudhary, and Prabhudeva, with music by Yuvan Shankar Raja.

4. Venice Film Festival 2024 Concludes with Major Awards
The 81st Venice Film Festival wrapped up on September 7 with Spain’s Pedro Almodovar taking home the Golden Lion for his pro-euthanasia film “The Room Next Door.” Nicole Kidman and French actor Vincent Lindon received the acting awards. The Grand Jury Prize was awarded to the Italian film “Vermiglio,” directed by Maura Delpero, which explores the impact of World War II on a mountain village. The festival showcased a diverse range of international cinema and celebrated significant contributions to the film industry.

5. Stree 2″ Surpasses Box Office Records in India
Amar Kaushik’s horror comedy “Stree 2,” starring Shraddha Kapoor and Rajkummar Rao, has quickly become a major hit. After surpassing the Hindi version of SS Rajamouli’s “Baahubali 2: The Conclusion,” “Stree 2” has now overtaken Siddharth Anand’s spy thriller “Pathaan” to become the second highest-grossing Hindi film in India. The film has amassed a domestic box office collection of ₹516.25 crore nett, trailing only behind Shah Rukh Khan’s blockbusters “Jawan” and “Pathaan.”

What Happened Today in History

Let’s take a moment to remember important events that happened on this day in history. Discover significant milestones and historical moments from the past:

1. 1897 – First DWI Arrest in History
On September 10, 1897, George Smith became the first person arrested for driving under the influence after crashing his cab in London. This marked the beginning of stricter traffic laws worldwide.

2. 1919 – Treaty of Saint-Germain Signed
The Treaty of Saint-Germain was signed on September 10, 1919, officially dissolving the Austro-Hungarian Empire after World War I. It redefined Europe’s political map and paved the way for the creation of new nations.

3. 1960 – OPEC Established
On September 10, 1960, the Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC) was formed in Baghdad. The founding members—Saudi Arabia, Venezuela, Kuwait, Iraq, and Iran—came together to regulate oil production and prices globally.

4. 1977 – Last Execution by Guillotine in France
On September 10, 1977, Hamida Djandoubi became the last person to be executed by guillotine in France. His death was the final use of the historic execution method before the country abolished capital punishment in 1981.

5. 2008 – CERN’s Large Hadron Collider Activated
The Large Hadron Collider, the world’s largest particle accelerator, was activated by CERN on September 10, 2008. This groundbreaking event aimed to explore fundamental questions of particle physics, including the search for the Higgs boson.
