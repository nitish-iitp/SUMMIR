Kenya’s 24-Man squad announced for AFCON 2025 Qualifiers against Cameroon
Kenya’s 24-Man squad announced for AFCON 2025 Qualifiers against Cameroon
SPORTS
By

SAMSON JURA


October 10, 2024
Share it    
Topics:

Head coach Engin Firat has revealed Kenya’s final 24-man squad ahead of their crucial 2025 Africa Cup of Nations (AFCON) qualifiers against Cameroon. The two-leg fixtures will take place on October 11 and October 14, 2024, in what is expected to be fierce competition.

Among the standout selections, Alfred Scriven makes a much-anticipated return to the national team after a long absence, while Adam Wilson receives his second international call-up, adding depth to the attacking options.

Match Schedule
First Leg: Friday, October 11, 2024, at Ahmadou Ahidjo Omnisports Stadium in Yaoundé, Cameroon.
Second Leg: Monday, October 14, 2024, at Mandela National Stadium in Uganda.
Kenya’s Final 24-Man Squad
Goalkeepers:

Ian Otieno,Patrick Matasi,Byrne Omondi,

Defenders:

Alphonce Omija, Daniel Anyembe, Amos Nondi, Sylvester Owino, Johnstone Omurwa, Collins Sichenje,Joseph Okumu,Geoffrey Ochieng,Eric Ouma

Midfielders:

Christine Erambo,Richard Odada,John Ochieng,Timothy Ouma,Anthony Akumu,Duke Abuya,Ronney Onyango

Forwards:

John Avire,Michael Olunga,Jonah Ayunga,Adam Wilson,Alfred Scriven

Key Highlights
Alfred Scriven returns to the squad, bringing added experience to the forward line.
Adam Wilson, who recently broke into the national team setup, earns his second call-up, further showcasing his potential.
The squad is led by captain Michael Olunga, who remains a key figure in Kenya’s attack.
Focus on Cameroon

The double-header against Cameroon is crucial for Kenya’s hopes of qualifying for the 2025 AFCON. The team will be relying on a blend of experienced players like Michael Olunga and rising stars such as Alfred Scriven and Adam Wilson to secure positive results in these fixtures.
