Indomitable Lions : Intense Focus As Senegal Clash Approaches
By Dilian WELLENG 18 Janv. 2024 11:13 0 Likes 
  

The Cameroonian team had another closed-door training session yesterday in preparation for the crucial day-two encounter which comes up tomorrow January 19, 2024 in Yamoussoukro.

The Indomitable Lions of Cameroon went back to training on Tuesday, January 16, 2024 just a day after their 2023 Africa Cup of Nations (AFCON) opening draw against Guinea. After a tight 1-1 stalemate, Coach Rigobert Song wasted no time in refocusing his troops on the next challenge: a mouthwatering clash against Senegal billed for tomorrow Friday, January 19, 2024 at the Charles Konan Banny Stadium in Yamoussoukro.
The session was a closed-door one, but information posted on the official Facebook page of the Lions indicates that recovery was the focus for players who battled it out against Guinea, while the substitutes honed their skills in dedicated exercises. Key figures Vincent Aboubakar, who is still nursing his injury, and Christopher Wooh, who suffered a knee injury against Guinea, stayed at the Hôtel Président, where they were monitored throughout the morning by the team's physiotherapist.The team was expected to have another close-door training session yesterday January 17, 2024 at the Institut National Polytechnique Félix Houphouët-Boigny field in Yamoussoukro. At the time of this report, there were no latest news about the two injured players.
