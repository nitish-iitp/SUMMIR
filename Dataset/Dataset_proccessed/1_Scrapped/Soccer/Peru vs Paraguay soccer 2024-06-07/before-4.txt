National News Headlines in English for 7 June 2024

Stay updated with national news headlines in English. Learn about various sectors’ happenings in our country:

1. NDA Elects Narendra Modi as Leader, Sets Stage for New Government Formation: The NDA elected Narendra Modi as its leader and started forming a new central government after a unanimous decision. He will take the oath as Prime Minister for the third time on Saturday, with South Asian leaders attending.

2. Pro-Khalistan Slogans Raised at Golden Temple on Operation Blue Star Anniversary: On the 40th anniversary of Operation Blue Star, the Sikh community raised pro-Khalistan slogans at the Golden Temple in Amritsar. The operation, which began on June 1, 1984, aimed to eliminate Jarnail Singh Bhindranwale from the temple.

3. Supreme Court Orders Himachal to Release Water to Delhi: The Supreme Court directed Himachal Pradesh to release 137 cusecs of water to Delhi on June 7. Haryana must help ensure the water reaches the capital amid the ongoing water shortage.

4. Devastating floods hit Assam 2.5 lakh people still suffering: The flood situation has improved due to better weather conditions during the last 24 hours. 2.5 lakh people were affected in multiple. Road and rail communication in several parts of the State remained disrupted following incessant rain in the aftermath of cyclone ‘Remal.’

5. Ministry of Mines and Central TB Division Join Forces to Eliminate Tuberculosis: The Ministry of Mines and Central TB Division of the Ministry of Health & Family Welfare sign a Memorandum of Understanding in New Delhi. The MoU aims for collaborative action to eliminate Tuberculosis by 2025.

6. India and France to Launch Satellite for Climate Monitoring: Indian and French space agencies are developing the TRISHNA satellite to study human-induced climate change and environmental anomalies. Expected to launch in 2026, the satellite will monitor Earth’s surface from a 761 km sun-synchronous orbit.

International News in English for 7 June 2024

Explore global stories through international news in English. Discover updates from different countries:

1. Former PM Modi Congratulates Claudia Sheinbaum, Mexico’s First Female President: Former PM Narendra Modi congratulated Claudia Sheinbaum on becoming Mexico’s first female president, praising President López Obrador’s leadership.

2. Boeing Successfully Launches Astronauts to Space for the First Time: Boeing launched astronauts for NASA, with two test pilots flying the Starliner capsule to the International Space Station. Sunita Williams, an Indian-origin astronaut, became the first woman to test Boeing’s Starliner spacecraft on its maiden mission.

3. E. Coli Outbreak Sickens Over 100 in the UK: The UK Health Security Agency reports 113 E. coli cases, with dozens hospitalized, due to contaminated food. The bacteria spreads through food, close contact with infected individuals, and animals.

Sports News in English for 7 June 2024

Get the latest sports news in English and follow your favourite sports events:

1. Indian Shooter Sarabjot Singh Tops Qualifying Round at ISSF World Cup: Indian Shooter Sarabjot Singh scored 588 in the 10-meter air pistol qualification, securing a spot in the ISSF World Cup final.

2. Sunil Chhetri to Retire After FIFA World Cup Qualifier Against Kuwait: Indian football star Sunil Chhetri will lead the national team for the last time in the crucial FIFA World Cup 2026 qualifier against Kuwait. Chhetri, with a record 94 goals in 150 international matches, will retire after the game at Kolkata’s Salt Lake Stadium, ending his 19-year career.

3. Tennis Giants Miss French Open Quarterfinals: For the third time in 20 years, Rafael Nadal, Roger Federer, and Novak Djokovic missed the French Open quarterfinals. Federer is out, Nadal lost in the first round, and Djokovic struggles with form and injury.

What Happened Today in History

Understand the significance of what happened today in history and learn how to make a difference:

1. Treaty of Tordesillas Redraws World Map (1494)

Spain and Portugal divide newly discovered lands outside Europe, shaping global exploration and colonization during the Age of Discovery.

2. Crusaders Seize Jerusalem (1099)

Jerusalem falls to Crusaders after a siege, marking a pivotal victory in the First Crusade and establishing Christian control over the Holy Land.

3. Apple II Unleashes Personal Computing (1977)

Apple releases the Apple II computer, revolutionizing personal computing with its user-friendly design and opening doors for home computing enthusiasts.

4. Six-Day War Reshapes Middle East (1967)

Israel launches a preemptive strike against neighbouring Arab states, sparking the Six-Day War and leading to significant territorial changes in the region.

5. Azerbaijan Declares Democratic Republic (1918)

The Azerbaijani National Council declared independence, establishing Azerbaijan as a democratic republic, although its independence lasts for only 23 months before collapsing under Soviet invasion.
