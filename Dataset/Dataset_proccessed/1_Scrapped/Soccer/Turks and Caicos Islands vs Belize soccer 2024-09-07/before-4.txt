THOMASVILLE — Ledford rolled past crosstown foe East Davidson 9-0 in nonconference boys soccer Thursday at Brown Middle.

Valentino Ontiveros had four goals and two assists to lead the Panthers. Joaquin Leal-Godoy added two goals, while Jose Gomez scored two goals. Emauel Galindo and Diego Araujo each had a goal. Kevin Palacios, Raul Reyes and Brooks Burick each had an assist. Antonio Palacios made three saves.

Ledford improved to 3-3-2 overall while the Golden Eagles dipped to 1-5.

HIGH POINT CHRISTIAN, FORSYTH COUNTRY DAY

HIGH POINT — High Point Christian lost 9-0 against Forsyth Country Day in PTAC boys soccer Thursday at Correll-Morris Field. The Cougars moved to 1-7 overall and 0-3 in the conference.

BISHOP McGUINNESS, SOUTH STOKES

KERNERSVILLE — Bishop McGuinness shut out South Stokes 9-0 in nonconference boys soccer Thursday at Bishop.

Mahol Chom had three goals and two assists while Patrick Duggan had three goals and one assist to lead the Villains (4-2-2). Dylan King had a goal and an assist, while Jack Brown and Leo Blacutt each had a goal. Hayden Connor, Luke Hall, James Rashford and Arop Chom each had an assist. Asael Guzman made two saves in goal.

RAGSDALE, ATKINS

WINSTON-SALEM — Ragsdale downed Atkins 8-1 in nonconference boys soccer Thursday at Atkins.

Owen Justice shots five goals to lead the Tigers (8-0), ranked No. 15 in the state overall. Edan Lam had a goal and an assist, while Wrenn Gardner and Bilal Hassan each had a goal. Anthony Blancas had three assists, and Aidyn Suarez, McCade Moody, Jackson Harlan and Ethan Lam each had an assist.

VOLLEYBALL
TW ANDREWS, WEST STOKES

HIGH POINT — TW Andrews fell 25-10, 25-16, 26-24 against West Stokes in Mid-State 2A Conference volleyball Thursday at Andrews. The Lady Raiders moved to 3-7 overall and 0-3 in the conference.

HIGH POINT CENTRAL, ROCKINGHAM COUNTY

WENTWORTH — High Point Central lost 25-8, 25-10, 25-11 against Rockingham County in Mid-State 3A Conference volleyball Thursday at Rockingham County. The Bison dipped to 0-9 overall and 0-4 in the conference.

SOUTHWEST GUILFORD, SOUTHEAST GUILFORD

PLEASANT GARDEN — Southwest Guilford topped Southeast Guilford 18-25, 27-25, 25-16, 26-24 in Metro 4A Conference volleyball Thursday at Southeast. The Cowgirls improved to 6-3 overall and 3-1 in the conference.

HIGH POINT CHRISTIAN, FORSYTH COUNTRY DAY

HIGH POINT — High Point Christian swept Forsyth Country Day 25-15, 25-11, 25-18 in PTAC volleyball Thursday at HPCA.

MJ Henning had 13 kills and 11 digs for the Cougars (3-8 overall, 1-3 conference). Kinslee Britton had 12 kills, 16 assists and six aces. Gracyn Goodrich added six blocks, while Blakely Bowman had 25 digs. Landyn Smith chipped in 18 assists.

OAK GROVE, ASHEBORO

ASHEBORO — Oak Grove beat handed Asheboro its first loss of the season, winning 25-13, 25-15, 16-25, 25-16 in Mid-Piedmont 3A Conference volleyball Thursday at Asheboro.

Emma Sechrist had 12 kills for the Grizzlies (6-3 overall, 1-0 conference). Sydnee High had 10 kills and four blocks. Savannah Tiller had 22 digs, followed by Chloe Smith with 18. Morgan Williams had 26 assists.

In the JV match, Oak Grove won 25-21, 25-18 to improve to 8-1 and 1-0. Sydney Williams had nine kills, while Emma High had 12 digs, Emory Hodge had a block and Hollis Fitzgerald had 19 assists.

TRINITY, PROVIDENCE GROVE

TRINITY — Trinity topped Providence Grove 27-25, 25-22, 23-25, 25-18 in PAC 1A/2A volleyball Thursday at Trinity.

Kaitlyn McCoy had 17 kills and 17 digs for the Bulldogs (8-2 overall, 2-2 conference). Karrington Batten had 15 kills, 12 digs and five blocks. Faith Powell had 19 digs. Avalynn Johnson had 43 assists and 10 digs.

GIRLS TENNIS
HIGH POINT CENTRAL, ROCKINGHAM COUNTY

WENTWORTH — High Point Central lost 9-0 against Rockingham County in Mid-State 3A Conference girls tennis Thursday at Rockingham County. The Bison dipped to 0-6 overall and 0-1 in the conference.

SOUTHWEST GUILFORD, SOUTHEAST GUILFORD

PLEASANT GARDEN — Southwest Guilford fell 5-4 against Southeast Guilford in Metro 4A Conference girls tennis Thursday at Southeast.

Caroline Christman (6-4, 6-2) and Elena Perko (6-7(6-8), 6-3, 10-8) won in singles. Evelyn McKnight/Christman (9-7) and Anna McGinnis/Bella Johnson (8-4) won in doubles.

The Cowgirls moved to 6-5 overall and 2-4 in the conference.

HIGH POINT CHRISTIAN, FORSYTH COUNTRY DAY

HIGH POINT — High Point Christian lost 8-1 against Forsyth Country Day in PTAC girls tennis Thursday at the Oak Hollow Tennis Center.

Samreen Kaur and Dallas Davis teamed to win 8-6 at No. 1 doubles for the Cougars (2-3 overall, 1-2 conference).

OAK GROVE, NORTH DAVIDSON

WELCOME — Oak Grove defeated rival North Davidson 8-1 in Mid-Piedmont 3A Conference girls tennis Thursday at North. The Grizzlies improved to 3-3 overall and 1-1 in the conference.

GIRLS GOLF
AT JAMESTOWN PARK

JAMESTOWN — Westchester Country Day defeated Southwest Guilford 126-159 in nonconference girls golf Thursday at Jamestown Park.

Madi Dial shot a 37 to lead the Wildcats, while Janiya Brown had a 39. Ava Hoffman added a 50, followed by Liz Shoaf with a 51 and Mary Frances Collins with a 53.

AT WINDING CREEK

THOMASVILLE — Oak Grove took second while East Davidson was third in Thursday’s girls golf match at Winding Creek.

Central Davidson, led by medalist Naomi Lanier with a 40, won with a team score of 139. Oak Grove followed with a 148 while Oak Grove shot a 154. North Davidson also shot a 166.

Gracie Eanes shot a 46 to lead Oak Grove, which also got counting scores from Brooklyn Bowman (50) and Alena Clubb (52). Daniella Robles led East with a 45, followed by Kaley Pham (53) and Zoey Sprinkle (55).

HIGH POINT UNIVERSITY
WOMEN’S BASKETBALL

HIGH POINT — High Point University’s women’s basketball team will play in the Gulf Coast Showcase over the Thanksgiving holiday.

The three-round tournament will be Nov. 29-Dec. 1 at the Hertz Arena in Estero, Florida. The Panthers will open against West Virginia on Nov. 29 at 1:30 p.m. The field will also include Boise State, Illinois State, Butler, Santa Clara, Texas and New Mexico State.

PRO SPORTS
ROCKERS BEAT LEXINGTON

HIGH POINT — The Rockers defeated the Lexington Legends 4-1 in Atlantic League baseball Thursday at Truist Point.

Ben Aklinski homered in a two-hit performance for High Point, which scored three runs over the seventh and eight to lead. DJ Burt added two hits and two RBIs, while Frank Nigro doubled and drove in a run. Michael Martinez also doubled.

Garrett Schilling got the win in an inning of relief. Jameson McGrane pitched a three-batter ninth to pick up the save.

The Rockers (67-50 overall, 31-23 second half), who are five games behind Charleston in the second-half divisional standings heading into Friday’s game, will continue their six-game series against Lexington through Sunday. They will travel to Long Island to wrap up the regular season.
