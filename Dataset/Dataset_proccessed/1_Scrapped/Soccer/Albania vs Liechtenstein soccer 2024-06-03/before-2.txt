Toronto police say they will not clear U of T encampment without court order

Police said they don’t believe they have “sufficient legal authority” to remove protesters from campus without a court order. 

Updated June 3, 2024 at 1:31 p.m.
May 31, 2024
4 min read
Save

Toronto police say they considered a request by the University of Toronto to send officers on a “24/7” basis to a pro-Palestine encampment on campus, but declined because they felt their presence could inflame tensions.

Nick Lachance/Toronto Star
By Ben CohenCity Hall Bureau

Toronto police say they will not clear out the protest encampment at the University of Toronto unless there is an emergency or they are ordered to by a judge.

In an email to the Star, police said they don’t believe they have “sufficient legal authority” to remove protesters from campus.
