Nigeria vs South Africa Match Preview
Nigeria (WWWWD)

Nigeria have reached the Africa Cup of Nations semi-finals for the record 15th time, and they remain one of the favorites to win the tournament. Nigeria advanced to the final four with a hard-fought 1-0 win over Angola, with Ademola Lookman scoring the winner for the Super Eagles, making it three goals at the AFCON 2024.

South Africa (WWDWL)

The Bafana Bafana Played 0-0 against Cape Verde both at full time and extra time to see the game on a penalty shootout. Their wonderful goalkeeper, Ronwen Willams, made four remarkable saves in the penalty shootout to see South Africa play in the semi-finals for the first time since 2000.  

Bet on Nigeria to Win at 1.64 odds
Ivory Coast vs D.R. Congo Match Preview
Ivory Coast (WWLLW)

Ivory Coast secured a place in the last four of the AFCON after claiming a 2-1 extra-time victory over Mali in the quarterfinals. The Elephants sacked their former manager after a disappointing start to the tournament and appointed interim coach Emerse Fae, who has led his team to a hard-fought victory in their last two matches.

How to Watch all African Cup of Nations Match

TV channel: In the UK & Ireland, the tournament will be televised live on Sky Sports, with some games also shown on the BBC.

South Africa can watch on either SABC (free) or SABC3 (free).


People in the USA, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand can catch the action live on BeIn Sports.

Nigerian fans can watch the match live by subscribing to DSTV, GOTv, Startimes, and AfroSport.

The other notable broadcasting channels for the match are CANAL+, GTV, and ZIGGO Sport.

Live stream: Subscribers can also watch matches live online via the Sky Go app, with the BBC games also broadcast live on the BBC Sport website and iPlayer, you can stream live on the Fancode app.
