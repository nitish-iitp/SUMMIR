2026 World Cup qualifiers: Leopards face Senegal this Thursday
Last updated: 2024/06/05 at 2:36 PM
Ronely Ntibonera
Share
2 minimum reading
Leopards face Senegal this Thursday
Share

Already on the ground of the Lions of Teranga, the Leopards of the Democratic Republic of Congo have set up their HQ at the Rhino Resort hotel in Dakar. After spending 3 hours 20 minutes flying from Morocco, where they were training to sharpen their skills, they are now preparing to face Senegal this Thursday, June 6, 2024 at the Abdoulaye Wade Stadium in Diamniadio.

This confrontation, counting for the 3rd day of the 2026 World Cup Qualifiers, promises to be decisive for both nations. Currently, Senegal has 4 points, while the Democratic Republic of Congo has 3. A victory would allow one of the teams to take a significant lead in the race for qualification.

Leopards face Senegal this Thursday

This Wednesday, June 5, the Leopards will train at the Abdoulaye Wade Stadium at 7 p.m., a crucial session to familiarize themselves with the pitch before the long-awaited match.

For the next two matches of these 2026 World Cup qualifiers, Senegal will be without Sadio Mané. He suffers from an injury to the aponeuroses of the biceps femoris and the lateral gastrocnemius, announced the Senegalese Football Federation on its social networks.
