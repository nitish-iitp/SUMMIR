Cameron Dukes throws three TDs, rushes for another in leading Argos past Lions

TORONTO - Cameron Dukes used his right arm and legs to make his first CFL home start a victorious one.

Updated June 10, 2024 at 5:35 a.m.

3 min read
Save

Toronto Argonauts defensive lineman Benoit Marion (93) and linebacker Brian Harelimana (26) celebrate after Marion scored a touchdown during first half CFL action against the B.C. Lions, in Toronto, on Sunday, June 9, 2024. THE CANADIAN PRESS/Christopher Katsarov

CKL
By Dan Ralph The Canadian Press

TORONTO - Cameron Dukes used his right arm and legs to make his first CFL home start a victorious one.

Dukes threw three touchdown passes and ran for another to lead the Toronto Argonauts past the B.C. Lions 35-27 on Sunday night in the season opener for both teams. Dukes made his third career start but first at BMO Field and the second-year pro said nerves weren’t an issue.
