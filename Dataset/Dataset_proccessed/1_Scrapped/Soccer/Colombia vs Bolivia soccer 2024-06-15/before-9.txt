Eagles close out spring sports at the Meet of Champions

DURHAM — For the first time in the history of the Kennett High track program, the Eagles qualified their two 4X100-meter relay teams for the prestigious New England Track and Field Championships, which were held at the University of New Hampshire in Durham on Saturday.

The KHS boys’ relay team of junior Logan Troon, sophomore Ross Stephens, junior Quintin Plourde and senior Tyler Walcott, set school records three weeks in a row, including running a sizzling 43.30 on the same track at the Meet of Champions on June 1, did not finish Saturday’s race. The Eagles were disqualified for passing the baton outside of the designated passing zone.
