The Republic of Ireland will host Belgium at Aviva Stadium this Saturday for an international friendly. Belgium aims to fine-tune their preparations for the upcoming European Championships, while Ireland seeks to secure some positive results as they rebuild following a disappointing qualifying campaign. Although Ireland has no imminent competitions to prepare for, they are focused on strengthening squad cohesion ahead of their pursuit of World Cup qualification.

In their qualifying journey, Ireland struggled, managing only two wins and suffering six losses across eight matches, accumulating a total of six points and falling short of qualification by 12 points. Conversely, Belgium finished top in Group F in the European Qualifiers. Their group included formidable opponents such as Austria, Sweden, Azerbaijan, and Estonia. Belgium remained undefeated, securing six victories and two draws in eight matches, finishing just one point ahead of Austria. This fixture promises to be an enticing clash as both teams seek to achieve their respective objectives.
