Burkina Faso have never lost in the fixture before

Burkina Faso and Malawi lock horns at the Stade du 26 Mars in neutral Mali on Tuesday for the 2025 Africa Cup of Nations qualifiers. Both are aiming for their first win of the campaign after dropping points in their respective openers.

Ranked 67th in the world, the Stallions earned a point against the big guns Senegal with a stoppage-time equalizer. Ousseni Bouda scored in the 95th minute to cancel out Sadio Mane's early opener for the Lions of Teranga.

They fell behind twice in the game, only to equalize each time, but then Mokono Eldhino struck the third goal for the Swallows with only three minutes of normal time remaining.

Malawi, sitting 126th in the world rankings, succumbed to a second consecutive defeat in all competition, having also lost 1-0 to Equatorial Guinea in the 2026 FIFA World Cup qualifiers in their last official game.

Burkina Faso vs Malawi Head-To-Head and Key Numbers
There have been six clashes between the sides in the past, with Burkina Faso going unbeaten in all of them.
All four of the Stallions' victories against the Flames had come in a row. They drew on either side of that run, including a 0-0 stalemate in their most recent encounter on November 2020.
In five official games this year, Malawi have lost four times.
The Stallions are winless in their last eight official clashes, while they have drawn three of their last four.
Burkina Faso are ranked 67th in the world while their next opponents are 59 places below them, according to the latest FIFA World Rankings.
Burkina Faso vs Malawi Prediction

The Stallions have a good record against the Flames, having never lost to them before. Although their form right now is not the best, they should be able to end that run here with a narrow win.

Prediction: Burkina Faso 2-0 Malawi
