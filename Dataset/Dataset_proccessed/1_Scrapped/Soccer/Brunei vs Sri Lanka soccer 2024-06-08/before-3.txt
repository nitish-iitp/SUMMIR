National News Headlines in English for 8 June 2024

Stay updated with 8 June 2024 news headlines in English for school assembly. Learn about various sectors’ happenings in our country:

1. PM Modi Praises Education Sector for QS World Rankings Improvement: Prime Minister Modi praised Indian universities for their better performance in the QS World University Rankings. He praised students and universities like IISc Bangalore and IITs Bombay and Delhi for their improved QS World University rankings. 

2. Sikkim Ranks Third in National Food Safety Index: Sikkim ranked third among small states in the National Food Safety Index 2023. Effective monitoring and digital platforms helped achieve this. Dr. Yudok Bhutia received the award from Health Minister Dr. Mansukh Mandaviya.

3. RBI Keeps Repo Rate Unchanged at 6.5 per cent: RBI Governor Shaktikanta Das announced that the repo rate remains at 6.5%. The central bank also raised the GDP growth forecast for FY25 to 7.2% and kept the inflation forecast at 4.5%.

4. NHRC Issues Notice to Four States on Nata Pratha: The National Human Rights Commission India (NHRC) has asked Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, and Gujarat to report on actions against the practice of Nata Pratha, where girls are sold under the guise of marriage. This follows a tragic 2020 case of a girl who was sold and later found dead.

5. UPSC Releases Admit Card for 2024 Civil Services Exam: The UPSC released the admit card for the Civil Services Preliminary Exam 2024, rescheduled for June 16. Registered candidates can download their admit cards from the UPSC official website using their login details.

6. India’s Forex Reserves Reach Record High: India’s foreign exchange reserves surged by $4.83 billion, reaching a new high of $651.5 billion as of May 31. RBI Governor Shaktikanta Das highlighted the country’s resilient external sector, improvements in the current account deficit, external debt to GDP ratio, and net international investment position, expressing confidence in meeting external financing needs.

International News in English for 8 June 2024

Explore global stories through international news in English. Discover updates from different countries:

1. Japan Launches Dating App to Boost Birth Rate: Japan is launching a dating app called ‘Tokyo Futari Story’ to address its declining birth rate. This government initiative is part of broader efforts to encourage marriage and family formation.

2. Violence in Myanmar’s Rakhine State Displaces Thousands: Renewed violence between the Myanmar military and the Arakan Army has displaced tens of thousands in Rakhine State. Many attacks targeted the Rohingya community, exacerbating an ongoing humanitarian crisis.

3. Maldives President to Attend PM Modi’s Oath Ceremony: Maldives President Muizzu, known for his pro-China stance, will make his first official visit to India to attend PM Modi’s oath ceremony in New Delhi. This visit marks his first since taking office on November 17 last year.

Sports News in English for 8 June 2024

Get the latest sports news in English and follow your favourite sports events:

1. USA Defeats Pakistan in Thrilling Super Over in T20 World Cup: USA beat Pakistan in a thrilling Super Over in the T20 World Cup. India-born Saurabh Netravalkar starred by stopping Pakistan’s chase, leading to a memorable win for the USA team.

2. Jasmine Paolini Reaches First Grand Slam Final at French Open: Italy’s Jasmine Paolini reached her first Grand Slam final at the French Open. She won the semi-final against Russia’s Mirra Andreeva with a decisive 6-3, 6-1 victory, fulfilling a lifelong dream.

3. US Cricket Team Stuns Pakistan in Historic T20 World Cup Victory: The US cricket team shocked powerhouse Pakistan with a dramatic Super Over win at the Men’s T20 Cricket World Cup in Dallas on Thursday, 7 June 2024. Captain Monank Patel, named player of the match, hailed the landmark victory as the biggest in US cricket history and a significant achievement for the team and the country.

What Happened Today in History

Understand the significance of what happened today in history and learn how to make a difference:

1. George Orwell’s “1984” is Published (1949)

On June 8, 1949, George Orwell’s famous book “1984” was published. This book tells a story about a future where the government watches everything people do. It made people think a lot about freedom and privacy.

2. James Earl Ray Captured (1968)

On June 8, 1968, police caught James Earl Ray in London. He was the man who killed Martin Luther King Jr., a leader who fought for equal rights for all people. His arrest helped bring justice to a tragic event.

3. Serene County Suffers Severe Flooding (1972)

On June 8, 1972, a major flood hit Serene County in the United States. Heavy rains caused rivers to overflow, leading to widespread damage and many people losing their homes. This disaster showed the importance of emergency planning and response.

4. Kurt Waldheim Becomes President of Austria (1986)

On June 8, 1986, Kurt Waldheim was elected President of Austria. His presidency was controversial because of his past military service during World War II, which led to debates about leadership and historical responsibility.

5. Venus Transits the Sun (2004)

On June 8, 2004, people around the world watched as the planet Venus passed directly between the Earth and the Sun. This rare event, called a transit, was visible as a small black dot moving across the Sun. It was a spectacular sight for astronomers and skywatchers.
