Kirani James National Stadium

This is a stadium located in the capital city of Grenada, St. George's. This name has a purpose, and it was inspired by Kiran James, a Grenadian athlete who was recognized for being a specialist in the 400 meters. He was the winner of the first gold medal for Grenada in 2012. For that reason the stadium was named after him.

This multi-sport facility was founded in 1983 and since its construction has been a key developmental facility for many of the country's athletes. The Kirani James National Stadium has a capacity of 5,000 people. In addition, it now has modern facilities for the ongoing development of its athletes.

Best achievement for Grenada

The Grenada national soccer team has had several highlights throughout its history, but one of the most significant achievements is having qualified for the 2023 CONCACAF Gold Cup.


In the Gold Cup, which is the most important national team tournament in the CONCACAF region (which includes North America, Central America and the Caribbean), Grenada made it to the quarterfinals in the 2023 edition. This was a remarkable feat for a soccer team that has historically been in development and has not had a consistent presence in high-profile international tournaments.
