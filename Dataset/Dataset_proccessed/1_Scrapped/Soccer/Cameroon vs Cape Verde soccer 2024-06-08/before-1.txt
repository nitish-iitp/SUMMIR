2026 World Cup Qualifiers : Lions Begin Training
By Eulalia AMABO 04 Juin 2024 11:28 0 Likes 
  

The team principally had a relaxation walk and its first training session yesterday June 3, 2024 at the Ngoa-Ekelle Stadium in Yaounde.


With two critical World Cup qualifiers at the corner, the Cameroon national football team kicked off their training camp yesterday, June 3, 2024. Facing off against Cape Verde on June 8, 2024 and moving on to meet Angola on June 11, 2024 in Luanda; the Lions understand the importance of these matches. Principally, they had a relaxation walk in the morning yesterday and later in day, they had their first training session ahead of the matches.
The relaxation walk around their base at the Hilton Hotel, fostered a positive atmosphere with smiles and friendly conversations among the players. This positive spirit extended beyond the team, attracting a large crowd of fans eager to show their support for their national heroes.
Later in the day, the Lions headed to Ngoa-Ekelle Stadium for their first training session. However, a delay marred the planned schedule. Though the session was announced for 3:30 P.M., the bus carrying the Indomitable Lions arrived at a few minutes past 5 P.M. Despite the scorching sun, the dedicated fans remained patient, cheering for each player as they emerged, dressed in their training attire. No official explanation was provided for the delay, but the players' determination was undeniable as they took to the pitch.
