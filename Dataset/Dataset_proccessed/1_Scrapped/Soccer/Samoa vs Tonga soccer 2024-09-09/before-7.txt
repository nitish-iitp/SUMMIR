The 2026 World Cup cycle begins for the Canadian men’s soccer team — with the drone scandal still hovering

Heading into Saturday’s friendly against the U.S., Jesse Marsch says a report in the aftermath of the Paris spying scandal is nearly ready.

Updated Sept. 9, 2024 at 2:19 p.m.
Sept. 6, 2024
3 min read
Save

Canadian soccer coach Jesse Marsch will be in familiar territory for Saturday’s friendly against the U.S.

CHARLY TRIBALLEAU Getty Images
By Joe CallaghanSpecial to the Star

Clear skies overhead? Apparently so. 

Among many things Canada Soccer’s social video team has done consistently well with its behind-the-curtain, hype-building content ahead of games are the sweeping overhead shots of national teams limbering up in a new city. Moody music and dramatic aerial views of the arena are usually thrown in. Based on everything that has emerged from Kansas City in recent days, however, the aerial footage may have been paused. The drones have perhaps (and perhaps wisely) been left at home.
