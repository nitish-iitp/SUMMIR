‘Boulder beach’: The city filled this once sandy west-end beach with cobblestones and residents are dismayed

The city started a revitalization project in 2021 for Humber Bay Park East to maintain the shoreline and protect it from erosion.

Updated March 22, 2024 at 2:04 p.m.
March 20, 2024
3 min read
Save

The beach at Humber Bay Park East has been covered with large stones (as seen in another part of the beach here)to protect it from erosion, the city says. 

Andrew Francis Wallace/Toronto Star
By Omar MoslehStaff Reporter

Humber Bay Shores residents are slamming the city’s decision to replace the sand on a popular west-end beach with large rocks, which they say has ruined their only natural refuge in a neighbourhood dominated by condo developments.

The city, in partnership with the Toronto and Region Conservation Authority, started a revitalization project in 2021 for Humber Bay Park East to maintain the shoreline and protect it from erosion, which they say is necessary because wind, waves and other environmental factors have battered the existing protection structures, which have been there for about 50 years.
