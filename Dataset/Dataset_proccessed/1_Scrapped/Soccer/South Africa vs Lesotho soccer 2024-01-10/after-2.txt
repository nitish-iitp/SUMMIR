Bafana Bafana Held to a Goalless Draw by Lesotho in Pre-AFCON Friendly
Published January 11, 2024
12 months ago
Sports
Football

On a sunny Wednesday afternoon, South Africa's pride, Bafana Bafana, clashed with neighboring Lesotho in what can be described as a lackluster pre-Africa Cup of Nations friendly match, culminating in a 0-0 draw. The encounter, which was the team's sole preparatory fixture ahead of the prestigious tournament, left many fans desiring more from the national side but also questioning the readiness of Hugo Broos’ men for the challenges ahead.




Opting for a blend of experience and new talent, Coach Broos named a strong lineup that saw the likes of Orlando Pirates’ Evidence Makgopa spearhead the attack, joined by Thapelo Maseko and Mihlali Mayambela, with talisman Percy Tau situated behind the front three. Notable by his presence on the sidelines was Mamelodi Sundowns' goalkeeper Ronwen Williams, as Veli Mothwa was awarded the start between the posts.




The first half of the encounter set a tone for the rest of the match, with both sides struggling to carve out clear-cut opportunities. The stakes of the game did little to inspire creativity or tactical risk-taking, resulting in a deadlock that held firm throughout the 90 minutes.




The introduction of fresh legs in the second half, including the likes of goalkeeper Ricardo Goss and field players Nyiko Mobbie, Katlego Mashego, Thabang Monare, Thapelo Morena, and Zakehle Lepasa, did not provide the spark needed to break the impasse.




This outcome casts a shadow over Bafana Bafana’s attacking prowess as they look forward to their group stage campaign against stronger opponents. Coach Broos is expected to reflect deeply on his tactical approach as well as the efficiency of his offensive selections if the side is to have a significant impact in the AFCON tournament.




Furthermore, the team is dealt a potential defensive blow, awaiting assessment of the extent of Mothobi Mvala’s groin injury. Mvala, a defensive stalwart, could be a critical absentee, should recovery not align with the competitive schedule.




Looking ahead, Bafana Bafana’s quest to reignite the spark they have been missing on the continental stage begins against Mali on January 16 at the Stade Amadou Gon Coulibaly in Korhogo. Fans will eagerly await to see if the team can shake off the pre-tournament jitters and Live up to the nation's expectations.




With the friendly match offering little to gauge, the true test for Hugo Broos' Bafana Bafana will undeniably come when the whistle blows at AFCON.
