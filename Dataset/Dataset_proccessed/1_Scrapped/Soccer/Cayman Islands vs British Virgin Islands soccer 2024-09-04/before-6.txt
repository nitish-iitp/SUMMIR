Charley Johnson put together the kind of lifetime résumé that even sports Hall of Famers rarely dream about.

Johnson, who passed away Tuesday at the age of 85, is probably best known for his successful 15-year career as an NFL quarterback. He won an NFL passing title with the St. Louis Cardinals and is a member of the Denver Broncos Ring of Honor.

Charles L. (Charley) Johnson

Charles L. (Charley) Johnson

Nov. 22, 1938—Sept. 3, 2024

Notable career highlights:

New Mexico State Football (1958-60)

Quarterbacked Aggies to 19-3 record over final two seasons, including 11-0 mark in 1960.

Back-to-back Sun Bowl MVP honors after leading NMSU to consecutive wins in 1959-60.

Led NCAA in touchdown passes with 18 in 1959 — five more than any other player.

First player to have his uniform number (33) retired by NMSU.

Briefly served as NMSU interim football coach in spring of 2010.

St. Louis Cardinals (1961-69)

Named to the NFL Pro Bowl in 1963.

Won NFL Passing title (3,045 yards) in 1964.

Houston Oilers (1970-71)

Denver Broncos (1972-75)

Led Broncos to franchise’s first winning season (7-5-2) in 1973.

Posted NFL-best 8.1 yards per passing attempt in 1974.

Elected to Broncos Hall of Fame in 1986 and is a member of the franchise Ring of Honor.

NFL career record: Passed for 24,410 yards and 170 touchdowns in 165 games.

Halls of fame

New Mexico Sports Hall of Fame inductee 2008

Texas Sports Hall of Fame inductee 2010

Outside of football

U.S. Army Reserve: Worked for NASA serving as a second lieutenant while playing for the St. Louis Cardinals.

Opened Johnson Compression Services in Houston and worked as an engineering a product development consultant for 18 years.

Served as head of New Mexico State’s chemical engineering department (2000-04). Worked as a professor in the department until 2012.
