National News Headlines in English for 12 June 2024

Stay updated with 12 June 2024 news headlines in English for school assembly. Learn about various sectors’ happenings in our country.

1. Supreme Court to Hear Plea Challenging NEET Medical Exam over Leak:  

The Supreme Court issued notice to the Centre and National Testing Agency (NTA) about a petition to cancel this year’s NEET exam due to an alleged paper leak. The NTA must respond by July 8, aligning the case with a similar petition from May 17.

2. India Welcomes New Members to BRICS Economic Group:

India welcomed Egypt, Iran, the UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Ethiopia as new BRICS members. Their representatives attended their first key BRICS Foreign Ministers’ Meeting in Nizhny Novgorod, Russia, following the expansion of the group in 2023.

3. Maldives Reviews Agreements Signed with India:

Maldives’ Parliament will investigate four agreements signed with India during President Solih’s administration, claiming they threaten the country’s sovereignty and independence. This decision comes amid President Muizzu’s visit to India.

4. Cabinet Approves Funds for 3 Crore Houses under PMAY:

The Union Cabinet approved government assistance for constructing three crore houses under the Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY). Since 2015, the scheme has completed 4.21 crore houses for eligible poor families, aiming to provide basic amenities.

5. Centre Releases Rs 1.39 Lakh Crore Tax Devolution to States:  

The Centre authorized the release of Rs 1,39,750 crore in tax devolution to states for June, including an extra installment. This funding aims to accelerate development and capital spending across the states.

6. Congress Launches ‘Thank You’ Campaign in Uttar Pradesh:

The Congress party will start a ‘Dhanyavad Yatra’ across Uttar Pradesh’s 403 assemblies. Rahul Gandhi and Priyanka Gandhi Vadra will thank Raebareli and Amethi supporters at an event today, with the yatra continuing until June 15.

7. Telangana CM Announces Formation of Farmer & Education Commissions:  

Telangana’s Chief Minister A Revanth Reddy announced plans to form a farmers’ commission and an education commission. These commissions aim to address farmers’ grievances and improve the education system by formulating new policies.

8. Meghalaya Adopts CBSE Syllabus for Class 11 Arts Stream:

The Meghalaya government adopted the CBSE syllabus for ten Class 11 Arts subjects, including English, Geography, and History. Teachers are concerned due to the delayed release of the syllabus by the Directorate of Educational Research and Training (DERT).

9. CBSE Warns Students, and Parents of Misinformation About Syllabus:

CBSE advised students and parents to beware of misinformation about the 2024-25 academic session syllabus and materials. The Board recommended checking the official CBSE website for accurate information and updates.

10. University Grants Commission (UGC) to Offer Admissions Twice a Year:

The University Grants Commission (UGC) announced that Indian universities will offer admissions twice a year, starting from the 2024-25 session. This aligns with foreign institutions and aims to enhance employment opportunities through biannual campus recruitment.

1. Elon Musk Threatens to Ban Apple Devices Over OpenAI Integration:

Elon Musk threatened to ban Apple devices at his companies if Apple integrates OpenAI’s technology at the operating system level. This followed Apple’s announcement of new AI features and a partnership with OpenAI to incorporate ChatGPT.

2. UN Security Council Urges Ceasefire in Gaza, Russia Abstains:

The United Nations Security Council adopted a U.S.-drafted resolution urging Hamas to accept a ceasefire proposal by President Joe Biden. The resolution passed with 14 votes in favour and Russia abstaining, calls for immediate and unconditional implementation.

3. India’s PM Responds to Pakistan’s Greetings, Prioritizes Security:

 Prime Minister Narendra Modi responded to congratulatory messages from Pakistan’s Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif and former premier Nawaz Sharif, stating that while India seeks peace and progress, the security of its people remains a priority.

4. Superbug Found on International Space Station Raises Health Concerns:

NASA’s Indian-origin astronaut Sunita Williams and eight ISS crew members face a threat from a multi-drug resistant superbug, Enterobacter bugandensis, which infects the respiratory system and has evolved in the ISS’s closed environment.

5. UN Security Council Backs US-Drafted Ceasefire Plan for Gaza:

The UN Security Council adopted a U.S.-drafted resolution supporting a ceasefire plan in Gaza, urging Hamas and Israel to implement the truce and hostage release proposal announced by President Joe Biden on May 31.

6. Apple Unveils New AI Features, Partners with OpenAI:

Apple unveiled “Apple Intelligence,” a suite of AI features and a partnership with OpenAI to integrate ChatGPT technology. This move, announced at the annual Worldwide Developers Conference, aims to enhance Apple products and address its AI strategy.

7. Plane Carrying Malawian Vice President Goes Missing:

A plane carrying Malawi’s Vice President Saulos Klaus Chilima and nine others went missing after failing to land. The Malawi Defense Force Aircraft lost contact after departing Lilongwe and was scheduled to land at Mzuzu International Airport.

8. Palestinian Group Appeals Dismissal of Lawsuit Accusing US of Inaction in Gaza Conflict:

The 9th Circuit Court of Appeals in San Francisco began hearing an expedited appeal by Palestinian plaintiffs accusing U.S. leaders of failing to prevent Israel’s alleged genocide in Gaza. The appeal follows the dismissal of a federal lawsuit earlier this year.

9. UN Security Council Approves US-Proposed Israel-Gaza Ceasefire Plan:

The UN Security Council approved a U.S.-proposed Israel-Gaza ceasefire plan, calling for a full ceasefire, hostage release, and Palestinian prisoner exchange. The resolution passed with 14 votes in favour and Russia abstaining.

10. Fed Stuck in Holding Pattern on Interest Rates to Combat Inflation:

Federal Reserve officials are expected to maintain the current interest rate at a 23-year high of 5.25-5.5 percent during their June meeting, as inflation concerns prevent rate cuts and keep borrowing costs in a holding pattern.

Sports News in English for 12 June 2024

Get the latest sports news in English and follow your favourite sports events:

1. Gulveer Singh Secures Silver in 5000m Race at Portland Track Festival:

In athletics, India’s Gulveer Singh won the silver medal in the Men’s 5000-metre race at the Portland Track Festival High-Performance Meet in the US. The Asian Games bronze medallist also set a new record as the fastest Indian in the event.

2. Mumbai Cricket Association President Amol Kale Passes Away:

Mumbai Cricket Association president Amol Kale passed away due to a cardiac arrest in New York. Kale was in the US to watch the India vs. Pakistan match at the Nassau County International Cricket Stadium, accompanied by MCA secretary Ajinkya Naik and council member Suraj Samat.

3. South Africa Clinches Victory Over Bangladesh in T20 World Cup:

In the ICC Men’s T20 World Cup, South Africa defeated Bangladesh by four runs at the Nassau County International Cricket Stadium in New York. South Africa scored 113 for 6, with Heinrich Klaasen top-scoring with 46. Bangladesh, needing 114, fell short at 109 for 7, with Keshav Maharaj taking three wickets.

4. Three Fans Jailed for Racial Abuse Against Vinicius Junior:

Three Valencia football fans were sentenced to eight months in prison for racially insulting Real Madrid player Vinicius Junior. This marks Spain’s first conviction for racism at a professional football game. The incident occurred on May 21, 2023, during a La Liga game, prompting widespread support for Vinicius and anti-racism campaigns.

5. Matthew Wade Reprimanded by ICC for Umpire Dispute:

Australia’s keeper-batter Matthew Wade was reprimanded by the ICC for dissenting against an umpire’s decision during a T20 World Cup match against England in Barbados. Wade received one demerit point for arguing with the umpire in the 18th over, expecting a dead ball call.

Today’s Entertainment News in English 

Now, let’s dive into the entertainment world and see what new has taken place.

1. Global Cinema Celebration: 18th Mumbai International Film Festival Lights Up Screens Across India:

Explore the 18th Mumbai International Film Festival, held in Mumbai from June 15 to 21, with screenings in Delhi, Chennai, Pune, and Kolkata. Enjoy 314 films from 59 countries, including 17 from diverse nations, with premieres and curated award-winning packages.

2. Jitendra Kumar Responds to Salary Rumors Amid Panchayat 3 Excitement:

Jitendra Kumar addressed rumours about being the highest-paid actor in the Panchayat 3 series, earning ₹70,000 per episode. He stated that discussing salaries is unfair, amidst the excitement surrounding the show’s release.

3. Star-Studded Kalki 2898 AD Trailer Launches, Fans Thrilled for June 27 Release:

The trailer for Kalki 2898 AD, written and directed by Nag Ashwin, was launched on June 9. The film, featuring Prabhas, Amitabh Bachchan, Kamal Haasan, and Deepika Padukone, is set in a post-apocalyptic world inspired by Hindu scriptures. With a ₹600 crore budget, it is India’s most expensive film to date, now releasing on June 27.

4. Telugu Superstar Nandamuri Balakrishna and Boyapti Sreenu Reunite for Fourth Film ‘BB4’:

Telugu superstar Nandamuri Balakrishna reunited with ‘Simha’ director Boyapti Sreenu for their fourth collaboration, tentatively titled ‘BB4’. Announced on Balakrishna’s 64th birthday, the film follows their previous hits ‘Simha’, ‘Legend’, and ‘Akhanda’.

5.Terrorists Attack Bus to Katra, Nine Pilgrims Killed; Celebrities Condemn Act :

Nine people died after a 53-seater bus heading to Katra fell into a gorge following gunfire by terrorists. The attack, targeting pilgrims en route to Mata Vaishno Devi shrine, drew condemnation from celebrities like Priyanka Chopra, Varun Dhawan, and Kangana Ranaut. Ranaut and Dhawan both condemned the attack on social media, expressing grief and solidarity with the victims.

What Happened Today in History

Understand the significance of what happened today in history and learn how to make a difference:

1. Anne Frank’s Birthday (1929)

Anne Frank, the young Jewish diarist who documented her experiences hiding from the Nazis during World War II, was born on June 12, 1929, in Frankfurt, Germany.

2. South African Students Uprising (1976)

Thousands of black students in South Africa protested against the apartheid government’s imposition of Afrikaans as the language of instruction in schools, leading to the Soweto Uprising on June 12, 1976.

3. Philippines Independence Day (1898)

On June 12, 1898, the Philippines declared independence from Spanish colonial rule after more than three centuries, marking the beginning of their struggle for self-governance.

4. Nelson Mandela’s Release (1964)

Nelson Mandela, anti-apartheid revolutionary and future President of South Africa, was sentenced to life in prison on June 12, 1964, after being convicted of sabotage and other charges.

5. Women’s Suffrage Movement in Switzerland (1959)

Swiss women gained the right to vote in national elections and run for office on June 12, 1959, following a referendum that ended decades of activism by women’s suffrage supporters.
