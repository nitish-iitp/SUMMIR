China draws with Thailand in World Cup qualifier
By Xinhua
Share
Liu Yang (top) of China vies for a header during the Group C match of 2026 FIFA World Cup Asian qualifiers between China and Thailand in Shenyang, Northeast China's Liaoning province, on June 6, 2024. (PHOTO / XINHUA)

SHENYANG - Behram Abduweli scored a late equalizer in his international debut as China drew with Thailand 1-1 at home in the 2026 FIFA World Cup qualifier here on Thursday.

Before the match, China rank second in Group C with seven points, three points ahead of Thailand. Branko Ivankovic's team would ensure themselves of a place in the final stage of Asian qualifiers with a win.

Wang Dalei (second right), goalkeeper of China, makes a save during the Group C match of 2026 FIFA World Cup Asian qualifiers between China and Thailand in Shenyang, Northeast China's Liaoning province, on June 6, 2024. (PHOTO / XINHUA)

However, the away team broke the deadlock first in the 20th minute, when Supachai Chaided cut into penalty area from left flank, delivering a cross for unmarked Supachok Sarachat to score a free header.

China nearly found a quick equalizer two minutes later, but A Lan's low strike from distance hit the post, before a rebound shot from Fei Nanduo denied by Thailand goalkeeper.

Fei Nanduo (right) of China vies with Kritsada Kaman of Thailand during the Group C match of 2026 FIFA World Cup Asian qualifiers between China and Thailand in Shenyang, Northeast China's Liaoning province, on June 6, 2024. (PHOTO / XINHUA)

In the 28th minute, Ivankovic made an early substitution, replacing Gao Tianyi with 21-year-old Behram.

Thailand was close to extend the lead in the first half as they had ten shots and hit the woodwork twice.

Six minutes into the second half, China was awarded a penalty thanks to a handball foul from Kritsada Kaman, but Fei Nanduo's shot went high off the crossbar.

A Lan (right) of China vies with Suphanan Bureerat of Thailand during the Group C match of 2026 FIFA World Cup Asian qualifiers between China and Thailand in Shenyang, Northeast China's Liaoning province, on June 6, 2024. (PHOTO / XINHUA)

The home team finally equalized in the 79th minute as Behram volleyed home a headed pass from Zhu Chenjie, after a free kick delivered by Xie Pengfei.

China had several opportunities in the final minutes by playing long ball, but they couldn't find the winning goal.

"It was a great pity that we couldn't win the match. My players were under great pressure, and they tried their best. As a professional coach, I need to take responsibility for everything on the pitch," Ivankovic said after the match.

Suphanat Mueanta (center) of Thailand competes during the Group C match of 2026 FIFA World Cup Asian qualifiers between China and Thailand in Shenyang, Northeast China's Liaoning province, on June 6, 2024. (PHOTO / XINHUA)

"Young players including Behram, Xie Wenneng, Wang Zhenao and Xu Haoyang, who played their first international match, did a great job. That missed penalty kick affected our tempo a lot, and we missed several chances in the last minutes. We need to be more patient to organize our offense," he added.

Thailand head coach Masatada Ishii also felt disappointed for not winning the match. "We had many chances but didn't fulfill our target."

In the other group C match on Thursday, South Korea smashed Singapore 7-0 to secure a ticket in the final stage of Asian qualifiers as group leaders.

Wang Dalei (top), goalkeeper of China, makes a save during the Group C match of 2026 FIFA World Cup Asian qualifiers between China and Thailand in Shenyang, Northeast China's Liaoning province, on June 6, 2024. (PHOTO / XINHUA)

In the last round of second stage of Asian qualifiers, China is scheduled to play against South Korea away next Tuesday, while Thailand will meet Singapore at home.

China needs to get at least one point in South Korea to qualify for the final stage of qualifying tournament.
