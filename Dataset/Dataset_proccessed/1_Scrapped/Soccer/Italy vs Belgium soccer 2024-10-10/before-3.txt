Italy Prepares for Crucial Nations League Matches Against Belgium and Israel
3
2 Minutes of Reading
Tuesday 8 October 2024, 16:03 - Last updated: 9 October, 00:20
New international break and Luciano Spalletti's Italy returns to the field for the Nations League. The coach is preparing for two home matches: on October 10th at the Stadio Olimpico in Rome, Belgium will arrive, while on the 14th it will be Israel's turn, who will face the Azzurri at the Bluenergy Stadium in Udine. Meanwhile, the first withdrawal has already occurred: Fiorentina's forward Moise Kean had to leave the training camp due to a back problem. In his place, Spalletti has called up Lorenzo Lucca from Udinese. Italy has regained confidence in the last two matches of the competition (victories against France and Israel), and now has an opportunity to secure qualification for the quarterfinals. Italy-Belgium: time The match between Italy and Belgium, valid for the third day of Group B of the UEFA Nations League League A, will be held at the Stadio Olimpico in Rome on Thursday, October 10th at 20:45. Where to watch it on TV and streaming Italy-Belgium will be broadcast live on Rai TV on channel Rai Uno and, in HD version, on 501. It will also be available for streaming in Italy on RaiPlay. Italy-Belgium: probable line-ups ITALY (3-5-2): Donnarumma; Di Lorenzo, Bastoni, Calafiori; Cambiaso, Frattesi, Ricci, Tonali, Dimarco; Raspadori, Retegui. Coach: Spalletti. BELGIUM (4-2-3-1): Casteels; Faes, Theate, Zeno, Castagne; Tielemans, Onana; Doku, Tielemans, Lukebakio; Openda. Coach: Tedesco. The referee The referee for Italy-Norway in the Nations League will be Norwegian Espen Eskas, 36 years old, international since 2017. He officiated the Champions League match Milan-Liverpool on September 17th. The new Nations League format Compared to the previous edition, which provided direct access for the first-place team to the Final Four, the top two in the group will advance this time. The eight teams will compete in the quarterfinals (home and away) in March 2024. The winners of the quarterfinals will then earn a place in the Finals, scheduled for June 2025 in the home of one of the four finalists. The fourth-place team in the group will be relegated to League B, while - another novelty - the third-place team will play a playoff to avoid relegation with one of the second-place teams from League B. The playoff spot for the 2026 World Cup will be reserved for the four finalists. Italy's group The Italy-Belgium match could be decisive for the Azzurri's progress in the Nations League. A victory would bring them very close to qualifying for the quarterfinals. A double victory, including against Israel, could even guarantee it, with a favorable result in the match between France and Belgium. Standings: Italy 6 points, France and Belgium 3, Israel 0 Italy's upcoming matches October 10, 2024: Italy-Belgium (20:45 – Stadio Olimpico, Rome) October 14, 2024: Italy-Israel (20:45 – Stadio Friuli, Udine) November 14, 2024: Belgium-Italy (20:45 – King Baudouin Stadium, Brussels) November 17, 2024: Italy-France (20:45 – Stadio Giuseppe Meazza, Milan)
