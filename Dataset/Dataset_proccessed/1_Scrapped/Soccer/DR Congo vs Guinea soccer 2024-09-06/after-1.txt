The  triumphant 1976 MWV Hogs: Front from left: Steve Eastman, Danny Grant, Curt Bartlett, Duck Skolfield, Freddy “Go-Nuts” Jones, Drew Phillips, Bob Wentowrth, Peter Duluca and team captain Richard DeAngelis. Back row: Jim Donatelli, David Alden, Phil Haynes, Bob “Tanaka” Tara, Bill “Boomer” Benson, Peter Mead, Skip Moody, Gary “Hog of Steel” Sheldon, Ricky Tibbetts and Tinker Shelly. (FILE PHOTO)

A Rowley Mud Ducks player dives for North Country Mud Crocs quarterback Jason Veno during their game at the North Conway's Hog Coliseum for the second day of Mud Bowl 2021. The Crocs won 36-6. (RACHEL SHARPLES PHOTO)

Mud Bowl Tournament of Mud Parade chair and North Conway Mud Bowl co-founder Steve Eastman (1949-2008), co-founder of the MWV Hogs in 2007 with his parade chairman successor Bobbie Steele-Marotta. (TOM EASTMAN PHOTO)

The Muddas cheerleaders celebrate with Josh Phillips after his touchdown in the game against the Nashua Mud Gumbys on the final day of the Mud Bowl tournament at the North Conway Community Center's Hog Coliseum on Sept. 11. The Muddas topped the Gumbys 20-6. (RACHEL SHARPLES PHOTO)

Over the years, the Tournament of Mud parade has featured such entertaining themes as “Swine Lake,” “2001: A Mud Odyssey,” “and “Children’s Muddy Shows.” The latter, with parade mascots, is seen in 2003. (FILE PHOTO)

Jason "Dr. J" Holder is shown throwing a pass in the packed, then newly-built Hog Coliseum in 1981. (DREW PHILLIPS PHOTO)

The North Country Mud Crocs pose with their trophy after the Mud Bowl championship game between the Nashua Mud Gumbys and the North Country Mud Crocs at the North Conway Community Center's Hog Coliseum on Sept. 10. The teams battled hard to tie 6-6 in regulation time, but the Crocs came out on top 14-6 to win the championship for the second year in a row. (RACHEL SHARPLES PHOTO)

Then-Mud Bowl Committee members seen at the Steve Eastman Memorial Boulder in 2009 (back row, from left) Tom Eastman, Bobbi Steele-Marotta, Audley Williams, Ryan Sommer, Lisa Brunelle and Jackie Howe; and (front, from left) Monica Belkin and Cathy McDonald. (COURTESY PHOTO)

CONWAY — Hey Mud Bowl!

For the past 50 years, ever since the first local mud football team was formed in 1975, that has been the rallying cry the weekend after Labor Day in the mud-friendly environs of Mount Washington Valley, the mud capital of New England and, yes, the world!
