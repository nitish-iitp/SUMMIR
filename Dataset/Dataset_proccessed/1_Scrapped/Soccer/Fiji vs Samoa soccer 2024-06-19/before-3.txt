National News Headlines in English for 19 June 2024

Stay updated with 19 June 2024 news headlines in English for school assembly. Learn about various sectors´ happenings in our country. 

1. China Expands Nuclear Arsenal; India Surpasses Pakistan:

China increased its nuclear warheads from 410 in January 2023 to 500 by January 2024, says SIPRI. India holds more nuclear weapons than Pakistan. Nine nuclear-armed nations, including the US and Russia, modernized their arsenals, deploying new nuclear-capable systems in 2023.

2. Fitch Boosts India’s Growth Forecast to 7.2%:

Fitch Ratings increased India’s growth forecast from 7% to 7.2%, citing a recovery in consumer spending and rising investment. The agency expects India’s economy to grow by 6.5% in 2025-26 and 6.2% in 2026-27, reflecting sustained economic momentum.

3. NHRC Demands Action on Poor Healthcare Facilities:

The NHRC issued a notice to the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, demanding an action report within four weeks due to poor healthcare at the grassroots level. This follows a petition highlighting inadequate infrastructure, with specific cases like actor Tapu Misra’s struggles in Odisha.

4. Global Leaders Reaffirm Support for Ukraine’s Integrity:

Leaders from nearly 90 countries, including India, met in Switzerland to support Ukraine’s territorial integrity. India’s Ministry of External Affairs reiterated its backing for a peaceful resolution, though it did not endorse any specific summit documents.

5. CBSE Introduces New Exam Format for Classes 11 and 12:

The CBSE will focus on developing critical, analytical, and creative thinking in students for the 2024-2025 academic year. The new curriculum emphasizes skills, comprehension, and problem-solving, aiming to prepare students better for higher education.

6. World Sickle Cell Awareness Day Observed on June 19:

Union Minister for Tribal Affairs highlighted World Sickle Cell Awareness Day, observed annually on June 19. The day promotes global awareness campaigns focusing on early diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of sickle cell disease, involving various organizations.

7. DoT Unveils 5G and Quantum Encryption Initiatives:

The Department of Telecommunications announced the 5G Intelligent Village and Quantum Encryption Algorithm proposals. Funded by the Telecom Technology Development Fund, these initiatives aim to advance 5G utilization and secure communications in the telecom sector.

8. NCERT to Use “Bharat” and “India” Interchangeably:
NCERT will use “Bharat” and “India” interchangeably in textbooks, following a high-level panel’s recommendation. This aligns with the Constitution and aims to reflect both terms in the educational curriculum without favoring either.

9. Congress’ Sarcastic Post on PM Modi Sparks BJP Outrage:

A deleted Congress post mocking PM Modi’s meeting with Pope Francis at the G7 summit sparked BJP outrage. The post, referring to Modi as “God,” drew criticism for disrespecting the Prime Minister’s statement about his divine purpose.

10. Indian Railways Inspects World’s Highest Rail Bridge:

Indian Railway officials inspected the newly built world’s highest railway bridge over the Chenab River in Jammu and Kashmir. The first trial train successfully ran from Sangaldan to Reasi, with plans to launch train services soon, connecting the valley with the rest of India.

International News in English for 19 June 2024

Explore global stories through international news in English. Discover updates from different countries:

1. U.S. Criticizes Vietnam for Inviting Putin Amid War Crimes Allegations:

The United States criticized Vietnam for inviting Russia’s President Vladimir Putin for a state visit on June 19-20. The U.S. embassy in Hanoi condemned providing Putin, wanted by the ICC for alleged war crimes, a platform to normalize his actions.

2. Rohingya Trapped Amid Fighting in Myanmar’s Western Region:

Tens of thousands of Rohingya Muslims are caught in escalating violence in western Myanmar. The powerful Arakan Army is attacking junta positions near the Bangladesh border, where authorities are hesitant to accept the fleeing Rohingya.

3. NATO Members Increase Defense Spending Amid Rising Threats:

NATO Secretary General Jens Stoltenberg announced that over 20 NATO members will meet the 2% GDP defence spending target this year. This marks significant growth since Russia’s 2014 Crimea annexation, highlighting allies’ commitment to enhanced military capabilities.

4. UK’s 26°C Heatwave Alert Amuses Indians Accustomed to Extreme Heat:

The UK’s heatwave alert for 26°C by late June has amused Indians, who are used to summer temperatures exceeding 50°C. Social media reactions reflect India’s familiarity with intense heat, contrasting sharply with the UK’s relatively mild weather alert.

5. Arakan Army Warns Rohingya to Evacuate Before Planned Offensive:

The Arakan Army has advised Maungdaw’s residents, mainly Rohingya, to evacuate ahead of a planned attack on the town. This move is part of a broader offensive against Myanmar’s junta, which has struggled to maintain control since the 2021 coup.

6. Kuwait Acts Swiftly After Fatal Fire Kills 49 Migrant Workers:

Following a fire that killed 49 migrant workers, mostly Indians, in Kuwait’s Al Ahmadi, officials took immediate action. Kuwait’s Interior Minister blamed employer greed and announced criminal charges, suspending municipal officials for failing to uphold building codes.

7. Fitch Raises India’s GDP Growth Forecast to 7.2%:

Fitch Ratings revised India’s GDP growth forecast for 2024-25 from 7% to 7.2%. The update reflects improved consumer spending and increased investment, indicating stronger economic performance than previously anticipated.

8. Google Launches Gemini AI App in India, Supporting Nine Languages:

Google launched its Gemini AI app in India, supporting Hindi, Bengali, Gujarati, Kannada, Malayalam, Marathi, Tamil, Telugu, and Urdu. Despite the February rebranding from Bard AI, Indian users will wait four months for the standalone app.

9. . Ten Migrants Dead, Dozens Missing After Shipwrecks Off Italy:

Two shipwrecks off Italy left ten migrants dead and dozens missing. Rescuers found ten bodies on a boat near Lampedusa, while another shipwreck off Calabria has left up to 60 people unaccounted for. Rescue efforts continue amid the tragedy.

10. Putin to Visit North Korea Amid Growing Russia-North Korea Ties:

Russian President Vladimir Putin will visit North Korea for the first time in 24 years on June 19-20. The visit, at Kim Jong Un’s invitation, highlights deepening ties between the two nations since Russia’s Ukraine invasion, alarming U.S. officials.

1. UEFA Bans Scotland’s Ryan Porteous for “Serious Rough Play:

UEFA has banned Scotland defender Ryan Porteous for two games following a studs-up tackle on Ilkay Gundogan during Scotland’s 5-1 loss to Germany in the Euro 2024 opener. The ban stems from “serious rough play” during the match on Friday.

2. Porteous Sees Red as Germany Defeats Scotland 5-1:

Germany led 2-0 when Ryan Porteous received a red card just before halftime for a two-footed tackle on Ilkay Gundogan. Kai Havertz scored the penalty, contributing to Germany’s 5-1 victory. Porteous will miss Scotland’s games against Switzerland and Hungary.

3. Pakistan Cricketers Extend Stay in London Post T20 World Cup:

Pakistan captain Babar Azam and five players, including Mohammad Amir and Imad Wasim, will stay in London for holidays after a poor T20 World Cup campaign. They will not return with the squad on Tuesday. Some may join local UK leagues, per The Express Tribune.

4. Super 8 Stage of T20 World Cup Begins June 19:

The 2024 T20 World Cup Super 8 stage starts June 19. India, topping Group A, will face Australia on June 24, repeating the 2023 World Cup Final. India’s Super 8 matches move from the USA to the Caribbean, starting against Afghanistan on June 20.

5. Neeraj Chopra Returns to Competition at Paavo Nurmi Games:

Olympic champion javelin thrower Neeraj Chopra returns from a short injury break to compete at the Paavo Nurmi Games on June 18 in Turku. Chopra, aiming to join the 90m club, will face Germany’s teen sensation Max Dehning, the youngest in the elite group.

Today’s Entertainment News in English 

Now, let’s dive into the entertainment world and see what new has taken place.

1. Lauryn Goodman Defies FA’s Warning, Heads to Euro 2024 for WAG Documentary:

Lauryn Goodman, former mistress of Manchester City defender Kyle Walker, plans to attend Euro 2024 with a camera crew despite warnings from the Football Association. The FA urged her to stay away to avoid off-field distractions for the Three Lions.

2. Mumbai International Film Festival Celebrates Asian Women Filmmakers:

The 18th Mumbai International Film Festival (MIFF) features a special “Asian Women Filmmakers” package, showcasing the talents of six female directors. The collection, curated by the International Association of Women in Radio and Television, celebrates empowerment, success, and equality through five exceptional films.

3. ‘Inside Out 2’ Breaks Records with $155 Million Domestic Opening:

Disney and Pixar’s ‘Inside Out 2’ has achieved a record-breaking debut, grossing $155 million domestically in its opening weekend. It surpasses major 2024 releases like ‘Dune 2’ and ‘Godzilla x King: The New Empire’, and has earned $295 million globally, the highest for an animated film’s opening weekend.

4. ‘Pushpa 2: The Rule’ Release Delayed to December 2024:

The release of ‘Pushpa 2: The Rule’ has been postponed from August to December 6, 2024, causing disappointment among fans of stars Allu Arjun and Rashmika Mandanna. The delay has sparked fan reactions, with some even threatening legal action. The film continues the saga started by ‘Pushpa: The Rise’.

5. Photographer Disqualified for Submitting Genuine Photo to AI Contest:

Photographer Miles Astray was disqualified from the 1839 Awards after his winning photo of a flamingo, initially thought to be AI-generated, was revealed to be genuine. Astray entered the real photo to challenge the trend of AI images winning competitions, saying he wanted to twist the story in a uniquely human way.

What Happened Today in History

Understand the significance of what happened today in history and learn how to make a difference:

1. Juneteenth Marks End of Slavery in Texas (1865)

General Gordon Granger declared freedom for Texas slaves on June 19, 1865. Juneteenth now celebrates the end of slavery in the United States, emphasizing freedom and African American heritage.

2. Alcatraz Becomes Infamous Federal Prison (1934)

The U.S. designated Alcatraz Island as a federal prison on June 19, 1934. Known for housing notorious criminals like Al Capone, it becomes a symbol of America’s toughest penal facilities.

3. Landmark Civil Rights Act Signed (1964)

President Lyndon B. Johnson signed the Civil Rights Act on June 19, 1964. It outlaws discrimination based on race, colour, religion, sex, or national origin, reshaping equality laws in the United States.

4. Valentina Tereshkova: First Woman in Space (1963)

Soviet cosmonaut Valentina Tereshkova orbits Earth aboard Vostok 6 on June 19, 1963. She pioneers space travel for women, inspiring future generations and advancing Soviet space exploration.

5. India’s Historic Cricket World Cup Victory (1983)

India triumphed in the Cricket World Cup on June 19, 1983, defeating the West Indies. Led by Kapil Dev, this victory sparks national pride and transforms cricket into a passion across India.
