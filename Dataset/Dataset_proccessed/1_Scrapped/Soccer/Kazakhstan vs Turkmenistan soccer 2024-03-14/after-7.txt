Older adults don’t use emojis as much. But is it because they don’t understand them?

A new study at the University of Ottawa looks at whether older adults can decode the emotional meaning of emojis, even if they use them less.

Updated March 15, 2024 at 3:50 p.m.
March 14, 2024
3 min read
Save

A new study at the University of Ottawa looks at whether older adults are able to decode the emotional meaning of emojis, even if they use them less and are less comfortable doing so. 

Toronto Star illustration with Dreamstime images
By Mark ColleyStaff Reporter

When Isabelle Boutet’s parents send her texts, they often include a mess of emojis.

They’re stuck at the end of the message, multiple in a row. She doesn’t know why her parents chose those emojis or what they mean.
