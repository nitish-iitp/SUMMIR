Opinion | Does the Ontario Special Investigations Unit need a broader mandate to improve police oversight?

Policy reviews could play a vital role in addressing institutional and systemic issues that may prevent future cases of police misconduct, Kate Puddister and Danielle McNabb write.

June 5, 2024
3 min read
Save

The Special Investigations Unit was called to investigate after Hamilton police shot and killed a woman armed with a knife in 2018. The SIU is charged with investigating potential misconduct by Ontario police officers.

Barry Gray The Hamilton Spectator file photo
By Kate Puddister and Danielle McNabb

Kate Puddister is an associate professor in the Department of Political Science at the University of Guelph. Danielle McNabb is an assistant professor in the Department of Political Science at Brock University. This was originally published on The Conversation.

At the end of April, just outside of Toronto, a police pursuit of a robbery suspect ended in a fatal vehicle collision. Four people were killed, including two grandparents and their infant grandchild, as well as the person being pursued by the police.

The Special Investigations Unit (SIU) of Ontario, a police oversight agency, has launched an investigation into the police conduct to determine if criminal charges are warranted.
