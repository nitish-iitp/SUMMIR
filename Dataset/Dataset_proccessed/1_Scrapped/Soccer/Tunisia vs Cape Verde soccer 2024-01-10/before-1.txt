HOME AND GARDEN
They’re going back home... to Oshawa. Why?

Couple moves back to Oshawa, one of Canada’s fastest-growing cities and the GTA’s most affordable. It boasts urban amenities and access to green space.

Updated Jan. 10, 2024 at 3:46 p.m.
Jan. 6, 2024
3 min read
Save

Ranjana Nayar and her son Connor in Oshawa. Nayar and her husband have purchased a house that is “Net Zero Ready.”

Nick Lachance
By Tracy Hanes Special to the Star

Like many people who grew up in Scarborough, Ranjana Nayar and her husband Ajay migrated east. They moved to Ajax after they married and came to love Durham Region. As Ajax started to get denser and they were ready for a new home, they looked to Oshawa. It was an easy commute to Nayar’s teaching job in Ajax, had extensive urban amenities and access to green spaces. It’s where they want to spend their retirement years.

They bought their first house in north Oshawa in 2016 and sold it nearly two years ago.
