Senegal Edges Mauritania 1-0 in 2026 World Cup Qualifier
Published on June 8, 2024 - Updated on June 9, 2024
Écouter cet article
By 
The editorial staff
 
Suivre

In the 2026 World Cup qualifiers, Senegal secured a hard-fought victory against Mauritania, earning three crucial points. The match, held on June 9, 2024, saw Habib Diallo scoring the decisive goal with an assist from Pape Matar Sarr. This win followed a 1-1 draw with the Democratic Republic of Congo. Under coach Aliou Cissé, Senegal’s national team, the Lions of Teranga, now leads Group B waiting for Sudan play.

In a crucial 2026 World Cup qualifying match, Senegal triumphed over Mauritania with a 1-0 victory. The game, held on June 9, 2024, showcased a tight contest with Habib Diallo scoring the decisive goal. Diallo’s strike came in the second half, thanks to an assist from Pape Matar Sarr. This win was vital for Senegal, following their 1-1 draw with the Democratic Republic of Congo. The victory propels Senegal to the top of Group B, reinforcing their strong position under coach Aliou Cissé.
