Padres don’t get much, give away plenty in loss to Mariners
The Mariners’ Victor Robles slides into second as Padres second baseman Jake Cronenworth loses the ball on a missed-catch error during the third inning of Wednesday’s game. (AP Photo/Jason Redmond)
By KEVIN ACEE | kevin.acee@sduniontribune.com | The San Diego Union-Tribune
UPDATED: September 11, 2024 at 10:17 PM PDT

SEATTLE — The Padres got here, participating in games that matter in  September, by playing generally clean baseball and pouncing when their opponents have not done so.

On Wednesday, they had it done to them.

The Padres gave away runs, did not have a baserunner until the seventh inning and lost 5-2 to the Mariners.

It didn’t feel that close.
