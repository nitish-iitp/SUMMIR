Complete coverage: Astros blow out Rangers in crucial ALCS Game 4
Houston first baseman Jose Abreu opened up a once-tied game with a three-run homer in the fourth inning.

By SportsDay Staff

Oct. 19, 2023
|
Updated 10:17 p.m. CDT
|
4 min. read
Texas Rangers first baseman Nathaniel Lowe , third baseman Josh Jung, shortstop Corey Seager, and second baseman Marcus Semien during a pitching change in the fourth inning against the Houston Astros in Game 4 of the American League Championship Series on Thursday, Oct. 19, in Arlington.(Smiley N. Pool / Staff Photographer)

The Texas Rangers failed to bounce back after suffering their first loss of the 2023 postseason.

Texas fell again to the Houston Astros at home, 10-3, on Thursday night, making the best-of-seven American League Championship Series a 2-2 tie heading into Friday’s Game 5.

The roof was open. But in the end, that didn’t help the home team enough. Continue reading for full coverage from Game 4:

Game updates

(10:17 p.m.) Ninth inning: Astros 10, Rangers 3

Top: Martin Perez retired the side in order.

ADVERTISEMENT

Bottom: Rafael Montero came on to close it out for Houston, surrendering only a walk to Jonah Heim. The Astros have tied the ALCS at two games apiece.

ADVERTISEMENT

(10:04 p.m.) Eighth inning: Astros 10, Rangers 3

Top: Jose Altuve greeted new pitcher Martin Perez with a first-pitch double to start the eighth. It was initially called a home run, but was overturned upon review. He would later score on a Yordan Alvarez RBI single.

Bottom: Corey Seager picked up his second hit of the night, a single to right field, but Texas could not do any further damage.

(9:42 p.m.) Seventh inning: Astros 9, Rangers 3

ADVERTISEMENT

Top: Houston extended its lead thanks to a two-run homer from Chas McCormick.

Bottom: Phil Maton is on to pitch for Houston. Texas was again retired in order, including a highlight-reel play from Jose Altuve.

ADVERTISEMENT

(9:25 p.m.) Sixth inning: Astros 7, Rangers 3

ADVERTISEMENT

Top: Chris Stratton returned to the mound, and almost recorded another quick inning. But a two-out walk to Alex Bregman became his last batter of the night. He was replaced by Will Smith, who got Yordan Alvarez to ground out to end the frame.

Bottom: The Rangers were retired in order, another inning of work from Houston reliver Hunter Brown.

ADVERTISEMENT

(9:08 p.m.) Fifth inning: Astros 7, Rangers 3

Top: Chris Stratton came on to pitch the fifth. He retired the side in order, including two strikeouts.

ADVERTISEMENT

Bottom: Two singles from Leody Taveras and Marcus Semien gave the start to the inning promise. Corey Seager then rocketed a line drive at first baseman Jose Abreu, who caught it, then tagged a diving Semien as he tried to tag up at first base (he was called safe initially, but the call was overturned on review). Evan Carter then flied out to center to end the inning.

ADVERTISEMENT

(8:52 p.m.) Fourth inning: Astros 7, Rangers 3

Top: Dane Dunning found trouble in the fourth, loading the bases after two walks and a single to open the inning. Dunning then struck out Alex Bregman, his final batter, and was replaced by Cody Bradford. Yordan Alvarez then hit an RBI sacrifice fly to the wall in center field, retaking the lead for Houston, before Jose Abreu demolished a three-run homer 438 feet to left field.

ADVERTISEMENT

Bottom: The Rangers went quietly in the bottom of the inning in order. Nathaniel Lowe and Josh Jung both struck out against new pitcher Hunter Brown.

ADVERTISEMENT

(8:22 p.m.) Third inning: Rangers 3, Astros 3

Top: Dane Dunning provided a much-needed quick inning, including a strikeout of Jeremy Pena to end the frame.

ADVERTISEMENT

Bottom: Corey Seager tied the game with a 401-foot blast the opposite way, igniting the crowd. A pair of singles from Evan Carter and Adolis Garcia knocked Houston starter Jose Urquidy from the game. Ryne Stanek replace him, and rolled a double play grounder from Mitch Garver to end the inning.

ADVERTISEMENT

(8:03 p.m.) Second inning: Astros 3, Rangers 2

Top: Houston put runners on base again thanks to a Jose Altuve single and Alex Bregman walk, but were unable to add to the scoreboard.

ADVERTISEMENT

Bottom: Adolis Garcia brought the crowd back to life with a leadoff homer in the second inning. After a Mitch Garver walk and a Nathaniel Lowe double, Josh Jung brought the Rangers within one on an RBI sacrifice fly to left field.

ADVERTISEMENT

(7:31 p.m.) First inning: Astros 3, Rangers 0

Top: A leadoff double by Jose Altuve was followed by a Mauricio Dubon single to put runners on the corners with no outs. An Alex Bregman and a Yordan Alvarez single later, and Houston scored three runs before Andrew Heaney recorded one out in the inning. Dane Dunning replaced him on the mound after a two-out walk to Chas McCormick. Dunning struck out Martin Maldonado with the bases loaded to end a long first inning.

ADVERTISEMENT

Bottom: The Rangers were unable to respond in the bottom of the inning. José Urquidy retired Marcus Semien, Corey Seager and Evan Carter in order.
