The Milwaukee Brewers (90-67) will visit the Pittsburgh Pirates (73-84), in the second game of a three-game series, Wednesday at 6:40 p.m. ET.

The Brewers are a favorite (-154) against the Pirates (+130). The Brewers will start Freddy Peralta (11-8) against the Pirates and Luis Ortiz (6-6).

The Brewers defeated the Pirates 7-2 yesterday, with Hoby Milner getting the win (1.0 IP, 0 R, 0 H, 1 K) and Willy Adames leading the way offensively (going 2-for-4 with a double and an RBI). Bailey Falter (5.0 IP, 4 R, 5 H, 5 K) took the loss for the Pirates.

Brewers stats and trends
Brewers betting records
This season, the Brewers have won 53 out of the 89 games, or 59.6%, in which they’ve been favored.
Milwaukee has a record of 17-9 in games when oddsmakers favor them by at least -154 on the moneyline.
The implied probability of a win from the Brewers, based on the moneyline, is 60.6%.
So far this season, Milwaukee and its opponents have hit the over in 81 of 157 games with a total.
The Brewers have an ATS record of 82-74-0 in 156 games with a spread this season.
Freddy Peralta (Brewers probable starter)
Peralta makes the start for the Brewers, his 32nd of the season. He is 11-8 with a 3.69 ERA and 193 strikeouts in 168 1/3 innings pitched.
The righty last appeared on Wednesday against the Philadelphia Phillies, when he threw five innings, allowing one earned run while giving up two hits.
The 28-year-old has an ERA of 3.69, with 10.3 strikeouts per nine innings, in 31 games this season. Opponents are hitting .227 against him.
Peralta has registered 11 quality starts this season.
Peralta is aiming for his third straight appearance lasting five or more innings. He averages 5.4 frames per appearance on the mound.
He has made five appearances this season in which he did not surrender an earned run.
The opposing Pirates offense has a collective .236 batting average, and is 22nd in the league with 1256 total hits and 23rd in MLB action with 644 runs scored. It has the 28th-ranked slugging percentage (.371) and ranks 26th in home runs (151) in all of MLB.
Peralta has pitched 4 2/3 innings, giving up five earned runs on five hits while striking out five against the Pirates this season.
This season, the 28-year-old ranks 30th in ERA (3.69), 39th in WHIP (1.230), and ninth in K/9 (10.3) among pitchers who qualify.
Brewers batting stats
The Brewers’ 174 home runs rank 17th in Major League Baseball.
The offense for Milwaukee has a slugging percentage of .404 this season, 13th in MLB.
The Brewers have a team batting average of .249 this season, which ranks eighth among MLB teams.
Milwaukee is among the highest-scoring teams in the majors, ranking fifth with 757 total runs this season.
The Brewers have an on-base percentage of .326 this season, which ranks fourth in the league.
Milwaukee ranks 20th in strikeouts per game (nine) among MLB offenses.
Pirates stats and trends
Pirates betting records
The Pirates have been chosen as underdogs in 93 games this year and have walked away with the win 37 times (39.8%) in those games.
Pittsburgh has a win-loss record of 12-25 when favored by +130 or worse by sportsbooks this year.
The moneyline set for this matchup implies the Pirates have a 43.5% chance of walking away with the win.
Pittsburgh and its opponents have gone over in 75 of its 157 games with a total set by sportsbooks this season.
In 156 games with a line this season, the Pirates have a mark of 83-73-0 against the spread.
Luis Ortiz (Pirates probable starter)
Ortiz (6-6) gets the starting nod for the Pirates in his 15th start of the season. He has a 3.43 ERA in 128 2/3 innings pitched, with 102 strikeouts.
The right-hander’s last time out was on Thursday against the St. Louis Cardinals, when he tossed six innings, surrendering two earned runs while giving up five hits.
The 25-year-old has an ERA of 3.43, with 7.1 strikeouts per nine innings in 36 games this season. Opposing batters have a .221 batting average against him.
Ortiz has registered six quality starts this season.
Ortiz is trying to pick up his 15th start of five or more innings this season in this outing.
He has had 16 appearances this season that he held his opponents to zero earned runs.
The opposing Brewers offense has a collective .249 batting average, and is ninth in the league with 1320 total hits and fifth in MLB play with 757 runs scored. They have the 13th-ranked slugging percentage (.404) and are 17th in all of MLB with 174 home runs.
Head-to-head against the Brewers this season, Ortiz has thrown 4 2/3 innings, giving up two earned runs on four hits while striking out nine.
Pirates batting stats
The Pirates are fifth-worst in MLB action with 151 home runs.
So far this season, Pittsburgh has the third-lowest slugging percentage in baseball (.371).
The Pirates are 23rd in MLB with a .236 batting average.
Pittsburgh scores the 23rd-most runs in baseball (644 total, 4.1 per game).
The Pirates’ .302 on-base percentage is 25th in the majors.
