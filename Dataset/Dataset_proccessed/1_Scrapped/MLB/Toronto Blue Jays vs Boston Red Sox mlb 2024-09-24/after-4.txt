BOTTOM LINE: The Toronto Blue Jays play the Boston Red Sox after losing four games in a row.

Toronto has a 73-84 record overall and a 38-38 record at home. Blue Jays hitters have a collective .313 on-base percentage, the sixth-ranked percentage in the AL.

Boston has a 79-78 record overall and a 42-37 record in road games. The Red Sox have a 34-20 record in games when they did not give up a home run.

Tuesday's game is the 12th time these teams match up this season. The Red Sox hold a 7-4 advantage in the season series.

TOP PERFORMERS: Vladimir Guerrero Jr. has 42 doubles, a triple and 30 home runs for the Blue Jays. Alejandro Kirk is 10-for-35 with two doubles and a triple over the past 10 games.
