Tigers fight to incredible finish, beat Baltimore in 10 innings
Updated: Sep. 22, 2024, 7:52 a.m.|Published: Sep. 21, 2024, 7:45 p.m.

Detroit Tigers catcher Dillon Dingler, right, tags out Baltimore Orioles' Adley Rutschman, at home on a single by Heston Kjerstad during the fourth inning of a baseball game, Saturday, Sept. 21, 2024, in Baltimore. (AP Photo/Nick Wass)AP

By Evan Woodbery | ewoodber@mlive.com

The Detroit Tigers survived a ninth inning in which they appeared doomed, then scored two runs in the 10th to beat the Baltimore Orioles 6-4 on Saturday afternoon at Camden Yards.

The wild sequence of events -- taking place within about a five-minute span -- will go down as one of the critical moments of the 2024 season if the Tigers end up making the playoffs.
