Toronto Blue Jays vs Tampa Bay Rays Betting Pick & Prediction 9/21/24
Sep 21, 2024
By: Ryan Knuppel

The Blue Jays and Rays are set to face off in an AL East matchup at 4:10 PM ET at Tropicana Field in St. Petersburg, FL. Toronto is 73-81 and they are 5th in the AL East, while the Rays have won two in a row to move to 76-78 overall.

The money line odds have the Rays at -137 compared to the Blue Jays at +116, and the over/under line is currently at 7.5 runs. BSSUN is televising this matchup.

TAMPA BAY RAYS VS TORONTO BLUE JAYS BETTING PICK

The Pick: Tampa Bay Rays Moneyline -137

This game will be played at Tropicana Field at 4:10 ET on Saturday, September 21st.

HOW TO BET THE BLUE JAYS VS RAYS:
We have the Rays winning by a score of 5 to 4
However, if you're looking for a run line pick, we like the Blue Jays to cover
As for an over/under pick, we have this game finishing with 9 runs and like the over

Tampa Bay picked up a 1-0 win over the Blue Jays in the most recent game of this series. The Rays had just one more hit than the Blue Jays and struck out nine times, but still picked up the win. Heading into the game, they were at -101 on the money line.

Both teams wasted good starting pitching performances in this game. Tyler Alexander went just 4 1/3 innings for the Rays but didn't give up a hit or a run. Jose Berrios went six innings for the Blue Jays, giving up just one run on six hits.

The only run of the game came in the 6th inning when Jonathan Aranda hit a solo home run for the Rays. Aranda was the only player in the game to have more than one hit.

Blue Jays Records & Stats

Toronto is 73-81 overall, putting them in 5th place in the AL East. Currently, they trail the Red Sox by three games for 4th place in the division, and they are 17 games behind the Yankees for the division lead. So far, they have gone just 20-27 in AL East matchups.

At home, the Blue Jays are above .500 at 38-37 this year. On the road, they are 35-44 heading into today's matchup. The Blue Jays have been good as the favorite this year, going 44-29, but they are just 29-52 as the underdog. Toronto's overall series record is 17-26-5, and they are currently down 0-1 in this series vs. the Rays.

When the Blue Jays win, they do so by an average of 3.3 runs per game. In their losses, they lose by an average of 3.6 runs per game. They are 77-77 on the run line this season, including 49-30 on the road. They are 28-47 on the run line at home, where their average run line is -0.6. Their overall run line average is -0.3. They are 32-41 on the run line as the favorite and 45-36 as the underdog.

The Blue Jays are on the road against the Rays today, with the over/under line set at 7.5 runs. The combined run average in Blue Jays games this season is 8.7 runs per game, and their over/under record is 78-72. When the over/under line has been set at 7.5 runs, their record is 18-17. Overall, 74.0% of their games have had higher over/under lines than 7.5 runs, and their current under streak is at 3 games.

Right-hander Yariel Rodríguez gets the start for the Blue Jays today as he faces the Rays on the road. He has made 19 starts this season and has a record of 1-6 with an ERA of 4.29. Opposing batters have hit .222 this season off Rodríguez, and his WHIP is currently 1.30. In his 19 appearances, he has turned in two quality starts and is averaging 8.92 strikeouts per nine innings. Rodríguez's last outing came on September 15th, where he finished with a no-decision, giving up one earned run in 4 1/3 innings of work. He has not taken a loss since August 27th.

So far this season, the Blue Jays offense has been pretty mediocre, as they are 20th in runs per game at 4.2 and are also just 22nd in home runs. As a team, they are batting .242, which is 11th in the league, and have the 6th fewest strikeouts per game in the league. Vladimir Guerrero Jr. has been a bright spot for the team, as he is batting .322 for the season and is 10th in the league with 99 RBIs. Guerrero Jr. also leads the team with 30 homers.

Over his last six games, Davis Schneider has three home runs and is batting .348. Justin Turner and Ernie Clement are both on four-game hitting streaks, while Alejandro Kirk has a hit in each of his last three games. George Springer is 2nd on the team with 19 homers, but is batting just .221 for the season.

Rays Records & Stats

Tampa Bay is 76-78 overall and are 14 games behind the Yankees for the AL East lead. Currently, they are in 3rd place in the division, 14 games behind the Yankees and tied with the Red Sox. The Rays have gone 22-25 against other AL East teams this year.

The Rays have won two straight games, and they are 5-5 across their last 10. At home, they are 40-39 this year and 36-39 on the road. As the favorite, the Rays are 35-31 and 41-47 as the underdog this year. So far, they have put together an overall series record of 23-19-6.

When the Rays win, they win big, with an average run margin of 2.8 runs per game. When they lose, they lose big, with an average run margin of -3.4 runs per game. Overall, they have a run line record of 80-74, including a 37-42 mark at home and a 43-32 mark on the road. They are 56-32 against the run line as an underdog and 24-42 as a favorite.

With an over/under line of 7.5 runs, the Tampa Bay Rays are involved in a game with a lower O/U line than their season average of 8 runs per game. The Rays have played in 87 games this season with higher O/U lines than 7.5 runs, making this a lower-scoring game based on the season's trends. The under has hit in three straight games for the Rays.

Taj Bradley gets the start for the Rays today vs. the Blue Jays and comes in with a record of 6-11 and an ERA of 4.39. Looking back at his last outing, Bradley took the loss vs. the Guardians, going 4 1/3 innings and giving up two earned runs, six hits, and three walks. Before that outing, he had given up at least four earned runs in three straight starts. Bradley has made nine quality starts this year and is averaging 10.13 strikeouts per nine innings. For the season, he has allowed 22 homers and is averaging 3.12 walks per nine innings.

For the season, the Rays are one of the worst offensive teams in the league, averaging just 3.8 runs per game, which is 29th in the MLB. They are also near the bottom of the league in home runs and have a team batting average of just .231. However, they do have three players with at least 19 homers, led by Christopher Morel, who has gone deep 21 times but is batting just .197.

Yandy Diaz has been the Rays' most consistent hitter this season, batting .283 with 14 homers and a team-leading 65 RBIs. Over his last eight games, Brandon Lowe has two homers but is just 7/33 (.212). Jonathan Aranda has also gone 2/8 in that stretch.
