Baseball: Dodgers' Shohei Ohtani steals 3 bases, closes on "50-50" landmark

 KYODO NEWS - Sep 3, 2024 - 14:52 | Sports, All

Shohei Ohtani stole three bases to edge closer to the "50-50" milestone as the Los Angeles Dodgers overpowered their National League West rival Arizona Diamondbacks 11-6 on Monday.

The two-way star, who on Friday became the first Major League Baseball player to register 43 home runs and the same number of stolen bases in a single season, now stands at 44-46 with 24 games to go.

"If I can play in as many games as possible while staying healthy, there's a chance (of reaching 50-50)," said Ohtani, who has been kept off the mound this year following surgery to his throwing right elbow last September.

Ohtani ripped a line drive to right to open the game at Chase Field and drew a walk in the fourth inning before stealing second with Arizona starter Eduardo Rodriguez (2-1), a left-hander, on the mound.

Shohei Ohtani (R) of the Los Angeles Dodgers safely slides to steal second base in the seventh inning of a baseball game against the Arizona Diamondbacks on Sept. 2, 2024, at Chase Field in Phoenix, Arizona, marking his 45th stolen base of the season. (Kyodo) ==Kyodo

Ohtani then singled in the seventh in a similar manner to his first at-bat and went on to steal second with reliever Jordan Montgomery, another southpaw, pitching. Ohtani did not stop there as he stole third on the next pitch, again beating the ball from catcher Adrian Del Castillo to the base.

"I think of a situation holistically and take off when I think I can reach safely," Ohtani said. "It's ideal when I can advance. I'm glad if the steals lead to my team winning."

Mookie Betts drove in three runs, while Freddie Freeman hit a pair of two-run shots and had five RBIs for the Dodgers. Ohtani, who went 2-for-4 and drew two walks, is eying his first MLB postseason appearance as the NL West-leading Dodgers bolstered their title hopes by winning three games in the important four-game series against Arizona.

"I've never been in such a good position in September," he said. "Competing with teams from the same league at this time of the year is another experience I haven't had before. There are lots of games where the tension is high."

Ohtani will next face his former team the Los Angeles Angels in a two-game series, starting Tuesday at Angel Stadium. It will be his first return to his former home stadium in the regular season.

"I'm looking forward to it. It's a ballpark where I've spent longest," he said. "It's special for me and I'll look to give my all."
