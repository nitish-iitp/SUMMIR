Rays call up reliever Mason Montgomery, drop catcher Alex Jackson
Notes | Left-hander Tyler Alexander is optioned to Triple-A Durham, and infielder Austin Shenton is called up on a busy morning of moves.
The Rays promoted hard-throwing, left-handed reliever Mason Montgomery as part of a series of roster moves before Thursday’s game against the Twins. [ IVY CEBALLO | Times ]
By
Marc TopkinTimes staff
Published Sept. 5, 2024|Updated Sept. 6, 2024

ST. PETERSBURG — Wednesday night, Mason Montgomery and his fiancee, Madison, were walking around a Target store in Columbus, Ohio, looking for a few things after taking advantage of a rare midweek night off for Triple-A Durham to see the movie, “Blink Twice.”

Thursday afternoon, after a couple of hours of sleep and an early morning Southwest flight to Tampa, the left-handed reliever was on the mound at Tropicana Field making his major-league debut in the Rays’ 4-3 loss to Minnesota.

“It’s all been kind of a whirlwind,” Montgomery said.

His call-up was one of several moves the Rays made Thursday.
