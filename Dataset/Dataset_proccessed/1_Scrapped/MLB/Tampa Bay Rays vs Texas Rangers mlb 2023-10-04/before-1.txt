The Rays didn’t show up for Game 1, and neither did their fans
John Romano | The smallest postseason crowd in 104 years watched Tampa Bay commit four errors and lose to Texas in the Wild Card Series opener.
Rays starting pitcher Tyler Glasnow reacts after giving up a double to Rangers shortstop Corey Seager during the fifth inning in Game 1 of the Wild Card Series Tuesday at Tropicana Field. [ JEFFEREE WOO | Times ]
By
John RomanoTimes staff
Published Oct. 4, 2023|Updated Oct. 4, 2023

ST. PETERSBURG — Thank goodness the crowd was so small or that might have really been embarrassing.

Never have so few shown up to watch such ugly postseason baseball as Tuesday afternoon’s Game 1 of the American League Wild Card Series. The record books will show the Rangers beat the Rays 4-0, but future scholars will focus on how ineptitude clashed with indifference in what should have been the most joyous game of the season.

“We didn’t hit, pitch or defend,” Rays manager Kevin Cash said in the most perfect summation imaginable.
