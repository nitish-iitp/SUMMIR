BLUE JAYS
The Blue Jays’ final indignity? A sweep at the hands of the 100-loss Marlins

Jays wrap up their first losing season since 2019.

Updated Sept. 29, 2024 at 7:35 p.m.
Sept. 29, 2024
1 min read
Save

Blue Jays shortstop Ernie Clement can’t corral this ground ball in a 3-1 loss to the Miami Marlins, the Jays’ 88th loss of 2004.

Steve Russell/Toronto Star
By Mike WilnerBaseball Columnist

The Blue Jays slunk meekly into the off-season, once again not putting up much of a fight against the 100-loss Miami Marlins, losing 3-1 at the Rogers Centre on the season’s final day.

Their horrible 2024 ended with some kind of whimper: a three-game sweep at the hands of one of baseball’s worst teams, outscored by a combined 26-7 while managing just eight hits over the final two games.
