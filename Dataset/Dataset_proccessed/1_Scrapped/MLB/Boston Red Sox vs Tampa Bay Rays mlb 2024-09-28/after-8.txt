Junior Caminero, a key to Rays’ future, sparks victory over Red Sox
The prized 21-year-old has a three-hit game, including a homer over the Green Monster, and Shane Baz delivers a strong start.
The Rays' Junior Caminero celebrates his two-run home run during the third inning of Saturday's game against the Red Sox in Boston. [ MICHAEL DWYER | AP ]
By
Marc TopkinTimes staff
Published Sept. 28, 2024|Updated Sept. 29, 2024

BOSTON — Saturday’s penultimate game of the Rays’ season started in a way that seemed a fitting tribute to many of the others that preceded it. They got their first two hitters on, loaded the bases with one out, yet got nothing out of it.

But as the crisp afternoon went on, the Rays showed — despite a historically bad performance over six months with runners in scoring position — that they occasionally can deliver big hits in key situations, and beat the Red Sox 7-2.
