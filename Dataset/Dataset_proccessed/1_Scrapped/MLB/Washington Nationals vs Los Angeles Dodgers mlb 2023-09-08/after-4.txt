LA Dodgers say Mookie Betts is 'in a tube somewhere' in concerning injury update
The Los Angeles Dodgers have yet to officially place star outfielder Mookie Betts on the Injured List, but he will likely miss the entire series against the Washington Nationals this weekend
Mookie Betts fouled a pitch off his foot and must now undergo an assortment of scans to assess the injury. (Image: Getty Images)

Los Angeles Dodgers manager Dave Roberts has clarified the condition of injured MVP candidate Mookie Betts - and the outlook sounds concerning after the outfielder fouled a pitch off his left foot Thursday night and left the stadium on crutches.

Roberts told reporters that Betts is "in a tube somewhere" in Washington, D.C., for a CT scan. The skipper added that the 30-year-old will receive a full assortment of evaluations, including an X-ray and MRI.

"Just to kind of make sure that we have all the information," Roberts said. "[He felt] better today, but I just don't see him in [the lineup] tomorrow. And [probably] not on Sunday."

Betts has been the catalyst for Los Angeles' scintillating second half. The club trailed the Arizona Diamondbacks by several games in the NL West earlier this year before accelerating 12.5 games clear of its rivals entering Friday night's game against the Washington Nationals.

Betts is batting .314 with 38 home runs and 99 RBIs in 133 games - his best all-around statistical year since he won MVP with the Boston Red Sox in 2018. He has hammered opposing pitchers from the leadoff spot throughout this season, but particularly in recent months; he's posted an absurd 1.122 on-base plus slugging from the top of the order in the second half.

The seven-time All-Star is far from the only ailing member of the World Series-hopeful Dodgers. On Friday, Roberts also announced that right-hander Walker Buehler would be shut down for the rest of the season. The organization had hoped Buehler could return from Tommy John surgery before the playoffs but determined it wasn't worth rushing his recovery. Roberts said he hopes Buehler can fully participate in 2024 Spring Training.

"I think that for us [it was best] to try to kick the can and see what's next and wait for the next progression," Roberts said. "The smart thing to do was just to kind of say, 'That's enough for the rehab games. No more rehab games.' And then now our focus is to build him for 2024."

Wrote Buehler in a statement: "My goal since last year has been to return to a major league mound this season. After many conversations with my doctor, the Dodgers' front office, training staff and my family, we concluded that waiting until next season is the right course of action. I am disappointed that I will not be able to help this team go after a title in the 2023 postseason, but I look forward to returning fully healthy in 2024 and bringing another World Series to L.A."

Los Angeles also waits for relief pitchers Joe Kelly and Blake Treinen to return from injuries. Starters Dustin May and Tony Gonsolin will miss the rest of the season, while infielder Gavin Lux is uncertain in his recovery from his ACL problem. And ace Julio Urias was placed on administrative leave after his arrest last week on suspicion of felony domestic violence.

Betts, though, is now the chief concern for a team that must get through the dominant Atlanta Braves to reach the World Series. On Friday, Chris Taylor will occupy the leadoff spot.
