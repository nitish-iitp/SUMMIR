AL Wild Card Game 2 Preview: Kansas City Royals vs. Baltimore Orioles Prediction - October 2, 2024

Matchup: Kansas City Royals (86-76) at Baltimore Orioles (91-71)

Venue: Oriole Park at Camden Yards, Baltimore, Maryland - 1:30 PM ET

 

Introduction

The Kansas City Royals have taken a 1-0 series lead in the AL Wild Card after a strong Game 1 performance, and now they look to close out the series against the Baltimore Orioles in Game 2. Kansas City will send Seth Lugo to the mound, who has been a consistent force for the Royals all season, while the Orioles will counter with Zach Eflin, hoping to keep their playoff hopes alive. With Kansas City carrying momentum into Game 2, they will aim to complete the upset and advance to the next round of the postseason.

Team Analysis
Kansas City Royals

The Royals have been strong heading into Game 2, winning four of their last five games and now leading the series 1-0. Offensively, Kansas City has been led by Bobby Witt Jr., who posted an MVP-caliber season with 32 home runs, 109 RBIs, and a stellar .332 batting average. The Royals’ pitching has also been impressive, with Seth Lugo coming off a dominant regular season where he went 16-9 with a 3.00 ERA and a 1.09 WHIP. Lugo’s command has been a key to his success, striking out 181 batters while keeping walks to a minimum.

Baltimore Orioles

The Orioles, after finishing second in the AL East with a 91-71 record, are now on the brink of elimination. Offensively, they are powered by Anthony Santander, who hit 44 home runs and drove in 102 RBIs. However, Baltimore’s offense was largely neutralized in Game 1, scoring just one run. Zach Eflin will take the mound for the Orioles, bringing a 10-9 record, a 3.59 ERA, and a 1.15 WHIP from the regular season. Eflin’s ability to limit damage will be critical if Baltimore is to extend the series and force a Game 3.

Starting Pitcher Analysis
Seth Lugo (KC)

Seth Lugo has been one of the most consistent arms for the Royals this season, finishing with a 16-9 record and a 3.00 ERA over 196 innings pitched. He posted a strong 1.09 WHIP, ranking him among the league’s best at controlling base runners. Lugo’s ability to mix pitches effectively, combining a fastball and curveball to generate strikeouts, has resulted in 181 strikeouts for the season. His control has been excellent, allowing just 47 walks, making him a tough matchup for Baltimore’s lineup, which struggled in Game 1.

Zach Eflin (BAL)

Zach Eflin finished the regular season with a 10-9 record and a 3.59 ERA, along with 134 strikeouts over 177 innings. While not a high strikeout pitcher, Eflin relies on his ability to generate weak contact and work deep into games. His 1.15 WHIP shows his effectiveness at limiting base runners, but he has had bouts of inconsistency this season, especially against power-hitting teams. Eflin will need to be at his best to contain Kansas City’s offense, which has been riding high after their Game 1 win.

Head-to-Head Comparison

In head-to-head matchups this season, the Royals and Orioles have been evenly matched, with Kansas City taking a 1-0 lead in the Wild Card series after their 1-0 victory in Game 1. Kansas City’s pitching staff has consistently outperformed Baltimore’s hitters, and with Seth Lugo on the mound, the Royals are in a strong position to close out the series.

Against the Spread (ATS) Records
Kansas City Royals

The Royals are 85-77 ATS this season, performing well as underdogs. They’ve been effective in low-scoring games, which has helped them cover spreads in tight contests, especially on the road.

Baltimore Orioles

The Orioles are 87-75 ATS but have struggled when favored by significant margins. They’ll need to step up offensively to cover the spread as home favorites in Game 2, but Kansas City’s pitching could make that difficult.

Prediction

Kansas City’s momentum from Game 1, combined with Seth Lugo’s dominant regular-season performance, gives the Royals a strong edge in Game 2. Baltimore’s offense will need a significant turnaround to avoid elimination, but Lugo’s ability to control the strike zone makes that a difficult task. Look for Kansas City to close out the series and advance to the next round of the postseason.
