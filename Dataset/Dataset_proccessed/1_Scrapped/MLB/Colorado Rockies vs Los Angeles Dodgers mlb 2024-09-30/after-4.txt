Dodgers’ Shohei Ohtani claims 4th NL Player of the Week award this season
Ohtani finished the season on a 12-game hitting streak, including 13 hits in his last 25 at-bats. He ended up with a franchise-record 54 home runs and 59 stolen bases.
Dodgers star Shohei Ohtani hits a single during the eighth inning of their game against the Colorado Rockies on Sunday at Coors Field in Denver. Ohtani finished the regular season by collecting another National League Player of the Week award – his fourth of the season and second in a row. (Photo by Matthew Stockman/Getty Images)
By BILL PLUNKETT | bplunkett@scng.com | Orange County Register
UPDATED: September 30, 2024 at 2:27 PM PST

Shohei Ohtani finished the regular season by collecting another National League Player of the Week award – his fourth of the season and second in a row.

Ohtani finished his first season with the Dodgers by going 13 for his last 25 with four doubles, one home run, seven RBIs, four stolen bases and a 1.371 OPS over the final six games.
