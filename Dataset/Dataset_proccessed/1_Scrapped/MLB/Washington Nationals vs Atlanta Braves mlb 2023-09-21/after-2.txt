BRAVES STATS & TRENDS
BRAVES BATTING
The Atlanta Braves have hit a league-leading 291 home runs, averaging 1.9 per game.
Atlanta leads the majors with a .275 batting average.
The Braves have a league-best .343 on-base percentage.
Atlanta’s hitters strike out 7.9 times per game, the fifth-lowest average in the majors.
The Braves average 1.9 homers per road game this season (148 total on the road).
Atlanta is averaging 3.8 extra-base hits per game while slugging .491 on the road.
Away from home this season the team averages 9.5 hits per game and is batting .270.
BRAVES KEY BATTERS
Acuna has an OPS of 1.009, fueled by an OBP of .416 to go with a slugging percentage of .592. He has a .337 batting average, as well.
Among qualifying hitters in MLB, he ranks third in batting average, second in on-base percentage, and fifth in slugging.
Acuna hopes to build on a six-game hitting streak in this matchup. In his last 10 outings he is batting .381 with a double, four home runs, six walks and 10 RBI.
Matt Olson leads Atlanta in total hits (160) this season while batting .279 with 81 extra-base hits. He’s slugging .606 with an on-base percentage of .386.
Among qualifying hitters, his batting average places him 24th, his on-base percentage 10th, and his slugging percentage third.
Olson takes a three-game streak with at least one hit into this contest. During his last five outings he is batting .278 with a home run, four walks and three RBI.
Ozzie Albies has collected 149 base hits, an OBP of .325 and a slugging percentage of .500 this season.
Albies takes a seven-game hitting streak into this matchup. In his last 10 games he is hitting .318 with five doubles, two home runs, three walks and seven RBI.
Austin Riley has been key for Atlanta with 167 hits, an OBP of .341 plus a slugging percentage of .512.
Riley enters this game on a two-game hitting streak. In his last five games he is batting .250 with a home run, three walks and two RBI.
NATIONALS STATS & TRENDS
NATIONALS BATTING
The Nationals have hit just 143 homers this season, which ranks 29th in the league.
Washington ranks 12th in MLB with a .253 team batting average.
The Nationals have an OBP of .313 this season, which ranks 22nd in MLB.
Washington has shown good bat-to-ball skills at the plate this season with the second-best strikeout rate (7.1 per game) among MLB offenses.
They have averaged 0.9 home runs per game in their 77 home contests this season.
Washington has a team slugging percentage of .404 in home games this season with 213 extra-base hits.
They are averaging 8.8 base hits per game and batting .264 as a team at home this season.
NATIONALS KEY BATTERS
Lane Thomas has collected 159 hits, a team-high for the Nationals. He’s batting .271 and slugging .473 with an on-base percentage of .321.
Including all qualifying hitters in the majors, he ranks 46th in batting average, 92nd in on-base percentage and 39th in slugging percentage.
C.J. Abrams has 25 doubles, six triples, 18 home runs and 31 walks while batting .247. He’s slugging .421 with an on-base percentage of .305.
Including all qualified players, his batting average puts him 102nd, his on-base percentage is 119th, and he is 90th in slugging.
Joey Meneses has put up a team-best OBP (.328) and slugging percentage (.413).
Keibert Ruiz is batting .253 with 21 doubles, 17 home runs and 30 walks.
