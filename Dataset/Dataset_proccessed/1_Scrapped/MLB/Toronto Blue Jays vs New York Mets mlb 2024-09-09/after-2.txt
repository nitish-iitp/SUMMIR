BOTTOM LINE: The Toronto Blue Jays host the New York Mets on Monday to begin a three-game series.

Toronto has a 68-76 record overall and a 34-35 record at home. The Blue Jays have a 22-9 record in games when they did not allow a home run.

New York is 38-31 on the road and 78-65 overall. The Mets have gone 53-18 in games when they scored five or more runs.

The teams meet Monday for the first time this season.

TOP PERFORMERS: Vladimir Guerrero Jr. leads Toronto with 28 home runs while slugging .554. Spencer Horwitz is 13-for-36 with four home runs and eight RBI over the last 10 games.
