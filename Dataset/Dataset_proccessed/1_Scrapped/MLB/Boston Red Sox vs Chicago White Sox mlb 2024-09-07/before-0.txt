BOTTOM LINE: The Kansas City Royals host the Minnesota Twins to open a three-game series.

Kansas City has a 76-65 record overall and a 42-30 record at home. The Royals have a 55-29 record in games when they record at least eight hits.

Minnesota has a 37-34 record on the road and a 76-64 record overall. The Twins have a 32-15 record in games when they hit two or more home runs.

The matchup Friday is the 11th time these teams match up this season. The Twins hold a 7-3 advantage in the season series.

TOP PERFORMERS: Bobby Witt Jr. has 40 doubles, 11 triples and 30 home runs for the Royals. Maikel Garcia is 9-for-29 with three doubles and four RBI over the last 10 games.
