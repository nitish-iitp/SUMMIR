IND vs BAN 2024: India’s best playing XI for the 2nd Test against Bangladesh
Published - Sep 25, 2024, 03:00 UTC | Updated - Sep 25, 2024, 03:00 UTC
by Akash Thapa 
India sealed a dominating victory over Bangladesh in the first Test by 280 runs.
The final Test of the 2-match series is set to take place in the iconic Green Park stadium at Kanpur.
India’s best playing XI for the 2nd Test against Bangladesh (Image source: X)

Table of Contents

India’s best-playing XI for the second Test against Bangladesh
Key Players to Watch

The second Test match between India and Bangladesh is set to commence on September 27, 2024, at the historic Green Park Stadium in Kanpur. Following India’s commanding victory by 280 runs in the first Test, the home team is eager to maintain their momentum and secure a series win.

India’s performance in the first Test showcased their dominance, with notable contributions from both batters and bowlers. The team retained its squad for the second Test, which includes key players such as Rohit Sharma (captain), Virat Kohli, and Rishabh Pant. The Indian bowling attack will likely be bolstered by spinners, given the expected pitch conditions in Kanpur.

India’s best-playing XI for the second Test against Bangladesh
Rohit Sharma (C)
Role: Opening Batter and Captain
Expectation: As the captain, Rohit is expected to lead from the front by providing a strong start to the innings. His ability to set the tone early is crucial, especially in a Test match where building partnerships is key.
Strengths: Rohit is known for his aggressive batting style combined with the ability to play long innings. His experience in handling pressure situations makes him a key player. He has a wide range of shots and can dominate bowlers, especially in home conditions.
Yashasvi Jaiswal
Role: Opening Batter
Expectation: Jaiswal is anticipated to complement Rohit at the top of the order by establishing a solid foundation. His role will involve scoring quickly while also being cautious against the new ball.
Strengths: Jaiswal has shown exceptional talent with his aggressive stroke play and ability to handle spin effectively. His youthful exuberance can be an asset, especially in capitalizing on scoring opportunities early in the innings.
Shubman Gill
Role: Top-order Batter
Expectation: Gill is expected to anchor the innings, building partnerships with both Rohit and Kohli. His ability to convert starts into significant scores will be crucial for India.
Strengths: With excellent technique and footwork, Gill is adept at playing both pace and spin bowling. His calm demeanour under pressure allows him to navigate challenging situations effectively.
Virat Kohli
Role: Middle-order Batter
Expectation: Kohli’s role will be vital in stabilizing the innings, particularly if early wickets fall. His experience in high-pressure situations is invaluable for guiding the team through tough phases.
Strengths: Known for his consistency and aggressive mindset, Kohli has an exceptional record in Test cricket. His ability to chase down targets and build innings makes him a formidable opponent for any bowling attack.
KL Rahul
Role: Middle-order Batter
Expectation: Rahul is expected to provide stability in the middle order while rotating the strike effectively. His adaptability allows him to play both defensive and aggressive roles as per match requirements.
Strengths: Rahul’s versatility enables him to adjust his game according to match situations. He has a solid technique and can counter both pace and spin, making him a reliable option in varying conditions.
Rishabh Pant (WK)
Role: Wicketkeeper-Batter
Expectation: Pant is anticipated to play a crucial role in scoring quick runs down the order while also being sharp behind the stumps. His aggressive batting can change the game’s momentum.
Strengths: Known for his explosive batting style, Pant can accelerate scoring during critical phases of an innings. His agility as a wicketkeeper adds value, particularly in dismissing batters who attempt risky shots against spinners.
Ravindra Jadeja
Role: All-rounder
Expectation: Jadeja is expected to contribute significantly with both bat and ball, providing balance to the team’s composition and depth in batting.
Strengths: As a left-arm spinner, Jadeja can be particularly effective on turning tracks, while his lower-order batting capability makes him a valuable asset who can stabilize or accelerate as needed.
Ravichandran Ashwin
Role: All-rounder
Expectation: Ashwin is expected to lead India’s spin attack and take crucial wickets while also contributing runs when needed down the order.
Strengths: Known for his variations and tactical acumen, Ashwin is one of the best spinners in Test cricket today. He can exploit weaknesses in opposition batting lineups with his clever bowling strategies.
Kuldeep Yadav
Role: Wrist spinner
Expectation: Kuldeep is anticipated to provide support in the spin department, especially on a pitch that may assist spinners as it wears down over time.
Strengths: As a wrist-spinner, Kuldeep offers a different angle of attack compared to traditional finger spinners. His ability to turn the ball sharply makes him unpredictable for batters.
Jasprit Bumrah
Role: Fast Bowler
Expectation: Bumrah is expected to spearhead India’s bowling attack with his unique bowling style and ability to bowl yorkers at will, targeting early breakthroughs.
Strengths: With remarkable pace, accuracy, and variations (including slower balls), Bumrah is lethal in any format of cricket. His capability to break partnerships with crucial wickets makes him an invaluable asset.
Mohammed Siraj
Role: Fast Bowler
Expectation: Siraj’s role will be to support Bumrah by providing early breakthroughs with his seam movement and bounce.
Strengths: Known for his aggression and ability to generate bounce off the pitch, Siraj can trouble batters with his pace and swing, making him an effective weapon against any batting lineup.

This lineup combines experience with youthful talent while ensuring that India has a balanced team capable of tackling Bangladesh’s challenges effectively. Each player’s unique strengths contribute significantly towards India’s chances of success in this Test series.

Key Players to Watch

For India, Ravichandran Ashwin and Ravindra Jadeja are expected to play pivotal roles as spinners on a pitch that traditionally favours their style of bowling. Meanwhile, Rishabh Pant will be crucial in stabilizing the batting order and capitalizing on scoring opportunities.

For Bangladesh, Mushfiqur Rahim has been a consistent performer in India and will need to lead from the front with the bat. The performance of their bowlers, particularly if they include more spin options, will be critical in challenging India’s batting lineup. As both teams prepare for this exciting encounter, the unique conditions at Kanpur promise a thrilling match filled with strategic battles and potential turning points.
