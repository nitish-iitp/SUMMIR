10 Lines Today News Headlines in English for School Assembly

Here are the latest updates and significant stories affecting our country today. Stay informed about the key events and developments from across India:

1. On August 16, ISRO’s SSLV-D3 mission aims to test the Small Satellite Launch Vehicle (SSLV) and place a new Earth observation satellite into orbit. This mission marks a crucial step in ISRO’s strategy to enhance its capabilities in launching small satellites, potentially benefiting Indian industries.

2. A leaked image of the iPhone 16 has shown a redesigned back panel featuring a vertically aligned dual camera system. Anticipation builds as the new iPhone is rumoured to include advanced features like spatial video recording, previously available only on Pro models, sparking excitement among Apple enthusiasts.

National News Headlines in English for 18 August 2024

Turning to state news, let’s review the major events and updates from different regions. These stories will keep you updated on what’s happening closer to home:

1. India Hosts 3rd Voice of Global South Summit Virtually to Address Global ChallengesIndia is hosting the 3rd Voice of Global South Summit today in a virtual format. The summit, themed “An Empowered Global South for a Sustainable Future,” will expand discussions on conflicts, food and energy security crises, and climate change. Prime Minister Narendra Modi will lead the inaugural session, and the third round of the India-Japan 2+2 Foreign-Defence Ministers meeting is scheduled for August 20 in Delhi.

2. Passenger Ferry Service Between India and Sri Lanka Resumes with Government Support
The passenger ferry service between Nagapattinam, India, and Kankesanthurai, Sri Lanka, resumed yesterday. Operated by IndSri Ferry Services, the ferry completed its maiden voyage in about 4 hours. To support affordability, the Indian government will provide 25 million Sri Lankan Rupees per month for a year.

3. Indian Government Announces High-Level Bureaucratic Appointments Across Key Ministries
The Appointments Committee of the Cabinet (ACC) has approved several high-level appointments. Vivek Joshi will now serve as Secretary of the Department of Personnel and Training, while Nagaraju Maddirala moves to the Department of Financial Services. Manoj Govil will head the Department of Expenditure, and Deepti Gaur Mukherjee will take over as Secretary of the Ministry of Corporate Affairs.

4. India Dispatches 1400 Kgs of Anti-Cancer Drugs to Syria as Humanitarian Aid
India has sent a consignment of approximately 1400 kgs of anti-cancer drugs to Syria as part of its humanitarian assistance efforts. This support aligns with India’s ongoing commitment to Syria, reflecting the deep-rooted ties and previous collaborative efforts in education and capacity building.

5. PM Modi and Israeli PM Netanyahu Discuss Urgent Need for De-Escalation in West Asia
Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi and Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu discussed the escalating situation in West Asia. Modi emphasised the need for de-escalation, the immediate release of hostages, and continued humanitarian aid. This conversation followed Netanyahu’s call to congratulate Modi on India’s Independence Day.

6. US Court Rules Tahawwur Rana Extraditable to India for Mumbai Attacks
The US Court of Appeals has ruled that Tahawwur Rana is extraditable to India for his alleged involvement in the 2008 Mumbai terror attacks. The court upheld the extradition under the US-India treaty, despite Rana’s prior convictions, citing that his alleged offences fall within the treaty’s provisions.

7. Sabarmati Express Derails Near Kanpur: No Injuries Reported
The Sabarmati Express, travelling from Varanasi to Ahmedabad, derailed near Govindpuri station in Kanpur early on August 17. Twenty coaches were affected, causing significant disruptions. Fortunately, no injuries or fatalities have been reported. Initial findings suggest a boulder damaged the train’s engine.

8. India Reshuffles Top Bureaucrats: New Appointments in Health, Defence, and HousingThe Indian government has announced a major reshuffle in top bureaucratic positions. Punya Salila Srivastava will become Health Secretary, Rajesh Kumar Singh will take over as Defence Secretary, and Katikithala Srinivas will be the new Housing and Urban Affairs Secretary. These changes are set to take effect later this year.

9. Foxconn Expands EV Production Capabilities with New Facility in Bengaluru
Foxconn is establishing a new manufacturing and assembly facility in Bengaluru for EV components under Project Cheetah. The company has leased land for this project, which aims to produce 5-7 lakh EVs by 2025 and generate over 50,000 jobs. Foxconn is also setting up an iPhone assembly plant in Karnataka as part of its expansion in India.

1. Karnataka Governor Grants Prosecution Sanction for CM Siddaramaiah Over MUDA Scam
Karnataka Governor Thaawarchand Gehlot has authorised the prosecution of Chief Minister Siddaramaiah in connection with the Mysore Urban Development Authority (MUDA) site allotment scandal. This decision follows allegations of illegal land acquisition worth ₹55.8 crore for Siddaramaiah’s family and a petition against him. The BJP has praised the Governor’s action, accusing Siddaramaiah of major corruption.

2. Jammu and Kashmir Assembly Elections Scheduled in Three Phases
The Jammu and Kashmir assembly elections will be conducted in three phases starting September 18, with results announced on October 4. The elections will cover all 90 constituencies, including 74 general, seven reserved for SC candidates, and nine for ST candidates. Voting will occur in different districts in each phase, with widespread political support for the election schedule.

3. Karnataka CM Siddaramaiah Discusses State Stance Before 16th Finance Commission
Karnataka Chief Minister Siddaramaiah met with senior finance officials and GST Council members to prepare the state’s position for the 16th Finance Commission’s visit on August 29. They proposed increasing vertical devolution from 41% to 50% to improve financial allocation.

4. Manipur Security Forces Seize Hidden Arms and Ammunition in Kangpokpi District
A joint operation by Assam Rifles and Manipur Police in Kangpokpi district led to the recovery of concealed arms and ammunition, including improvised mortars and rifles. The weapons, found in the Loiching Ridge area, have been handed over to Manipur Police for further investigation.

5. Union Cabinet Approves Major Metro Rail Projects to Enhance Urban Connectivity
The Union Cabinet has approved three significant metro rail projects: Bangalore Metro Phase 3, Thane Integral Ring, and Pune Metro extension. Bangalore Metro Phase 3 will add 44.65 km to the network by 2029, enhancing connectivity in the city. Thane and Pune projects will also improve transportation infrastructure.

6. Assam CM Criticises Jharkhand Government for “Appeasement” Politics After Tribal Death
Assam Chief Minister Himanta Biswa Sarma accused the Jharkhand JMM-led government of “appeasement” politics after a tribal victim’s family received no support following the victim’s murder by a prisoner at a Hazaribag hospital. Sarma met with the victim’s family and criticised the administration’s inaction.
International News in English for 18 July 2024

Turning to international news, let’s review the major regional events and updates. These stories will keep you updated on what’s happening in the world:

1. Polio Threat Escalates in Gaza Amid Ongoing Conflict
In Gaza, the resurgence of polio is alarming aid groups who are calling for a temporary halt in fighting to ramp up vaccinations. With confirmed cases and viruses detected in wastewater, urgent action is needed to prevent a major outbreak.

2. Indian Astronaut Candidates Begin NASA Training for Space Mission
Indian astronaut candidates Shubhanshu Shukla and Prashant Balakrishnan Nair are undergoing extensive training at NASA’s Johnson Space Center in Houston. They are preparing for a space mission to the International Space Station, facilitated by Axiom Space Inc.

3. UN Reports Severe Casualties in Bangladesh Unrest
The UN Human Rights Office reports nearly 650 deaths in recent unrest in Bangladesh, with allegations of extrajudicial killings and arbitrary detentions. The violence, triggered by anti-discrimination protests, demands a thorough investigation.

4. Russia Accuses Ukraine of Using Western Rockets in Kursk Attack
Russia claims Ukraine used Western-made HIMARS rockets to destroy a bridge in the Kursk region, resulting in the death of volunteers. This incident comes as Ukrainian forces make advances in the region, with Kyiv claiming control of several settlements.

5. Rising LNG Exports to India and Southeast Asia Driven by Increased Demand
LNG exports to India and Southeast Asia surged by 9% in 2023, driven by high temperatures and decarbonization efforts. New importers like Vietnam and the Philippines are contributing to this growth, with shipments reaching record levels.

6. Bangladesh Interim Government Promises Inclusive Democracy Amid Violence
Bangladesh’s interim Chief Adviser Mohammad Yunus vows to foster a pluralistic democracy and ensure free elections amid ongoing violence and attacks on minorities. The unrest following Sheikh Hasina’s departure has resulted in significant casualties and property damage.
Business News Today Headlines

Turning to state news, let’s review the major events and updates from different regions. These stories will keep you updated on what’s happening closer to home:

1. Investors Brace for Extended Market Decline Amid Economic Uncertainty
Concerns over a potential broader stock sell-off persist as investors react to recent market turbulence linked to US recession fears and currency speculation by the Bank of Japan. Despite a modest rebound in global stocks, asset managers are likely to continue selling off equities due to ongoing weakness in US job markets and global consumer trends.

2. Lenovo Reports Strong Profit Growth Driven by AI Demand
Lenovo Group Ltd. exceeded profit expectations with a 38% rise in net income to $243 million, buoyed by increased global spending on AI and computing infrastructure. The 20% revenue growth reflects a recovery in the computing hardware market, driven by heightened investments in data centres by major tech companies preparing for an AI boom.

3. Temasek Holdings’ Tech Investments Hit Amid Sector Decline
Singapore’s state investor Temasek Holdings increased its stakes in major US tech firms by $3.3 billion in Q2, including significant investments in Microsoft, Apple, Nvidia, Alphabet, Meta, and Amazon. However, by July, stock prices for these companies fell, with Alphabet and Amazon down 12% and Microsoft down 7%, amid AI-related concerns and recession fears.

4. SEBI Advocates for Transparency in AI Usage by Investment Advisers
The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) calls for greater transparency and security regarding the use of AI tools by investment advisers and research analysts. A new consultation paper mandates clear disclosure of AI usage to ensure clients understand how AI impacts their investment decisions, balancing efficiency with human judgment.
Science and Technology News for 18 August 2024

Explore the latest science and technology news today, including advancements in AI, shifts in the smartphone market, and key industry updates. Stay current with these essential developments.

1. ISRO to Launch SSLV-D3/EOS-08 Mission: Testing New Satellite Rocket
The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is preparing for the third developmental flight of its Small Satellite Launch Vehicle (SSLV), scheduled for August 16 at 9:17 am from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre. This mission, named SSLV-D3/EOS-08, aims to test the SSLV’s capabilities by placing an Earth observation satellite into orbit. Successful completion of this flight will finalise the SSLV’s development, making it available for future missions by Indian industries with support from NewSpace India Limited.

2. Leaked Image Reveals iPhone 16 Design and Features Ahead of Launch
An unofficial image of the upcoming iPhone 16 has emerged on Reddit, showcasing the new design ahead of its anticipated launch. The image highlights a vertically aligned dual rear camera system and suggests potential new features, such as spatial video recording, which was previously available only on the Pro models of the iPhone 15. This leak hints at Apple integrating advanced features into its standard iPhone 16 models.

3. Lamborghini Unveils ‘Temerario’ Supercar: A High-Performance Electrified Vehicle
Lamborghini has introduced the ‘Temerario,’ its new supercar and successor to the Huracan. The Temerario features a groundbreaking powertrain, including a 4.0-litre twin-turbocharged V8 engine and a plug-in hybrid system with three electric motors. The supercar delivers 920 BHP and accelerates from 0-62 mph in just 2.7 seconds, with a top speed of 211 mph. It includes a 3.8 kWh lithium-ion battery pack with fast-charging capabilities, although the EV-only range is not specified.

4. Google Announces Pixel Studio: New App for Creating and Editing Images
At its Pixel 9 event, Google unveiled Pixel Studio, an image generation application that will come preinstalled on Pixel 9 devices. This app allows users to create and edit images from text prompts using on-device AI and Google’s Imagen 3 model. Pixel Studio features tools for modifying images by adding or changing elements and styles. It requires an internet connection for cloud-based processing and aims to enhance the creative capabilities of Pixel 9 users.
Sports News in English for 18 August 2024

Turning to state news, let’s review the major events and updates from different regions. These stories will keep you updated on what’s happening closer to home:

1. Carlos Alcaraz Suffers Disappointing Defeat at Cincinnati Open: ‘Worst Match’ of His Career
Carlos Alcaraz expressed his frustration with a forceful racquet smash after suffering a 4-6, 7-6 (7/5), 6-4 loss to Gael Monfils at the ATP and WTA Cincinnati Open. Alcaraz, the reigning Roland Garros and Wimbledon champion, described the match as the worst of his career, citing a lack of control and inability to perform at his best. Despite his efforts, the seasoned French player, who boasts two decades of experience, proved too formidable. Alcaraz heads to the US Open without a hard court win this summer and just one second-round summer match under his belt.

2. Heroic Welcome for Vinesh Phogat After the Paris 2024 Olympic Setback
Indian wrestling star Vinesh Phogat received a rousing welcome upon her return to New Delhi on August 17, following her disappointment at the Paris 2024 Olympics. Fans and fellow athletes, including Congress MP Deepender Hooda, wrestlers Sakshi Malik and Bajrang Punia, and boxer Vijender Singh, gathered at IGI airport to celebrate her. Emotional scenes unfolded as Vinesh, moved by the warm reception, broke down in tears while being carried on the shoulders of her supporters. The video of the reception has gone viral on social media.

3. MS Dhoni Could Return as Uncapped Player for Chennai Super Kings
Former Chennai Super Kings captain MS Dhoni may make a surprising return to the IPL as an uncapped player, according to recent reports. The BCCI is considering reactivating a rule that categorises international cricketers who have been retired for over five years as uncapped players. Dhoni, who retired from international cricket in 2020 and last played during the 2019 ODI World Cup, is now eligible under this rule. This move would allow CSK to retain Dhoni at a lower price while retaining other key players. During the 2022 mega auction, Dhoni was retained for INR 12 crore.

4. Alcaraz’s Frustration Boils Over in Cincinnati: A Racquet Smash and a Hard Court Struggle
Carlos Alcaraz’s match at the ATP and WTA Cincinnati Open ended with him smashing his racquet in frustration following a 4-6, 7-6 (7/5), 6-4 defeat to Gael Monfils. Alcaraz, who described the match as his worst ever, struggled to match the veteran French player’s skill and composure. The Spaniard, known for his typically strong performances, admitted he couldn’t control his emotions or his game, marking this as the first time he had ever broken a racquet in anger. As he prepares for the US Open, Alcaraz heads into the tournament with minimal hardcourt match experience this summer.

5. Joshua Zirkzee Secures Manchester United’s Opening Victory with Late Goal
In their Premier League season opener, Manchester United secured a tense 1-0 victory over Fulham thanks to a late goal from new signing Joshua Zirkzee. The match, held at Old Trafford, saw Manchester United struggle to break down Fulham’s defence in the first half, with captain Bruno Fernandes coming closest to scoring. It was Zirkzee, who came on as a substitute, who finally delivered the decisive goal three minutes before the final whistle. Expressing his delight, Zirkzee described scoring at the Stretford End as one of the best feelings, reflecting on the supportive atmosphere of the club and the high standards it upholds.

2. Formation of the United Nations (1945)
On August 18, 1945, the United Nations Charter was ratified, formally establishing the UN as an international organisation dedicated to maintaining peace and fostering global cooperation.

3. Death of American Revolutionary War Hero General Richard Montgomery (1775)
On August 18, 1775, General Richard Montgomery, a key figure in the American Revolutionary War, died during the Siege of Quebec, contributing significantly to the early efforts of the Continental Army.

4. Birth of the Nobel Prize-Winning Author Hermann Hesse (1877)
On August 18, 1877, Hermann Hesse, the German-Swiss author known for his philosophical novels like “Siddhartha” and “Steppenwolf,” was born, influencing modern literature and existential thought.

5. The Launch of the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter (2005)
