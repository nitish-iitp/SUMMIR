By Tarkesh Jha  
November 4, 2023, 9:20:03 PM IST (Published)
2 Min Read
India will clash with South Africa at the Eden Gardens in Kolkata on November 5, 2023, in the ICC Men's ODI World Cup 2023. Weather will be in focus in the West Bengal capital on Sunday, especially after the Pakistan vs Australia match in Bengaluru was affected by rain. Following is the weather update as the Proteas take on the Rohit Sharma-led side, which has been unbeaten in the tournament so far.



Kolkata Weather Update - November 5, 2023 

The weather will be hazy in Kolkata with the temperature ranging around 33°C when the game begins. Wind will blow towards the west and northwestern direction at 11 km/hr, with the gusts at 20 km/h. Humidity will be fairly high at 51% and the dew point will be at 20°C. There will be significant cloud cover, i.e. 99%, but the probability of precipitation will be negligible at 4%.

The temperature will cool down to 25°C when the game progresses to the second innings. Humidity will rise sharply to 69%, but the wind will be change its direction to southwest at 9 km/hr. The gusts will be at 15 km/hr, and the cloud cover will reduce just marginally to 46%. The dew point will be at 20°C. The presence of clouds always create a threat of rainfall, but the forecast appears to pave the way for uninterrupted action.

Predicted Playing XI

India: Rohit Sharma (c), Shubman Gill, Virat Kohli, Shreyas Iyer, Suryakumar Yadav, KL Rahul (wk), Ravindra Jadeja, Mohammed Shami, Kuldeep Yadav, Mohammed Siraj, Jasprit Bumrah

South Africa: Temba Bavuma (c), Quinton de Kock, Rassie van der Dussen, Aiden Markram, Heinrich Klaasen (wk), David Miller, Marco Jansen, Gerald Coetzee, Kagiso Rabada, Keshav Maharaj, Lungi Ngidi

Pitch Report

The average first innings score in 37 ODIs on this ground is 240, which suggests that a fair contest between the bat and the ball is on the cards on Sunday.
