Today’s 10 lines today news headlines in English for school assembly cover diverse topics ranging from national and international news, sports updates, the latest technological advancements, and weather reports. We’ll also share a motivational thought and an interesting word to expand your vocabulary.

1. India Dominates Japan in Men’s Hockey: 5-1 Victory
India’s hockey team put on a stunning performance against Japan, winning 5-1 in their second match at the Men’s Asian Champions Trophy. Goals by Sukhjeet Singh, Abhishek, Sanjay, and Uttam Singh secured a strong win for the defending champions.

2. Jannik Sinner Makes History at the US Open
Jannik Sinner celebrated his second Grand Slam win of the year by clinching the US Open title. The world number one defeated Taylor Fritz in straight sets and became the first Italian man to win the prestigious tournament.

3. Aaron Rodgers’ Tough Return: Jets Lose to 49ers
Aaron Rodgers faced a challenging comeback as the New York Jets were beaten 32-19 by the San Francisco 49ers. Despite a touchdown from Breece Hall, the Jets couldn’t hold off the 49ers’ strong performance.

4. England Crushes Ireland in Record ODI Win
England’s women’s cricket team set a new record with a massive 275-run victory over Ireland. Tammy Beaumont’s unbeaten 150 led the way, and England’s bowlers skittled out Ireland for just 45 runs.

5. Italy Fans Protest During Israel’s National Anthem   
Italian fans protested during Israel’s national anthem at a Nations League match in Hungary, turning their backs and displaying a flag with “Liberta” (Freedom). The gesture highlighted ongoing tensions related to the Israeli-Palestinian conflict.

6. Severe Rainfall Expected Across Several Indian States
Heavy rains are forecasted for Odisha, Madhya Pradesh, and other regions, with warnings for flooding and strong showers. Several states will experience widespread rains, impacting daily life and activities.

7. SEBI Lifts Restrictions on Infosys Employees
The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) has removed the trading restrictions on Infosys employees and related entities. This decision follows the conclusion of an insider trading investigation and allows the individuals to resume trading.

8. EU Economy Shows Resilience Despite ChallengesDespite predictions of economic downturns, the European Union’s economy is showing slow but steady growth. EU Commissioner Paolo Gentiloni highlighted the need for balance in trade relationships and ongoing economic recovery efforts.

National News Headlines in English for 11 September 2024

In national news headlines in English, we will focus on significant events and developments in the country, including government initiatives, new policies, and national celebrations that impact daily life.

1. Defence Secretary Giridhar Aramane to Co-Chair Key Defence Meeting in Manila
Defence Secretary Giridhar Aramane will co-chair the fifth India-Philippines Joint Defence Cooperation Committee meeting in Manila tomorrow. This crucial meeting aims to strengthen defence cooperation as both nations celebrate 75 years of diplomatic ties and 10 years of India’s Act East Policy.

2. Kolkata Doctors Continue Strike Despite Supreme Court’s Deadline
Doctors in Kolkata are defying a Supreme Court order to end their strike, demanding justice for a junior doctor who was raped and murdered. They are calling for the resignation of key health officials and continuing their protest until their demands are met.

3. NIT Manipur Fails to Secure Position in NIRF Engineering Rankings
NIT Manipur has dropped out of the National Institutional Ranking Framework (NIRF) for engineering this year. The institute, which was previously ranked among the top 100, saw a significant decline in its performance, impacting its visibility and standing in the engineering education sector.

4. Sonam Wangchuk Urges PM to Include Ladakh in Sixth Schedule for Local Autonomy
Climate activist Sonam Wangchuk has requested Prime Minister Narendra Modi to include Ladakh in the Sixth Schedule of the Constitution. This move aims to grant local residents law-making powers to protect their land and cultural identity. Wangchuk and volunteers are advocating for renewed dialogue on their demands.

5. Kerala High Court Criticizes Government for Ignoring Key Film Industry Report
The Kerala High Court has condemned the state government’s failure to act on the Hema Committee report regarding the Malayalam film industry. The court has directed the government to provide an unredacted report to the Special Investigation Team and to address unresolved issues highlighted by the report.

6. Philippines Diplomat Ma. Theresa P. Lazaro Promotes E-Visa and Air Services with India
During her visit to India, Philippines Foreign Affairs Undersecretary Ma. Theresa P. Lazaro announced efforts to introduce full e-visa services for Indian tourists and an air services agreement. She highlighted growing bilateral relations and thanked India for supporting the 2016 arbitral award on Philippines’ sovereignty amidst Chinese aggression.

7. Indian Military Leaders Fly Tejas in Historic Joint Exercise
In a landmark event, the vice chiefs of the Indian Air Force, Army, and Navy piloted the indigenous Tejas aircraft during the Tarang Shakti air exercise in Jodhpur. This joint exercise showcased India’s commitment to self-reliance and cross-domain military collaboration.

8. Supreme Court Overturns Penalty Against SEBI and Stock Exchanges
The Supreme Court has overturned a Bombay High Court decision imposing an Rs 80 lakh penalty on SEBI, BSE, and NSE for freezing demat accounts of Mumbai’s Mehta family. The court found procedural errors and has ordered a re-hearing of the case.

9. US and India Collaborate on Semiconductor Growth under CHIPS Act
The US will team up with India’s Semiconductor Mission to enhance the global semiconductor ecosystem through the International Technology Security and Innovation (ITSI) Fund. US Deputy Secretary of State Kurt Campbell highlighted this partnership as crucial for creating a secure and sustainable semiconductor value chain, reflecting strong US-India strategic ties.

10. Amit Shah to Launch Key Cybercrime Initiatives on I4C Foundation Day
Union Home Minister Amit Shah will mark the first Foundation Day of the Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (I4C) in New Delhi. He will unveil several initiatives, including the Cyber Fraud Mitigation Centre, a cybercrime data platform, the ‘Cyber Commandos’ program, and a new Suspect Registry to improve cybersecurity and fraud management.
State News Headlines in English for 11 September 2024

The state news headlines today bring you regional updates, including state government decisions, local developments, and events that influence the lives of people in the state.

1. AAP Announces Second List of Candidates Amid Haryana Seat-Sharing Dispute
As seat-sharing talks with Congress stall, Aam Aadmi Party (AAP) released its second list of nine candidates for the Haryana assembly polls. Key candidates include Anurag Dhandha for Kalayat and Pawan Fauji for Uchana Kala, with elections scheduled for October 5.

2. Madhya Pradesh Forms Delimitation Commission for Redrawing Administrative Boundaries
Madhya Pradesh has established a three-member delimitation commission to redraw the boundaries of districts and divisions. Retired Additional Chief Secretary Manoj Shrivastava will lead the commission, aimed at addressing public convenience and reducing administrative issues.

3. AIIMS Delhi Opens Tobacco Cessation Clinic to Combat Addiction
AIIMS Delhi has launched a Tobacco Cessation Clinic in partnership with the National Drug Dependence Treatment Centre. This new initiative aims to support individuals battling tobacco addiction and will operate every Tuesday in the New Rajkumari Amrit Kaur OPD building.

4. Bihar Hosts National Workshop on Social Justice for Panchayati Raj Institutions
A three-day national workshop on social justice and social security for Panchayati Raj institutions has begun in Patna. The event, inaugurated by Union Minister Rajiv Ranjan Singh, brings together representatives from across the country to discuss key issues in gram panchayats.

5. Maharashtra to Establish Tribal University with 80% Reservation in Nashik
Maharashtra Governor C.P. Radhakrishnan announced the creation of a Tribal University in Nashik, featuring 80% reservation for tribal students. The university will offer advanced infrastructure for IT, engineering, and healthcare, promoting social development and improved facilities in the region.

1. Nepal’s Minister for Forest and Environment Leads Delegation to Rio de Janeiro for International Conference
Ain Bahadur Shahi Thakuri, Nepal’s Minister for Forest and Environment, has embarked on a visit to Brazil to represent Nepal at an international conference in Rio de Janeiro on September 12 and 13. The conference, organized by the Brazilian government and the UN Environment Programme (UNEP), focuses on creating equitable and sustainable solutions through sustainable consumption and production, with an emphasis on the circular economy. Minister Shahi will address the conference on reducing inequalities and achieving just transitions, highlighting the event’s role in fostering global sustainability.

2. Australia Plans Social Media Ban for Children Amid Health Concerns
Australia has proposed a ban on social media use for children, citing health risks. Prime Minister Anthony Albanese announced a forthcoming age-verification trial, with potential legislation setting the minimum age for social media at 14 to 16 years. This initiative, positioning Australia as a pioneer in this area, faces challenges related to age verification and enforcement. Experts like Toby Murray from the University of Melbourne criticize current methods as unreliable, while Daniel Angus from the Queensland University of Technology argues that such a ban might exclude young people from valuable digital experiences.

3. Kim Jong-un Declares Increase in North Korea’s Nuclear Arsenal
North Korea’s leader Kim Jong-un has announced a significant expansion of the country’s nuclear arsenal during celebrations marking the 76th anniversary of North Korea’s founding. Kim described North Korea as a “responsible” nuclear state and stated that the expansion is a response to perceived threats from the United States and its allies. He emphasized the need to enhance the country’s nuclear capabilities and readiness for combat, while asserting that North Korea’s nuclear weapons are for self-defense and do not pose a threat to others.

4. China’s Exports Surge While Imports Fall Short of Expectations
China’s exports experienced their fastest growth in nearly 18 months in August, with an 8.7% increase year-on-year, surpassing forecasts. This surge indicates that manufacturers are accelerating exports in anticipation of upcoming tariffs. However, imports grew only 0.5%, missing expectations and reflecting weak domestic demand. This mixed trade data underscores the challenge for Beijing to stimulate growth without over-relying on exports, amidst a struggling property sector and declining factory gate prices.

5. Ukraine Launches Major Drone Attack on Moscow, Causing Significant Damage
Ukraine has executed one of its largest drone attacks to date, targeting Moscow and western Russia on September 10. The attack resulted in the death of at least one woman, extensive damage to homes, and the closure of major airports in Moscow. Russia reported intercepting numerous drones, with significant damage in the Ramenskoye district, where high-rise apartments were set ablaze. The attack disrupted air traffic and led to evacuations, highlighting the escalating conflict and its impact on civilian infrastructure.
Sports News in English for 11 September 2024

In sports news headlines, we cover the latest achievements of athletes, major tournament updates, and upcoming events in various sports categories, inspiring students to stay active and motivated.

1. India Dominates Japan 5-1 in Men’s Asian Champions Trophy, Continues Winning Streak
Defending champions India showcased their prowess with a resounding 5-1 victory over Japan in their second match of the Men’s Asian Champions Trophy in Hulunbuir, China. Sukhjeet Singh scored twice, including a decisive goal in the 60th minute, while Abhishek, Sanjay, and Uttam Singh added to the tally. Japan managed to score through Matsumoto Kazumasa. This win follows India’s 3-0 triumph over hosts China. Next, the Harmanpreet Singh-led team will take on last year’s runners-up, Malaysia.

2. Jannik Sinner Claims US Open Title, Becomes First Italian Man to Win the Major
Jannik Sinner secured his second Grand Slam title of the year by defeating Taylor Fritz 6-3, 6-4, 7-5 in the US Open final. Sinner, the world number one, made history as the first Italian man to win the US Open, adding this victory to his earlier triumph at the Australian Open. The win, marking Sinner’s 55th match victory and sixth title of the season, solidifies his top ranking. Fritz, the home favorite, fell short despite a strong tournament performance.

3. Aaron Rodgers’ Return to NFL Ends in Defeat as New York Jets Fall to San Francisco 49ers
Aaron Rodgers made a disappointing return to NFL action, with the New York Jets losing 32-19 to the San Francisco 49ers. This game marked almost a year since Rodgers’ Achilles tendon injury during his debut with the Jets. Rodgers threw for 167 yards with one touchdown and one interception. Despite an early lead from Breece Hall, the Jets were outplayed by the 49ers, who asserted their dominance after a close first quarter.

4. England Sets Record with 275-Run Win Over Ireland in Women’s ODI
England achieved a record-breaking 275-run victory over Ireland in the second women’s ODI in Belfast. Tammy Beaumont scored an unbeaten 150, her 10th century in ODIs, leading England to a total of 320-8. Ireland struggled against England’s bowlers, being dismissed for just 45 runs. England’s captain Kate Cross and Lauren Filer took key wickets, with Cross claiming 3-8 and Filer 3-10. England now leads the series 2-0.

5. Italy Fans Protest During Israel’s Anthem at Nations League Match in HungaryA group of around 50 Italian fans protested during Israel’s national anthem before a Nations League match in Hungary. The fans, dressed in black, turned their backs and displayed banners with the word “Liberta” (Freedom). This act of protest comes amid Israel’s relocation of home games to Hungary due to the ongoing conflict with Hamas. The fans also voiced opposition against Italian anti-fascist activist Ilaria Salis, adding a political dimension to their protest.

1. India Faces Severe Weather Conditions with Heavy Rainfall Expected Across Major States
India is preparing for severe weather as very heavy rains are anticipated in several regions, including Odisha, Gangetic West Bengal, Madhya Pradesh, and Vidarbha. These areas are likely to experience intense downpours, potentially leading to significant disruptions. Business News Today Headlines

In business news today headlines, we highlight the economic trends, stock market movements, and innovations impacting businesses, keeping you informed about the world of commerce.

1. SEBI Lifts Trading Restrictions on Infosys Employees Following Insider Trading Probe
The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) has officially lifted the trading restrictions imposed on employees of Infosys and associated entities. This decision marks the end of a prolonged investigation into insider trading allegations that began with an interim order on May 31, 2021, barring several individuals and entities from trading in Infosys securities. The restrictions were upheld in September 2021 but were later eased by the Securities Appellate Tribunal in May 2022. Following continued investigation, SEBI issued a final order on September 9, 2024, which vacated the previous trading bans and concluded the regulatory scrutiny.

2. EU Economy Shows Resilience Despite Global Challenges, According to Outgoing Commissioner Paolo Gentiloni
In a recent statement, Paolo Gentiloni, the outgoing European Commissioner for Economy, praised the resilience of the EU economy amid significant global challenges. Despite concerns over potential recessions and instability linked to the Ukraine conflict and other factors, the EU has demonstrated slow yet steady economic growth. Gentiloni highlighted the importance of balancing support for Ukraine with maintaining vital trade relationships, particularly with China. The EU’s GDP grew by 0.3% in the second quarter of this year, and a 1% increase is forecasted for 2024, driven by robust private consumption and a strong labor market.

3. Jabil to Invest Rs 2,000 Crore in Electronics Manufacturing Facility in Tamil Nadu, Creating 5,000 Jobs
Jabil, a major electronics manufacturer, has announced plans to invest Rs 2,000 crore in a new manufacturing facility in Trichy, Tamil Nadu. This significant investment is expected to create approximately 5,000 jobs, bolstering the local economy and expanding Jabil’s production capabilities. The memorandum of understanding for this project was signed between Tamil Nadu Chief Minister MK Stalin and Jabil’s vice president (operations), Matt Crowley, during a meeting in Chicago. This development underscores Tamil Nadu’s growing prominence as a key player in the electronics manufacturing sector.

4. Indian Government Approves Funding for Chip Design Projects by Start-ups
The Ministry of Electronics and IT (MeitY) has approved funding for 12 Indian start-ups working on chip design projects, committing over Rs 130 crore to support their innovations. These projects cover a range of applications, from telecommunications to artificial intelligence (AI) hardware accelerators. The total cost of these projects is Rs 342 crore, with the government having released just over Rs 7 crore so far. This initiative is part of the Design Linked Incentive (DLI) scheme, aimed at boosting India’s capabilities in semiconductor design and manufacturing. Out of 59 applications received, 12 have been cleared, with 21 more still under review.

5. Antitrust Case Against Google Begins in the U.S., Could Reshape the Digital Landscape
The highly anticipated antitrust case against Google has commenced in the United States, with significant implications for the tech industry. The case focuses on Google’s dominance in the online search and advertising markets, with allegations of monopolistic practices and anti-competitive behavior. Judge Amit P Mehta, who has previously labeled Google as a “monopolist,” will oversee the proceedings, which may lead to substantial changes in how Google operates and how digital businesses manage their online presence. The outcome of this case, expected to be resolved by August 2025, could have far-reaching effects on Google’s business practices and the broader tech industry.
Science and Technology News for 11 September 2024

Today’s science and technology news covers the latest discoveries, breakthroughs in research, and cutting-edge technology advancements shaping the future of science.

1. Apple Unveils iPhone 16 with Groundbreaking Visual Intelligence Feature
Apple’s latest iPhone 16, announced on September 9, introduces Visual Intelligence—a cutting-edge AI tool embedded in iOS 18. This feature allows users to instantly gather information about their surroundings through photos, processing data directly on the device rather than relying on cloud services.

2. Fake Tim Cook Video Promotes Cryptocurrency Scam on Apple’s Official YouTube Channel
On August 9, an AI-generated video falsely portraying Apple CEO Tim Cook was streamed on the company’s official YouTube channel. The video, which promised cryptocurrency giveaways, misled thousands of viewers before the launch of the iPhone 16. Apple has yet to address the incident officially.

3. Apple Launches New AirPods 4 with Enhanced Features and Pricing Details
During its Glowtime event, Apple introduced two new AirPods 4 models, upgrading from previous generations. The higher-end model features Active Noise Cancellation for the first time at this price point. The AirPods 4 are available for pre-order with a release date set for September 20 in India and 58 other countries.

4. NASA and Boeing Successfully Return Starliner Spacecraft After Key Flight Test
NASA and Boeing concluded a significant flight test on September 6 with the successful return of the uncrewed Starliner spacecraft. The spacecraft landed at White Sands Space Harbor in New Mexico after a three-month mission to the International Space Station, marking a crucial step toward future crewed missions.

5. SpaceX Prepares for Historic Private Spacewalk Mission with New Spacesuits
A crew of four private astronauts is preparing for SpaceX’s groundbreaking mission to conduct the first-ever private spacewalk. Scheduled to launch from NASA’s Kennedy Space Center, the mission involves new spacesuits and a redesigned spacecraft, marking SpaceX’s fifth private space mission and its most ambitious to date.

What Happened Today in History

What happened today in history recounts significant historical events that occurred on this date, offering insights into important milestones and notable occurrences from the past.


1. The 9/11 Terrorist Attacks in the U.S. (2001)
The world changed on September 11, 2001, when four coordinated terrorist attacks were carried out by al-Qaeda against the United States. The attacks targeted the Twin Towers of the World Trade Center in New York City, the Pentagon in Washington, D.C., and led to a plane crash in Pennsylvania. This tragic event led to nearly 3,000 deaths and reshaped global security policies.

2. Chilean Military Coup Overthrows President Allende (1973)
On September 11, 1973, Chilean President Salvador Allende was overthrown in a military coup led by General Augusto Pinochet. This marked the end of Allende’s socialist government and ushered in Pinochet’s authoritarian regime, which lasted until 1990. The coup had a lasting impact on Chile’s politics and sparked international attention.

3. The Beginning of the Battle of Stirling Bridge (1297)
On September 11, 1297, Scottish forces led by William Wallace won a significant victory against the English army at the Battle of Stirling Bridge. This battle was a key moment in the First War of Scottish Independence and demonstrated the Scots’ resilience against English rule. It’s often remembered as one of Wallace’s greatest triumphs.

4. Founding of the World Wildlife Fund (1961)
The World Wildlife Fund (WWF) was founded on September 11, 1961, with a mission to conserve nature and reduce the most pressing threats to the environment. Since then, WWF has grown into one of the largest independent conservation organizations in the world, playing a key role in preserving biodiversity.

5. Nikita Khrushchev Elected as the First Secretary of the Communist Party (1953)
On September 11, 1953, Nikita Khrushchev was elected First Secretary of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union. His leadership marked the beginning of significant political and economic reforms in the USSR, particularly his policy of de-Stalinization, which shifted the Soviet Union away from the harsh policies of Joseph Stalin.
