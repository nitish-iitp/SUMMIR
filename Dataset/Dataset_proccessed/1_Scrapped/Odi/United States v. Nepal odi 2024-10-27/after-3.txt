1. Spanish President Visits India for Historic Talks with PM Modi
Spanish President Pedro Sanchez has arrived in India, marking the first visit by a Spanish leader in 18 years. During his trip, he’ll meet Prime Minister Modi to strengthen India-Spain ties in diplomacy, economy, and culture.

2. IndiGo Airlines Tightens Security After Multiple Alerts
IndiGo Airlines issued alerts for 15 flights, including routes like Delhi-Istanbul and Bengaluru-Delhi, over security concerns. The airline worked closely with authorities to ensure passenger and staff safety, reinforcing security measures.

3. PM Modi Warns of New Online Scam Targeting Indians
Prime Minister Modi recently warned citizens about “digital arrest” fraud, where people are tricked into fake legal troubles online. This scam has cost Indians over ₹120 crore this year, with cybercriminals from Myanmar, Laos, and Cambodia behind the scheme.

4. Tata and Airbus Team Up to Build Military Aircraft in India
India has expanded its defense production by partnering with Airbus to build 15 C-295 aircraft in Vadodara. PM Modi and Spanish President Sanchez will inaugurate the Tata Aircraft Complex, which will be India’s first private military aircraft production facility.

5. Northern Railways Introduces Safety Measures for Festive TravelersTo manage the holiday rush, Northern Railways has introduced crowd control at major Delhi stations like New Delhi and Anand Vihar. These measures, in place until November 7, aim to prevent overcrowding and ensure safe travels during Diwali and Chhath Puja.

6. India to Issue Green Bonds for Eco-Friendly Projects in Cities
Indian cities like Surat and Coimbatore are planning to issue green bonds to fund eco-friendly projects. These bonds will help cities invest in sustainable infrastructure, boosting India’s efforts to combat climate change.

7. Hyderabad Bans Public Gatherings for a Month to Ensure Public Safety
The city of Hyderabad has banned public gatherings and rallies for a month to maintain public order. This decision was taken under a new safety regulation, prioritizing safety during potentially tense times.

8. UK Labour Party Suspends MP Mike Amesbury Over Video Incident
UK’s Labour Party suspended MP Mike Amesbury after a video showed him allegedly striking a man. The party will investigate the incident, underscoring its stance on maintaining discipline among members.

9. China’s Central Bank Launches New System to Support Banks
China’s central bank has set up a facility to help banks manage their funds, aiming to maintain a steady flow of money. This monthly initiative will support three- and six-month adjustments to boost the economy and keep it stable.

10. Survey Shows Fewer Indian Americans Identify as Democrats
A recent survey revealed that only 47% of Indian Americans now identify as Democrats, a drop from previous years. This shift shows a gradual change in political preferences within the community in the U.S.

1. Spanish President Pedro Sanchez Arrives in India for Bilateral Talks with PM Modi
Spanish President Pedro Sanchez has landed in Vadodara, Gujarat, marking the first visit by a Spanish leader to India in 18 years. During his three-day trip, he will participate in delegation-level talks with Prime Minister Narendra Modi, focusing on strengthening India-Spain diplomatic, economic, and cultural relations.

2. IndiGo Airlines Implements Safety Measures After Security Alerts for 15 Flights
IndiGo Airlines faced a major security concern on Sunday when alerts were issued for 15 flights, including routes like Delhi-Istanbul and Bengaluru-Delhi. The airline responded by following strict precautionary steps and coordinating closely with relevant security authorities to ensure the safety of passengers and staff.

3. Rising Cybercrime: Indians Lose ₹120 Crore in “Digital Arrest” Fraud, PM Modi Warns
In a recent address, Prime Minister Modi brought attention to the significant rise in “digital arrest” fraud, where individuals are tricked into fake legal troubles online, leading to over ₹120 crore lost by Indians in the first quarter of 2024. The Ministry of Home Affairs, monitoring this issue, identified cybercriminals based in Myanmar, Laos, and Cambodia as key perpetrators.
 
4. Government Clarifies,  MGNREGS Enrollment Remains Steady Despite Reports of Decline
The Rural Development Ministry refuted recent claims of declining enrollment in the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (MGNREGS). This clarification follows a report from Libtech India indicating an 8% drop in active worker numbers. The Ministry emphasized that the scheme is demand-driven, making it challenging to predict exact enrollment figures. As the financial year continues, officials stress that MGNREGS remains a robust support for rural employment in India.

5. India Expands Defense Manufacturing: Tata and Airbus to Produce C-295 Aircraft in Vadodara
India is set to bolster its defense capabilities with an additional order of 15 C-295 transport aircraft from Airbus, adding to the 56 already procured. On October 28, Prime Minister Modi and Spanish President Sanchez will inaugurate the Tata Aircraft Complex in Vadodara, which will become India’s first private-sector facility for military aircraft manufacturing. The plant will create a complete production and maintenance ecosystem, marking a significant step for India’s aerospace industry.

6. Swiss Investment in India Grows as Companies Seek Alternatives to China
Swiss firms like ABB and Kuehne+Nagel are increasing their investments in India, encouraged by a $100 billion trade deal. As global companies seek alternatives to China, India’s expanding market and stable growth make it an attractive option for European investors.

7. India’s First Crewed Space Mission Delayed to 2026, ISRO Chairman Confirms
The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) announced that its crewed Gaganyaan mission is postponed to 2026. The delay allows for extensive testing of safety systems before launching astronauts into space, prioritizing mission success and astronaut safety.

8. Government Launches Youth Disaster Training Program for Over 2 Lakh Volunteers
The Yuva Aapda Mitra scheme, launched by the Union Home Secretary, will train 2.37 lakh youth volunteers in disaster response. The program, with a 50% female participation goal, aims to prepare communities for emergencies, empowering young volunteers as first responders.

9. Kashmir Hosts First-Ever All-Women Boat Race to Encourage Female Athletes
In a historic event, over 150 women participated in a boat race on Srinagar’s Dal Lake, promoting women’s empowerment in Kashmir. The event aims to highlight and nurture female athletic talent from the region, breaking stereotypes and promoting inclusivity.

10. India’s Economic Growth Expected to Reach 7.2% in 2024-25, Driven by Investment
India’s GDP is projected to grow by 7.2% in the upcoming fiscal year, fueled by investment and consumer demand, Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman announced. Addressing global finance leaders, she highlighted the country’s potential for sustained economic growth amid global challenges.
State News Headlines in English for 29 October 2024

This section of State News Headlines in English will focus on news pertinent to different Indian states. It will encompass local government announcements, educational initiatives, and regional development project. 

1. Uttarakhand CM Pushkar Singh Dhami and Union Minister Gajendra Shekhawat Discuss Tourism Boost
Uttarakhand Chief Minister Pushkar Singh Dhami met with Union Minister Gajendra Singh Shekhawat to discuss plans to promote religious tourism, adventure activities, eco-tourism, and cultural heritage across Uttarakhand. The leaders exchanged Diwali greetings, signaling a collaborative effort to enrich tourism and preserve the state’s natural and cultural sites.

2. Northern Railways Enforces Crowd Control at Key Delhi Stations for Festive Travel Rush
Due to increased holiday travel, Northern Railways has implemented crowd management measures at New Delhi and Anand Vihar stations, effective until November 7. This decision follows a stampede incident at Mumbai’s Bandra station, where 10 people were injured as crowds surged to board trains heading home for Diwali and Chhath Puja. These controls aim to ensure passenger safety during the festive season.

3. Indian Cities Set to Issue Green Bonds to Finance Climate Initiatives
Indian cities, including Surat, Pimpri Chinchwad, and Coimbatore, are planning to issue green bonds to fund climate projects, expanding India’s municipal debt market for sustainability. Following Rajkot’s recent announcement of a $200 million green bond, these initiatives aim to provide eco-friendly infrastructure, aligning with India’s climate action goals.

4. Hyderabad Imposes Ban on Public Gatherings to Maintain Order
The Hyderabad Police Commissioner has enforced a one-month ban on public gatherings, rallies, and protests across the city to ensure public safety and prevent disturbances. Effective from October 27, the order restricts gatherings under the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita 2023, formerly Section 144, prioritizing order during potentially tense times in the city.

5. Amit Shah Urges BJP Membership Drive and Calls for Change in West Bengal Government
Union Minister Amit Shah, at a party event in Kolkata, called on BJP leaders to reach a goal of 1 crore members in West Bengal, aiming for a stronger presence by the 2026 assembly elections. Shah highlighted the party’s commitment to ending illegal border infiltration and corruption if they secure governance, urging citizens to consider change in leadership.


International News in English for 29 October 2024

Stay updated with the latest Global Stories and International News. This section highlights significant events and developments from around the world.

1. Ukrainian President Zelenskyy Highlights India’s Potential Role in Peace Talks with Russia
Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy emphasized Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s significant influence in mediating peace talks between Ukraine and Russia. In an interview with the Times of India, Zelenskyy expressed hope for India as a potential venue for negotiations, underscoring PM Modi’s diplomatic value in resolving conflicts. 

2. Social Media Platform X Suspends Iranian Leader’s New Hebrew-Language Account
The social media platform X (formerly Twitter) suspended an account opened by Iran’s Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, which had begun posting in Hebrew. The account was removed a day after its creation for violating platform rules, though the specific reason was not disclosed. Owned by Elon Musk, X has not provided further comments on the suspension.

3. UK’s Labour Party Suspends MP Mike Amesbury Following Video of Alleged Altercation
British Labour MP Mike Amesbury has been suspended from the party after a video reportedly showed him in an altercation, allegedly striking a man. The incident occurred in Frodsham, and Amesbury, representing Runcorn and Helsby, will not be counted as a Labour MP pending further investigation. Labour’s decision underscores a commitment to discipline within the party ranks.

4. China’s Central Bank Launches New Liquidity Facility to Stabilize Banking Sector
The People’s Bank of China introduced a monthly reverse repo facility to manage liquidity within the banking system. Aimed at maintaining liquidity and diversifying monetary policy tools, the facility will provide support for three- and six-month adjustments. This step is expected to reinforce the central bank’s liquidity measures over the next year, promoting financial stability in China.

5. Survey Shows Decline in Democratic Support Among Indian American Voters
A new Indian American Attitude Survey (IAAS) reveals a decline in Democratic Party support among Indian American voters, dropping to 47% from 56% in 2020. While the community continues to lean Democratic, the decrease suggests a shift in political alignment, providing insights into the evolving political landscape within this demographic group.

6. Shanghai Police Enforce Strict Regulations on Halloween Costumes Amid Political Tensions
In a bid to prevent politically themed gatherings during Halloween, authorities in Shanghai have implemented stringent measures, including detaining individuals in costumes. Police actions, particularly on Julu Road, have raised concerns about freedom of expression and public celebration. This reflects the broader political environment in China, where cultural events are closely monitored to avoid dissent and maintain social order.

7. Iran Warns of Retaliation Following Israeli Attack, Promising a Strong Response
Following an Israeli attack, Iran’s Foreign Ministry has declared that it will utilize all available means to respond effectively. Foreign Ministry spokesperson Esmaeil Baghaei emphasized that the nature of Iran’s response would depend on the specifics of the Israeli actions. This announcement highlights the escalating tensions between Iran and Israel and the potential for further conflict in the region.

8. Delaware Gears Up for Nationwide Elections on November 5, Encouraging Civic Participation
Delaware will participate in nationwide elections on November 5, allowing citizens to cast their votes on critical local and national issues. This election represents a vital opportunity for residents to engage in democracy, highlighting the importance of civic responsibility and participation in shaping their government and policies.

9. NATO Confirms Deployment of North Korean Troops to Assist Russia in Ukraine Conflict
NATO has confirmed that North Korean military units have been sent to Russia to support its operations in the ongoing conflict with Ukraine. Secretary-General Mark Rutte’s statement underscores the increasing complexity of international alliances and the implications of foreign troop involvement in the war, raising concerns about the balance of power in the region.

10. Microsoft Faces Slowest Revenue Growth Amid Concerns Over AI Product Adoption
Microsoft is projected to report its slowest quarterly revenue growth in a year, as investors express concerns about the pace of adoption for its artificial intelligence products. Despite being a leader in the AI sector, with significant investments in OpenAI, the company faces challenges in demonstrating strong demand for its offerings, particularly the enterprise-focused Copilot assistant.

1. Yashaswini Ghorpade and Krittwika Roy Bring Home Women’s Doubles Title from Italy
In a stunning display of skill, India’s Yashaswini Ghorpade and Krittwika Roy clinched the women’s doubles title at the WTT Feeder Cagliari 2024 table tennis tournament in Italy. The Indian duo triumphed over South Korea’s Yoo Siwoo and Kim Haeun with a score of 3-1 in the final.

Indian para badminton star Sukant Kadam excelled at the Japan Para Badminton International 2024, securing a gold medal in men’s singles and a silver medal in men’s doubles. In the singles final, Sukant dominated fellow Indian player Tarun, winning decisively with scores of 21-12 and 21-10.

Gary Kirsten has stepped down as the head coach of Pakistan’s white-ball cricket teams just six months into his two-year contract with the Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB). His departure comes just ahead of the upcoming ODI and T20 series against Australia. The PCB has appointed Jason Gillespie, the bowling coach, as the interim head coach.

4. Salima Tete Appointed Captain of Indian Women’s Hockey Team for Asian Champions Trophy
Salima Tete has been named captain of the 18-member Indian women’s hockey team for the Asian Champions Trophy, set to take place from November 11 to 20 at the newly developed Rajgir Hockey Stadium in Bihar. Tete will be supported by vice-captain Navneet Kaur. The Indian team enters the tournament with high expectations, aiming to defend their title after last year’s victory in Ranchi.

5. Inter Milan and Juventus Share Spoils in Epic 4-4 Serie A Draw
In an exhilarating match, Inter Milan and Juventus played to a dramatic 4-4 draw in Serie A on Sunday. Piotr Zielinski was instrumental for Inter, converting two penalties. The match was notable for its high intensity and for being the first in Serie A history where five goals were scored in the first half between these two rivals.

What Happened Today in History

The What Happened Today in History segment in the school assembly news headlines highlights significant events from history that occurred on the current date.

1. 1929: Stock Market Crash: “Black Tuesday”
On October 29, 1929, the U.S. stock market crashed in what is known as “Black Tuesday,” marking the start of the Great Depression. This economic crisis affected millions, leading to job losses and poverty around the world. It’s a reminder of how interconnected the world’s economies are.

2. 1945: First Computer Bug Recorded
On this day, Grace Hopper, a computer scientist, recorded the first “computer bug” when a moth got stuck in the circuits of the Harvard Mark II computer. This is where the term “debugging” originated, showing the quirky side of technology and the importance of problem-solving.

3. 1969: First-Ever ARPANET Message Sent
The first message over ARPANET, the precursor to the internet, was sent on October 29, 1969. Although only two letters, “LO,” were transmitted before the system crashed, it marked the beginning of the internet we use today, connecting people around the world.

4. 1998: John Glenn Returns to Space
On October 29, 1998, astronaut John Glenn returned to space at the age of 77, becoming the oldest person to fly in space. He was one of the original Mercury astronauts and served as an inspiration, showing that age doesn’t limit our curiosity or achievements.

5. 2004: European Union Approves its First Constitution
The European Union (EU) approved its first constitution on October 29, 2004, aiming to streamline its processes and improve cooperation between member countries. This historic step helped to strengthen the EU, making it a symbol of unity and collaboration in Europe.
