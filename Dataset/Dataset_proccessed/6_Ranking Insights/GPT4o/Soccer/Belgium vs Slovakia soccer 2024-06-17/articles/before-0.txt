EURO 2024 – Group E



Group E of EURO 2024 comprises Belgium, Slovakia, Romania, and Ukraine. The group matches are set from June 17 to June 26, 2024. Belgium, the group favorites, are led by Kevin De Bruyne and are expected to perform strongly under new manager Domenico Tedesco. Slovakia, with players like Milan Škriniar and Marek Hamšík, aim to leverage their solid defense to advance. Romania, featuring emerging talents like Ianis Hagi, seek to make a significant impact, while Ukraine, led by Oleksandr Zinchenko, hopes to draw strength from their resilient performances in recent years.

Group Review

Group E of EURO 2024 features Belgium, Slovakia, Romania, and Ukraine, ensuring a blend of competitive football and intriguing matchups. Belgium, under Domenico Tedesco, is the standout favorite, boasting world-class talent such as Kevin De Bruyne and Romelu Lukaku. Slovakia, managed by Francesco Calzona, hopes to build on a robust defensive lineup, with key players like Milan Škriniar. Romania, under the leadership of Edward Iordănescu, will rely on their emerging stars like Ianis Hagi to make an impact. Ukraine, led by Serhiy Rebrov, aims to capitalize on their strong team spirit and the skills of players like Oleksandr Zinchenko to progress beyond the group stage.

Belgium, with their experience and depth, are expected to dominate the group. Slovakia, with a balanced squad, looks to secure their place in the knockout stages. Romania, driven by a mix of youth and experience, is aiming to make a significant impact. Ukraine, often seen as the dark horses, could surprise with their resilience and teamwork. This group promises excitement and closely contested matches, with every team capable of advancing to the knockout rounds.

Predictions

Belgium is heavily favored to top Group E, given their star-studded lineup and consistent performances. Slovakia, with a solid defense, is expected to secure the second spot. Romania and Ukraine will likely battle for the third position, with both teams having the potential to cause upsets. Romania’s youthful energy and Ukraine’s resilience could make the group more unpredictable than expected.

Belgium vs Slovakia (June 17, Frankfurt): Belgium to win, utilizing their attacking prowess.

Romania vs Ukraine (June 17, Munich): A tightly contested match, potentially ending in a draw.

Slovakia vs Ukraine (June 21, Düsseldorf): Slovakia to edge out a win, relying on their defensive strength.

Belgium vs Romania (June 22, Cologne): Belgium to secure a comfortable win.

Slovakia vs Romania (June 26, Frankfurt): Romania to win, leveraging their attacking talents.

Ukraine vs Belgium (June 26, Stuttgart): Belgium to win, although Ukraine could provide a tough challenge.
