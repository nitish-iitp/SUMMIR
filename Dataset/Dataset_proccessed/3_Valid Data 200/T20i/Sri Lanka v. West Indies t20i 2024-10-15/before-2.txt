Match Preview

Sri Lankan fans are in for a treat with a three-match T20I series taking place on their home turf. The opening match delivered an electrifying contest, showcasing the fierce rivalry between Sri Lanka and West Indies. With the West Indies currently leading the series 1-0, the dynamics of the series have shifted, setting the stage for an intense showdown.

Historically, the two teams have been evenly matched in their last ten encounters, with the West Indies securing six victories while Sri Lanka has claimed four. As the second match approaches, Sri Lanka is determined to level the series, while the West Indies aim to secure an unassailable lead. Fans are eagerly anticipating another exciting clash as the teams battle it out on the field, promising high-stakes action and memorable moments for all.

In a thrilling encounter in Sri Lanka, the home team set a challenging total of 179 runs, losing seven wickets in their 20 overs. Charith Asalanka starred with a strong 59 runs off 35 balls, while Kamindu Mendis supported him well with 51 runs from 40 balls. Kusal Mendis added a quick 19 runs, but Sri Lanka ended their innings with a difficult target for the West Indies. In response, the visitors showcased their batting prowess, reaching 180 runs for the loss of five wickets in just 19.1 overs. Romario Shepherd excelled with the ball, taking two wickets for 39 runs. Gudakesh Motie and Shamar Springer also contributed with one wicket each, leading to a convincing five-wicket victory for the West Indies, demonstrating their strength in the series.

In the exciting second innings, West Indies displayed their explosive batting as they chased down the target of 180 runs comfortably. Brandon King was exceptional, scoring 63 runs off just 33 balls, showcasing his aggressive hitting. Evin Lewis also made a vital contribution with 50 runs from 28 deliveries, keeping the team well ahead of the required run rate. Roston Chase added 19 runs off 16 balls, helping secure a convincing victory. On the bowling side, Sri Lankan bowlers battled hard but struggled against the strong West Indies lineup. Matheesha Pathirana stood out with two wickets for 27 runs in 3.1 overs, while Kamindu Mendis and Maheesh Theekshana each took one wicket. Despite their efforts, the West Indies clinched the match, underscoring their batting strength and resilience.
