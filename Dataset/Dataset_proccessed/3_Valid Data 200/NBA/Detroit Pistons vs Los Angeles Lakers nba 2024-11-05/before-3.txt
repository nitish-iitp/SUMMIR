The LA Lakers continued their five-game road swing with a matchup against the Detroit Pistons on Monday. LA lost 115-103, dropping to 1-3 away from Crypto.com Arena.

Anthony Davis, a game-time decision, finished with 37 points, nine rebounds and four assists. LeBron James contributed 20 points, 11 assists and eight rebounds.

Meanwhile, the Pistons leaned on a more concerted attack to win. Jaden Ivey had 26 points while five others hit double-digit scoring. Cade Cunningham stuffed the box score with 17 points, 11 rebounds and 11 assists. Jalen Duren battled AD all game and tallied 11 points, 14 rebounds, one steal and one block.

Detroit improved its record to 3-5 while Los Angeles dropped to 4-3.

LA Lakers vs Detroit Pistons: Game summary

Playing on short rest, the Pistons came out fast and grabbed a 15-7 lead after five minutes. LA pushed the pace to open the game, but the hosts thrived in the run-and-gun style. Behind Tim Hardaway Jr. and Tobias Harris, Detroit put the home team ahead 33-22 after one quarter.

LeBron James had eight second-quarter points while Anthony Davis finished the first half with 18. The Pistons leaned on a balanced scoring attack to offset the Lakers superstars' output. Detroit led 67-53 at halftime.
