ODI World Cup 2023, Match 5: India vs Australia Match Preview
India and Australia lock horns in the fifth match in ODI World Cup 2023 on Sunday, October 8.

Anticipate an epic cricketing spectacle as two cricket powerhouses, Australia and India, gear up to clash on October 8, 2023, at the revered MA Chidambaram Stadium. This Indo-Australian rivalry, which has consistently lit up the cricketing world, boasts a history of 12 memorable encounters in the ICC World Cup. Australia has often held the upper hand in this historic rivalry, securing victory on 8 occasions, firmly establishing their dominance.

Both teams are fiercely determined to secure a crucial World Cup win to set their campaign in motion. Cricket fanatics globally can look forward to an exhilarating contest that promises to keep them perched on the edge of their seats. India enters the ICC World Cup 2023 as one of the frontrunners, bolstered by a talented squad. As hosts of the tournament, the Men in Blue not only relish home advantage but also field a formidable team.

The hosts have enjoyed a spectacular year in ODI cricket, emerging victorious in four out of five series and triumphing in the Asia Cup. Their two ODI series against Australia this year, with a win and a loss, add an element of anticipation and competitiveness to their upcoming clash.

Australia boasts a remarkable history of success in World Cup tournaments, having clinched the prestigious title on five occasions. Their track record cements them as the undisputed giants of World Cup history. Their legacy of success and reputation for fearless cricket make them an undeniable force in the cricketing realm.

Australia's performance in ODIs this year has been full of ups and downs. They started strongly with a series victory against India but encountered a setback when they narrowly lost a series to South Africa (2-3). The challenges continued as they later suffered another series defeat at the hands of India.

IND vs AUS Pitch and Conditions

The Chepauk Stadium pitch traditionally favours spin bowlers, creating a heaven for spinners. It tends to be slow and low, offering turn and grip to spinners as the match unfolds. Batters must exercise patience and adapt to the sluggish pace of the pitch. Batting first is generally seen as an advantage here, as chasing becomes an arduous task due to the pitch's deteriorating condition.

Batters who can settle in have the opportunity to accumulate runs, but they must remain vigilant against the spinning deliveries. The pitch's slow nature can pose challenges for playing aggressive shots. Teams would be wise to opt for bowling attacks that heavily feature spin to exploit these conditions, and a total of 270-280 runs would be considered as a competitive score in this setting.

IND vs AUS, Probable Playing XIs for ODI World Cup 2023, Match 5
India

India possesses a strong batting lineup, although the potential absence of Shubman Gill in the opening game due to health concerns could pose a challenge. However, Ishan Kishan, who has had success as an opener in the past, may partner Rohit Sharma on October 8th, offering a viable alternative. In terms of exploiting the pitch conditions, India might consider including Ravichandran Ashwin in the squad instead of Shardul Thakur. The middle order appears stable, with Shreyas Iyer's proficiency in handling spin making him well-equipped to counter the threat posed by Adam Zampa.

Rohit Sharma (C), Ishan Kishan, Virat Kohli, Shreyas Iyer, KL Rahul (WK), Hardik Pandya, Ravindra Jadeja, Ravichandran Ashwin, Jasprit Bumrah, Mohammed Siraj, Kuldeep Yadav

Australia

Australia, on the contrary, appears to be in a miss and a hit form. Key players like David Warner and Mitchel Marsh have been performing well, and Steve Smith, with his experience in these conditions, leads a strong middle order. However, Australia's spin bowling department is a concern, with Adam Zampa being the primary spinner in conditions favouring spin. If the conditions persist, Mitchell Starc might attempt to extract some late swing from the ball.

Mitchell Marsh, David Warner, Steven Smith, Marnus Labuschagne, Alex Carey (WK), Marcus Stoinis, Glenn Maxwell, Pat Cummins (c), Mitchell Starc, Josh Hazlewood, Adam Zampa
