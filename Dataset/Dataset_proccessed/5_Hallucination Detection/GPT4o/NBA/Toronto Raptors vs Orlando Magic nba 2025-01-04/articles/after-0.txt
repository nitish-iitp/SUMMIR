RAPTORS
And, poof, the Raptors put the magic back in Orlando’s shooting with poor defensive effort

The normally poor-shooting Magic score 37 points in the opening quarter and hit 43 per cent of their three-pointers. 

Updated Jan. 4, 2025 at 1:24 p.m.
Jan. 4, 2025
3 min read
Save

Raptors forward Scottie Barnes misses a dunk against the Orlando Magic on Friday. Barnes still finished with 20 points and Jakob Poeltl had 25 but Toronto couldn’t overcome a slow start.

Steve Russell/Toronto Star
By Doug SmithSports Reporter

The Raptors proved to be the perfect tonic for the low-scoring, poor-shooting Orlando Magic.

And while some of the credit needs to go to the Magic, a large chunk of blame has to go to the Raptors, who came up with a lackadaisical defensive effort at the Scotiabank Arena on Friday, beaten 106-97 by injury-ravaged Orlando.
